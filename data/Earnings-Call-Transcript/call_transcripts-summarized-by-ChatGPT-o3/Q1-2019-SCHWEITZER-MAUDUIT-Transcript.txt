# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q1 2019 Earnings Call Summary  

Schweitzer-Mauduit International opened 2019 with consolidated net sales of **$258 million** and **adjusted EPS of $0.68**, a result the Company said was “in line with plan,” allowing management to reaffirm full-year guidance.  Key non-operational headwinds—chiefly a $0.06 EPS hit from deferred-compensation accounting tied to the stock’s rebound, $0.04 of incremental interest expense from last year’s refinancing, $0.02 of higher IT investment, and a $0.02 currency drag—masked otherwise solid operating momentum, particularly in the Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS) segment.  

### Consolidated Highlights  
• Net sales slipped 1 % year over year (up 1 % ex-FX) to **$258 million**.  
• Adjusted operating profit was **$35.5 million**, and adjusted EBITDA **$44.7 million** (both down yr/yr, chiefly on FX, deferred-comp accruals and higher interest).  
• GAAP EPS was **$0.56** versus $0.68 a year ago; **adjusted EPS was $0.68 versus $0.83**.  
• Normalized tax rate was **22.7 %**; management still expects a “slightly higher” full-year rate versus 2018.  
• Q1 free cash flow was **$4 million** (seasonally weak quarter); full-year **> $100 million** FCF outlook and **$35-40 million** CapEx plan are unchanged.  
• Net leverage (credit-facility definition) ticked up to **2.6× net-debt/adjusted-EBITDA** from 2.5× at FY-end 2018.  

### Segment Performance  

Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• Net sales rose **5 % organically to $121 million**.  
• Adjusted operating profit jumped **23 % to $20 million**; margin expanded **250 bp to 16.6 %**.  
• Growth drivers: robust demand for transportation surface-protection films (outperforming global auto builds), strong filtration (RO water, specialty media), double-digit growth in medical products and stable infrastructure/construction.  
• Tailwinds: full-quarter benefit of the **Austin plant closure** (footprint-optimization synergies) and lower polypropylene costs flowing through the P&L.  
• Management expects the normal seasonality—stronger Q2/Q3, softer Q1/Q4—to hold; additional upside could come from resin deflation, though prices may rise modestly later in 2019.  

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• Net sales declined **6 % (-2 % ex-FX)** to roughly **$137 million**; euro weakness the principal FX culprit.  
• Volume fell **9 %**, but **price/mix was a positive 7 %**, driven by contractual pulp-based escalators and stronger LIP (low-ignition-propensity) cigarette-paper mix.  
• Heat-not-burn (HnB) recon paper volumes were down versus an exceptionally strong launch-driven Q1-18; management still views HnB as a long-term growth opportunity, but order patterns will remain “lumpy.”  
• Non-tobacco declines (printing & writing papers, certain battery-separator grades) also pressured volume.  
• Adjusted operating margin was **20.9 %**, down **220 bp** year over year; pulp escalators recouped most wood-pulp inflation, but lower volumes, higher energy and other material costs weighed on efficiency.  
• Pulp: NBSK costs were still up yr/yr in Q1; modest easing could occur in Q2/Q3, but management is not counting on a sharp decline.  

### Raw-Material Update  
• Polypropylene peaked in Q3-18; Q1 benefited from lower-cost inventory, with another modest benefit expected in Q2. Biggest yr/yr raw-material tailwind should appear in Q3.  
• Wood pulp prices remain above prior-year levels, though contractual pass-throughs offset most of the impact; management sees potential moderation later in the year but no meaningful deflator mechanisms on contract pricing.  

### Strategic & Operational Developments  
• The **new surface-protection film line in Europe** is in customer qualification; commercial shipments should ramp in 2H-19. A follow-on line in China is under evaluation.  
• A **specialty filtration paper** developed by the EP mills and sold through AMS channels is gaining traction, illustrating cross-selling progress.  
• M&A remains an active part of SWM’s capital-allocation strategy; no specific targets disclosed.  

### Outlook & Guidance  
Management reiterated all full-year targets:  
• **Adjusted EPS range unchanged** (specific figure not restated on the call).  
• **> $100 million free cash flow** and **$35-40 million CapEx**.  
• Continued organic growth and margin expansion in AMS; stable-to-slightly lower volumes but healthier mix and pricing in EP.  
• Net-debt/EBITDA expected to decline as free cash flow accelerates in seasonally stronger quarters.  

In sum, SWM delivered Q1 results consistent with internal expectations, maintained leverage discipline, funded growth initiatives (IT upgrade, new film capacity) and kept 2019 financial guidance intact despite FX headwinds, deferred-comp volatility and higher interest expense from its 2018 refinancing.
