# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) Q1 2020 Earnings Call – Financial Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International entered the COVID-19 crisis from a position of relative strength, and first-quarter 2020 results exceeded internal expectations despite early pandemic disruptions.

Management’s highest priority has been employee safety; 25 of 26 global sites remained operational throughout the quarter and the lone closure in upstate New York is expected to reopen in May.  The company’s diversified mix—roughly 40 % tobacco papers, plus replenishment-driven filtration, growing medical products, and niche industrial films—helped insulate results, although transportation films tied to automotive demand were hurt in March and are expected to face greater pressure in Q2.

## Consolidated Q1 2020 Performance
• Net sales increased 1 % year over year.  
• Adjusted operating profit grew 12 %; adjusted EBITDA rose 10 %.  
• GAAP EPS climbed 29 % to $0.72; adjusted EPS rose 25 % to $0.85.  
• Lower pulp and resin costs aided margins and are expected to stay favorable while global demand is weak.  

Because of the pandemic’s uncertain trajectory, SWM withdrew full-year guidance but reiterated that it “expects to remain solidly profitable with strong cash flow.”

## Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)
• Sales rose 2 % to $— (Tekra contributed $5.5 million after the 13 March close); excluding Tekra, organic sales fell 3 %.  
• Adjusted operating profit declined $0.7 million (-4 %) as the drop in higher-margin transportation films outweighed margin gains elsewhere and lower resin costs.  
• Transportation Film: aftermarket paint-protection and surface-protection films weakened sharply with global auto shutdowns; management foresees the biggest profit hit in Q2 but remains bullish long term, citing low consumer penetration and new capacity in China.  
• Medical Products: fastest-growing AMS line; melt-blown media for N95 masks, hospital netting, gowns, and packaging are at full capacity (lines debottlenecked >20 % and running 24/7 yet remain order-constrained).  
• Filtration: revenue grew and is expected to trend higher through 2020; water and process sub-segments remain stable, while automotive filtration is softer.  
• Infrastructure & Construction: recorded solid Q1 growth but likely to soften as global stimulus timing is unclear.  
• Tekra Acquisition: 25 % of Tekra revenues are medical, another portion transportation; team is reallocating coating assets toward medical demand.

## Engineered Papers (EP)
• Sales rose 1 % (3 % ex-FX). Volumes fell 4 % in line with normal tobacco attrition, but price/mix improved 7 %.  
• Segment margin expanded 330 bps; wood-pulp deflation, higher-value Recon and heat-not-burn products, and ongoing cost cuts produced double-digit profit growth.  
• Management sees the tobacco-paper franchise as relatively COVID-resilient; Q1 may have included modest customer pull-forward and pantry stocking, but no fundamental demand shift is expected.  
• Wrapper & Binder output was temporarily hampered by the New York plant closure; catch-up production is planned once the site reopens.

## Liquidity, Leverage & Capital Allocation
• Cash on hand: $127 million at 31 March.  
• Revolving credit facility: $500 million capacity; $212 million drawn, leaving $284 million available.  
• Total liquidity: approximately $411 million.  
• Net debt: $628 million; net-debt-to-adjusted-EBITDA 2.7× versus a 2020 covenant ceiling of 5.0×—ample cushion.  
• Debt maturities: revolver due 2023, $197 million term loan due 2025, $350 million senior notes due 2026.  
• Q1 CapEx was $8 million; the original 2020 plan of $35-$40 million includes discretionary growth projects that can be deferred if conditions worsen.  
• The dividend and overall capital allocation strategy remain unchanged.

## Outlook & Strategic Priorities
Management believes roughly two-thirds of SWM’s revenue (tobacco, filtration, medical) should stay relatively durable. Near-term headwinds are concentrated in transportation films and, potentially, certain industrial and construction markets. The company is actively pressure-testing scenarios, maintains sufficient liquidity to fund operations and dividends, and is using the downturn to accelerate product development and integration of Tekra. Leadership views the crisis as an opportunity to gain share against less nimble competitors and to emerge with an even stronger, more diversified portfolio.
