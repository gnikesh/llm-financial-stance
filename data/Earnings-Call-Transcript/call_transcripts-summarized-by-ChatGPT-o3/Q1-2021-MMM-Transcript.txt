# 3M Company – Q1 2021 Earnings Call Summary  

3M opened 2021 with record first-quarter sales and strong earnings while keeping full-year guidance intact despite mounting raw-material and logistics inflation.

Sales, earnings and cash flow  
• Net sales were $8.85 billion, up 9.6 percent year-over-year and up 8 percent organically.  
• Adjusted EPS rose 27 percent to $2.77, beating consensus by $0.48.  
• Adjusted operating income reached $2.0 billion; the 22.5 percent operating margin expanded 170 basis points.  
• Adjusted EBITDA margin approached 28 percent.  
• Free cash flow of $1.4 billion jumped 49 percent; conversion was 86 percent.  

Balance-sheet and capital deployment  
• Net debt fell to $13 billion, almost $5 billion lower than a year ago; net-debt/EBITDA is now 1.4× (down from 2.2×).  
• 3M redeemed an additional $450 million of 2022 debt early, lifting Q1 interest expense by $0.02 per share.  
• The quarterly dividend was raised for the 63rd consecutive year and, together with $231 million of share repurchases, returned $1.1 billion to shareholders.  
• Q1 capex was $310 million; full-year capex is still seen at $1.8–$2.0 billion.  

Cost inflation and pricing  
• Higher raw-material and freight costs trimmed Q1 margin by 20 bps and EPS by $0.01.  
• Management now expects a $0.30–$0.50 EPS headwind for 2021 (was flat to –$0.10) and sees a 75–125 bp operating-margin drag in Q2 even after price increases.  
• Pricing was a 70 bp positive in Q1; benefits should strengthen in the second half as increases gain traction.  

Guidance unchanged  
• Organic sales growth: 3 – 6 percent.  
• Adjusted EPS: $9.20 – $9.70.  
• Free-cash-flow conversion: 95 – 105 percent.  
Uncertainties cited include COVID-19 dynamics (respirator demand, elective medical procedures), semiconductor shortages, supply-chain tightness, and rising legal expenses (PFAS and Combat Arms litigation).  

Segment performance (organic growth, Q1)  
• Safety & Industrial +10.3 % (6.4 pts from pandemic respirators); margin 24.4 % (+70 bps).  
• Transportation & Electronics +9.8 %; electronics high-teens, auto OEM +21 % vs +14 % global builds; margin 23.3 % (+260 bps).  
• Healthcare +9.3 %; respirator demand offset soft elective procedures; margin 22.7 % (+120 bps).  
• Consumer +7.8 %; Home Improvement double-digit, Stationery & Office returned to growth; margin 21.1 % (flat).  

Geographies  
• Asia-Pacific +13 % organic (China +32 %), Americas +6 % (U.S. +7 %), EMEA +6 %.  

Sustainability and growth investments  
• 3M will invest ~$1 billion over 20 years to reach carbon neutrality by 2050 (50 % reduction by 2030) and cut water use 25 %.  
• 40 % of global electricity is renewable (target 100 %).  
• Commitment to cut virgin fossil-based plastic by 125 million lbs by 2025.  
• Priority growth platforms (e-mobility, biopharma filtration, etc.) grew 10 % in Q1.  

Restructuring & legal  
• Q2 restructuring charge expected at $25 – $50 million (part of a $250 – $300 million 2021 program; $150 million booked in Q1).  
• PFAS reserve rose ~$50 million and respirator litigation accrual by ~$20 million; legal costs will rise in 2H as trials approach.  

Other notable items  
• FX added 40 bps to Q1 margin and $0.13 to EPS; full-year EPS benefit still estimated at ~$0.15.  
• Lower tax rate (16.4 % vs expected 20-21 %) contributed $0.14 to Q1 EPS.  
• Distributed 630 million respirators in Q1 (over 90 % to healthcare).  

Management outlook  
3M sees the global economy strengthening but uneven, expects respirator demand to remain solid yet deliver a smaller year-on-year uplift, and is actively raising prices, improving yields and navigating supply-chain constraints to protect earnings and cash flow while continuing to invest in growth, productivity and sustainability.
