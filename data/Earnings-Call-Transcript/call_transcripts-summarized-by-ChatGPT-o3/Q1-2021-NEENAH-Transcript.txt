# Neenah, Inc. – Q1 2021 Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah began 2021 with results that exceeded internal plans and set new highs in several operating metrics, while also closing the strategic acquisition of ITASA in early April. Management reiterated its long-term strategy of focusing on four growth platforms—Filtration, Specialty Coatings, Engineered Materials and Premium Packaging—backed by disciplined capital allocation and an active M&A program.

### Consolidated first-quarter results  
• Net sales were $227 million, up 10 % versus Q4 2020 but still slightly below the prior-year quarter because Fine Paper & Packaging (FPP) has not yet fully recovered.  
• Adjusted operating income reached $26 million, a 25 % sequential improvement; consolidated operating margins expanded to the mid-teens in both reportable segments.  
• Adjusted EPS was $1.04, excluding $0.55 per share of primarily ITASA-related transaction costs. In Q1 2020 adjusted EPS was $1.12 (excluding $0.15 of unusual items); the current quarter marks only the second time in 11 quarters that EPS has exceeded $1.  
• Cash from operations increased to $20.7 million (Q1 2020: $14.2 million).  
• Capital spending was $4.8 million; full-year capex is projected at roughly $35 million.  
• The company ended the quarter with $41 million of cash, no borrowings on the revolver and total liquidity just under $200 million. Balance-sheet leverage after the ITASA closing is projected at roughly 3× debt/EBITDA.

### Segment performance  
Technical Products  
• Sales rose 10 % year on year to $145 million, driven by volume and a stronger euro; Filtration alone grew more than 20 % and delivered record sales and profit.  
• Adjusted operating income was $20 million, more than 20 % above Q1 2020; margin improvement reflected higher volume, disciplined spending and favorable FX.  
• Segment mix has been regrouped: Filtration (~40 % of segment sales), the newly formed Specialty Coatings sub-segment that now includes ITASA (~30 %), and Industrial Solutions (~30 %).  

Fine Paper & Packaging  
• Sales were $82 million, up 8 % sequentially but below the prior year because Commercial Print (advertising/marketing) remains weak.  
• Adjusted operating income was $13 million versus $17 million a year ago; lower volume and mix were partly offset by SG&A savings.  
• Premium Packaging and Consumer Products now account for almost half of the segment and are pacing the recovery; Commercial Print (including Publishing) makes up the other half.

### Cost inflation and pricing actions  
Raw-material escalation for fibers, chemicals and freight has accelerated. Management now forecasts 2021 input-cost headwinds “north of $30 million” versus the $20 million increase discussed in February. Technical Products will bear roughly twice the dollar impact of FPP because of its higher polymer and chemical exposure.  
• The second quarter alone is expected to absorb a net incremental cost hit of about $7 million versus Q1.  
• Multiple price increases have already been announced or implemented; contractual pass-through mechanisms, additional direct pricing, volume growth, improved mix and Neenah Operating System savings are expected to fully offset inflation, though timing lags will pressure Q2.

### Liquidity, debt and tax  
• The ITASA acquisition was financed by upsizing the Term Loan B from $200 million to $450 million; the variable rate dropped from roughly 5 % to 3.5 %.  
• Quarterly interest expense going forward is estimated at $4.7 million, of which $1.2 million is non-cash amortization.  
• The effective tax rate was 21 % in Q1; full-year 2021 is expected to be around 22 %, with cash taxes running at roughly two-thirds of book taxes thanks to R&D credits.

### ITASA acquisition  
• Closed early April; ITASA is a global producer of release liners with annual sales of ~€140 million (≈$140 million) and mid-teen EBITDA margins, equating to roughly $35 million in sales per quarter.  
• Management expects the deal to be immediately accretive to EPS even after higher post-acquisition D&A (acquired assets typically represent 6–8 % of sales versus Neenah’s organic 3–4 %).  
• Targeted run-rate synergies are about $4 million, split between commercial revenue opportunities (notably in North America where Neenah has strong tape and abrasive relationships) and cost savings in procurement, logistics and overhead.  
• ITASA’s corporate tax rate is in the mid-20 % range.

### Strategic, operational and ESG highlights  
• Filtration continues to see robust demand across transportation, air, water and industrial markets; recurring face-mask media sales (~$4 million per quarter) reinforce Neenah’s position in indoor-air quality applications.  
• Premium Packaging and Consumer Products booked sizeable wins with major retailers and in the spirits, beauty and tech verticals; most of this business ships in 2H 2021.  
• The Neenah Operating System (lean manufacturing) has begun at two large facilities and is expected to unlock $20 million of annual value once fully deployed.  
• March represented the first month in company history with zero recordable safety incidents across the global footprint.  
• The 2021 Corporate Sustainability Report details progress on carbon, water and energy reduction and highlights greater board- and workforce diversity; the CEO has joined the “CEO Action for Diversity & Inclusion” coalition.

### Outlook  
• Technical Products should revert to normal seasonality—softer mid-year volumes, particularly in Europe—while Fine Paper & Packaging is expected to keep recovering sequentially through 2021.  
• Despite unprecedented raw-material inflation, management believes the combination of price, mix, volume growth, cost controls and synergy capture will fully neutralize the headwinds over the course of the year.  
• Neenah’s multi-year objectives remain intact: approximately 5 % average annual sales growth, sustainable mid-teen EBIT margins, and disciplined M&A to expand the four targeted growth platforms.

Management closed by reaffirming a solid balance-sheet position (cash plus undrawn revolver), a clear path to integrating ITASA, and an unwavering commitment to shareholder returns and safety.
