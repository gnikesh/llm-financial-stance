# NVIDIA FY2021 Q1 Earnings Call – Comprehensive Financial & Strategic Summary

NVIDIA entered fiscal-year 2021 with record data-center sales, resilient gaming demand and the first partial contribution from the Mellanox acquisition, offsetting COVID-19 related pressure in automotive and Pro Visualization. Management reiterated strong visibility in data center, guided to further sequential revenue growth in Q2, and set a new non-GAAP gross-margin baseline of roughly 66 %.

## Q1 FY2021 (13 weeks ended 26 April 2020)

• Revenue reached **$3.08 bn** (+39 % y/y;  –1 % q/q), edging past guidance on upside in Gaming and Data Center.  
• Segment performance:  
 – **Data Center:** $1.14 bn (+80 % y/y; +18 % q/q), first quarter above $1 bn; A100 (Ampere) already in production and contributed “meaningfully”.  
 – **Gaming:** $1.34 bn (+27 % y/y;  –10 % q/q). Stay-at-home trends drove 50 % surge in GeForce gaming hours; GeForce laptop revenue hit fastest y/y growth in six quarters; Nintendo Switch demand remained strong.  
 – **Professional Visualization:** $307 m (+15 % y/y;  –7 % q/q) as Turing and mobile workstations offset COVID-19 slow-downs.  
 – **Automotive:** $155 m ( –7 % y/y;  –5 % q/q); industry shutdowns weighed on revenue.  
• GAAP gross margin 65.1 %; non-GAAP 65.8 % (+60 bps y/y, +40 bps q/q) on favourable mix and higher data-center volumes.  
• Operating expenses: GAAP $1.03 bn (+10 % y/y); non-GAAP $821 m (+9 % y/y).  
• EPS: GAAP **$1.47** (+130 % y/y); non-GAAP **$1.80** (+105 % y/y).  
• Operating cash flow **$909 m**.  
• Mellanox closed 27 April; its March-quarter revenue was **$429 m** (+40 % y/y) with gross margins in the mid/high-60 % range. Management expects the deal to be immediately accretive to non-GAAP GM, EPS and free cash flow.

## Q2 FY2021 Guidance (includes full-quarter Mellanox)

• Revenue **$3.65 bn ± 2 %**; Mellanox to contribute “low-teens” percent of total.  
• GAAP / non-GAAP gross margin **58.6 % / 66.0 % ± 50 bps**. The GAAP step-down reflects one-time acquisition accounting charges; underlying non-GAAP margin stays at the new 66 % level.  
• Operating expenses: GAAP **$1.52 bn**, non-GAAP **$1.04 bn** (full-year targets: $5.7 bn / $4.1 bn). Increase driven by Mellanox integration, stock-based comp (pulled forward six months to support employees during COVID-19), 53rd fiscal week, and continued R&D hiring.  
• Other income/expense: GAAP +$50 m; non-GAAP +$45 m.  
• Tax rate: **9 % ± 1 %** GAAP and non-GAAP.  
• Cap-ex: **$225–250 m**.  
• Segment outlook:  
 – **Automotive** expected **–40 % q/q** as global production remains depressed.  
 – **Pro Visualization** to decline sequentially.  
 – **Gaming** to rise low-to-mid single digits q/q; desktop seasonally down but offset by strong laptop and console (Switch) demand; continued e-tail strength mitigates iCafe/retail closures.  
 – **Data Center** visibility “solid”; A100 ramp under way across hyperscale, verticals and HPC.

## Balance Sheet & Debt

Management did not disclose new borrowings; Mellanox was financed with cash and previously issued debt (unchanged from prior disclosures). Free cash flow is expected to remain positive and accretive post-acquisition.

## Strategic & Product Highlights

1. **Ampere Architecture & A100 GPU**  
   • Delivers up to **20×** perf-per-socket in both training and inference versus Volta, introducing new TF32 and FP64 tensor cores plus sparsity support.  
   • First elastic GPU: one A100 can be partitioned into seven isolated instances, or multiple A100s can be fused via third-gen NVLink.  
   • Adopted by AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, Alibaba, Baidu, Dell, HPE, and leading super-computing centres (NERSC, Jülich, Argonne).  
   • Powers **DGX A100** (1–56 GPU configurable) and edge servers **EGX A100** / **EGX Jetson Xavier NX**.

2. **Mellanox Integration & Networking Stack**  
   • Mellanox becomes NVIDIA’s Networking BU; Israel now a key technology hub.  
   • Combines high-performance InfiniBand/Ethernet NICs & switches with NVIDIA compute for data-center-scale architectures.  
   • Cumulus Networks acquisition (open networking OS) enhances full-stack innovation.

3. **Software & Vertical Frameworks**  
   • CUDA 11 with RAPIDS accelerates **Apache Spark** end-to-end (Adobe saw 7× faster, 90 % cost savings).  
   • New frameworks: **Merlin** (deep recommender systems), **Jarvis** (real-time conversational AI).  
   • Continued build-out of **Clara** (genomics, imaging, guardian), **Isaac** (robotics), **Drive** (autonomous vehicles), **Metropolis** (smart cities), **Aerial** (5G).  

4. **Gaming & RTX Momentum**  
   • 100 new RTX/Max-Q laptop models (RTX 2080 SUPER/2070 SUPER; RTX 2060 laptops at $999).  
   • Minecraft RTX beta and 33+ forthcoming ray-traced titles (e.g., Cyberpunk 2077) enlarge upgrade cycle.  
   • GeForce NOW exited beta; 2 m users, 650 streaming titles; ray-tracing remains a unique advantage.

5. **Automotive & Edge AI**  
   • COVID-19 halting auto production, but long-term ADAS/AV roadmap intact: Xpeng P7 shipping with Drive Xavier; **Orin** (Ampere-based) will offer 6× Xavier performance.  
   • BMW adopting **NVIDIA Isaac** robotics for factory automation; USPS and Walmart cited as early EGX edge-AI customers.

## Management Commentary on COVID-19 Impact & Structural Trends

• Pandemic accelerates enterprise cloud migration, AI adoption, and demand for remote working/gaming.  
• Emphasis on “computational defence systems” for future global health challenges drives HPC/AI spending.  
• Expect lasting growth in robotics, logistics automation and edge computing as industries adapt to social-distancing norms.

## Capital Allocation & Events

• Integration spending front-loaded; no change to dividend policy (unchanged $0.16 per share quarterly).  
• Upcoming investor engagements: virtual presentations at Cowen, Morgan Stanley, BofA, Needham, NASDAQ, and an Evercore-hosted Q&A with Jensen Huang on 28 May.

NVIDIA exits the quarter with record data-center scale, a premium margin profile, and a broadened addressable market spanning GPUs, networking and full-stack AI solutions, positioning the company for continued top-line growth and EPS accretion despite near-term automotive weakness.
