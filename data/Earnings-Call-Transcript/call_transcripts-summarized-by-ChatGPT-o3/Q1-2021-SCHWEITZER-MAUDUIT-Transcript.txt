# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q1 2021 Earnings Call Summary  

Schweitzer-Mauduit International opened 2021 with robust operating momentum, continued strength in its Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS) franchise, disciplined cost control in Engineered Papers (EP), and the closing of the transformational Scapa Group acquisition. Management reiterated confidence in sustaining growth despite rising input‐cost inflation and logistics friction, and for the first time since announcing the deal, provided full-year earnings guidance that incorporates Scapa.

### Consolidated Q1 2021 Performance  
• Net sales rose 10 % year-on-year (3 % organically).  
• Adjusted operating profit grew 14 %; adjusted EBITDA increased 13 %.  
• Adjusted EPS reached **$1.02**, up 20 % from $0.85 a year ago (GAAP EPS $0.68 versus $0.72).  
• The adjusted tax rate was 20.5 %, 80 bp lower than last year, and JV income improved slightly.  
• EPS reconciliation items included $0.22 per share of Scapa transaction costs and a $0.11 per-share Brazil tax settlement gain.

### Segment Results  

Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• Sales advanced **33 %** to approximately $252 million; organic growth was **15 %** (about 10 % even if Transportation is excluded).  
• Transportation sales climbed 20 %; within that, paint-protection film exceeded 50 % growth as OEM and aftermarket demand normalized and customers restocked.  
• Filtration revenue increased more than 20 %; air-filtration media exceeded 50 % growth, and water as well as semiconductor-related filtration registered solid double-digit gains.  
• The existing healthcare (formerly “medical”) business posted strong growth in both consumer finger-bandage substrates and specialty packaging/face-mask materials.  
• Construction products benefited from higher oil-and-gas perimeter-control demand, solar-farm installations, and highway infrastructure projects.  
• Segment adjusted operating margin expanded 140 bp to **17.1 %**, matching record first-quarter levels despite higher polypropylene and logistics costs. AMS adjusted operating profit was up more than 40 %.

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• Revenue declined **10 %**: (i) the Spotswood, NJ facility closure led a key customer to deplete legacy inventory prior to switching to other sites, and (ii) the company continued to exit low-margin printing papers.  
• Heat-not-burn tobacco papers posted “very strong” growth, and select specialty papers were stable.  
• Adjusted segment operating margin widened 100 bp to **25.2 %**; adjusted operating profit fell just 6 %, demonstrating effective cost actions and benefits from the Spotswood shutdown.  

### Inflation & Pricing  
• Polypropylene resin, pulp and other inputs are rising sharply; the company is implementing price increases across most product lines and expects offsets to be visible by Q3.  
• Management acknowledged possible near-term “choppiness,” but reiterated full confidence in safeguarding profitability.

### Scapa Acquisition (closed 15 Apr 2021)  
Transaction economics  
• Purchase price: **~$630 million** including net debt.  
• Financing: new $350 million Term Loan B plus revolver borrowings.  
• Pro-forma covenant net-debt/EBITDA at close: **~4.3×**.  

Scapa financial profile (GBP/USD 1.38)  
• FY20 (pre-COVID, year ended Mar-2020): **$440 million sales, $55 million EBITDA**.  
• FY21: pandemic depressed both healthcare (fewer elective procedures) and industrial sales; industrial trends are steadily recovering, healthcare lags.  

Accretion outlook  
• 2021 guidance assumes **$0.10** of EPS accretion from nine months of ownership plus **$5 million** of near-term cost synergies (mainly public-company and administrative).  
• 2022 accretion expected at **≥ $0.50** per share without assuming a full return to pre-COVID revenue; faster elective-surgery recovery would create upside.  
• Incremental interest expense estimated at **$22 million** in 2021 and **$29 million** in 2022; tax rate modeled in the low-to-mid-20 % range.

Strategic fit  
• Scapa nearly triples SWM’s healthcare revenue to roughly **$250 million** annually, adds adhesive formulation, advanced coating, converting, packaging and regulatory support capabilities, and strengthens industrial positions in cable-wraps, construction tapes, automotive wire harnesses and consumer products.  
• Integration leverages SWM’s proven M&A playbook (e.g., Tekra) and accelerates the “full-solutions provider” vision; cross-selling opportunities span healthcare substrates, transportation surface-protection, construction products and industrial tapes.  

### FY 2021 Outlook (including Scapa)  
• Adjusted EPS is projected at **$3.75–$4.05**, implying mid- to high-single-digit growth (up to ~10 % at the top end) despite raw-material inflation and one quarter of Scapa ownership foregone.  
• EP operating profit is expected to normalize toward the **mid-$120 million** range after an unusually strong 2020; AMS organic growth and Scapa accretion should more than offset the EP step-down.  
• Management reiterated it “has never been better positioned” for sustainable top- and bottom-line expansion, with nearly two-thirds of revenue now exposed to faster-growing, specialized end markets.

### Balance-Sheet & Capital Allocation  
• Post-acquisition leverage stands at 4.3×; the company intends to delever while continuing to invest for organic growth.  
• Pricing actions, operating efficiencies and cost synergies are expected to preserve margins and support deleveraging despite supply-chain headwinds.

### Management Commentary  
• CEO Jeff Kramer praised the global workforce’s agility amid “new COVID normal” logistics constraints and affirmed confidence in executing on both Scapa integration and price-cost management.  
• The company foresees persistent demand strength in transportation, filtration and healthcare, structural share gains in paint-protection films and air-filtration media, and a long-term secular tailwind from heightened hygiene standards.  
• Leadership emphasized that the enterprise’s growth outlook, diversified end-market exposure, and expanded capability set place SWM on its strongest footing to date.

In summary, SWM delivered a strong Q1, raised its growth profile with the Scapa acquisition, issued EPS guidance that embeds immediate albeit modest accretion, and outlined a clear path to significantly higher earnings in 2022 as synergies materialize and healthcare volumes rebound.
