# 3M Company – Q1 2022 Earnings Call Summary  
*(Results reported 26 April 2022, covering quarter ended 31 March 2022)*  

3M opened 2022 with solid operational execution in the face of escalating supply-chain frictions, inflation, COVID-related disruptions in China and geopolitical turmoil in Eastern Europe. Management reaffirmed full-year sales, earnings and cash-flow targets and announced a new non-GAAP reporting convention that excludes significant litigation-related charges so investors can more clearly follow underlying performance.

### Headline Quarterly Results  
• Net sales were $8.83 billion, essentially flat year on year (-0.25 %) but **up 1.7 % organically** once currency (-2 pts) and M&A (+0.6 pts) effects are removed.  
• Adjusted operating income reached $1.89 billion; the corresponding **adjusted operating margin was 21.4 %**, down 110 bp Y/Y but up 140 bp sequentially.  
• **Adjusted EPS was $2.65**, benefitting from higher price realisation and lower share count and exceeding consensus by $0.33. GAAP EPS of $2.26 includes $0.39 of special items – $0.13 of litigation costs and a $0.26 PFAS-remediation charge tied to the Zwijndrecht, Belgium site.  
• Price increases averaged **just above 3 %** and more than offset ~$215 million of raw-material and logistics inflation in the quarter.  
• **Free cash flow was $715 million** (47 % conversion); the company continues to target 90–100 % conversion for the full year.  
• Capital expenditures were $424 million, up 37 %, as 3M accelerates growth and sustainability projects; full-year cap-ex is still expected at $1.7–2.0 billion.  
• Net debt finished the quarter at $13.3 billion. 3M returned **$1.6 billion** to shareholders ($852 million in dividends – marking the 64th consecutive annual increase – and $773 million of share repurchases) and plans around **$2 billion of buy-backs** for 2022.

### Segment Performance (organic growth; Q1 operating margin)  
1. **Safety & Industrial**: +0.5 %; 22.9 % margin (-350 bp). Core industrial adhesives, tapes, electrical and abrasives all grew mid-single digits, but personal-safety sales fell mid-single digits as COVID-related disposable-respirator demand dropped ~$50 million.  
2. **Transportation & Electronics**: ‑0.3 %; 21.2 % margin (-200 bp). Auto OEM sales were flat and outperformed global builds (-5 %), while consumer-electronics related revenue declined low-single digits amid smartphone and TV softness. Semiconductor end-market demand remained strong.  
3. **Health Care**: +4.7 %; 21.1 % margin (-140 bp). Medical Solutions grew mid-single digits despite U.S. elective procedures running only 85–90 % of pre-COVID levels in January–February; Oral Care recovered to low-single-digit growth; biopharma filtration (Separation & Purification) rose mid-single digits; Food Safety rose high-single digits ahead of its planned Q3 divestiture.  
4. **Consumer**: +3.4 %; 17.1 % margin (-370 bp). Home Improvement (Filtrete, Command) and Home Care posted low-single-digit increases, while Consumer Health & Safety was up low-teens. Margins were pressured by higher raw-material and outsourced manufacturing costs.

### 2022 Outlook (unchanged despite a softer macro backdrop)  
• **Organic sales growth**: 2 %–5 %.  
• **Adjusted EPS**: $10.75–$11.25, which embeds roughly $0.60 of litigation-related expense but excludes any future reserve adjustments; GAAP EPS will differ by such future items.  
• **Free cash-flow conversion**: 90 %–100 %.  
Management cautioned that Q2 is starting slowly because of China lockdowns and the Ukraine crisis. Additional Q2 headwinds include another ~$225 million of raw-material/logistics inflation and a further $100–200 million sequential decline in disposable-respirator sales. Global auto builds are forecast to rise 2 % Y/Y but fall 3 % sequentially, and smartphones are expected to dip 5 % sequentially.

### Pricing, Cost and Productivity  
The company is leveraging daily management tools, enhanced analytics and a new dynamic pricing process to neutralise inflation. Leadership reiterated its long-term goal of **30 %–40 % incremental operating leverage**, driven by volume growth, factory productivity, strategic sourcing and ongoing organisational simplification.

### Litigation and Regulatory Matters  
• Beginning in Q1, 3M’s adjusted results exclude all **legal-accrual changes and external defence fees for PFAS, Combat Arms earplugs and respirator masks**. 2022 guidance assumes roughly $600 million pretax ($0.86 per share) of such costs, including the $0.26 Q1 Belgium remediation charge; it does **not** assume future reserve changes.  
• Six of fourteen Combat Arms bellwether trials have resulted in defence verdicts; two more trials are scheduled in May. PFAS-related bellwethers are slated for June (Michigan) and October (Alabama); the large AFFF multidistrict litigation should not see its first trial until 2023.  
• At Zwijndrecht, Belgium, certain PFAS-related processes remain idled until environmental-permit issues are resolved. 3M has already invested €125 million in new filtration systems and committed a further €150 million to legacy site remediation. The company is supplying affected customers from alternate global sites when possible.

### Portfolio Actions and Capital Allocation  
• The divestiture of the **Food Safety** business to Neogen remains on track to close in Q3 2022.  
• 3M completed the sale of its Western European floor-care products in March and acquired the technology assets of **LeanTec**, deepening its “connected body-shop” digital platform for the automotive aftermarket.  
• Sustained investment continues in high-growth areas such as automotive electrification (Q1 organic sales +20 % on top of +30 % in 2021) and biopharma filtration (+15 %). A $35 million capacity-doubling project is under way in Columbia, Missouri.

### Sustainability Progress  
3M has already cut absolute greenhouse-gas emissions 25 % and water usage 10 % against its 2019 baseline and is investing **$1 billion through 2030** to meet new goals: carbon neutrality by 2050 with interim 50 % GHG reduction by 2030; 25 % water-use reduction and universal advanced filtration at major sites by 2030; and a 125-million-pound reduction in virgin fossil-based plastic by 2025. The 2021 Global Impact Report will be published on 11 May.

### Balance-Sheet and Liquidity  
3M’s BBB+-equivalent credit profile is supported by robust cash generation and moderate leverage. Management emphasised flexibility to fund organic growth, strategic M&A, the dividend and opportunistic buybacks while holding net-debt/EBITDA well within its targeted range.

### Management’s Closing View  
CEO Mike Roman characterised Q1 as “a good start to the year” and expressed confidence that 3M can continue to outgrow its end markets, defend margins with pricing and productivity, generate strong cash flow and progress on both portfolio reshaping and sustainability commitments, even as inflation, supply-chain disruptions, COVID lockdowns and geopolitical tensions persist.
