# Neenah, Inc. – Q1 2022 Earnings Call Summary

Neenah opened 2022 with another record top-line quarter and reaffirmed its full-year outlook, while also providing additional detail on the transformational merger of equals with Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM).  Management emphasised that price realisation is now running ahead of inflation, operational bottlenecks are easing, and demand remains strong across core growth platforms.

Sales, Volume and Pricing  
• 1Q22 net sales were $285 million, a year-on-year increase of 25 percent (third consecutive record).  
• Total volume rose 17 percent: Fine Paper & Packaging (FPP) +7 percent, Technical Products (TP) +23 percent.  
• Average selling prices added 13 percent; price actions contributed +$30 million versus 1Q21 and out-paced input-cost inflation of +$29 million.  
• ITASA, acquired in 2Q21, accounted for $39 million of incremental revenue; organic volume (ex-ITASA) advanced 3 percent.

Profitability and EPS  
• Adjusted EBITDA was $30.3 million (10.6 percent margin), down from $35.7 million a year ago but up sequentially from $25 million in 4Q21; margins improved 130 bps quarter-on-quarter.  
• Adjusted EPS was $0.63 excluding $0.29 per share of unusual items (vs. $1.04 EPS excluding $0.55 in 1Q21).  
• Operating costs were $15 million higher than the exceptionally strong prior-year quarter, reflecting raw-material and labour shortages, $3 million higher distribution costs, and a $2 million impact from the January fire in Brownville, NY (better than the $3 million initially expected).

Segment Performance  
Technical Products  
– Sales $186 million, +28 percent YoY (+6 percent ex-ITASA/plant closure).  
– Adjusted EBITDA $20.2 million vs. $24.9 million LY; specialty-chemical tightness and throughput inefficiencies drove the decline.  
Fine Paper & Packaging  
– Sales just under $100 million, +21 percent YoY (price +14 percent, volume +7 percent).  
– Adjusted EBITDA $14.5 million vs. $15.3 million LY; Brownville fire and input availability weighed, partly offset by strong packaging and consumer demand.

Cash, Debt and Liquidity  
• Quarter-end liquidity was $16 million; net leverage stood at 3.9× EBITDA.  
• Operating cash flow was a $2 million use, mainly from working-capital inflation and higher tax outflows.  
• Capital expenditure totalled $8.3 million (vs. $4.8 million LY); full-year CapEx is projected at 4–5 percent of sales, focused on growth projects (e.g., release-liner coater expansion in Mexico and a fourth melt-blown filtration line in Germany).  
• Management targets year-end leverage of ~3× through stronger EBITDA and improved working capital.

2022 Guidance Reaffirmed  
• Adjusted EBITDA of $135–145 million (up 15–25 percent YoY).  
• 2Q22 is expected to resemble 1Q22; a more pronounced margin expansion is planned for 2H22 as incremental price increases (already announced for 2Q/3Q) catch up with still-rising pulp, energy and chemical costs, and as operating efficiencies improve.

Merger with SWM – Strategic and Financial Highlights  
• Combined company to generate ~$3 billion revenue and ~$450 million EBITDA (pro-forma, fully synergised).  
• At least $65 million of cost synergies targeted: ~50 percent in the first 12 months post-close, full run-rate within 24–36 months. Roughly half will come from SG&A/public-company overlap, with additional savings from procurement, vertical integration, freight/warehousing and other operational efficiencies.  
• Revenue synergies (cross-selling, geographic expansion, product innovation) are expected but not yet quantified.  
• Pro-forma leverage is projected to approach 4× by year-end 2022 and will be a priority for deleveraging; the board intends to maintain an “ongoing commitment to a strong dividend”, with a post-close dividend policy to be communicated shortly after completion.  
• Closing remains on track for 2H22; an integration “transformation office” reporting to CEO Julie Schertell is already in place.

Operational and Market Commentary  
• Orders for some products are now booked into 4Q22, underscoring robust demand.  Fine Paper & Packaging capacity is tight; new coating and filtration assets are scheduled for 2023–2024 start-ups to support high-single-digit growth in release liners and continued expansion in melt-blown filtration.  
• Input-cost escalation continues, especially in pulp and energy; surcharges and accelerated pricing mechanisms are mitigating timing lags. Specialty-chemical inflation is moderating and availability is improving.  
• The company has minimal direct exposure to Russia/Ukraine but is monitoring indirect effects on European energy and logistics.  
• Each 1-cent strengthening of the U.S. dollar versus the euro reduces annual EBITDA by roughly $0.65 million.

Long-Term Targets and Strategy  
Management reiterated its medium-term objectives of 5 percent revenue CAGR, 10 percent earnings growth, and EBITDA margins above 15 percent, to be driven by:  
– Growth platforms in air & liquid filtration, release liners, premium & sustainable packaging, and healthcare;  
– Continuous pricing discipline and mix upgrade;  
– Operational excellence (Neenah Operating System);  
– Innovation (e.g., recently launched radiation-resistant medical packaging).  

Overall, Neenah entered 2022 with accelerating price-cost gains, strong order books, ongoing capacity investments and a clear path to de-levering.  The forthcoming SWM merger is expected to enhance scale, margins, cash flow and strategic optionality while delivering meaningful cost and potential revenue synergies.
