# NVIDIA FY22 Q1 Earnings Call – Detailed Financial Summary

NVIDIA opened fiscal 2022 with record‐setting first-quarter results, broad‐based momentum across Gaming, Data Center and Professional Visualization, and a notably stronger outlook for the balance of the year. Management repeatedly emphasised that supply remains tight, but demand visibility, long-term purchase commitments and new capacity underpin expectations for sequential growth in both Gaming and Data Center throughout FY22.

### Consolidated Financial Performance  
• Revenue reached **$5.66 billion**, rising **84 % year-on-year (YoY)** and **13 % sequentially (QoQ)**, an all-time high.  
• GAAP gross margin was **64.1 %** (down 100 bp YoY, up 100 bp QoQ); non-GAAP gross margin **66.2 %** (up 40 bp YoY, up 70 bp QoQ) helped by favourable Data Center mix and the addition of CMP crypto-specific products.  
• GAAP EPS increased **106 % YoY** to **$3.03**; non-GAAP EPS grew **103 % YoY** to **$3.66**.  
• Operating cash flow was **$1.9 billion**.  
• Long-term component purchase obligations were up roughly **45 % YoY**, reflecting lengthening lead-times and multi-quarter Data Center builds.

### Segment Highlights  

Gaming  
• Revenue set another record at **$2.76 billion** (≈$2.8 billion stated), **+11 % QoQ, +106 % YoY**.  
• Three consecutive quarters of accelerating YoY growth are being driven by RTX 30-series demand across desktops and a record 140+ laptop designs.  
• Channel inventory remains “lean”; NVIDIA expects to stay supply-constrained into H2.  
• Crypto-currency mining contributed but remains hard to size. To separate demand pools, newly manufactured RTX 3080/3070/3060 Ti/3060 carry “LHR” low-hash-rate firmware, while dedicated **CMP** devices address miners.  
  • **CMP revenue was $155 million in Q1; Q2 guidance assumes $400 million.**  
• GeForce NOW cloud service surpassed **10 million registered users**; library nears 1,000 titles.

Professional Visualization  
• Revenue **$372 million**, **+21 % QoQ and YoY**.  
• Notebook workstations (Ampere-based, Max-Q) led growth; desktop demand rebounded as offices reopen.  
• Omniverse Enterprise—paid, per-user and per-server subscription for collaborative 3-D “metaverse” workflows—enters general availability this year; 400+ companies and ~17,000 beta users are evaluating the platform.

Automotive  
• Revenue **$154 million**, **+6 % QoQ, −1 % YoY**.  
• AI-cockpit growth offset the planned wind-down of legacy infotainment.  
• Next-generation **DRIVE Atlan SoC delivers 1,000 TOPS** and targets 2025 models, following the 254-TOPS **DRIVE Orin** that begins production in 2022.  
• Automotive design-win pipeline now **exceeds $8 billion through FY27**; recent wins include Volvo Cars, GM Cruise, Navistar/TuSimple, and multiple Chinese NEV makers.

Data Center  
• Revenue surpassed the $2 billion mark for the first time, **≈$2.05 billion**, **+8 % QoQ, +79 % YoY** (prior-year quarter excluded Mellanox).  
• Hyperscale customers led growth as they commercialise AI services; Google Cloud made NVIDIA A100 generally available in March.  
• Record GPU shipments for inference; T4, A10, A30 and universal A100 all grew.  
• Mellanox networking posted another sequential increase; BlueField-2 DPU is in proofs-of-concept, and **BlueField-3 (400 Gb/s, 300 CPU-core equivalent offload) launches 2022**, promising a 100× DPU-road-map performance gain over three years.  
• The Data Center product line now spans GPU, **DPU** and newly announced Arm-based **“Grace” CPU** (10× performance-per-watt vs. today’s leading servers, sampling 2023).  
• Enterprise AI push:  
  • **NVIDIA AI Enterprise** (perpetual licence or subscription) supports VMware vSphere and ships with EGX-certified servers from major OEMs.  
  • **Fleet Command** manages edge AI deployment; **AI on 5G** platform pairs Aerial software with BlueField-2 A100 converged cards.  
  • Early enterprise customers include Lockheed Martin and Mass General Brigham.

### Balance Sheet & Capital Allocation  
• No specific debt metrics were disclosed on the call.  
• **Capital expenditures for Q2 are guided to $300 million-$325 million.**

### Q2 FY22 Outlook (guidance issued 26 May 2021)  
• Revenue **$6.30 billion ± 2 %** (includes **$400 million CMP**).  
• GAAP gross margin **64.6 % ± 50 bp**; non-GAAP **66.5 % ± 50 bp**.  
• GAAP operating expenses **≈$1.76 billion**; non-GAAP **≈$1.26 billion**.  
• Other income/expense: **−$50 million**.  
• Effective tax rate: **10 % ± 1 %**.  
• Management expects sequential growth in every market platform, led by Gaming (core demand plus CMP segregation) and Data Center (compute and networking). Supply flexibility exists to satisfy additional CMP demand without diverting GeForce units.

### Strategic & Regulatory Updates  
• Arm acquisition is “on track” for closure by early 2022; NVIDIA pledges to expand Arm R&D and IP footprints into Data Center, IoT and embedded markets.  
• Government interest in semiconductor self-sufficiency was acknowledged; the Company did not quantify potential subsidy benefits but highlighted GPU/DPU/CPU leadership as strategically critical.  
• GTC drew 200,000+ registrants and 14 million keynote views, underscoring developer adoption of new platforms such as Grace CPU, Omniverse, and enterprise AI stacks.

### Management’s Forward View  
• RTX penetration is still only about **15 %** of the GeForce installed base, supplying a long upgrade runway.  
• Both Gaming and Data Center are expected to **grow sequentially every quarter through FY22**, supported by new supply, multiple product ramps and expanding software and subscription revenue streams.  
• BlueField DPUs should begin contributing “meaningful, if not significant” revenue in CY22 as cloud and enterprise deployments scale.  
• Overall, NVIDIA positions itself as a **full-stack accelerated computing company**—chips, systems, software and services—targeting growth vectors in gaming, AI, metaverse collaboration, autonomous vehicles, 5G/edge and biology.
