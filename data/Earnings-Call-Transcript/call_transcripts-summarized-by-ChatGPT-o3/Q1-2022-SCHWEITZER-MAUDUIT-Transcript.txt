# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q1 2022 Earnings Call Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International started 2022 with robust demand across its portfolio and, importantly, with price increases that are now keeping pace with inflationary pressures. Consolidated first-quarter sales rose 41 % year on year to USD 407 million and were 5 % higher on an organic basis despite a difficult comparison with a very strong Q1 2021. Adjusted operating profit declined 7 % to USD 42 million, but margin improved 300 basis points versus the fourth quarter, confirming that sequential momentum is intact.

In the Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS) segment, revenue expanded 67 % to USD 273 million, including roughly USD 105 million from the 2021 Scapa acquisition. Organic growth was 3 % on top of the prior year’s 15 % surge, and would have been 7 % if transportation-film sales—constrained by a global shortage of a key TPU raw material—were excluded. Pricing actions more than offset higher resin and other input costs, lifting adjusted operating profit by almost USD 7 million (+24 %). Although AMS margins were lower than last year, they rebounded 540 basis points from Q4 and are expected to expand further as the year progresses. Filtration, construction and general industrial markets led growth, and management believes quarterly organic growth will accelerate as comparisons normalise.

Engineered Papers (EP) posted a 7 % sales increase (10 % in constant currency), with strength across traditional products and more than 25 % growth in reduced-risk Heat-not-Burn offerings. Pricing and additional volumes fully offset pulp costs, but a USD 5 million spike in energy and other inflationary items pushed adjusted operating profit down by USD 5.6 million and compressed margins year on year. Sequentially, however, EP margins improved 100 basis points. Energy surcharges are being implemented and customers are also awarding incremental volumes, giving management confidence that EP will deliver stable operating profit for 2022 despite the volatile cost environment.

GAAP EPS fell to USD 0.05 from USD 0.68, burdened by USD 0.34 of restructuring and impairment charges (largely a non-cash write-down of a small construction-products business held for sale), USD 0.18 of Scapa integration and Neenah merger expenses, and USD 0.28 of purchase-accounting amortisation. Excluding these items, adjusted EPS was USD 0.89 versus USD 1.02 last year. Management reiterated full-year guidance for adjusted EPS of USD 3.50–3.95 and for 20–30 % adjusted EBITDA growth, implying that EPS should average about USD 1.00 in each of the remaining quarters and land toward the top of the range if current demand and pricing trends persist.

Operating cash flow was a seasonally low USD 5 million, reflecting a USD 15 million working-capital build tied to higher sales and inflation; free cash flow was negative but is projected to reach roughly USD 100 million for the year. Net leverage ended the quarter a little above 5× EBITDA but is expected to decline by about one-third of a turn each quarter, finishing 2022 at roughly 4× on stronger earnings and cash generation. Liquidity remains sound, with USD 56 million in cash and USD 100 million of revolver availability against covenant headroom.

Management emphasised that pricing discipline will continue because inflation “has not yet settled,” and further increases and surcharges have already been announced. Raw-material availability—in particular the TPU component that constrains transportation films—should improve gradually, although tight conditions are likely to persist until 2023. Outside of that bottleneck, the supply chain is becoming more manageable, and volume opportunities are emerging, especially as European customers shift production to SWM’s global facilities in response to the Russia-Ukraine conflict.

The pending merger of equals with Neenah remains on track for a second-half close; the joint S-4 registration statement was filed alongside the Q1 10-Q. The deal will create a USD 3 billion specialty-materials company with EBITDA “earnings power” above USD 450 million. Management has identified more than USD 65 million of highly actionable annual cost synergies—about half in SG&A and the rest in procurement and supply-chain optimisation—with over 50 % expected within twelve months of closing. The combined entity is projected to exit 2022 at roughly 4× net leverage and to delever rapidly thereafter, while sustaining the attractive dividend histories of both companies. Customers have responded favourably, noting the complementary product portfolios and the expanded innovation capabilities in filtration, medical, tape and sustainable-packaging applications.

In summary, SWM has moved beyond the worst inflation-versus-pricing lag of 2021, is enjoying solid demand in virtually every end market, and expects sequential and year-on-year improvements throughout 2022. The company is reaffirming all financial targets, forecasting stronger free cash flow that will reduce debt materially, and simultaneously preparing to unlock significant strategic and financial benefits from its merger with Neenah in the back half of the year.
