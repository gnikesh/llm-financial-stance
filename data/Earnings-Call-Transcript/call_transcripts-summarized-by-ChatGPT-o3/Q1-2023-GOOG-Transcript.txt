# Alphabet Inc. – Q1 2023 Earnings Call Summary

Alphabet opened 2023 with modest top-line growth, its first ever quarterly profit in Google Cloud, and a heavy strategic focus on artificial intelligence, while simultaneously accelerating cost-reduction initiatives launched in January. Management reiterated that Search remains resilient, YouTube advertising is stabilising, Cloud growth is healthy though tempering as customers optimise spend, and that AI is now the organising principle for products, capex and workforce priorities.

### Consolidated financial performance  
• Revenue was $69.8 billion, up 3 % year-on-year (6 % in constant currency).  
• Operating income fell 13 % to $17.4 billion; operating margin contracted to 25 %.  
• Net income totalled $15.1 billion. (Diluted EPS was not disclosed on the call but will be in the 10-Q.)  
• Free cash flow was $17.2 billion for the quarter and $62 billion for the trailing 12 months.  
• Alphabet ended March with $115 billion in cash and marketable securities and carries effectively no net debt.  
• One-off charges of $2.6 billion tied to the January workforce reduction (≈12,000 roles) and office-space rationalisation inflated operating expenses.  
• A change in server and network‐equipment useful lives reduced Q1 depreciation expense by $988 million, partially offsetting the restructuring charges.  
• Stock-based-compensation awards were shifted from January to March, moving the Q1 SBC step-up into Q2.

### Segment results  
GOOGLE SERVICES  
• Revenue: $62.0 billion (+1 %).  
  – Search & Other Ads: $40.4 billion (+2 %); strength in travel and retail offset weakness in finance, media & entertainment.  
  – YouTube Ads: $6.7 billion (-3 %); sequential signs of stabilisation.  
  – Network Ads: $7.5 billion (-8 %) amid an “incremental pull-back” in advertiser spend.  
  – Other Revenues: $7.4 billion (+9 %), driven mainly by YouTube TV and YouTube Music Premium subscriber growth; Google Play remained down YoY.  
• TAC (traffic-acquisition costs) declined 2 % to $11.7 billion, reflecting mix shift.  
• Operating income: $21.7 billion (-1 %); 35 % margin.

GOOGLE CLOUD  
• Revenue: $7.45 billion (+28 %).  
• Delivered first-ever GAAP profit: operating income $191 million, a 2.6 % margin versus a loss a year ago.  
• GCP deal volume is up nearly 500 % over three years; >60 % of the Global 1000 now customers.  
• Macro headwinds are prompting clients to “optimise consumption,” slowing growth rates, but Alphabet is “leaning in” to help, expecting long-term gains.  
• New AI offerings (PaLM API, Vertex AI upgrades, Workspace generative features) are already driving enterprise uptake.

OTHER BETS  
• Revenue: $288 million; operating loss $1.2 billion.  
• Workforce and cost rationalisation measures were also applied to the portfolio.  

### Capital expenditures and cost discipline  
• 2023 capex is now expected to be “modestly higher” than 2022, with spending weighted to 2H and skewed toward data-centres, TPUs and GPUs; real-estate capex will fall.  
• AI and automation are being deployed to lower operational costs, improve machine utilisation and renegotiate supplier spend.  
• Headcount growth will slow “meaningfully” in 2023; most laid-off employees roll off payroll by end-Q2.

### AI strategy and organisational changes  
• Google unveiled Bard (now upgraded with the PaLM model), opened the PaLM API with MakerSuite for developers, and is embedding generative AI across Search, Ads, Workspace and Cloud Security.  
• “Google DeepMind” combines the Brain team (Google Research) with DeepMind to pool talent and compute, aiming to build “the most capable AI systems safely and responsibly.”  
• Management compared the AI shift to the earlier desktop-to-mobile transition, calling it a once-in-a-generation opportunity for new products and revenue streams.

### YouTube highlights  
• Shorts exceeds 50 billion daily views (up from 30 billion last spring); channels posting weekly saw most new subs come via Shorts.  
• Ads on Shorts are now available through Video Action, App, Discovery and Performance Max campaigns; monetisation “closing the gap” with long-form remains a top priority.  
• Connected-TV viewing is YouTube’s fastest-growing screen; NFL Sunday Ticket pricing is expected to fuel subscription growth.  
• Over 100,000 creators and brands have linked Shopify and other storefronts, advancing YouTube’s long-term “shoppable video” initiative.

### Outlook & management commentary  
• Macro environment remains uncertain; FX headwinds are moderating into Q2.  
• Search continues to prove resilient, valued for measurable ROI; YouTube ad spend has stabilised sequentially; Network ads still face pressure.  
• Cloud will balance aggressive AI-driven innovation with “long-term profitable growth,” acknowledging near-term optimisation by customers.  
• Alphabet will keep expense growth below revenue growth, aiming for durable savings while preserving capacity for AI, Search/Ads, Cloud, YouTube and hardware investments.

Alphabet emerged from Q1 with Search stability, YouTube green shoots, Cloud profitability, a fortified AI roadmap, and $115 billion of cash to fund both capital requirements and share-holder returns, while intensified cost controls are intended to protect margins in a still-uncertain economy.
