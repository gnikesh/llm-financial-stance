# Meta Platforms, Inc. – Q1 2023 Earnings Call Summary  

Meta’s first-quarter results show modest top-line growth, a sharp focus on cost efficiency and heavy, but more disciplined, investment in AI infrastructure and the Metaverse.

Meta reported Q1 2023 revenue of **$28.6 billion**, up **3 % year over year (YoY)** or **6 % in constant currency**; foreign-exchange headwinds reduced revenue by roughly **$816 million**.  
• Net income came in at **$5.7 billion**, equal to **$2.20 diluted EPS**.  
• Operating income was **$7.2 billion** with an **operating margin of 25 %**.  
• The tax rate was **22 %**.  
• Free cash flow reached **$6.9 billion**; Meta repurchased **$9.2 billion** of its own shares and ended the quarter with **$37.4 billion** in cash and marketable securities.  
• Capital expenditures (including finance-lease principal payments) totaled **$7.1 billion**, largely for data-center build-outs, servers and network gear to support AI workloads.

## Cost base and restructuring

Total expenses were **$21.4 billion**, up **10 % YoY**.  
– Cost of revenue rose 2 % on higher infrastructure spend, partly offset by lower Reality Labs COGS.  
– R&D rose 22 % (severance, facility-consolidation charges and head-count costs).  
– Marketing & sales fell 8 %.  
– G&A grew 22 % (severance, facility and legal charges).  

Meta finished Q1 with **77,100 employees**, 11 % fewer than Q4 because November-2022 lay-offs have rolled off reported head-count; March-2023 lay-off victims are still counted. Two of three planned 2023 lay-off waves are done, with the final (business-groups) wave set for May. 2023 restructuring charges are estimated at **$3 – 5 billion**; Q1 already booked about **$1.1 billion**.

Management reiterated an “efficiency” mindset: flatter org structure, a higher share of technical roles, continued automation and more rigorous project prioritization. After the current downsizing, hiring will resume in priority areas (generative AI, ads, infra, Reality Labs); 2024 head-count growth is expected to exceed 1–2 %.

## Segment detail  

Family of Apps (FoA)  
• Revenue: **$28.3 billion**, +4 % YoY (ad revenue **$28.1 billion**, +4 %—or +7 % cc).  
• FoA expenses: **$17.1 billion** (+9 %).  
• FoA operating income: **$11.2 billion**, margin **40 %**.  

User metrics:  
– Family DAU passed **3 billion**; Family MAU ≈ **3.8 billion**.  
– Facebook DAU: **2.04 billion** (+4 % / +77 million YoY).  
– Facebook MAU: **2.99 billion** (+2 %), DAU/MAU ratio 68 %.  
– Facebook hit **200 million** DAU in the U.S./Canada.  

Advertising dynamics:  
– Total ad impressions +26 %; average price per ad –17 %.  
– Geographic ad-revenue growth: Rest of World +9 %, North America +6 %, APAC +4 %, Europe –1 %.  
– Verticals: online commerce (largest contributor), healthcare, entertainment/media strong; financial services and technology weak.  

Other FoA revenue (chiefly WhatsApp Business and VR app store) was **$205 million**, –5 % YoY; robust WhatsApp paid-messaging growth (+40 % QoQ in paying businesses) was offset by declines elsewhere.

Reality Labs (Metaverse hardware/software)  
• Revenue: **$339 million**, –51 % YoY owing to lower Quest 2 sales.  
• Expenses: **$4.3 billion**, +18 %.  
• Operating loss: **$4.0 billion**.  
Meta still expects Reality Labs losses to rise YoY in 2023 but remains committed to long-term Metaverse investment (1 billion+ avatars created, Quest store titles earning ≥ $25 million have doubled; > 50 % of Quest DAU spend > 1 hour per day). A next-gen VR/MR headset will launch later in 2023.

## Product, engagement and monetization trends

– AI-driven recommendations now supply **>20 %** of content in Facebook and Instagram feeds, and about **40 %** of total Instagram views.  
– Since global launch, Reels-driven ranking lifted Instagram time spent by **24 %**. Daily Reels reshares exceed **2 billion**, doubling in six months.  
– Reels monetization efficiency improved **>30 % on Instagram** and **>40 % on Facebook** quarter on quarter; Meta expects Reels to become revenue-neutral by late 2023 or early 2024 despite structurally lower ads-per-minute versus Feed/Stories.  
– Advantage+ Shopping campaigns deliver 7-fold daily-revenue growth in six months, and click-to-message ads are at a **$10 billion** run-rate. Generative-AI tools are slated to help advertisers create assets automatically and to power business-messaging agents for “tens of millions” of SMBs.  

## AI infrastructure and open-source strategy

CapEx growth in recent years was driven primarily by building AI capacity; management now believes Meta is “no longer behind” and can “do leading work at scale.” 2023 capex guidance remains **$30–33 billion**, with rising allocations to generative-AI projects. Meta continues to open-source key models (e.g., 65-billion-parameter LLaMA LLM, Segment Anything, DINOv2) to build an industry-standard tool chain that supports its own products without selling cloud services.

## Capital structure and outlook

– Meta plans “periodic” debt issuance to lower its cost of capital while keeping a positive or neutral net-cash position.  

Guidance provided:  
• Q2 2023 revenue **$29.5 – 32.0 billion**; FX headwind expected to be <1 pp.  
• Full-year 2023 expenses **$86 – 90 billion** (including $3–5 billion restructuring).  
• 2023 CapEx unchanged at **$30 – 33 billion**; more color on 2024+ spend will follow once generative-AI product usage is clearer.  
• 2023 tax rate ~**20 %**.

## Regulation and risks

The Irish DPC is expected to rule in May on EU-U.S. data transfers; a suspension order and fine are possible. About **10 %** of Meta’s ad revenue comes from ads served to Facebook users in EU countries. Management is “hopeful” that a new EU-U.S. Privacy Framework will be implemented in time but is evaluating contingency plans.

## Strategic takeaways

1. Meta’s aggressive efficiency drive (lay-offs, project pruning, flatter structure) has already lowered the 2023 cost outlook by roughly $10–15 billion versus initial targets without compromising near-term revenue growth.  
2. AI is the dominant near-term investment theme: ranking, ads, generative-AI experiences, and capacity build-out—all now backed by ample infrastructure.  
3. Reels and the broader discovery engine are materially lifting engagement and should be neutral to revenue within nine months, with monetization still ramping.  
4. Business messaging (click-to-message ads, paid messages on WhatsApp) is scaling rapidly and represents the “next pillar” of revenue diversification.  
5. Despite skepticism, Meta reiterated full commitment to the Metaverse; Reality Labs losses will widen, but a new headset and growing Quest ecosystem underpin the long-term vision.
