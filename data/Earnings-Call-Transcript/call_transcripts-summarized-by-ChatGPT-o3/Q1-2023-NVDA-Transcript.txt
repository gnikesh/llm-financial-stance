# NVIDIA Fiscal Q1 2023 Earnings Call – Financial & Strategic Highlights  

NVIDIA delivered record first-quarter results and reiterated confidence in long-term demand for its accelerated-computing portfolio, while acknowledging near-term macro headwinds from the Russia-Ukraine war and COVID-related lockdowns in China.

### Consolidated Financial Performance (GAAP unless stated)  
• Revenue reached an all-time high of **$8.29 billion**, up **8 % sequentially** and **46 % year-on-year**.  
• GAAP gross margin was **65.5 %**; non-GAAP gross margin **67.1 %** (up 90 bps YoY, +10 bps QoQ), as higher component costs were offset by pricing and mix.  
• GAAP operating margin fell to **22.5 %** because of the **$1.35 billion** ARM-deal termination charge; non-GAAP operating margin stayed robust at **47.7 %**.  
• Operating expenses will rise “high-20 %” for FY23, but management is moderating hiring to integrate recent additions and offset inflation.  
• NVIDIA repurchased **$2 billion** of stock in Q1; the board expanded the authorization to **$15 billion through December 2023**.  
• No debt figures were provided; NVIDIA’s balance sheet remains largely debt-free.

### Segment Highlights  
1. **Data Center (now the largest business)**  
   • Revenue **$3.75 billion**, +15 % QoQ, +83 % YoY.  
   • Growth fueled by hyperscalers and cloud providers (>2× YoY) and double-digit growth in vertical industries (financial services, telecom, consumer internet).  
   • Strong adoption of A100 GPUs for both training and inference; “supply-constrained” networking (InfiniBand, Ethernet) continues to limit upside.  
   • New products: Hopper H100 GPU (80 billion transistors) sampling; DGX H100 systems in volume late CY-2022; Grace CPU and Grace/Hopper Superchips to launch 1H 2023; Spectrum-4 400 Gb/s Ethernet platform and BlueField-3 DPU ramp in coming quarters.  
   • Management expects **record Data Center revenue again in Q2** and continued sequential growth through the second half.

2. **Gaming**  
   • Revenue **$3.62 billion**, +6 % QoQ, +31 % YoY.  
   • RTX 30-series remains “best gaming cycle ever”; ~33 % of installed GeForce base now on RTX.  
   • Channel inventory has “nearly normalized”; crypto-mining impact viewed as diminishing.  
   • Regional softness emerging in Europe (war) and China (lockdowns).  
   • Guidance: **Gaming to decline sequentially “in the teens” in Q2**, with ~$400 million of the $500 million Russia/China headwind attributed to Gaming.

3. **Professional Visualization**  
   • Revenue **$622 million**, –3 % QoQ, +67 % YoY; mobile workstations strong, desktop GPUs softer.  
   • Omniverse Enterprise (only 2nd quarter of availability) now licensed by ~10 % of Global 100 corporations; early adopters include Amazon (warehouse simulation), Kroger (store digital twins), PepsiCo (supply-chain optimization).

4. **Automotive & Embedded**  
   • Revenue **$138 million**, +10 % QoQ, –10 % YoY; expected to grow strongly from Q2 as DRIVE Orin enters volume production.  
   • Design-win pipeline expanded to **>$11 billion over six years** (vs. $8 billion a year ago); 35+ customers including BYD, Lucid, truck OEMs and robotaxi platforms.

### Cash-Flow & Capital Commitments  
• In response to long lead-times, NVIDIA has increased **purchase obligations and prepayments** to secure capacity (particularly for Data Center products).  
• FY23 capital expenditures are slated at **$400–450 million** in Q2.  

### Q2 FY23 Outlook (includes ~$500 million Russia/China impact)  
• Revenue: **$8.10 billion ± 2 %** (implying –2 % QoQ at midpoint).  
• GAAP / non-GAAP gross margin: **65.1 % / 67.1 % ± 50 bps**.  
• GAAP opex: **$2.46 billion**; non-GAAP opex: **$1.75 billion**.  
• Other income/expense: **~$40 million expense**.  
• Tax rate: **12.5 % ± 1 %**.  
• Sequential strength expected in Data Center and Automotive; Gaming to contract, Pro-Viz roughly stable.

### Strategic & Qualitative Takeaways  
• AI adoption is broadening beyond hyperscalers to every major industry; transformer-based large language models, deep recommender systems, and conversational AI are driving significant compute demand.  
• Management calls the future edge-to-cloud “AI factory” build-out a new secular growth driver; NVIDIA’s universal accelerator approach (GPUs + CPUs + DPUs tied by NVLink) positions it to capture diverse workloads.  
• Omniverse is viewed as the software platform for “physical AI” and digital-twin simulation; NVIDIA AI and Omniverse software should enlarge recurring revenue and lift margins over time.  
• Networking remains the tightest supply bottleneck, but quarterly availability is improving.  
• Underlying Gaming fundamentals (100 million new PC gamers in two years, creator economy, GeForce NOW cloud service) remain solid once geopolitical and pandemic effects subside.

Management reiterated confidence that, despite near-term macro volatility, its multi-year product roadmap, expanding software stack, and broadening market exposure will sustain long-term growth and margin expansion.
