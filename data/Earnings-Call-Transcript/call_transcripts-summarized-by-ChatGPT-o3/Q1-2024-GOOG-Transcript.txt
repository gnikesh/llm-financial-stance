# Alphabet Inc. Q1 2024 Earnings – Comprehensive Financial and Strategic Summary

Alphabet reported a strong first quarter of 2024, combining double-digit revenue growth with continued margin expansion and substantial investment in artificial-intelligence infrastructure.

Alphabet-wide performance  
Alphabet generated revenue of $80.5 billion, a year-over-year increase of 15 percent (16 percent in constant currency), supported chiefly by continued strength in Search, YouTube and Google Cloud.  Net income rose to $23.7 billion and diluted EPS reached $1.89.  Operating income climbed 46 percent to $25.5 billion, lifting the operating margin to 32 percent; excluding the severance and real-estate charges taken in both years, the adjusted margin was 33 percent.  Free cash flow was $16.8 billion for the quarter and $69.1 billion for the trailing twelve months.  Alphabet closed March with $108 billion in cash and marketable securities and no net debt.

Introduction of shareholder returns  
Management complemented Alphabet’s existing buy-back program with a new quarterly cash dividend of $0.20 per share and authorized a further $70 billion in share repurchases, underscoring confidence in long-term cash generation.

Segment detail  
• Google Services revenue advanced 14 percent to $70.4 billion.  
 – Search & Other ads revenue grew 14 percent to $46.2 billion, powered again by the retail vertical—especially APAC-based merchants.  
 – YouTube advertising revenue accelerated to 21 percent growth, reaching $8.1 billion; both direct-response and brand demand improved and shorts monetization in the U.S. more than doubled year-on-year.  
 – Network advertising slipped 1 percent to $7.4 billion.  
 – Subscriptions, Platforms & Devices revenue rose 18 percent to $8.7 billion, driven by YouTube Music, Premium and YouTube TV; Google One surpassed 100 million paid subscribers.  
 – Traffic-acquisition costs (TAC) increased 10 percent to $12.9 billion.  
 – Google Services delivered operating income of $27.9 billion with a 40 percent margin.  

• Google Cloud revenue climbed 28 percent to $9.6 billion as demand for GCP infrastructure, AI-related workloads and Workspace ARPU continued to rise.  Cloud produced operating income of $0.9 billion (9 percent margin), its third consecutive profitable quarter.  Management reiterated that Google Cloud and YouTube together are on pace to exit 2024 at a combined annualized run-rate above $100 billion.  

• Other Bets generated $495 million in revenue (benefiting from a milestone payment) and recorded a $1 billion operating loss.  Waymo expanded fully driverless paid rides in San Francisco, Phoenix and Los Angeles and began rider-only tests in Austin.

Capital allocation and cost discipline  
Quarterly capital expenditure soared to $12 billion, almost entirely for servers, data-center construction and proprietary AI hardware such as fifth-generation TPUs.  CapEx is expected to remain “at or above” the Q1 level throughout 2024, while facility spending will stay below 10 percent of the total.  Despite heavier depreciation and cloud infrastructure costs, Alphabet still projects full-year 2024 operating-margin expansion, enabled by ongoing “durable re-engineering” of the cost base, slower head-count growth and continued real-estate optimization.

Artificial-intelligence leadership and product momentum  
• Research & Models:  Gemini 1.5 Pro, boasting the industry’s longest context window and native multimodal capabilities, entered wider distribution; Gemma open models and Imagen 2.0 advanced visual generation.  
• Infrastructure:  New algorithms and the TPU v5p have improved model efficiency more than 100-fold versus 18 months ago.  Alphabet also introduced Axion, its in-house Arm-based CPU, and will offer NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs.  
• Search:  Generative AI overviews (formerly SGE) migrated from Search Labs onto the main results page for a subset of queries, having already served “billions” of queries.  Early data show higher user satisfaction, increased search usage and an 80 percent decline in per-query compute cost since initial testing.  Management sees no material latency, cost or monetization barriers to broader rollout.  
• Advertising tools:  Gemini has been embedded into Performance Max, Demand Gen and automatically created assets; advertisers who upgrade asset strength to “Excellent” are seeing 6 percent more conversions, and ACA users gain roughly 5 percent more conversions at flat CPA.  
• YouTube:  Shorts viewership averages about 70 billion daily views; viewers now stream more than 1 billion hours of YouTube on connected TVs each day.  Pause Ads on CTVs are commanding premium pricing, and Shorts ads are now available on mobile, tablet, living-room and desktop.  
• Cloud adoption:  More than 300 enterprises—including Bayer, Walmart, Mercedes-Benz, PayPal and Discover—publicly highlighted Gen-AI projects at Google Cloud Next.  Over 60 percent of funded generative-AI start-ups and nearly 90 percent of Gen-AI “unicorns” run on Google Cloud.  
• Workspace:  Companies such as Uber, Pepperdine and PennyMac adopted Gemini to boost productivity, while Google Vids and Gemini for Meetings & Messaging enriched the collaboration suite.  
• Cybersecurity:  Alphabet emphasized its perceived leadership, citing Gemini’s capacity to accelerate threat detection for customers like American Family Insurance.

Financial outlook  
Q1 revenue benefited by slightly more than one percentage point from leap-year timing; conversely, management anticipates a larger foreign-exchange headwind in Q2.  Advertising comparisons will lap 2023’s surge in APAC retailer spend and the prior-year YouTube softness, and YouTube TV will anniversary last year’s price increase starting in May.

Balance-sheet strength, accelerating AI adoption, disciplined spending and a newly enlarged capital-return program place Alphabet on track for both continued revenue expansion and further operating-margin gains in 2024.
