# Meta Platforms, Inc. – Q1 2024 Earnings Call Summary  

Meta reported a strong first quarter, highlighted by double-digit top-line growth and expanding profitability, but management stressed that a multi-year, capital-intensive AI investment cycle is now under way.  

Management on the call: CEO Mark Zuckerberg, CFO Susan Li, Director of IR Kenneth Dorell.  

---

### 1. Consolidated Q1 2024 Results  

• Revenue was $36.46 billion, up 27 % year-over-year (constant currency growth identical).  
• GAAP expenses totaled $22.64 billion (+6 % y/y).  
• Operating income was $13.80 billion; operating margin expanded to 38 % (vs. 25 % a year ago).  
• Effective tax rate: 13 %.  
• Net income reached $12.37 billion, translating to diluted EPS of $4.71 (up 114 % y/y).  
• Free cash flow came in at $12.49 billion.  
• Capital expenditures (cash plus finance-lease principal) were $6.75 billion, driven by servers, data-centre build-outs and network gear.  
• Balance sheet: $58.1 billion in cash & marketable securities against $18.4 billion of debt.  
• Shareholder returns: $14.6 billion of share buybacks plus $1.3 billion of dividends during the quarter.  
• Headcount ended at ≈69,300, up 3 % sequentially but still well below pre-Efficiency-Year levels.  

---

### 2. Segment Performance  

Family of Apps (FoA)  
• Revenue: $36.02 billion (+27 % y/y).  
• Advertising revenue: $35.64 billion (+27 %; +26 % c.c.).  
    – Impressions +20 %; average price per ad +6 %.  
    – Online commerce was the largest incremental vertical, followed by gaming, entertainment & media.  
    – Regional ad-revenue growth: Rest-of-World +40 %, Europe +33 %, APAC +25 %, North America +22 %.  
• Other FoA revenue (mainly WhatsApp Business messaging): $380 million (+85 %).  
• Expenses: $18.4 billion (+7 %).  
• Operating income: $17.72 billion; operating margin 49 %.  
• Community metrics: 3.24 billion Daily Active People; U.S. engagement called out as “healthy.”  

Reality Labs (RL)  
• Revenue: $440 million (+30 %), helped by Quest headset sales.  
• Expenses: $4.32 billion (-1 %, lapping prior-year inventory write-downs).  
• Operating loss: ‑$3.85 billion (management reiterates RL losses will “increase meaningfully” in 2024).  

---

### 3. Guidance & Outlook  

Revenue  
• Q2 2024 guidance: $36.5 – $39.0 billion. FX assumed to be a 1 ppt headwind to y/y growth.  

Expenses  
• 2024 total expense outlook raised to $96 – $99 billion (from $94 – $99 billion) due to higher infrastructure and legal costs.  
• Reality Labs losses to widen “meaningfully.”  

Capital Expenditures  
• 2024 CapEx guidance lifted to $35 – $40 billion (was $30 – $37 billion), reflecting accelerated AI infrastructure build-out.  
• CapEx expected to “continue to increase next year” as Meta invests aggressively in AI research, training and inference capacity.  

Tax  
• Full-year 2024 effective tax rate expected in the mid-teens.  

---

### 4. Strategic & Operational Highlights  

Artificial Intelligence  
• Meta AI, powered by newly released Llama 3 (8-billion & 70-billion-parameter models now, 400-billion model in training), rolled out last week. Tens of millions have tried it; expansion to additional languages/markets coming in months.  
• Goal: build “the world’s leading AI assistant” and scale usage across WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, Facebook and Ray-Ban Meta smart-glasses.  
• AI-recommended content now constitutes >30 % of Facebook Feed posts (double two years ago) and >50 % of Instagram content.  
• End-to-end AI ad-buying tools Advantage+ Shopping and App Campaigns: revenue flowing through them has more than doubled y/y.  
• Meta Lattice ads-ranking architecture and new unified recommendation model driving 8-10 % watch-time lift for Facebook Reels.  
• CapEx ramp driven by GPUs/ASICs, data-centre redesigns (liquid cooling), and Meta’s in-house MTIA inference/training silicon.  

Metaverse / Reality Labs  
• Ray-Ban Meta smart-glasses remain supply-constrained; new Cat-Eye “Skyler” design launched. Glasses now support multimodal Meta AI with vision.  
• Meta Horizon OS opened to third-party headset OEMs (ASUS ROG, Lenovo, Xbox/ Microsoft), reflecting a more open ecosystem strategy.  
• Zuckerberg notes increasing overlap between RL hardware and AI (e.g., glasses as “ideal device for an AI assistant”).  

Video & Engagement Trends  
• Reels accounts for 50 % of time spent on Instagram; video overall >60 % of time on both Facebook and Instagram.  
• New unified, full-screen Facebook video player (Reels + long-form + Live) launched in U.S./Canada.  
• Threads surpassed 150 million monthly active users.  

Business Messaging & Monetization Potential  
• WhatsApp Business Platform drove 85 % “Other” revenue growth; business-messaging AI agents now in early testing to scale commerce & support.  
• Management sees future monetization vectors: business messaging fees, ads/paid content inside AI interactions, subscription tiers for higher-compute AI usage.  

---

### 5. Capital Allocation & Balance-Sheet Discipline  

• Despite larger AI/infra budgets, Meta reiterated commitment to “operating discipline,” slow headcount growth and continued share repurchases & dividends.  
• Company highlighted fungibility of compute capacity across products to maximize ROI.  

---

### 6. Regulatory & Legal Matters  

• Meta faces “increasing legal and regulatory headwinds,” especially in the EU and U.S.; a Texas facial-recognition jury trial is scheduled for June and could result in a material loss.  

---

### 7. Key Takeaways  

1. Core ad business remains robust—FoA revenue +27 %, price per ad stabilizing, conversion growth outpacing impressions.  
2. A pronounced spending up-cycle is beginning: 2024 CapEx guide raised by up to $10 billion, driven almost entirely by AI infrastructure.  
3. Management’s ambition for AI has grown; Meta believes it can build “industry-leading” models and is prepared to spend ahead of revenue.  
4. Reality Labs losses will deepen, but Zuckerberg argues that glasses and mixed-reality headsets will converge with AI to create future computing platforms.  
5. Balance sheet (net cash ≈$40 billion) affords flexibility to fund elevated CapEx, absorb regulatory costs and maintain shareholder returns.
