# 3M Company – Q1 2024 Earnings Call Summary  

3M started 2024 with results above internal expectations, completed the spin-off of its Health Care business (Solventum), and secured court approval for two major legal settlements. Management reaffirmed its restructuring program, reset its dividend framework and provided full-year guidance on a post-spin, continuing-operations basis.

### Consolidated Q1 2024 performance (pre-spin view that still includes Health Care)

• Net sales were $8.0 billion (-0.3 % y/y) and exceeded consensus by c. $340 million.  
• Adjusted (non-GAAP) sales used for internal guidance were $7.7 billion and grew ~1 % organically (+2.4 % when excluding geographic/portfolio pruning and the respirator comparison).  
• Adjusted operating margin reached 21.9 %, up 400 bp y/y.  
• Adjusted EPS of $2.39 rose 21 % y/y and beat consensus by $0.28.  
• Free cash flow was $0.8 billion; conversion was 63 %, consistent with normal first-quarter seasonality.  
• Net debt declined 13 % y/y to $10.4 billion after retiring $2.9 billion of debt; the balance sheet also carries $7.7 billion of proceeds from Solventum’s pre-spin debt raise.

Key non-recurring items that helped Q1 margins/EPS  
– Stock-based-compensation grant delayed to Q2 added ~140 bp to margin and $0.15 to EPS.  
– Asset-light property sales added ~70 bp and $0.08 EPS.  
– Restructuring charges of $122 million (vs $52 million prior year) reduced margin 90 bp and EPS $0.10.  
– Stronger US-dollar cut margin by 60 bp and EPS $0.09.

### Segment detail (Q1, still including Health Care)

| Segment | Sales | Org. Growth | Operating Margin |
|---------|-------|-------------|------------------|
| Safety & Industrial | $2.7 bn | ‑1.4 % | 24.3 % (+410 bp) |
| Transportation & Electronics | $1.8 bn | +6.7 % | 26.3 % (+960 bp) |

*Health Care reported only in the appendix; spun on 1 April as Solventum.

Transportation & Electronics strength came largely from new spec-ins in mobile devices and automotive electrification; management estimates ~two-thirds of the Q1 growth reflected customers’ launch-related buy-ahead and channel restocking.

### Major strategic actions

Spin-off of Health Care (Solventum)  
• Completed 1 April 2024. Shareholders received 80.1 % of Solventum; 3M retained 19.9 % (accounted for as an equity investment).  
• Solventum issued $8.4 billion of debt; 3M retained ~$7.7 billion of the proceeds, enhancing liquidity.  
• Post-spin dis-synergies estimated at $150–175 million annually and are now allocated to the three remaining business groups beginning Q2.

Legal settlements finalised  
• U.S. Public Water Suppliers PFAS settlement: court approval on 29 March; pre-tax PV of payments up to $10.3 billion spread over 13 years, first payment expected 3Q 2024.  
• Combat Arms earplug litigation: >99 % claimant participation; up to $5.3 billion pre-tax PV payable through 2029.  
• 3M reiterated commitment to end all PFAS manufacturing by end-2025; transition discussions are underway with customers.

### 2024 guidance (continuing operations: Health Care treated as discontinued for full year)

• Organic sales growth: flat to +2 % (+1 % to +3 % after backing out portfolio/geographic actions).  
• Adjusted operating margin: up ~200–275 bp from an illustrative 18.7 % 2023 base.  
• Adjusted EPS: $6.80 – $7.30 (mid-point implies >15 % y/y growth).  
• Adjusted free-cash-flow conversion: 90 % – 110 %.  
• FX headwind: ~1 % to sales and ~$0.20 EPS at current spot.  
• Restructuring charges: $250 – $300 million (≈70 % in 1H).  
• Shifted stock-comp expense: $125 – $150 million will hit Q2.  
• H1/H2 cadence: revenue 49 %/51 %; operating income 47 %/53 %.

Segment-level organic growth outlook  
– Safety & Industrial: flat to low-single-digit growth.  
– Transportation & Electronics: low-single-digit growth (range nudged up after strong Q1).  
– Consumer: down low-single digits (≈2 pp drag from ongoing product/geography rationalisations).

### Dividend, share repurchase and capital policy

• Dividend to be reset to ~40 % of adjusted free cash flow, aligning with industrial-peer median but “well above” S&P 500 median; Board expected to declare new quarterly dividend in May for June payment.  
• After the spin, 3M has re-entered the market for share repurchases; pace will depend on macro conditions, performance and valuation.  
• Strong cash generation and the $7.7 billion Solventum proceeds give flexibility to fund growth, dividends, buybacks and legal obligations.

### Additional reporting changes

• From Q2, business-unit P&Ls will include stranded-cost dis-synergies.  
• A new “Other” category records transition-service-agreement (TSA) costs; after April these will be reimbursed by Solventum and should break even for the year.  
• “Corporate & Unallocated” now includes commercial-agreement sales to Solventum ($225 – $275 million 2024E) and a net operating loss of $125 – $175 million.  
• Recast 2023 results on a continuing-operations basis will be filed via 8-K before Q3.

### Leadership transition

Bill Brown becomes Chief Executive Officer on 1 May 2024; Mike Roman moves to Executive Chairman. Management reiterated commitment to its three strategic priorities: (1) reshaping the portfolio, (2) effectively managing legal risk, and (3) driving operational excellence to unlock growth and margin expansion.

3M’s strong Q1 execution, improved balance-sheet flexibility, and clarity on litigation payments underpin management’s confidence in delivering full-year growth, 200-plus basis points of margin expansion and high-teens EPS growth despite macro and FX headwinds.
