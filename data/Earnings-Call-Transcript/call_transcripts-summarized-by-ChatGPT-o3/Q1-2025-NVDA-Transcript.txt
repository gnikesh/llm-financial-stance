# NVIDIA Q1 FY 2025 Earnings Call – Financial and Strategic Highlights

NVIDIA reported another record quarter, with fiscal first-quarter 2025 revenue of **$26 billion**, rising **18 % sequentially** and **262 % year-on-year**, beating the company’s own $24 billion outlook.  Growth was again led by Data Center, but every platform except Gaming and Pro Viz (both seasonally lower) grew versus last year.  Management raised near-term guidance, detailed a wave of new products (H200, Blackwell, Spectrum-X, Grace Hopper) and large “AI factory’’ wins, announced a 10-for-1 stock split with a 150 % dividend increase, and reiterated that demand continues to exceed supply well into calendar 2025.

## Core Financial Results

• **Revenue**  
  – Total: **$26.0 billion** (+18 % Q/Q; +262 % Y/Y)  
  – Data Center: **$22.6 billion** (+23 % Q/Q; +427 % Y/Y)  
  – Gaming: **$2.65 billion** (-8 % Q/Q; +18 % Y/Y)  
  – Professional Visualization: **$427 million** (-8 % Q/Q; +45 % Y/Y)  
  – Automotive: **$329 million** (+17 % Q/Q; +11 % Y/Y)  

• **Margins & Operating Costs**  
  – GAAP gross margin **78.4 %**; non-GAAP **78.9 %** (both benefited from favourable component costs).  
  – GAAP operating expenses **$4.02 billion** (+10 % Q/Q); non-GAAP **$2.78 billion** (+13 % Q/Q), driven by compensation, compute and infrastructure spend.

• **Capital Returns & Balance Sheet**  
  – **$7.8 billion** returned via share repurchases and dividends in Q1.  
  – Announced **10-for-1 stock split** effective **June 10 2024** and a **150 % dividend increase**.  
  – No new debt figures were provided; management gave no indication of material balance-sheet stress.

## Guidance (Q2 FY 2025)

• **Revenue**: **$28 billion ± 2 %**; sequential growth expected across every segment.  
• **Margins**: GAAP gross margin **74.8 % ± 50 bp**; non-GAAP **75.5 % ± 50 bp**; full-year margin to remain “mid-70 %”.  
• **Operating Expenses**: GAAP **≈ $4.0 billion**; non-GAAP **≈ $2.8 billion**.  Full-year OpEx to rise “low-40 %” versus FY 2024.  
• **Other Income/Expense**: ~**+$300 million**.  
• **Tax rate**: **17 % ± 1 %**.

## Strategic, Product and Market Commentary

1. **Data Center Momentum & Mix**  
   • Compute revenue grew **>5×** Y/Y; networking **>3×**.  
   • Cloud providers still mid-40 % of Data Center revenue; inference accounted for ~**40 %** of trailing-four-quarter Data Center sales and is scaling rapidly alongside training.  
   • Demand is “well ahead of supply” for H200 and Blackwell GPUs; constraints may persist “well into next year.”

2. **New Platforms & Road-map**  
   • **H100** demand remains robust; **H200** sampling in Q1, production shipments start Q2. An HGX H200 server can produce **$7** of token revenue for every **$1** spent (company estimate).  
   • **Blackwell** architecture is now in “full production,” with first revenue recognized this year; shipments ramp Q2/Q3, customer clusters operational by Q4.  Blackwell promises **4× faster training** and up to **30× faster inference** than H100, plus **25× lower TCO**.  
   • **Grace-Hopper** Superchips shipping in volume; 80 % attach rate in new supercomputers (Alps, Isambard-AI, JUPITER).  
   • **Spectrum-X** Ethernet AI networking (Spectrum-4 switch + Bluefield-3 DPU) began shipping; expected to become a “multibillion-dollar” product line within a year.  
   • **NVIDIA Inference Microservices (NIM)** introduced: containerized, CUDA-optimized inference stacks sold via NVIDIA AI Enterprise.

3. **Sovereign AI & Geographic Diversification**  
   • NVIDIA is engaged with **100+** customers building “AI factories” from hundreds to 100 k GPUs.  
   • Government-backed “Sovereign AI” projects (Japan, France, Italy, Singapore, etc.) could approach “high-single-digit billions” of revenue in CY 2024 from effectively zero last year.  
   • China revenue “down significantly” after October export controls; market expected to remain “very competitive.” NVIDIA is shipping lower-spec H20 parts that do not require licenses but still faces supply triage.

4. **End-Market Updates**  
   • **Automotive** to be the largest enterprise vertical in Data Center by year-end, driven by Tesla (35 k H100s), BYD, XPeng, GAC, Xiaomi (SU7) and others.  
   • Gaming RTX installed base tops **100 million**; AI PCs benefit from recent TensorRT-LLM and Windows optimizations that boost local model throughput up to **3×**.  
   • **Professional Visualization** positioned for next wave of growth via Omniverse Cloud APIs; industrial customers like Wistron (-50 % cycle time, ‑40 % defects) and BYD adopting digital-twin workflows.

5. **Supply, Utilization & Competitive Landscape**  
   • Management emphasized that every GPU shipped is quickly deployed and monetized; for cloud operators each **$1** spent on NVIDIA infrastructure can yield **$5** in hosting revenue over four years.  
   • Demand remains broad-based—cloud, consumer internet, enterprise, automotive, healthcare and Sovereign AI—leaving “no signs of double-ordering.”  
   • NVIDIA claims architectural versatility (CUDA, full-stack software, NVLink/InfiniBand/Ethernet fabrics) and rapid one-year product cadence will keep it ahead of in-house ASIC and rival GPU efforts.

## Management’s Broader Vision

CEO Jensen Huang framed the current phase as “the next industrial revolution,” in which data centers transition from general-purpose computing to accelerated “AI factories” that manufacture intelligence.  NVIDIA intends to supply the full stack—GPUs, CPUs, interconnects, networking, systems and software—on a yearly innovation rhythm, driving both **performance leadership** and **lower total cost of ownership** for customers.
