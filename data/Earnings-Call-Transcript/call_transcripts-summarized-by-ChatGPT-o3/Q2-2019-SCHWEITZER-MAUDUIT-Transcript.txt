# Schweitzer-Mauduit International Q2 2019 Earnings Call – Financial Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) reported second-quarter 2019 net sales of **$270 million**, unchanged from the prior-year quarter, and delivered **adjusted earnings per share (EPS) of $1.06**, a year-on-year increase of **6 percent**. Consolidated **adjusted operating profit rose 6 percent to $50.6 million**, while **adjusted EBITDA reached $60.2 million**, up 4 percent.  The company generated **$37 million of free cash flow through June** and reiterated its full-year free-cash-flow target of **approximately $100 million**.  

GAAP EPS declined to **$0.66** from $0.83 a year earlier, reflecting a **$0.24 per-share hit from Brazilian tax assessments tied to pre-2000 activities**.  The assessments—covering raw-material and employee social-benefit taxes—include sizeable interest and penalties; SWM intends to appeal, use offsetting tax credits, or seek relief programs, but booked the full amount under GAAP.  Excluding the charge, the **normalized effective tax rate was 21.6 percent**.

## Segment Performance

Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• **Sales:** **$127 million**, up 2 percent as reported and 3 percent in constant currency.  
• **Adjusted operating profit:** **$25.5 million**, up 13 percent; **operating margin exceeded 20 percent for the first time**, expanding 190 basis points versus the strongest prior-year quarter (≈18 percent).  
• Growth drivers included double-digit gains in transportation surface-protection films and reverse-osmosis (RO) water-filtration products, fixed-cost reductions, efficiency initiatives, and lower polypropylene resin costs, which continued to decline during the quarter.  
• SWM commissioned its first European transportation-film line last year; with capacity now full, it announced a **follow-on TPU film investment in Suzhou, China**.  When operational, SWM will be the only paint-protection-film supplier with production on three continents.  
• A newly launched **membrane-vacuum paper**—manufactured on Engineered Papers (EP) assets in France and sold through AMS channels—broadens the RO product bundle; customer feedback highlights superior uniformity and defect-free quality.  
• Medical sales slipped slightly in Q2 but remain positive year-to-date; infrastructure and construction sales were down modestly, hurt by extreme weather and tight hay supply (a key blanket component). Management views the decline as transitory.  

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• **Sales:** **$143 million**, down 2 percent as reported but **up 2 percent excluding a 4 percent currency headwind**.  
• **Adjusted operating profit:** **$33.8 million**, essentially flat; **margin improved 70 basis points to 23.6 percent**.  
• **Price/mix improved 11 percent and fully offset a 9 percent volume decline**.  Volume weakness stemmed from (i) deliberately reduced low-margin printing & writing grades, (ii) tough comparisons in heat-not-burn reconstituted (Recon) papers as customers filled pipelines last year, and (iii) softer traditional RTL volumes.  Offsetting these were stable low-ignition-propensity (LIP) and other cigarette papers, plus strong wrapper and binder Recon demand for Asian small-cigar launches.  
• Contractual price increases implemented earlier in 2019 recaptured prior wood-pulp inflation; pulp costs were modestly higher year-on-year but are now easing.  Energy, chemical, and labor costs remain inflationary, but efficiency actions and favorable mix preserved the >20 percent segment margin.

## Cash Flow, Capital Spending, and Balance Sheet

• **Capital expenditures** totaled **$9 million in Q2** and **$18 million year-to-date**, on track for **$35–40 million** in 2019.  Spending is concentrated on the new transportation-film line and on IT systems that enhance business-intelligence capabilities.  
• **Net leverage** (credit-facility definition) remained **2.5× net debt to adjusted EBITDA**, unchanged from year-end 2018; management expects leverage to drift lower by year-end.  Interest expense rose versus last year because of the debt financing completed in Q3 2018, but the company separated normal debt interest from the Brazil-related interest booked in GAAP results.

## Outlook and Management Commentary

Management is pleased with mid-year progress: both segments are expanding margins, raw-material pressure is easing, and organic initiatives are gaining traction without acquisition assistance.  AMS margins are likely to moderate from the Q2 peak but should remain higher than 2018 levels, supported by high-value product launches and ongoing cost discipline.  EP price/mix should stay positive, and year-over-year volume comparisons will become less severe in the second half as last year’s heat-not-burn pipeline build laps.  

With free cash flow tracking toward the **$100 million** objective and capital commitments largely funded from operating cash, SWM expects **net leverage to decline modestly** while it continues to evaluate bolt-on M&A opportunities that add scale and diversification.  Management also affirmed that no contractual price give-backs are required as input costs retreat.  Overall, SWM believes it is executing effectively on the strategic roadmap laid out over the past five years and is positioned for continued earnings and cash-flow growth in the remainder of 2019.
