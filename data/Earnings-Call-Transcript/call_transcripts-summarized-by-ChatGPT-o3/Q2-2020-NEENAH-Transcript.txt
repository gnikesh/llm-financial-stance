# Neenah, Inc. ‒ Q2 2020 Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah’s second quarter was dominated by the COVID-19 shock, but management underscored decisive cost actions, strong cash generation and ample liquidity that they believe will position the company well for the eventual recovery.

Revenue and earnings  
• Net sales were $161 million, a 36 % year-on-year decline that largely mirrored the contraction in the U.S. economy.  
• Adjusted operating income fell to just $0.5 million from $23 million a year ago; GAAP operating income was negative after $59 million of mostly non-cash charges (asset impairments, write-offs and restructuring).  
• GAAP EPS was a loss of $2.98. Excluding adjusting items, EPS was a loss of $0.08 versus a $0.95 profit last year.  
• The largest non-cash charge was a partial impairment of the U.S. transportation filtration asset, triggered by a lengthened volume ramp-up and COVID-19–related demand weakness. Management still expects that asset to reach its original $70 million-plus revenue potential over time.

Segment performance  
Technical Products  
– Net sales: $106 million (-28 %).  
– Adjusted operating income: $5 million vs. $13 million in 2019.  
– Volume shortfall plus under-absorbed fixed costs cost more than $17 million; lower input costs net of price provided a $3 million benefit.  
– Bright spots: industrial filtration, N95-equivalent face-mask media, medical packaging and high-efficiency HVAC.  

Fine Paper & Packaging  
– Net sales: $55 million vs. $107 million (-49 %).  
– Adjusted operating profit: $0.5 million vs. $16 million.  
– Combined volume loss and fixed-cost under-absorption totalled $19 million.  
– Premium packaging, consumer products (e.g., ASTROBRIGHTS and SOUTHWORTH) and wine/spirit labels outperformed; commercial print (advertising) remained weak.

Cost, cash and liquidity  
• Manufacturing cost reductions totalled $15 million in the quarter; ongoing annual savings are expected to be roughly $7 million ($4 million SG&A, $3 million COGS).  
• SG&A dropped $6 million to $21 million; management now targets ~$22 million per quarter in 2H20 (historical run-rate: $25 million).  
• Cash from operations reached almost $30 million, helped by more than $20 million released from working capital and reduced pension contributions and CapEx.  
• Year-to-date CapEx was $8 million; full-year spending is projected at ~$15 million—about half of “normal” levels—focused on cost-savings and growth projects.  
• Free-cash-flow enhancement plan (targeting $50 million vs. the original 2020 plan) is expected to be surpassed.  

Balance sheet and refinancing  
• Quarter-end cash and debt were temporarily inflated by the new financing; pro forma for the redemption of the 2021 notes, cash would have been ~$28 million and gross debt ~$206 million.  
• In June Neenah put in place a $200 million Term Loan B at an initial 5 % rate and used the proceeds to redeem the $175 million 6⅞ % 2021 senior notes on 16 July.  
• The $175 million global revolver is now undrawn, leaving roughly $130 million of availability; management described both the revolver and Term Loan B as covenant-light.  
• Net interest expense was $3 million in Q2; it will tick up to ~$3.7 million in Q3 (overlap of borrowings) and then fall to about $3.3 million a quarter.  
• The ongoing effective tax rate is estimated at 22 % (Q2 showed a 19 % benefit due to losses and a $4 million valuation-allowance increase).

Outlook and guidance markers  
• Sequential revenue improvement has continued into July/August; Q3 sales are still expected to trail 2019 but at a much smaller percentage decline than Q2.  
• Scheduled annual maintenance shutdowns will lift Q3 and Q4 maintenance expense by about $1.5 million each quarter versus Q2.  
• Management anticipates modest inflation in input costs as economies reopen, which will diminish year-on-year commodity tailwinds.  
• Currency is becoming a tailwind: every $0.05 move in the euro adds roughly $2.5 million of sales and $0.5 million of operating income per quarter; the euro has risen from $1.10 in Q2 to around $1.18.  
• Technical Products is expected to return to pre-pandemic sales levels; Fine Paper & Packaging should recover more slowly, with premium packaging and consumer products leading and commercial print rebounding as advertising budgets revive.  
• Neenah remains committed to its quarterly dividend and to channeling cash from “foundational” businesses (fine paper, backings) into four growth platforms: filtration, custom-engineered materials, premium packaging & design, and specialty coatings—pursuing both organic projects and disciplined M&A.  

Strategic initiatives and COVID-driven opportunities  
• Face-mask and replacement-filter media (Europe) could scale toward ~$20 million of annual revenue, carrying above-average margins and reinforcing Neenah’s air-purification platform.  
• Growth is accelerating in high-efficiency HVAC media, data-storage–related industrial filtration, sustainable digital-transfer media for textiles/home décor, tape backings for DIY markets, and eco-friendly packaging/label substrates intended to replace plastics.  
• Injury rates have been reduced by nearly 30 % year-to-date, reflecting heightened safety protocols.  

Overall, Q2 demonstrated the depth of the COVID-19 demand shock, particularly in Fine Paper & Packaging, but also highlighted strong cash generation, permanent cost take-outs, a successful refinancing that extends maturities and lowers cash interest, and a pipeline of growth initiatives—especially in filtration—that management says will enable Neenah to emerge from the downturn in a stronger competitive and financial position.
