# Schweitzer-Mauduit International Q2 2020 Earnings – Financial Summary and Management Commentary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) delivered second-quarter results that management characterized as “positive in a challenging world,” with resilient margins, solid cash generation and rapid debt reduction despite COVID-19–related volume pressure and temporary plant closures.

Second-Quarter Consolidated Results  
• Net sales fell 6 % year-over-year.  
• Adjusted operating profit declined 15 %, while adjusted EBITDA was down 12 %.  
• GAAP EPS rose slightly to $0.68 (versus $0.66 a year ago) because last year’s figure included a $0.24/sh non-cash Brazilian tax charge.  
• Adjusted EPS decreased 15 % to $0.90, pressured mainly by lower AMS transportation volumes and COVID-driven production interruptions.  
• The adjusted tax rate was 21.8 %, up 60 bp.  

First-Half 2020  
• Adjusted EPS of $1.75 was essentially flat with 1H 2019, underscoring the stability of the portfolio.  

Cash Flow, Liquidity and Leverage  
• Free cash flow was described as “strong”; SWM generated enough cash to pay down nearly $90 million of debt during Q2.  
• Net-debt-to-adjusted-EBITDA stands at 2.8×, well inside covenant limits.  
• The company has > $430 million of liquidity (cash plus revolver).  
• Cap-ex was ~$17 million year-to-date, running below the original $40–$45 million full-year budget as some projects are being deferred.  
• Management still expects 2020 free cash flow to “potentially approach $100 million” and affirmed its commitment to the dividend.  

Capital Allocation & Guidance  
• No change to capital-allocation priorities: dividends, debt reduction, strategic investments/M&A.  
• Unallocated corporate expense should finish the year in the mid-$40 million range (includes Tekra deal fees).  
• Given COVID-19 uncertainty, the company did not reinstate full-year EPS guidance.  

Segment Performance  

Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• Reported sales +5 % to $—, driven by the Tekra acquisition, which contributed $25 million.  
• Organic sales –15 %; excluding transportation films, organic sales were down only 4 %.  
• Adjusted operating profit was $20.8 million, $4.7 million lower than last year; segment margin contracted 440 bp to 15.7 %.  
• Transportation films remain the most COVID-impacted business; body-shop and dealership traffic collapsed under stay-at-home orders, especially in China early in the quarter. Management is cautious for Q3 but sees “green shoots” that may lift Q4.  
• Medical materials grew solidly across melt-blown media for N95 masks, traditional surgical masks, gowns, drapes, bedding and instrument packaging; management expects momentum to continue.  
• Industrial films benefited from e-commerce packaging demand; netting used in wind-turbine blades also performed well.  
• Filtration was mixed: strong air-filtration sales (share gains and more frequent filter changes) offset mid-single-digit declines in water filtration and weaker process-filtration demand tied to lower industrial activity.  
• Infrastructure and construction weakened after a strong Q1, particularly in oil-and-gas erosion-control netting; potential stimulus for highways could be a future catalyst.  
• Tekra integration is on track; cultural fit is good, synergies and cross-selling opportunities are developing as planned.  

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• Sales –15 % (–13 % ex-FX), reflecting normal attrition, inventory destocking and pandemic-related shutdowns.  
• The Ancram, NY wrapper/binder facility was idled for six weeks under state orders; along with a short closure in France, the profit impact was “several million dollars.”  
• Adjusted operating profit slipped only $1 million (–3 %) as aggressive cost control, favorable mix (LIP cigarette papers), lower wood-pulp prices and FX tailwinds more than offset volume loss; segment margin expanded 340 bp to an impressive 27 %.  
• Heat-not-burn cigarette papers more than doubled versus 2019 YTD, and LIP papers remained robust.  
• Demand for wrapper and binder papers is high; with Ancram fully operational since mid-May and customers replenishing inventory, management expects volumes to normalize in 2H.  
• EP margins should stay “solid”; the main risk would be any new or prolonged production disruptions.  

COVID-19 Operational Update  
• All plants worldwide are now running, and SWM’s global supply-chain flexibility allowed rerouting of inventory and production to keep customer service high during closures.  
• Safety protocols are firmly in place; employee health and uninterrupted supply remain top priorities.  

Strategic Outlook  
Management reiterated its conviction in the portfolio’s long-term organic growth and cash-generation capabilities. While near-term visibility is clouded by the pandemic, SWM believes the diversity of its end-markets (tobacco papers, medical, filtration, industrial packaging, transportation, infrastructure) and its disciplined cost structure will continue to support strong cash flow, disciplined debt reduction and attractive shareholder returns.
