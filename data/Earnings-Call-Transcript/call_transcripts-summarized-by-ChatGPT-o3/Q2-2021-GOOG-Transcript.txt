# Alphabet Inc. – Q2 2021 Earnings Call Summary

Alphabet delivered a record‐setting second quarter, driven by broad-based advertising strength, continued momentum in Google Cloud and YouTube, and disciplined cost control. Management repeatedly cautioned that year-on-year comparisons are flattered by the pandemic-related trough in Q2 2020, yet the company still posted accelerating growth across most lines of business.

Alphabet’s consolidated revenue reached **$61.9 billion**, up **62 %** year over year (57 % in constant currency).  Total cost of revenues rose 41 % to **$26.2 billion** and included a **$10.9 billion** traffic-acquisition-cost (TAC) bill, up 63 %.  Operating expenses grew 22 % to **$16.3 billion**, reflecting head-count additions (+4,061 quarter over quarter) and a resumption of advertising and promotional spend that had been curtailed during 2020.  Operating income more than tripled to **$19.4 billion**, producing an operating margin of **31 %** versus 18 % a year ago.  Other income was **$2.6 billion**, driven by unrealised gains on equity investments, lifting **net income to $18.5 billion**.

Free cash flow for the quarter came in at **$16.4 billion** (trailing-twelve-month FCF: **$58.5 billion**).  Alphabet ended June with **$136 billion** in cash and marketable securities.  (The company did not disclose outstanding debt balances on the call.)  The board amended the existing **$50 billion** share-repurchase authorisation to permit repurchases of both Class A and Class C shares.

### Segment Performance  

• **Google Services** revenue surged **63 %** to **$57.1 billion**.  
  – **Search & Other Ads**: **$35.8 billion**, up **68 %**; retail remained the largest contributor, while travel, financial services, and media/entertainment also rebounded sharply.  
  – **YouTube Ads**: **$7.0 billion**, up **84 %**; brand advertising led growth, followed by direct-response.  Connected-TV viewing remains the fastest-growing consumer surface; YouTube now reaches 120-plus million U.S. viewers on TV screens each month.  Shorts exceeded **15 billion daily views**.  
  – **Network Ads** (Ad Manager & AdMob): **$7.6 billion**, up **60 %**.  
  – **Other Revenues** (YouTube subscriptions, Google Play, hardware including Fitbit): **$6.6 billion**, up **29 %**.  
  Services operating income doubled to **$22.3 billion**, a **39 %** margin.

• **Google Cloud** revenue grew **54 %** to **$4.6 billion**.  Google Cloud Platform again outpaced overall Cloud growth, driven by security (zero-trust architecture, Chronicle), data analytics (BigQuery), AI/ML differentiation and industry-specific solutions (e.g., SAP migrations, 5G edge with telecom partners).  Workspace continued to add seats and increase ARPU, particularly in enterprise accounts.  Segment operating loss narrowed to **$591 million**, evidencing early operating-leverage despite management’s pledge to “invest aggressively” in go-to-market, compute capacity and new regions (now 26).

• **Other Bets** brought in **$192 million** of revenue and posted a **$1.4 billion** operating loss.  Waymo has provided “tens of thousands” of fully driver-less rides in Phoenix since October 2020 and intends to scale materially during 2022.

### Cash Usage, Capital Expenditure and Hiring

Capital expenditures accelerated as Alphabet restarted datacentre builds and office fit-outs that had slowed during COVID.  Depreciation expense benefited by **$721 million** from the previously announced extension of server useful lives, though this tail-wind will diminish in the second half.  Management signalled seasonally higher Q3 head-count additions (new graduates) and heavier second-half sales-and-marketing spend around product launches and the holiday season.

### Outlook and Key Themes

•  Currency provided a >4 % revenue tail-wind in Q2 but is expected to be “more muted” in Q3.  
•  The lapping benefit of the pandemic trough will fade through H2 2021, and management warned that the global recovery remains “uneven and unpredictable,” especially given rising COVID-19 variants.  
•  Google Play faces head-winds from fee reductions (effective 1 July) and tough engagement comps.  
•  Cloud will continue to prioritise growth over near-term profit, expanding sales teams, regions and product breadth.  
•  Sustainability remains core: five datacentres already operate at ~90 % carbon-free energy as Alphabet works toward its 24/7 carbon-free goal by 2030.

### Strategic and Product Highlights

•  Alphabet’s AI investments were highlighted via new language models (MUM, Lambda) and DeepMind’s AlphaFold protein database.  
•  Android 12, launching this autumn alongside a new Pixel line-up, introduces a privacy dashboard and improved power efficiency.  
•  Google Play has now paid developers over **$120 billion** cumulatively.  
•  Alphabet set quarterly payout records both to publisher partners across Google’s ad network and to YouTube creators.  
•  Partnerships span industries: PayPal, Johnson Controls and Whirlpool running SAP on Google Cloud; MLB for cybersecurity; Siemens for AI-driven industrial automation; telecom carriers AT&T, Reliance Jio, TIM and Ericsson on 5G; and a cross-Google multi-year pact with Univision.

### Financial Takeaways

Alphabet exits Q2 2021 with:

•  **Revenue momentum** across Search, YouTube and Cloud well above pre-pandemic trends.  
•  **Record profitability** and robust free-cash-flow generation, enabling continued heavy R&D and capex while supporting large-scale share repurchases.  
•  **Fortress balance sheet** (net cash positive with $136 billion of liquidity and no material debt discussion).  
•  A management stance that remains **cautiously optimistic**: near-term comps will normalise, but secular shifts to digital advertising, e-commerce, cloud computing, AI-driven security and hybrid work provide a long runway for sustained growth.
