# 3M Company Q2 2021 Earnings – Comprehensive Financial Summary  

3M delivered a strong second quarter, combining broad‐based volume recovery with disciplined cost control and healthy cash generation, yet the company continues to navigate rising input costs, supply-chain bottlenecks, a tapering of pandemic-driven respirator demand and a heavier legal calendar.

### Consolidated Results  
• Net sales were $8.95 billion, up 24.7 % year-on-year; organic local-currency growth was 21 % versus a 13 % decline in the prior-year quarter.  
• GAAP and adjusted EPS were identical at **$2.59**, rising 44 % Y/Y and beating consensus by $0.31.  
• Adjusted operating income reached **$2.0 billion** (+40 % Y/Y); adjusted operating margin expanded 240 basis points to **22.0 %**.  
• Adjusted EBITDA margin exceeded **27 %**.  
• A Brazilian Supreme Court ruling cut past-due social taxes, adding $91 million to operating income (≈1 pp margin, $0.12 EPS).  
• Inflation (raw materials, outsourced manufacturing and logistics) created a 140 bp margin drag and a $0.17 EPS headwind in Q2; the full-year cost hit is now expected at **$0.65–$0.80 per share** (previously $0.30–$0.50).

### Cash Flow, Capital Allocation & Leverage  
• Adjusted free cash flow was **$1.6 billion** (+2 % Y/Y) with a **103 %** conversion rate; 1H FCF rose to **$3.0 billion** (vs. $2.5 billion).  
• Q2 cap-ex was **$394 million**; year-to-date ≈$0.7 billion. Management now expects full-year outlays at the **low end of the $1.8–$2.0 billion** range because of vendor constraints.  
• Shareholder returns in the quarter totaled **$1.4 billion** (dividends $858 million; buybacks $503 million); 1H returns are $2.5 billion.  
• Net debt ended at **$12.7 billion**, down $3.5 billion Y/Y; net-debt-to-EBITDA fell to **1.3×** from 1.9×.

### Operational & Market Backdrop  
• Annual respirator capacity quadrupled to 2.5 billion units; pandemic demand peaked in Q1 2021 and is now being wound down. 3M is reallocating some production from health-care channels to industrial/consumer users and will idle lines but can surge again if needed.  
• Semiconductor shortages continue to constrain automotive and some electronics volumes; raw material tightness and port congestion are driving broad-based price increases.  
• Priority Growth Platforms (e.g., advanced wound care, automotive electrification, home improvement adhesives) grew >40 % Y/Y and now exceed $500 million in revenue.  
• China (≈10 % of company sales) grew >20 % in Q2, led by general industrial, auto and electronics, but faces tougher comps in H2.  

### Inflation, Pricing & Margin Arithmetic  
• Management is executing “broad-based” price increases; net price was +10 bp in Q2 versus +70 bp in Q1 because of mix and volume rebates, but 3M expects sequential improvement.  
• Price–cost is projected to be a **50–100 bp margin headwind in Q3** and to turn **flat-to-positive in Q4** as pricing catches up with cost inflation.  

### Legal, Environmental & Restructuring  
• Q2 environmental accrual increased **$60 million**; respiratory-related liabilities rose **$20 million**.  
• Ongoing legal defense spending climbed ahead of PFAS (Wolverine, Michigan) and Combat Arms Earplug trials scheduled for September/October.  
• Q2 restructuring charge: **$40 million**; remaining 2021 charges expected at **$60–$110 million**, with **$50–$75 million** in Q3 tied to the December 2020 restructuring program.  

### Updated 2021 Outlook (raised)  
• Organic sales growth: **+6 % to +9 %** (prior +3 % to +6 %).  
• Full-year EPS: **$9.70 – $10.10** (prior $9.20 – $9.70).  
• FCF conversion: **90 %–100 %** (prior 95 %–105 %) owing to working-capital needs in tight supply chains.  
• Raw-material & logistics cost drag: **$0.65–$0.80/share**; **$0.40–$0.55** will hit in H2.  
• Disposable-respirator revenue expected to fall **$100–$300 million Y/Y** in H2 (Q3: down $50–$100 million).  
• Q3 macro assumptions: global auto builds –3 % Y/Y; smartphone shipments –high-single-digits; continued sequential recovery in elective medical procedures.  

### Balance-of-Year Priorities & Strategic Posture  
Management remains focused on:  
1. Passing through further price increases and driving productivity to protect margins against inflation.  
2. Sustaining elevated R&D and cap-ex in high-growth platforms while executing the December 2020 restructuring.  
3. Preserving balance-sheet strength (net-debt/EBITDA 1.3×) to retain flexibility for bolt-on M&A, ongoing share buybacks (YTD $0.7 billion) and the 63rd consecutive annual dividend increase.  
4. Mitigating supply-chain disruptions (semiconductors, chemicals, resins, logistics) and reallocating respirator capacity as pandemic-related demand fades but industrial demand recovers.  
5. Navigating an active litigation calendar while advancing sustainability commitments (carbon, water, plastics).  

Management reiterated confidence in delivering the raised guidance, citing robust end-market demand, an improving price-cost trajectory, disciplined execution and a robust innovation pipeline.
