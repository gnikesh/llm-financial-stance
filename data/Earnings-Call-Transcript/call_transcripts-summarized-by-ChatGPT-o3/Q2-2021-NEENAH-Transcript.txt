# Neenah, Inc. – Q2 2021 Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah delivered a second-consecutive quarter of rapid recovery, pairing near-record sales with the first contributions from the ITASA acquisition, while simultaneously wrestling with an unprecedented surge in raw-material costs.  Management reiterated its long-term goal of 5 % annual revenue growth, mid-teen operating margins and leverage below 3 ×, and said the group remains on track to exit 2021 with margins expanding again and pricing fully covering cost inflation.

Second-Quarter Headline Figures  
• Net sales climbed to $269 million, up $108 million versus the COVID-depressed second quarter of 2020 and up $42 million sequentially.  ITASA contributed $33 million.  
• Adjusted earnings per share were $0.65, reversing last year’s adjusted loss of $0.08.  GAAP EPS was reduced by $2.41 of unusual charges, primarily acquisition and restructuring costs.  
• Company-wide adjusted operating income reached $19.3 million (vs. $0.5 million a year ago) but fell from $26.1 million in Q1 because raw-material inflation outpaced price increases.  
• Trailing twelve-month adjusted EBITDA increased to $121 million (from $101 million for FY-2020).  
• Operating cash flow for the first six months totalled $23 million, down from $44 million in the prior-year period as working capital rebuilt with the sales rebound.  
• Year-to-date cap-ex was $11 million; full-year spending is now projected in the low-to-mid $30 million range, including the recently approved $13 million release-liner coater.  
• Net leverage, temporarily higher after the ITASA purchase, is forecast to drop to roughly 3 × adjusted EBITDA by year-end, supported by rising earnings and free cash flow.  

Segment Performance  
Technical Products set an all-time quarterly record with sales of $180 million, 79 % above last year and 24 % above Q1.  Adjusted operating income more than doubled year-over-year to $14.7 million but declined from $19.5 million in Q1 as chemicals, pulp and freight costs escalated disproportionately in this segment.  

Fine Paper & Packaging revenue improved to $90 million, up 48 % year-over-year and 10 % sequentially, as commercial print stabilised, premium packaging rebounded and consumer stationery gained share.  Adjusted operating income swung to a $10 million profit from last year’s $0.6 million loss, though margins were squeezed versus Q1 ($12.8 million) by rising input costs.  Demand has recovered to about 90 % of pre-pandemic levels, prompting management to restart an idled paper machine at the Neenah, Wisconsin mill.  

Raw-Material Inflation and Pricing Actions  
Management characterised the inflationary environment as “unprecedented,” citing record fibre prices and global chemical shortages.  Input-cost headwinds reduced Q2 profit materially and are expected to add a further $7–8 million in Q3; however, roughly half of that incremental burden should be offset in the quarter through already implemented price increases.  Most product lines have seen multiple price rounds, and any remaining contractual increases take effect early in 2022.  The company expects raw-material costs to peak in Q3 and stabilise in Q4, with the pricing/cost gap narrowing late in the year and fully closing as 2022 begins.  

Strategic Moves and Capital Allocation  
• ITASA Acquisition – Closed at the start of Q2.  The specialty-coated release-liner business generated $33 million of sales and EBITDA margins in the high teens during its first quarter inside Neenah, adding roughly $0.15 to EPS.  Integration is on schedule, synergy capture has begun, and cross-selling already produced two sizeable North American customer wins.  

• Growth Investment – A $13 million coater will be installed at ITASA’s three-year-old Mexican plant to keep pace with release-liner growth that has historically averaged more than 8 % annually.  

• Footprint Optimisation – The Appleton, Wisconsin filtration mill will be closed in Q4.  Annualised cost savings of $7–8 million will outweigh the $20–25 million revenue reduction; about one-quarter of that benefit and revenue loss will fall in Q4.  The action does not alter Neenah’s commitment to the filtration platform, which remains above 2019 levels (+11 % YTD).  

• Debt Refinancing – Completed on favourable terms to fund ITASA and lower ongoing interest expense; coupled with EBITDA growth, the company remains confident in returning leverage to ~3 × without curtailing strategic investment.  

Operational and ESG Highlights  
The Neenah Operating System—the company’s Lean manufacturing programme—continues to outpace initial targets, driving measurable capacity gains, efficiency improvements and waste reduction; deployments have now expanded beyond the first two pilot mills.  Safety performance set a new record for low recordable incidents, with more than half the sites operating 100+ days injury-free.  Neenah also received an external energy-efficiency award for its largest North American facility and appointed Shruti Singhal (ex-Dow, Henkel) to the board, enhancing ESG and industry expertise.  

Growth Platforms and Innovation  
Management reiterated its four-platform framework—Filtration Media, Specialty Coatings, Engineered Materials and Imaging & Packaging—as the lens for capital allocation, R&D and M&A.  Recent developments include:  
• Launch of NeenahPure™, an electrostatically charged HVAC/air-purifier media achieving up to 99.9 % efficiency.  
• Expansion of the DISPERSA™ water-dispersible label range, a first-and-only North American solution enabling fully recyclable labels with dissolvable graphics.  
• Strong retailer uptake of new back-to-school planners, journals and teacher tools, and renewed momentum in gift cards, box wraps and folding board packaging.  

Outlook  
Demand across all categories remains robust heading into the seasonally slower third quarter.  Management expects Q3 results to absorb peak inflation, $1–2 million of scheduled maintenance downtime and the early stages of Appleton’s wind-down, but believes volume strength, accelerating price realisation and ongoing efficiency gains will allow Neenah to exit 2021 on an improving margin trajectory.  With ITASA integration on track, incremental coating capacity approved, and leverage trending lower, the company says it is well positioned to deliver its targeted 5 % top-line growth and double-digit bottom-line expansion over time.
