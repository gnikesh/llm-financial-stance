# NVIDIA FY21 Q2 Earnings Call – Financial & Strategic Highlights

NVIDIA reported an exceptionally strong second quarter for fiscal 2021, with record-setting revenue and continued momentum across gaming, data-center compute, and high-performance networking, even as COVID-19 kept most employees working from home.

Revenue climbed to $3.87 billion, up 50 % year on year and 26 % sequentially, exceeding guidance by a wide margin.  GAAP gross margin fell to 58.8 % (primarily Mellanox-related acquisition costs), while non-GAAP gross margin – the metric management uses for operational performance – expanded six points YoY to 66 % thanks to a richer mix of data-center sales and lower automotive exposure.  GAAP operating expenses reached $1.62 billion; on a non-GAAP basis opex rose 38 % YoY to $1.04 billion, reflecting Mellanox consolidation and the accelerated product-road-map spend that underpinned multiple launches in the quarter.

GAAP EPS increased 10 % YoY to $0.99; non-GAAP EPS surged 76 % to $2.18.  Operating cash flow remained robust at $1.57 billion.

Segment performance underscored the breadth of NVIDIA’s franchise:

• Gaming revenue reached $1.65 billion, advancing 26 % YoY and 24 % sequentially.  A COVID-driven rise in at-home entertainment, vigorous demand for GeForce RTX-based desktops and laptops (over 100 new notebook designs ramped), as well as the early success of the subscription cloud service GeForce NOW (recently extended to Chromebooks and several international telecom partners), all contributed to the beat.  The install base is still early in the RTX upgrade cycle; management reiterated confidence that the upcoming September 1st GeForce event and next-generation consoles will catalyze what could be “the best gaming season ever.”

• Data Center set another record at $1.75 billion, up 167 % YoY and 54 % sequentially.  The Mellanox acquisition – fully consolidated for the first time – contributed roughly 14 % of company revenue and slightly over 30 % of the data-center total, with strength in both InfiniBand and Ethernet as hyperscale customers modernised high-speed fabrics for AI-intensive micro-services.  Compute revenue accelerated on the launch of the Ampere-based A100 GPU, a 54-billion-transistor “universal accelerator” that boosts AI training performance up to 20× versus Volta.  All major cloud vendors (Google was first; Microsoft Azure announced availability during the call, with AWS, Alibaba, Baidu and Tencent to follow) and more than 50 server OEM designs are ramping A100.  NVIDIA’s Selene supercomputer, built in under four weeks on the DGX SuperPOD architecture, debuted No. 7 on the TOP500 list while topping energy-efficiency charts at over 20 GFLOPS/W.

• Professional Visualization revenue fell to $203 million, a 30 % YoY and 34 % sequential decline as enterprise office closures dampened workstation purchases in automotive, AEC, manufacturing, M&E, and energy.  Offsetting this, vGPU bookings rose 60 % YoY as companies enabled remote creatives and engineers, and RTX adoption widened to 50+ leading content-creation applications.

• Automotive revenue dropped to $111 million, down 47 % YoY and 28 % sequentially, although results outperformed NVIDIA’s internally forecast 40 % seq. decline as global production recovered from an April trough.  A key strategic win came with Mercedes-Benz: beginning in 2024 every new model will ship with NVIDIA DRIVE AGX hardware, end-to-end AV/AI software, and cloud infrastructure, positioning NVIDIA to earn recurring over-the-air software revenue as vehicles transition into “data centers on wheels.”

NVIDIA also highlighted two cumulative milestones: shipment of its one-billionth CUDA-capable GPU and surpassing two million registered developers (the second million added in less than two years).

Management outlook for fiscal Q3-21 calls for revenue of approximately $4.4 billion ± 2 %.  Gaming is expected to rise just over 25 % sequentially, while data-center sales are guided to grow in the low- to mid-single digits; ProViz and Automotive should hold flat with Q2 levels.  GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins are projected at 62.5 % and 65.5 % respectively (± 50 bps), with GAAP opex of about $1.54 billion and non-GAAP opex of $1.09 billion.  The tax rate remains 8 % ±1 %, and FY-21 capital expenditures are planned at $225–250 million.  Full-year opex is still expected to total roughly $5.7 billion GAAP ( $4.1 billion non-GAAP).

CEO Jensen Huang emphasised that accelerated computing and high-speed networking are now integral to modern, disaggregated cloud architectures; that AI breakthroughs in natural-language understanding, recommendation systems and conversational agents are expanding the company’s addressable market; and that NVIDIA, Mellanox and recently acquired Cumulus Networks together give the firm unmatched ability to design “data-center-scale” platforms that can be monetised not only through hardware but also through recurring software and cloud services such as NVIDIA AI, GeForce NOW, NV virtual workstation and, longer term, autonomous-vehicle subscriptions.

With the Ampere product cycle still in its early stages, a large portion of the installed gaming and server bases yet to upgrade, and strategic bets in edge AI, 5G, robotics and software-defined vehicles starting to monetize, management believes the growth drivers evident in Q2 are fundamentally secular rather than COVID pull-ins, setting the stage for sustained revenue expansion and rising operating leverage in the periods ahead.
