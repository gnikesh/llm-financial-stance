# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q2 2021 Earnings Call Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International reported strong second-quarter 2021 results, highlighted by revenue growth across most end markets and the successful first contributions from the recently acquired Scapa Group. Consolidated net sales climbed 49 % year-over-year and 11 % on an organic basis, reflecting broad-based demand recovery. Despite pervasive inflation in resins and pulp, logistics bottlenecks and temporary shortages of specialty TPU resins that limited output in certain product lines, management reiterated that underlying demand and order books remain “full” and that pricing actions are largely offsetting cost pressures.

Management’s commentary and the detailed financial results are summarised below:

• Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS) – The segment’s sales jumped 90 % (18 % organically) and would have been higher if transportation films had not been constrained by limited specialty TPU availability; management estimates up to US $10 million of transportation revenue and roughly US $0.10 of EPS were deferred by this shortage. Transportation sales still rose nearly 60 %, filtration exceeded 25 % growth, and construction also grew more than 25 %. Ex-Scapa healthcare products faced a difficult comparison with last year’s COVID-driven spike in face-mask media but delivered gains in advanced wound care, dental films and packaging. AMS adjusted operating income increased 60 %; however, higher polypropylene costs compressed the segment margin by 250 basis points to 13.2 %. Approximately one-half of Q2 resin inflation was recouped through price increases, and further pricing actions are in process.

• Scapa Group – Acquired in mid-April, Scapa added about US $95 million of revenue in the period, up roughly 40 % versus Q2 2020 and just shy of Q2 2019 levels. The business contributed a little more than US $9 million of AMS operating profit (US $8 million net of SG&A that was booked in “Unallocated”). That profit essentially covered incremental acquisition-related interest expense, rendering Scapa neutral to adjusted EPS in the quarter, with management still targeting at least US $0.10 of full-year 2021 EPS accretion. Industrial demand within Scapa mirrors the strength of legacy AMS, whereas recovery in Scapa Healthcare is progressing more slowly because elective procedures have not fully normalised; a fuller rebound is expected in 2022.

• Engineered Papers (EP) – Segment sales increased 4 % on a 3 % volume gain and a 6 % tail-wind from currency, partly offset by adverse mix because lower-ignition-propensity (LIP) volumes declined from last year’s inventory build-ups. Higher wood-pulp prices and lingering inefficiencies from transferring production out of the Spotswood facility reduced profitability by “a couple of million dollars,” but those transition costs are now complete and price increases have been implemented to offset pulp inflation. Heat-not-burn papers continue to expand sharply, and early orders for new hemp and botanicals papers are encouraging.

• Consolidated profitability – Adjusted operating income grew 3 % and adjusted EBITDA 9 %. GAAP EPS fell to US $0.06 versus US $0.68 a year ago, entirely because of US $0.57 per share of Scapa-related deal costs and purchase-accounting amortisation. Excluding those items, adjusted EPS was US $0.90, flat with Q2 2020; year-to-date adjusted EPS is US $1.92, up 10 %. Management estimates that unrecovered resin and pulp inflation cut Q2 EPS by more than US $0.10, and lost transportation sales reduced EPS by a further US $0.10, implying underlying earnings power comfortably above the reported US $0.90.

• Cash flow and balance sheet – Year-to-date operating cash flow was approximately US $20 million and free cash flow roughly US $2 million, both down US $30 million year-over-year owing to US $13 million of Scapa transaction costs and a US $20 million working-capital outflow tied to rapid sales growth. Management expects a “very strong” cash-generation rebound in the second half. Net debt closed the quarter at about US $1.2 billion, equating to 4.4× net-debt-to-adjusted-EBITDA under the credit agreement; the company anticipates deleveraging to roughly 4× by year-end.

• Outlook and strategy – SWM will continue to pass through price increases, pursue alternate resin sources and integrate Scapa to expand its industrial coating, adhesives and skin-friendly healthcare platforms. Investment priorities include transportation film capabilities, filtration capacity, sustainable-product innovation and advanced analytics/Manufacturing 4.0 initiatives. Management emphasised that supply-chain and inflation headwinds are considered temporary, while demand fundamentals across filtration, transportation, construction, water/process liquids, heat-not-burn tobacco and the combined healthcare portfolio remain robust.

Overall, SWM delivered solid Q2 2021 results, maintaining adjusted EPS despite material cost inflation, closing its largest acquisition, and validating its expectation of stronger sales, profit and cash flow in the second half of the year, with a clear plan to deleverage toward its four-times net-debt target and deliver at least US $0.10 of full-year EPS accretion from Scapa.
