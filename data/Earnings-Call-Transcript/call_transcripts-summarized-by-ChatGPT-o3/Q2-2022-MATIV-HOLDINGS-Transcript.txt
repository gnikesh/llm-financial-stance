# Mativ Holdings Q2 2022 Earnings Call – Financial Summary

Mativ Holdings’ first post-merger conference call covered the stand-alone Q2 2022 results of legacy Schweitzer-Mauduit (SWM), provided color on legacy Neenah’s quarter, and outlined combined guidance and capital-structure intentions now that the merger closed on 6 July 2022.

The legacy SWM business generated second-quarter sales of $420 million, up 13 % year-on-year (11 % organically). Pricing and volume gains offset broad inflation, allowing adjusted EBITDA to rise modestly and to improve 5 % sequentially; adjusted EPS was $0.86 versus $0.90 a year ago.  

• Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS) grew revenue 14 % (11 % organically). Transportation films—especially paint-protection film—led the expansion. Segment adjusted operating profit rose 25 %, with margins expanding 120 bp year-on-year and 170 bp versus Q1 as price increases comfortably outpaced input-cost inflation and polypropylene prices began to retreat.  

• Engineered Papers (EP) revenue advanced 10 % on robust volumes (reduced-risk products were the fastest-growing category), but a spike in European energy costs drove a 19 % decline in adjusted operating profit. Surcharges and mid-year contract resets are expected to restore margins in the second half.

Legacy Neenah, whose results are not consolidated in Q2, also performed strongly. Sales climbed 14 % (17 % organic), and adjusted EBITDA and operating profit each increased 14 % and 28 %, respectively, as the business captured roughly half of its full-year $25 million price-cost target during the quarter. Fine Paper & Packaging sales surged 21 %, while Technical Products posted double-digit gains driven by release liners, water filtration and industrial applications; both segments recorded meaningful margin expansion (Technical Products +120 bp year-over-year, +240 bp sequential; Fine Paper & Packaging +230 bp and +130 bp, respectively).

Management reiterated the $65 million cost-synergy program, which is entirely cost-driven (roughly 50 % SG&A, 50 % procurement/supply-chain) and does not rely on plant closures. Mativ expects to be on a >$30 million run-rate within 12 months of closing; by year-end 2022 the run-rate should reach about $20 million, with only a partial P&L benefit reflected in 2H results. Revenue synergies are viewed as incremental upside and are not embedded in guidance.

For the second half of 2022—the first two quarters to include both legacy companies—management projects consolidated adjusted EBITDA of $210 million to $230 million. This range is consistent with the previously separate full-year outlooks for SWM and Neenah (combined $385 million–$415 million) plus an initial contribution from synergies. Pricing tailwinds are expected to continue, order books remain strong, and management characterises roughly 60 % of the combined portfolio (e.g., water and air filtration, health-care media, silicone-release liners and engineered papers) as recession-resilient.

Capital-allocation priorities are centred on deleveraging. At closing the company held $1.83 billion of total debt and $148 million of cash, for net debt of $1.68 billion and covenant leverage of 4.1×. With higher EBITDA in the back half and synergy realisation, leverage is forecast to fall naturally to ≤3.75× by year-end and below 3.5× during 2023, moving toward the 2.5×–3.5× target range. Approximately 75 % of floating-rate borrowings will be fixed through swaps; on a pro-forma basis annualised interest expense is estimated at $90 million–$95 million. Liquidity exceeds $450 million (cash plus a $306 million revolver). Normalised long-term free cash flow is expected to approximate $200 million per year after about $100 million of annual capex.

Reflecting confidence in cash-generation prospects, the Board approved the first Mativ dividend of $0.40 per share quarterly ($1.60 annualised), representing a cash commitment of roughly $88 million—essentially equal to the combined pre-merger payout of the two legacy companies. Additional share repurchases or bolt-on M&A will be considered once leverage objectives are met. Portfolio optimisation is under way; a small non-core SWM business was divested in late July.

From Q3 2022 onward Mativ will report under two operating segments:  
• Advanced Technical Materials (ATM) – combining SWM’s AMS and Neenah’s Technical Products; and  
• Fiber-Based Solutions (FBS) – combining SWM’s Engineered Papers with Neenah’s Fine Paper & Packaging.  
Unallocated corporate costs will be aggregated separately. Historical SWM results will not be restated, but management will provide transparency to facilitate year-over-year comparisons.

Energy inflation remains a principal headwind, yet management noted that two-thirds of production is outside Europe and that roughly 75 % of German energy demand is price-locked for the next two years. Surcharges, further pricing actions and operational initiatives are expected to cushion exposure; no energy-rationing scenario is embedded in current guidance.

Overall, Q2 performance met or exceeded internal expectations, integration is proceeding ahead of schedule with a dedicated transformation office, and management sees the broadened product mix, larger scale and actionable synergy pipeline positioning Mativ to expand margins, deliver on leverage targets and provide an attractive total-return profile for shareholders. The company has 54.7 million shares outstanding.
