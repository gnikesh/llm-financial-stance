# Meta Platforms Q2 2023 Earnings Call – Financial and Strategic Summary

Meta delivered a significantly improved second-quarter while continuing to spend heavily on AI infrastructure and Reality Labs (RL). Management reiterated that near-term capital priorities are “AI in the short run, the Metaverse in the long run,” and raised full-year revenue guidance, but also warned that RL operating losses will “increase meaningfully” again in 2024.

## Consolidated Q2 2023 results  
Meta generated revenue of **$32.0 billion**, up **11 % YoY** (up 12 % in constant currency). Total expenses increased 10 % to **$22.6 billion**, producing:

• Operating income **$9.4 billion** and operating margin **29 %**.  
• Net income **$7.8 billion**; diluted EPS **$2.98**.  
• Effective tax rate **16 %** (helped by a higher share price and the associated tax deduction).  
• Free cash flow **$11 billion**, boosted by income-tax payments deferred to Q4.  
• CapEx (cash plus finance-lease principal) **$6.4 billion** in the quarter; **$27-30 billion** expected for full-year 2023 (down from prior $30-33 billion).  
• Cash and marketable securities **$53.4 billion** and net cash still positive despite a second bond issuance in May; **$793 million** of shares repurchased in Q2.  
• Headcount **71,400** (-7 % QoQ); Q3 headcount will exclude almost all of the ~10,000 employees affected by 2023 layoffs.

## Segment performance  

Family of Apps (FoA)  
– Revenue **$31.7 billion** (+12 %).  
– Advertising revenue **$31.5 billion** (+12 %, +13 % cc).  
 • Impressions +34 %; average price per ad –16 %.  
 • Online commerce was the largest vertical contributor, followed by entertainment/media and CPG.  
 • Regional ad-revenue growth: Rest-of-World +16 %, Europe +14 %, North America +11 %, APAC +10 %.  
– Other FoA revenue **$225 million** (+3 %) driven by WhatsApp business messaging.  
– FoA expenses **$18.6 billion** (+8 %); FoA operating income **$13.1 billion**, margin **41 %**.  

Key FoA usage metrics  
– Monthly active people across all apps **3.88 billion**; daily **3.07 billion**.  
– Facebook MAU passed **3 billion** (3.03 billion, +3 % YoY); DAU **2.06 billion** (+5 %), DAU/MAU 68 %.  
– Reels plays exceed **200 billion per day**; Reels revenue run-rate now **>$10 billion** (vs. $3 billion last fall).  
– AI-based content recommendations from unfollowed accounts have added **7 %** to overall time spent on Facebook.  
– More than **¾ of advertisers** now use Reels ads.  
– WhatsApp Business app has **200 million** monthly users; daily click-to-WhatsApp ads revenue is growing **>80 % YoY**; the number of businesses paying for messaging has doubled.  

Reality Labs (RL)  
– Revenue **$276 million** (-39 % YoY) on lower Quest 2 sales.  
– Expenses **$4.0 billion** (+23 %).  
– Operating loss **$3.7 billion**; company guided that RL losses will **increase YoY in 2023** and “increase meaningfully” again in 2024.  
– Quest 3 mixed-reality headset ships this fall: 40 % thinner than Quest 2, double graphics performance; Quest 3 expected to be the first “mainstream” MR device.

## 2023 and preliminary 2024 outlook  

Revenue  
– Q3 2023 revenue guided to **$32.0-34.5 billion**; guidance embeds a **3 % FX tail-wind**.  

Expenses  
– 2023 total expense guidance **$88-91 billion** (raised by $2 billion due to Q2 legal accruals). Includes ~**$4 billion** of restructuring charges (severance and facilities).  
– 2024 expenses will be driven by:  
 1. Higher infrastructure depreciation from recent CapEx,  
 2. Higher payroll as the mix shifts toward higher-cost technical talent,  
 3. Much larger RL operating losses tied to AR/VR and neural-interface R&D.  

Capital Expenditures  
– 2023 CapEx **$27-30 billion** (cuts reflect non-AI server savings and project/equipment delays that shift spend into 2024).  
– CapEx expected to **grow in 2024** as Meta ramps both data-center construction (new architecture announced in late 2022) and servers “particularly in support of our AI work.”  

Tax and debt  
– Tax rate expected to remain similar to Q2 (≈16 %) absent U.S. tax-law changes.  
– Management intends to “modestly evolve” capital structure, adding debt at a measured pace while keeping a neutral-to-positive net cash position.

## Strategic highlights and qualitative commentary  

Year of Efficiency & Headcount  
– Major lay-off rounds are complete; remainder of 2023 will focus on removing internal bottlenecks, rolling out AI-powered productivity tools, and keeping the organization “as lean as possible.”  
– New hiring in 2024 will be limited and focused on high-priority technical roles; some 2023 back-fills will spill into next year.

Artificial Intelligence  
– Billions spent on AI infrastructure are “clearly paying off.” Meta Advantage/Advantage+ AI ads suite is now used by “almost all” advertisers; Meta Lattice new model architecture improves performance prediction; AI Sandbox pilots generative-ad tools (text variation, background generation, out-cropping).  
– Open-sourced **Llama 2** with Microsoft for both research and commercial use. Large-language-model community improvements expected to enhance model safety, efficiency, and Meta’s own products.  
– Meta will unveil consumer-facing generative-AI products and “agents” at the **Connect** conference (27 Sept); CEO projects that every business could eventually operate an AI agent for messaging.  
– Uncertainty around AI product adoption speed makes 2024 AI CapEx requirements difficult to forecast; Meta wants sufficient capacity should growth accelerate quickly.

Metaverse  
– Meta remains “fully committed” to its Metaverse vision; sees AI and Metaverse work as “overlapping and complementary.”  
– Roblox open beta launched on Quest; Horizon team prioritising retention; avatars are improving and will bridge mobile and VR environments.

Threads  
– Launched in early July by a “relatively small team”; achieved “unprecedented” early adoption and stronger-than-expected daily retention. Focus is now on core features and stability; growth will be pursued once retention is proven, monetisation only after scale is achieved. Management views Threads as an example of the efficiencies achieved in 2023.

Regulation  
– EU–US Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision allows continued data transfers, but Meta still faces “increasing legal and regulatory headwinds” in both the EU and U.S. that could materially affect results.

## Takeaways for investors  

1. Core ad business is re-accelerating: constant-currency growth improved to 13 % in Q2 and guidance points to ~20 % YoY in Q3 on easier comps, an FX tail-wind, continuing Reels monetisation progress and stronger ad-performance from AI targeting.  
2. Meta is holding operating margins near 30 % in the first full year of its “efficiency” drive while still funding AI and RL at scale.  
3. Capital intensity is being managed, but 2024 CapEx and RL losses will climb as AI clusters, data-centers and Quest 3/AR road-maps ramp; management nonetheless pledges to “keep the company as lean as possible.”  
4. Debt issuance will continue “at a measured pace” to lower cost-of-capital while maintaining a neutral net cash position; no change to the absence of a dividend.  
5. AI and business messaging (particularly click-to-WhatsApp) are emerging as incremental growth vectors, while Threads represents a new optionality lever built on Meta’s existing social graph and infrastructure.

Management insists the long-term Metaverse bet is essential despite cumulative RL losses now exceeding **$40 billion**; investors will need to weigh accelerating near-term cash-flow growth against that multi-year investment drag.
