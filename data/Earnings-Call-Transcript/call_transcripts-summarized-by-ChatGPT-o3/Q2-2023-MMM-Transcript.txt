# 3M Company – Q2 2023 Earnings Call Summary

3M reported second-quarter 2023 results that were stronger than expected operationally, driven by cost controls, supply-chain improvements and early restructuring benefits, even as several end-markets remained soft. Management also updated full-year guidance, detailed progress on the Health Care spin-off and addressed litigation matters, most notably a PFAS settlement. All figures below are adjusted (non-GAAP) unless otherwise noted.

Revenue and Earnings  
• Sales were $8.33 billion, down 4.3 % year on year but **at the high end of guidance and ≈$440 million above consensus**.  
• Organic sales declined 2.5 %; the drop includes a 1.7 pp (~$140 million) headwind from lower disposable-respirator demand. Ex-respirators, organic sales were down just 0.8 %.  
• Adjusted EPS was **$2.17**, beating expectations by $0.41.  
• Adjusted operating margin was **19.3 %**; $212 million of restructuring charges reduced margin by 2.7 pp. Ex-charges, margin was 22.0 %, up 40 bps YoY.  
• GAAP results include a **$10.3 billion (present value) PFAS-related charge**, to be paid over 13 years, tied to a pending nationwide water-systems settlement.

Cash Flow, Balance Sheet and Capital Allocation  
• Adjusted free cash flow was **$1.5 billion**, up 44 % YoY; conversion was 122 %.  
• Inventory was flat sequentially versus a typical seasonal build, reflecting tighter controls.  
• Net debt ended the quarter at **$11.7 billion**, down 12 % YoY.  
• Dividend outlay in Q2 was $828 million; no share repurchases were disclosed.  
• Capex totaled $328 million, focused on growth, productivity and sustainability.

Restructuring and Cost Actions  
• Company-wide restructuring program (total charges and benefits each targeted at $700-900 million) is progressing faster than planned.  
  – YTD charges: $264 million; 2023 charges now seen at $400-450 million with an equal amount of in-year benefits (self-funding).  
  – Q3 charges expected at $125-175 million with similar benefits.  
• Actions include eliminating layers, exiting rooftops, converting 24 countries to an export model and streamlining enterprise supply-chain operations; all segments recorded sequential margin improvement in Q2, helped by these measures.  
• Management now anticipates full-year operating margin of **19.5-20 %**, up roughly 100 bps versus initial outlook despite lower sales.

Segment Performance (Q2)  
Safety & Industrial  
• Sales $2.8 billion (-4.6 % org.; ex-respirators +0.2 %).  
• Strength in roofing granules and auto aftermarket; weakness in packaging and electronics-exposed adhesives.  
• Operating margin 22.2 % (+70 bps YoY; +200 bps QoQ).

Transportation & Electronics  
• Sales $1.9 billion (-2.4 % org.).  
• Auto OEM +21 %, outpacing global builds by ~600 bps; electronics ‑22 % amid broad consumer-device slump.  
• Margin 19.8 % (-360 bps YoY; +310 bps QoQ).

Health Care (to be spun)  
• Sales $2.1 billion; organic growth slightly positive.  
• Oral care low-single-digit growth; Medical Solutions up slightly; Separation & Purification ‑mid-single-digits; Health Information Systems ‑low-single-digits due to strained hospital budgets.  
• Margin 19.8 % (-280 bps YoY; +190 bps QoQ).

Consumer  
• Sales $1.3 billion (-2.2 % org.) as discretionary hard-line spending stayed weak.  
• Home Health & Auto Care up slightly; Home Improvement and Stationery/Office down.  
• Margin 18.2 % (-40 bps YoY; +320 bps QoQ).

Litigation Update  
• Proposed nationwide PFAS settlement with U.S. public water systems: **present-value cost $10.3 billion**, payable over 13 years; covers all PFAS compounds and future detections. Court approval is pending; 3M will litigate if necessary.  
• Combat Arms earplug MDL mediation continues; no new financial provisions disclosed.  
• 3M remains committed to exit all PFAS manufacturing by end-2025.

Health Care Spin-off  
• Regulatory filings and IT “soft-spin” preparation are on track; CEO selection is in final stages.  
• Transaction expected by YE 2023 or early 2024, subject to customary conditions.  
• Spin expected to deliver a one-time dividend to 3M equal to 3-3.5× the Health Care segment’s EBITDA; 3M will retain a 19.9 % stake, enhancing financial flexibility.

Full-Year 2023 Outlook (Revised)  
• Adjusted EPS: **$8.60 – $9.10** (up from $8.50 – $9.00).  
• Organic sales: tracking to **low end of prior range (flat to ‑3 %)**; disposable-respirator drag now pegged at ~$550 million (worst-case of guided range).  
• Free-cash-flow conversion: unchanged at 90 – 100 %.  
• Raw-material/logistics/energy inflation headwind trimmed to $150-200 million (from $150-250 million).  
• Key risks watched: prolonged electronics downturn, soft consumer retail, mixed industrial demand, slow China recovery.

Third-Quarter 2023 Guidance  
• Sales about **$8 billion** (includes ~$130 million YoY drag from respirators and 2022 Russia exit).  
• Adjusted EPS **$2.25 – $2.40**, incorporating $125-175 million of restructuring charges and roughly equivalent benefits.  
• End-market conditions expected to mirror Q2, with gradual margin improvement from cost actions.

Strategic Priorities  
1. Drive operational execution, including supply-chain efficiency and disciplined spending.  
2. Complete the Health Care spin-off on schedule.  
3. Proactively manage and resolve litigation to reduce overhang.  

Management emphasized that while macro uncertainty persists, restructuring momentum, supply-chain healing, disciplined pricing and reduced debt position 3M for improving margins, robust cash generation and a “stronger, leaner, more focused” organization exiting 2023.
