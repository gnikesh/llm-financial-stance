# 3M Company Q2 2024 Earnings Call – Comprehensive Financial & Strategic Summary

3M’s first full quarter as a pure-play industrial company (post-Solventum spin) showed meaningful progress on profitability, cash generation and balance-sheet strength, while new CEO Bill Brown laid out an aggressive operational and growth agenda.

Revenue, Earnings and Margins  
• Net sales were $6.26 billion, 24.9 % below the prior-year quarter because Health Care is no longer consolidated; on a like-for-like basis core 3M delivered 1.2 % organic growth (2.4 % excluding portfolio/geography pruning).  
• Adjusted operating margin expanded 440 bp to 21.6 % as price, productivity, restructuring savings and TSA income outweighed volume mix and the timing shift of stock-based compensation (–200 bp).  
• Adjusted EPS of $1.93 increased 39 % year-on-year, beating consensus by $0.25.  
• Foreign-currency translation cost $0.04 per share; lower restructuring charges added $0.23.

Cash Flow, Capex and Balance Sheet  
• Q2 adjusted free cash flow was $1.2 billion (109 % conversion); first-half FCF totals $2 billion.  
• Capex was held to $250 million, focused on growth, productivity and sustainability.  
• Net debt is just $3 billion, a leverage ratio of ~0.3×; ratings agencies have adopted an incrementally more positive stance.  
• 3M returned $800 million to shareholders in Q2: $400 million in dividends and $400 million of share repurchases; $4 billion of authorization remains and management “has capacity to do more.”  
• July cash outflows of $3.7 billion will fund Public Water System and Combat Arms settlements, but are already embedded in liquidity planning.

Legal, Pension and Insurance Updates  
• Insurance carriers paid 3M $120 million of Combat-Arms coverage recovery; further collections are expected.  
• A non-cash $800 million charge was recorded for a $2.5 billion U.S. pension risk-transfer to MetLife, reducing long-term pension risk.  
• PFAS production remains on track to cease by end-2025; management sees no change to previously disclosed liabilities.

Segment Highlights (all organic growth)  
Safety & Industrial: revenue $2.8 billion, +1.1 %; margin 22.6 % (+40 bp). Adhesives & Tapes mid-single-digit growth; Personal Safety and Auto Aftermarket low-single; Abrasives and Industrial Specialties declined.  
Transportation & Electronics: revenue $1.9 billion, +3.3 %; margin 22.3 % (+250 bp). Consumer electronics up low-double digits on specification wins; Auto OEM +5 % versus –0.5 % global builds; semiconductors improving.  
Consumer: revenue $1.3 billion, –1.4 % (–2.7 pp from pruning); margin 17.4 % (–80 bp). Home Improvement mid-single growth offset by soft Home & Auto Care and Packaging; discretionary retail spending remains weak.

Full-Year 2024 Outlook (revised)  
• Adjusted EPS raised to $7.00–$7.30 (from $6.80–$7.30), +16 %–21 % Y/Y.  
• Adjusted operating-margin expansion forecast widened to +225–275 bp.  
• Organic-sales guidance maintained at 0 %–2 % (implies H2 growth similar to H1).  
 – Safety & Industrial: flat to up low-single digits.  
 – Transportation & Electronics: up low-single digits (watch auto builds, holiday electronics).  
 – Consumer: down low-single digits.  
• Restructuring charges still $250–$300 million (about $165 million incurred YTD); non-operating expense trimmed to $50–$75 million.  
• Tax rate expected ~19 % in H2; free-cash-flow conversion target unchanged at 90 %–110 %.

CEO Bill Brown’s Three Priorities  
1. Sustained organic growth:  
 • R&D for core 3M is ~$1 billion (4.5 % of sales) but has been flat nominally; spend will be re-oriented toward new-product development and high-growth platforms (EVs, automation, data centers, climate tech).  
 • New-product introductions have fallen from >1,000 a decade ago to <150 expected in 2024; Brown aims to restore pipeline velocity and redeploy engineers currently working on PFAS exit.  
 • Commercial excellence initiatives include rebuilding and retraining a salesforce that has shrunk 25 % in five years, optimizing 30,000-strong distributor relationships, tightening net pricing and ramping advertising/merchandising.

2. Operational performance:  
 • Network of 110 factories and 95 distribution centers is “incredibly complex”; many SKUs (e.g., a Command Strip) touch five plants and two DCs before reaching customers.  
 • Key targets: raise OEE, cut logistics costs, diversify a supplier base where 80 % of raw materials are sole-sourced, reduce 5 % raw-material waste, and shrink inventory from 102 to ~75 days (≈$1 billion cash release) while pushing on-time-in-full above 90 % (mid-80s today).

3. Capital deployment:  
 • Continue funding R&D and capex, sustain recalibrated dividend, preserve low leverage.  
 • Use excess cash for share buybacks or selective M&A; no near-term acquisitions but a “dispassionate” portfolio review could prompt divestitures of non-core assets or bolt-on deals that exploit 3M’s materials-science edge.

Near-Term Financial Mechanics  
• Stock-comp expense was front-loaded into Q2; absence of that charge is a ~$0.10 headwind moving from Q2→Q3.  
• Solventum TSA reimbursements added 50 bp to Q2 margin and $0.05 EPS; income will step down as the former unit exits services (≈$0.09 EPS headwind in Q3).  
• Inventory unwinding and settlements will lower factory absorption and cash balances, raising non-operating expense by ~$0.06 EPS in Q3.  
• 3M still expects ~$110 million restructuring charges in H2, largely Q4-weighted.

Management & Governance  
• CFO Monish Patolawala will depart after four years; search for a successor is under way.  
• Brown has completed a 90-day listening tour and engaged outside advisors to accelerate initiatives; he will update investors on portfolio and long-term growth targets in coming quarters.

Debt & Liquidity  
• No change to strong investment-grade profile; pension actions, insurance recoveries and high FCF give flexibility to absorb PFAS liabilities while funding growth and buybacks.

Bottom Line  
3M beat Q2 expectations, raised the EPS floor for 2024 and is generating healthy free cash flow with minimal leverage. The new CEO is launching a multi-year program to reignite organic growth, simplify an over-complex supply chain and tighten capital deployment, all while navigating PFAS settlements and completing a broad portfolio review.
