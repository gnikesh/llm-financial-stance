# NVIDIA Fiscal Q2 2025 Earnings Call – Detailed Financial & Strategic Summary

NVIDIA reported another record‐setting quarter, reinforcing the company’s view that the industry is migrating from general-purpose CPU computing to accelerated computing and generative AI. Management emphasised that demand for current “Hopper” products remains robust, while anticipation for the next-generation “Blackwell” portfolio is “incredible.” Below is a comprehensive recap of the key financial metrics, operational trends and strategic commentary discussed on the 28 August 2024 call.

---

NVIDIA’s topline momentum continued to accelerate. Fiscal Q2 2025 revenue reached **$30.0 billion**, exceeding the company’s $28 billion outlook, and rising **15 % sequentially and 122 % year-on-year**. Data-centre products remained the primary growth engine, but every segment expanded versus a year ago.

1. **Data Centre revenue** hit an all-time high of **$26.3 billion**, up **16 % quarter-on-quarter and 154 % year-on-year**.  
   • Compute revenue grew **>2.5×** versus last year, while networking revenue more than doubled.  
   • Cloud service providers (CSPs) represented roughly **45 %** of data-centre sales; consumer internet and large enterprises contributed **>50 %**.  
   • Inference workloads accounted for **>40 %** of trailing-12-month data-centre revenue.  
   • Sales to China rose sequentially and were “a significant contributor,” although still below pre-export-control levels; management expects China to remain highly competitive.

2. **Gaming revenue** was **$2.88 billion**, up **9 % sequentially and 16 % year-on-year**. Channel inventory is “healthy,” and the installed base of RTX-equipped AI PCs has surpassed **100 million units** across **200 RTX laptop designs**.  

3. **Professional Visualisation** generated **$454 million**, growing **6 % sequentially and 20 % year-on-year**, helped by digital-twin and Omniverse demand in automotive and manufacturing.  

4. **Automotive & Robotics** delivered **$346 million**, an increase of **5 % sequentially and 37 % year-on-year**, reflecting ramps in autonomous-drive and AI cockpit platforms.

---

Gross profitability remained exceptional even as the product mix evolved. GAAP gross margin was **75.1 %** and non-GAAP gross margin **75.7 %**, both down modestly from Q1 because of a higher mix of new data-centre products and inventory provisions tied to low-yield Blackwell material. Operating expenses climbed **12 %** quarter-on-quarter owing mainly to compensation increases; full-year OpEx is now forecast to rise in the **mid-to-upper-40 %** range as R&D on next-gen architectures accelerates.

NVIDIA generated **$14.5 billion in operating cash flow** during the quarter, and returned **$7.4 billion** to shareholders via repurchases and dividends (reflecting the recently increased per-share dividend). The Board augmented the existing buy-back capacity by approving a new **$50 billion** authorisation, on top of the **$7.5 billion** that remained at the July quarter-end. No incremental debt metrics were disclosed, and management made no mention of any new borrowing.

---

Guidance for the October quarter (Fiscal Q3 2025) implies another set of record results:

• Revenue is projected at **$32.5 billion ± 2 %**.  
• GAAP gross margin should be **74.4 % ± 50 bp**; non-GAAP gross margin **75.0 % ± 50 bp**.  
• GAAP operating expense is expected near **$4.3 billion**; non-GAAP OpEx **$3.0 billion**.  
• Other income/expense should be about **$350 million**, and the tax rate **17 % ± 1 %**.  
Management reiterated full-year non-GAAP gross margin guidance in the “mid-70s” and confirmed that step-down pressure linked to new-product costs will persist into fiscal Q4.

---

Strategic and product highlights underscoring long-term growth prospects were prominent:

• **Hopper vs. Blackwell demand:** Hopper shipments will **increase in 2H FY25** even as Blackwell ramps. Blackwell sampling is under way across “~100” server designs. NVIDIA executed a mask revision to improve Blackwell yields; no functional changes were required. Although Hopper remains capacity-constrained, Blackwell production begins in fiscal Q4 and is expected to generate “**several billion dollars**” of revenue in that quarter, on top of growing Hopper sales.

• **Networking:** Sequential networking revenue was up **16 %**, with Ethernet for AI (Spectrum-X) doubling Q/Q. CEO Jensen Huang stated that Spectrum-X is on track to become a **multi-billion-dollar** product line within a year, scaling clusters from today’s tens of thousands to future millions of GPUs.

• **Sovereign AI:** Government-sponsored build-outs are expanding. Management now anticipates sovereign-AI revenue to reach the “**low double-digit billions**” in the current fiscal year, up several billion dollars versus prior expectations.

• **Software & SaaS:** NVIDIA’s recurring software, SaaS and support revenue run-rate is forecast to approach **$2 billion exiting FY25**, led by the NVIDIA AI Enterprise platform, NeMo, NIM inference microservices and Omniverse licences.

• **AI factory architecture:** Blackwell is positioned as a full platform comprising dual-GPU packages, Grace CPUs, NVLink switches, ConnectX/BlueField DPUs and InfiniBand/Ethernet options. A single NVLink-connected rack (GB200 NVL72) offers **259 TB/s** of aggregate bandwidth and up to **30×** faster inference on trillion-parameter models versus prior Hopper systems.

• **Liquid vs. air cooling:** Although liquid-cooled “Grace Blackwell” units deliver three-to-five-times the AI throughput of air-cooled racks within the same power envelope, NVIDIA will continue offering air-cooled HGX-based Blackwell variants to accommodate data-centre diversity. CSPs are preparing for both configurations.

• **ROI narrative:** Management repeatedly asserted that accelerated computing yields immediate cost and energy savings versus scaling CPU-only fleets, enabling customers to monetise AI capacity quickly through internal workloads and cloud rentals to generative-AI start-ups.

• **Geographic dynamics:** Reported U.S. invoiced revenue dipped sequentially while several Asian regions climbed, reflecting shipment timing to ODMs/OEMs rather than end-demand shifts. China remained material in absolute dollars but below historic share.

---

Neither EPS nor specific debt balances were provided on the call or in the associated commentary; focus remained on revenue, cash flow, margins and share-repurchase capacity.

Management closed by reiterating the dual secular drivers they believe will support multi-year growth: (1) the industry-wide transition of a roughly **$1 trillion** installed data-centre base from general-purpose CPUs to accelerated computing, and (2) the migration of human-engineered software toward generative AI. Against that backdrop, NVIDIA expects its data-centre business to grow “quite significantly” in fiscal 2026 as Blackwell shipments scale and software-subscription revenue expands.

The next earnings release (Q3 FY25) is scheduled for **20 November 2024**, and management will speak at Goldman Sachs Communacopia on **11 September 2024**.
