# Neenah, Inc. – Q3 2020 Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah, Inc. reported a solid sequential rebound in the third quarter, although results were still below the 2019 comparison because of COVID-19–related demand weakness. Management highlighted decisive cost-cutting, record free cash flow, a strong liquidity position, and multiple initiatives aimed at accelerating growth and restoring margins to mid-teen levels.

Sales, Earnings and Margins  
• Consolidated net sales were $191 million, up almost 20 % from Q2-20 but below the prior-year quarter (2019: c. $216 million).  
• Adjusted operating income was $16 million versus $21.5 million in Q3-19; GAAP operating income included $2 million of restructuring and other items.  
• GAAP EPS came in at $0.46 (Q3-19: $0.84). Adjusted EPS was $0.55, a sharp swing from a slight loss in Q2-20 but below the $0.95 earned a year ago.  
• Both business segments delivered double-digit EBIT margins. Segment operating income in Technical Products grew more than 30 % year-on-year despite lower sales.

Segment Performance  
Technical Products  
• Sales: $124 million, +16 % versus Q2-20, −6 % versus Q3-19.  
• Filtration revenue rose 9 % year-on-year; transportation filtration volumes increased 7 % (double-digit growth in North America) and new facemask media contributed incremental sales.  
• Adjusted operating income improved by $8 million sequentially and $3 million year-on-year, aided by mix, lower raw-material costs and aggressive cost reduction.

Fine Paper & Packaging  
• Sales: $67 million, +20 % sequentially but below the “strong” Q3-19 level, primarily on weaker commercial-print demand.  
• Adjusted operating profit rose sharply from Q2-20, though it remained below last year; lower volume was partly offset by spending cuts and modest input-cost relief.  
• The segment is expected to recover to roughly 90 % of its pre-COVID quarterly run-rate of ~$90 million in 2021.

Cost-Reduction and Efficiency  
• Q3 actions removed almost $15 million of manufacturing costs and $4 million of SG&A; $7 million of these savings are permanent.  
• Company-wide SG&A fell to $19 million (−$4 million YoY).  
• The newly launched Neenah Operating System (lean/continuous-improvement framework) is targeted to deliver >$20 million in annual savings over the next five years; early implementation has begun in the two largest plants.

Cash Flow, Liquidity and Balance Sheet  
• Cash from operations was a robust $36 million, including ~$20 million from working-capital release; Q3 free cash flow was the highest third-quarter level in company history.  
• Liquidity exceeded $180 million, consisting of >$40 million cash plus $140 million of undrawn revolver availability.  
• Gross debt stands at $196 million (primarily a $200 million Term Loan B priced near 5 %); no borrowings are outstanding on the $175 million credit facility.  
• Net interest expense was $3.6 million; run-rate will fall to roughly $3.3 million per quarter after July’s bond redemption.  
• The Q3 effective tax rate was 23 % versus 11 % in the prior year; 22 % remains the expected ongoing rate.  
• Capital spending was $4 million in Q3; full-year 2020 cap-ex should total ~$17 million (about half normal levels). Management plans to resume more typical spending of $30-35 million in 2021.

Outlook and Guidance  
• Q4 is seasonally the company’s slowest. Management expects sales roughly in line with Q3 and some margin pressure from $1.5-2 million of scheduled maintenance and modest chemical/energy cost increases.  
• Fiber costs are stable; contracts carry a one-quarter lag, allowing pricing adjustments if pulp prices rise.  
• Technical Products should return to pre-COVID revenue early in 2021.  
• Fine Paper & Packaging is projected to recapture ~90 % of its former run rate next year; mid-teen EBIT margins remain the target once volumes normalize.  
• Near-term cash will be used to pre-fund $6 million of 2021 pension contributions, capturing an offsetting tax benefit.  
• The balance sheet and liquidity leave room for bolt-on M&A; management will remain cautious but continues to evaluate a “robust” pipeline.

Strategic Growth Platforms  
Neenah is concentrating organic investment and future acquisitions in four platforms that more than double its addressable market and are expected to add 1-2 percentage points to organic growth:  
1. Filtration – expansion from transportation media into industrial air and water, consumer beverage and premium facemask materials.  
2. Specialty Coatings – leveraging digital and advanced chemistries for release liners, image and performance labels and other coated substrates.  
3. Custom Engineered Materials – utilizing expertise in fiber blending and composites to solve critical customer applications.  
4. Premium Packaging – sustainably produced, high-end packaging that can backfill secular declines in commercial-print papers while running on the same assets.

Capital Allocation Principles  
Management reiterated its commitment to disciplined capital deployment, return-on-capital focus, maintaining a prudent balance sheet and continuing an attractive dividend, while investing in growth and margin-enhancement initiatives.

Bottom Line  
Sequential improvements in volume, cost structure and cash flow show that Neenah is managing through the pandemic effectively. Liquidity is ample, debt is modest, and the company has clear strategic priorities—both operational (Neenah Operating System) and market-driven (four high-growth platforms)—that management believes can lift sales above pre-COVID levels, restore segment EBIT margins to the mid-teens and create substantial long-term shareholder value.
