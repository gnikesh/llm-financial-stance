# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q3 2020 Earnings Call Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International delivered third-quarter results that were markedly stronger than management’s expectations and demonstrated the resilience of its diversified portfolio in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic.  

Consolidated net sales rose 9 percent year-on-year, while adjusted operating profit and adjusted EBITDA expanded 26 percent and 25 percent respectively.  Adjusted EPS climbed 15 percent to **$1.16**, lifting year-to-date (YTD) adjusted EPS growth to 6 percent and putting the company in position to potentially surpass 2019’s full-year result.  GAAP EPS declined to **$0.78** from $0.90 a year ago because of $0.18 of one-time costs tied to the announced closure of the Spotswood, New Jersey engineered-papers facility; additional closure costs will be recorded in Q4 but excluded from adjusted metrics.

Management again refrained from reinstating formal 2020 guidance yet reiterated confidence that **free cash flow will exceed $100 million for a second consecutive year**.  Working capital is being allowed to run modestly higher so the company—and its customers—can carry additional safety stock during the pandemic, but strong cash generation is still enabling deleveraging: net debt to adjusted EBITDA improved to **2.5×** from 2.8× last quarter.  SWM holds more than **$475 million of liquidity** (cash plus revolver availability) and has already repaid roughly **$62 million of debt** since acquiring Tekra in March; year-end debt reduction could reach $80 million.  Capital spending through nine months was **$24 million**, reflecting a deliberately conservative posture.

Segment performance was notably bifurcated:

• **Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)**  
  – Net sales increased 10 percent to **$280 million** (Tekra added **$23 million**).  Excluding Tekra, organic sales were down 8 percent, a sharp sequential improvement from Q2’s double-digit decline.  
  – Transportation and surface-protection films remained the primary COVID headwind but trended better as Q3 progressed; management “cautiously” expects the category to return to year-on-year growth in Q4 provided fresh lockdowns do not shut distribution channels.  
  – Adjusted operating profit rose 2 percent to **$25 million**.  Margin compressed 130 bp to **18.0 percent** but rebounded strongly from 15.7 percent in Q2, helped by tight expense controls and lower resin costs.  
  – Ex-transportation, the AMS portfolio was down only 3 percent organically, underscoring demand resilience in medical (fastest-growing end-market), industrial packaging, HVAC air-filtration media, and wind-turbine blade materials.  
  – Tekra’s integration is “nearing its final stages”; over the next 12 months SWM plans to showcase commercial synergies, particularly in medical films, transportation films, and advanced coating/converting capabilities.

• **Engineered Papers (EP)**  
  – Sales advanced 8 percent on 2 percent volume growth plus 6 percent favorable price/mix, supported by higher Low-Ignition-Propensity (LIP) cigarette papers and by customers’ deliberate inventory builds for supply-chain security.  
  – Adjusted operating margin widened 400 bp to **26.6 percent**, driven by improved mix, disciplined cost management, and lower pulp costs (pulp index was down ~7 percent year-on-year).  
  – Heat-not-burn tobacco papers nearly tripled versus 2019 YTD and remain a small but rapidly growing contribution.  SWM is also pursuing botanical/hemp-based paper and pouch opportunities that draw on cross-segment synergies with AMS.  
  – The closure of Spotswood, NJ (part of ongoing supply-chain optimization) is tied to a newly signed multi-year agreement in which comparable volumes will be transferred to a more efficient SWM site using updated technology.

Unallocated corporate costs were **$9.6 million** in Q3 (down $2.4 million) and are trending toward the **mid-$40 million** level for the full year.

Raw-material tailwinds moderated: Q3 benefited by roughly $1.5 million from lower pulp and $5 million from lower polypropylene, but both indices have recently moved toward flat year-on-year levels, suggesting only modest benefit—or a slight headwind—in Q4.

Looking forward, management sees AMS growth resuming as transportation films rebound, while EP faces a tougher year-on-year comparison after customers’ Q3 safety-stock build.  Nevertheless, the full-year outlook remains favorable: free cash flow above $100 million, organic EPS growth potentially eclipsing last year, continued deleveraging, and ample liquidity to fund disciplined M&A that fits SWM’s strategic focus on filtration, medical, transportation/surface protection, and next-generation engineered papers.

SWM’s Board and executives also highlighted that November 9, 2020 marks the 25th anniversary of SWM as a stand-alone public company, emphasizing ongoing efforts to “grow, optimize, diversify, and innovate” while maintaining the culture of operational excellence that underpinned the company’s strong Q3 performance.
