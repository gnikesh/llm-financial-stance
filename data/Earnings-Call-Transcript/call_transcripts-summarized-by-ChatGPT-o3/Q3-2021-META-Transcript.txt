# Facebook, Inc. (Meta Platforms) Q3 2021 Earnings – Comprehensive Financial & Strategic Summary

Facebook (ticker FB; soon to be Meta) reported third-quarter 2021 results on 25 October 2021. Management discussed current performance, near-term headwinds, major spending plans, and long-term strategic priorities, especially the metaverse initiative. All figures are GAAP unless otherwise noted.

Financial Performance  
• Total revenue climbed 35 % year-over-year (34 % FX-neutral) to $29.01 billion.  
 – Advertising revenue was $28.28 billion (+33 %).  
 – “Other” revenue, driven mainly by Quest 2 hardware, was $734 million (+195 %).  
• Operating income reached $10.42 billion, yielding a 36 % operating margin (flat vs. 35 % a year ago despite sharply higher expenses).  
• Net income was $9.19 billion, or $3.22 diluted EPS (vs. $8.04 billion / $2.71 EPS in Q3-20).  
• Effective tax rate: 13 %.  
• Free cash flow: $9.5 billion; period-end cash & marketable securities: $58.1 billion.  
• Shareholder returns: $14.4 billion of Class A shares repurchased; board added a new $50 billion authorization (leaving $58 billion total available at 30 Sep).  

Expense & Capital Profile  
• Q3 total costs and expenses rose 38 % to $18.58 billion:  – Cost of revenue +38 %, R&D +33 %, marketing & sales +32 %, G&A +65 % (driven chiefly by legal and headcount).  
• Headcount grew 20 % Y/Y to ~68,200; net hiring in Q3 exceeded 4,700 employees, mostly technical talent.  
• Q3 capital expenditures were $4.54 billion (data centers, servers, network, offices).  
• 2021 outlook:  – Total expenses trimmed to $70–71 billion (from $70–73 billion).  
 – CapEx about $19 billion (prior $19–21 billion).  
• 2022 prelim outlook:  – Total expenses $91–97 billion (+29 %–38 %).  
 – CapEx $29–34 billion (+53 % at midpoint), largely to expand AI/ML infrastructure that supports ranking, recommendations, and ads performance.  
• Facebook Reality Labs (FRL) investment will cut 2021 operating profit by ≈$10 billion, with spending “to grow even further” each of the next several years. Beginning in Q4-21, FRL will be reported as a separate segment distinct from the core Family of Apps (FoA).

Liquidity & Debt  
The company continues to carry no long-term financial debt on the balance sheet.

User & Engagement Metrics (FoA)  
• Family Daily Active People (DAP): ≈2.8 billion (+ 11 million sequentially).  
• Family Monthly Active People (MAP): ≈3.58 billion.  
• Facebook DAUs: 1.93 billion (+6 % Y/Y; +110 million users).  
• Facebook MAUs: 2.91 billion (+6 % Y/Y; +170 million users).  
• DAU/MAU ratio steady at 66 %.  
Advertising supply & pricing: ad impressions +9 % Y/Y, average price per ad +22 %.

Geographic Ad-Revenue Growth  
• Rest-of-World +50 %. • Europe +35 %. • North America +31 %. • Asia-Pacific +28 %. Europe, APAC and ROW enjoyed modest currency tailwinds.

Management Guidance & Commentary  

Q4 2021 revenue is projected at $31.5–34 billion, reflecting:  
1) continued advertising headwinds from Apple’s iOS 14 App Tracking Transparency (ATT) changes;  
2) macro factors such as moderating e-commerce growth, global supply-chain/labor constraints, and lingering COVID lockdowns (especially in Southeast Asia);  
3) lapping strong Quest 2 hardware sales last holiday (non-ads revenue expected to decline Y/Y).  

Margins are expected to contract in 2022 because expense growth (accelerating hiring, larger FRL outlays, office re-openings) will exceed revenue growth.

Strategic & Operational Highlights  

Apple/iOS 14 impact  
• Adoption reached “critical mass” by late June, creating two problems: weaker ads targeting efficiency (higher cost per outcome) and under-reporting of conversions.  
• Facebook believes it is currently under-reporting iOS web conversions and expects to recover “more than half” of the missing signal by year-end, with further progress in 2022.  
• Long-term fix involves rebuilding targeting/measurement with privacy-enhancing technologies and increased first-party commerce solutions.

Re-tooling for Young Adults & Reels  
• Company will pivot product roadmaps so that “serving young adults (18-29) becomes the north star,” accepting slower growth among older cohorts.  
• Reels is now the “primary driver of engagement growth” on Instagram; Facebook rolled out Reels globally in Q3 and has begun limited ad monetization on Instagram. Management expects Reels to become “as important as Stories.”

Commerce Initiatives  
• Marketplace at scale; Shops gaining traction, with a major holiday program co-developed with leading advertisers.  
• Facebook continues to build on-platform checkout, dynamic ads that route to either external sites or Shops, and broader lead-generation tools (e.g., conversions API).  
• Despite near-term e-commerce deceleration, management believes the shift to online selling remains secular.

Metaverse & Facebook Reality Labs  
• Vision: unlock presence-based social experiences and a “massive increase in the creator economy and digital commerce.”  
• Goal by the end of the decade: 1 billion people in the metaverse, generating “hundreds of billions of dollars” in annual digital commerce.  
• Hardware progress: 128 GB Quest 2 ($299) replaced 64 GB model; Ray-Ban Stories smart glasses launched with EssilorLuxottica.  
• Connect conference (28 Oct) to reveal more details. Zuckerberg emphasized that returns are long-dated; near-term focus is building OS, devices, development tools, content studios, and a social platform (Horizon, Workrooms).  

Safety, Security & Transparency  
• Over 40,000 employees now work on safety and security; >$5 billion will be spent in 2021.  
• AI now detects >90 % of harmful content Facebook removes before users report it, even for harder categories like hate speech.  
• Quarterly Community Standards Enforcement Reports and the Oversight Board remain key transparency pillars.

Capital Allocation  
• Strong cash generation funds large FRL/CapEx investments while allowing aggressive share repurchases; management reiterated confidence in long-term ROI.

Key Takeaways  
Facebook delivered robust top- and bottom-line growth but faces significant near-term friction from Apple’s privacy changes, supply-chain disruptions, and softer pandemic-boosted e-commerce trends. Management is responding with:  
1) heavy AI/ML and infrastructure spending to restore ad performance and support new video formats;  
2) a multiyear shift toward young-adult engagement via Reels;  
3) accelerated on-platform commerce tools to regain signal; and  
4) a substantial, transparent, long-term investment in the metaverse through Facebook Reality Labs, which will materially dilute operating profit by ~$10 billion in 2021 and even more thereafter.
