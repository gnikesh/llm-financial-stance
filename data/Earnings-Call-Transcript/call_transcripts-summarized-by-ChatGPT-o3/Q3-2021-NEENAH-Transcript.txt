# Neenah, Inc. – Key Take-aways from the Q3 2021 Earnings Call  

Neenah reported a quarter of exceptional top-line growth but considerable margin pressure caused by rapidly escalating raw-material, energy and logistics costs. Management detailed the immediate impact on Q3 results, the pricing and cost-recovery actions already in place, and the structural and strategic moves that should boost profitability and cash generation in 2022 and beyond.

Q3-21 headline results  
• Net sales reached an all-time high of $268 million (management’s “almost $270 million”), 40 % above Q3-20 and 22 % higher excluding the April-acquired Itasa release-liner business.  
• Adjusted EPS was $0.38, down from $0.55 a year ago; GAAP EPS was reduced by $0.19 of unusual items, mainly Appleton mill closure costs and hurricane-related flood damage at the Pennsylvania plant (≈$0.06 per share).  
• Adjusted operating income fell to $12.7 million from $15.9 million; the $9 million net drag from input-cost inflation (≈300 bp of margin or $0.35 per share) more than offset an $8 million benefit from price/mix.  
• Trailing-12-month adjusted EBITDA climbed to $122 million (vs. $101 million for 2020) thanks to Itasa and volume growth, but Q3 EBITDA margin compressed sequentially.  

Segment performance  
• Technical Products (filtration, release liners, other engineered materials): sales $173 million, +46 % (or +15 % ex-Itasa); adjusted earnings $10.8 million vs. $13.3 million. The segment absorbed the bulk of the raw-material spike and faces a timing lag until contracted annual filtration price resets take effect on 1 Jan 22 (≈$20 million of incremental price in 2022).  
• Fine Paper & Packaging: sales $95 million, +32 %; adjusted earnings $6.6 million vs. $6.0 million. Pricing offset about 75 % of the input-cost hit in Q3, and management expects full recovery in Q4. Packaging revenue was almost 20 % above pre-pandemic levels.  

Cost inflation and pricing initiatives  
• Q3 input-cost inflation was ~$17 million YoY (chemical, energy, fiber, freight); price/mix recaptured about half ($8 million).  
• Q4 input costs are now expected to be >$20 million above Q4-20; Neenah anticipates offsetting roughly two-thirds through additional price increases and surcharges.  
• For full-year 2021, raw-material and energy inflation will exceed $40 million; roughly half will be recovered this year and the balance in 2022.  
• More than $200 million of annual sales have been reformulated to alternative chemistries to mitigate supply shortages.  

Balance sheet, cash flow and capital allocation  
• Operating cash flow YTD was $40 million (vs. $80 million in 2020) as higher sales and higher-cost inventories lifted working capital.  
• Net leverage (net debt/adjusted EBITDA) was 3.4× at 30 Sep and is expected to edge lower by year-end.  
• Capex will land in the low-to-mid-$30 million range for 2021 (YTD $19 million), directed at safety, productivity and growth projects (notably the new release-liner coater in Mexico scheduled for 2023 start-up).  
• Q3 share repurchases: 71 000 shares for $3.4 million at an average $47.85; the Board approved a $0.02 annual dividend increase, sustaining an 11-year record of raises.  

Structural cost actions and expected 2022 earnings lift  
• Appleton, Wisconsin mill closed at quarter-end; the action should contribute >$6 million of incremental EBITDA in 2022 (management also referred to ~$7-8 million annual savings).  
• Itasa integration is on track; the acquisition contributed $36 million of Q3 revenue and is expected to add another $5 million of EBITDA next year as synergies build.  
• Combined with the remaining $20 million of price recovery, these items amount to roughly $30 million of incremental 2022 EBITDA.  

Market and strategic highlights  
• Filtration sales are up >25 % year-to-date versus 2020 and 20 % over 2019, with strength in life-science and industrial applications. New high-efficiency media platforms NeenahGuard and NeenahPure target HVAC, gas-turbine, gasket, process-fluid and medical markets.  
• Release-liner business delivered three record sales months since the April close; long-term growth expectation remains about 8 % per year.  
• Engineered Materials introduced a recyclable paper-based carton-sealing tape and expanded the DISPERSA water-dispersible label and pouch line, both aimed at plastic substitution.  
• Packaging (beauty/cosmetics, alcohol, luxury retail, electronics) set a record quarter, benefiting from sustainability preference and new account wins.  

Fiscal outlook and long-term targets  
• Management expects Q3 to represent the trough margin for 2021; although energy and chemical costs remain volatile, sequential margin improvement is anticipated in Q4 as pricing gains accelerate.  
• Input-cost inflation should stabilise and, in some cases, retreat during 2022, while Neenah’s cumulative pricing, mix upgrades and cost actions flow through.  
• Long-term objectives reaffirmed: ~5 % annual revenue growth, ~10 % earnings growth and EBITDA margins in excess of 15 %, while maintaining a healthy balance sheet and funding organic growth, dividends and disciplined M&A.  

In summary, Neenah’s Q3 demonstrated robust demand across its specialty platforms and the initial benefit of Itasa, but unprecedented raw-material and energy inflation compressed earnings. With multiple price rounds already implemented, significant additional price scheduled for early 2022, structural cost savings from the Appleton closure, and synergies plus organic growth in filtration, release liners and premium packaging, management is confident of a visible step-up in EBITDA, margin and cash generation next year and remains committed to its longer-term financial targets.
