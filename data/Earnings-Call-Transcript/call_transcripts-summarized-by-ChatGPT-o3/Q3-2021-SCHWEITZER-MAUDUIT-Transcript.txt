# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q3 2021 Earnings Call Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International’s third-quarter 2021 results reflected vigorous end-market demand but also unprecedented cost inflation, raw-material scarcity, logistics bottlenecks and labour shortages that materially compressed margins and earnings.

Management delivered consolidated net sales of $384 million, a 37 % increase year-over-year (-1 % organically) but adjusted operating profit fell 24 % to $40 million.  Adjusted EPS dropped to $0.82 from $1.16 a year ago; GAAP EPS was $0.38 versus $0.78.  The company estimates that unrecouped resin and pulp inflation alone reduced EPS by more than $0.20 and lost transportation-film sales tied to resin shortages cut a further $0.10.

## Segment Performance

Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• Sales surged 87 % on the addition of the April-acquired Scapa Group and rose 10 % organically; transportation and filtration each grew roughly 25 %.  
• Without resin scarcity and labour gaps, organic growth could have exceeded 20 %.  
• Adjusted operating profit increased 9 % to $27 million, but the margin contracted 750 bp to 10.5 % because resin, freight and other inputs rose faster than price increases.  Polypropylene alone hit profits by about $5 million.  
• Scapa contributed >$9 million of operating profit; about $2.5 million of its SG&A was booked in corporate unallocated expense.  The deal was slightly dilutive in the quarter but is still expected to provide roughly $0.50 of EPS accretion by the back half of 2022 once synergies and normalised costs materialise.  
• Paint-protection-film (PPF) capacity remains fully utilised; tens of millions of dollars of 2021 PPF revenue have been deferred by thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) shortages.  Additional resin supply for 2022 has been secured, supporting management’s expectation of >10 % PPF growth next year on top of 2021’s strong gains.  
• Filtration growth was constrained by understaffed plants, costing an estimated $4–5 million of quarterly sales.

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• Sales declined 12 % (volume –10 %) against a difficult comparison that included last year’s COVID-related customer inventory builds of low-ignition-propensity (LIP) paper.  
• Higher pulp, freight, energy and natural-gas costs weighed heavily; net of partial price pass-throughs, pulp inflation alone reduced profit by roughly $3 million.  
• The NBSK pulp index peaked near $1,340/ton (+60 % YoY) during Q3; management believes prices have plateaued and expects modest relief in coming quarters.  
• Heat-not-burn and hemp/botanical products continued to ramp and carry attractive margin profiles.  A first meaningful hemp-filler contract is close to finalisation and could become a multimillion-dollar account within two years.  
• The company is closing its Winkler, Manitoba facility by year-end following the earlier Spotswood, New Jersey closure to further streamline the cost base.

## Inflation, Pricing & Supply Chain Actions

• Multiple price increases have been pushed through, the latest effective 1 October, yet benefits lag cost escalation by one to two quarters.  
• Resin (especially polypropylene) averaged ~$1.40/lb in Q3 and flows into Q4 COGS; October pricing has softened and industry forecasts call for sub-$1/lb by 2H 2022.  
• Contractual pulp price pass-throughs occur mainly at the start of each year; certain contracts are being renegotiated to incorporate more frequent adjustments.  
• Labour shortages are being tackled via wage increases, retention programmes and accelerated automation under SWM’s Manufacturing 4.0 initiative.

## Outlook and Guidance

• Q4 2021 adjusted EPS is projected to be about 20 % below the prior-year $0.77 (≈$0.62), reflecting high-cost raw materials purchased in Q3, ongoing material scarcity and labour constraints.  
• Full-year 2021 adjusted EPS is now expected to finish roughly 10 % below the low end of the original $3.90–$4.10 guidance range issued early in the year.  
• Management anticipates “strong operating-profit growth” in 2022 as resin and pulp costs ease, price increases catch up, Scapa synergies flow, and supply constraints abate.  They target exiting 2022 on a run-rate “approaching $4” of adjusted EPS, effectively returning to the earnings power assumed in the original 2021 plan.  

## Cash Flow, Balance Sheet & Liquidity

• Year-to-date operating cash flow was $28 million versus $108 million in 2020, impacted by $14 million of Scapa-related cash charges and a $50 million working-capital outflow (≈$30 million due to inflation-driven inventory cost).  
• Net debt stood at just over $1.2 billion; leverage under the credit agreement was 4.8 × EBITDA, comfortably below the 5.5 × covenant.  
• Total liquidity was about $160 million (cash $73 million plus $86 million undrawn revolver).  An asset sale from discontinued operations is expected to close in Q4, providing additional cash.  

## Strategic Priorities

Despite near-term margin pressure, SWM continues to:  
• Invest in capacity expansions for high-growth franchises such as paint-protection films, water/process filtration media and heat-not-burn papers.  
• Advance product innovation, especially in hemp, botanicals, and wound-care materials.  
• Pursue commercial and cost synergies from the Scapa integration.  
• Deploy Manufacturing 4.0 technologies to offset labour tightness, improve quality and enhance efficiency.

Management remains confident that these initiatives, combined with moderating input-cost headwinds and recovering supply chains, will restore profitability and generate sustained EPS growth in 2022 and beyond while maintaining a disciplined balance-sheet posture.
