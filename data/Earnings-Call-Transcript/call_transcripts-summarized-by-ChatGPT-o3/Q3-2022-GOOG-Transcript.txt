# Alphabet Inc. – Q3 2022 Earnings Call Summary  

Alphabet’s September-quarter results showed modest top-line growth, noticeable FX headwinds, widening operating-expense pressure and continuing investment discipline, while management reiterated its strategic focus on AI, Cloud and YouTube Shorts monetisation.

Alphabet (GAAP unless noted)  
• Total revenue grew 6 % Y/Y to **$69.1 bn** (up 11 % in constant currency).  
• Net income was **$13.9 bn**, equal to diluted EPS of **$1.06** (down 26 % Y/Y).  
• Operating income fell 19 % Y/Y to **$17.1 bn**; operating margin compressed to **25 %** (vs. 32 % a year ago).  
• Free cash flow reached **$16.1 bn** in the quarter, **$63 bn** for the TTM.  
• The balance sheet ended with **$116 bn** in cash and marketable securities and immaterial long-term debt, leaving the company in a net-cash position.  

Segment detail  
Google Services  
• Revenue: **$61.4 bn** (+2 % Y/Y).  
  – Search & Other Ads: **$39.5 bn** (+4 %). Travel and retail were the main positive verticals; finance (insurance, loans, mortgages, crypto) saw notable pull-backs.  
  – YouTube Ads: **$7.1 bn** (-2 %). Brand and direct-response budgets contracted; viewing shifted further to Shorts.  
  – Network Ads: **$7.9 bn** (-2 %).  
  – Other (subscriptions & hardware): **$6.9 bn** (+2 %). Growth in YouTube Music/Premium and Pixel 6a hardware offset a Y/Y decline in Google Play due to softer mobile-gaming spend.  
• TAC (traffic-acquisition costs) rose 3 % Y/Y to **$11.8 bn**.  
• Google Services operating income declined 17 % to **$19.8 bn**; margin fell to **32 %**.  

Google Cloud  
• Revenue rose **38 %** Y/Y to **$6.9 bn**; GCP again outpaced the segment average and Workspace benefited from higher seat count and ARPU.  
• Operating loss was **$699 mn**, reflecting continued investment but an improving loss ratio.  
• Management acknowledged some customers are signing shorter or smaller contracts amid macro uncertainty but reiterated long-term growth and a “path to profitability”.  

Other Bets  
• Revenue: **$209 mn**.  
• Operating loss: **$1.6 bn**.  
• Waymo named Los Angeles its third ride-hailing market; Wing surpassed 300 k commercial drone deliveries.  

Cost structure & capital allocation  
• Total cost of revenues rose **13 %** to **$31.2 bn**, led by data-centre and “other operations” spend, with hardware the second-largest driver; FX-adjusted unit costs also rose.  
• Operating expenses climbed **26 %** to **$20.8 bn** (R&D, G&A and S&M all driven primarily by head-count growth plus higher ad-promotion spend).  
• CapEx was up **31 % Y/Y**, still concentrated in servers and AI-related technical infrastructure; real-estate outlays moderate as offices are fitted for hybrid work.  
• Headcount grew by **12,765** in Q3 (includes ~2,600 Mandiant employees). Hiring will “slow to less than half” Q3 additions in Q4; 2023 hiring will prioritise “top engineering and technical talent”.  

Foreign-exchange impact  
• A strengthening U.S. dollar created a **6-ppt revenue headwind** Y/Y (5 ppt after hedging), versus a slight FX tailwind in 3Q 21. The company expects an even larger FX drag in Q4 and notes that the impact on operating income is greater than on revenue because most expenses are U.S.-dollar-denominated.  

Advertising environment & product commentary  
• Management characterised the macro backdrop as “uncertain”; pull-backs in advertiser spend intensified from Q2 to Q3, especially in YouTube and Network.  
• Search remains relatively resilient because of its measurable ROI. Alphabet continues to push Performance Max and other automation tools; early adopters cite conversion lifts (e.g., product-feed campaigns on Shorts deliver >70 % more conversions than videos without feeds).  
• YouTube user trends: Shorts reach **1.5 bn logged-in users per month** with **30 bn daily views**; ads on Shorts launched in September, and revenue-sharing for creators begins in early 2023. Connected-TV viewing exceeds 700 mn hours per day; Nielsen ranked YouTube #1 in U.S. streaming TV reach in September.  
• Retail innovations include “Shop + item” visual feeds, 200 mn daily deals surfaced in Q3, and shopping integrations across Search, YouTube and Discover.  
• Hardware: Pixel 7/7 Pro and Pixel Watch launched, driving Alphabet’s “highest-selling week ever for Pixel”; Pixel-tablet debut slated for 2023.  

Strategic priorities & 2023 outlook  
• Alphabet is “sharpening focus” on core growth areas—AI across the portfolio, cloud infrastructure/security, and monetisation of YouTube Shorts—while reallocating resources away from lower-priority projects.  
• Q4 will feature much lower net hiring; operating-expense growth is expected to moderate further in 2023.  
• Advertising comps remain very tough versus record Q4 21, and FX headwinds are accelerating; management did not give explicit revenue or EPS guidance.  
• Despite near-term margin pressure, management emphasised long-term opportunities: AI-powered product enhancements (BERT, MUM, LaMDA, Google Lens), the expanding cloud TAM, and the scaling creator-economy ecosystem on YouTube.  

Liquidity & leverage  
• With **$116 bn** of cash and marketable securities and limited debt, Alphabet continues to hold a fortress balance sheet, providing flexibility for ongoing CapEx, M&A (e.g., the completed **$5.4 bn** Mandiant deal) and share repurchases.  

Bottom line: Q3 revealed slower ad growth and higher cost intensity, but Search fundamentals, Cloud momentum, a strong cash position and disciplined re-prioritisation continue to underpin Alphabet’s long-term growth and capital-return capacity.
