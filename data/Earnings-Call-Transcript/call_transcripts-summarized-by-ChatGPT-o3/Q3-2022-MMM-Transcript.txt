# 3M Company – Q3 2022 Earnings Call Summary

3M’s third-quarter results and accompanying guidance depict a company that is squeezing cost levers and portfolio moves to counter slowing macro conditions, heavy inflation, a surging U.S. dollar and litigation overhangs, while still funding growth projects and a planned Healthcare spin-off.

Third-Quarter Headlines  
• Net sales were $8.62 billion, down 3.6 % year-on-year; a 5.1 % (≈ $450 million) currency drag and 0.5 % from portfolio changes outweighed 2 % organic growth. Excluding an expected $130 million drop in disposable-respirator demand, organic growth would have been 3.4 %.  
• Adjusted operating margin reached 21.5 % (↑ 40 bp Y/Y; ↑ 50 bp sequentially).  
• Adjusted EPS of $2.69 beat consensus by $0.10 and rose $0.11 Y/Y despite FX shaving $0.12 and respirator decline $0.07.  
• Adjusted free cash flow was $1.4 billion, an 88 % conversion rate; inventories improved sequentially, though supply-chain fluidity kept management from raising the full-year cash-flow outlook.  
• Net debt ended at $12.1 billion, down 3 % Y/Y and more than 30 % below 2019.  

Segment & Regional Performance  
Safety & Industrial: $2.9 billion sales (+1.7 % organic; +6 % ex-respirators); op margin 23.2 % (↑ 250 bp). Broad industrial demand and China backlog recovery offset respirator collapse.  
Transportation & Electronics: $2.2 billion (+3 % organic); margin 21.2 % (↑ 250 bp). Auto OEM rose 21 % versus a 27 % global build increase; electronics declined mid-single digits on soft smartphones/tablets.  
Health Care: $2.1 billion (+1.7 % organic) against an 8 % comparison; op margin 21.8 % (↓ 170 bp). Elective procedures ran about 90 % of pre-COVID; oral-care soft; U.S. Biopharma down versus big COVID-therapeutics orders last year.  
Consumer: $1.4 billion (+1.5 % organic); margin 21.3 % (flattish). Retailers cut inventories and back-to-school sell-through lagged; holiday spending expected to stay pressured.  
Geography: APAC grew 3 % (China +8 %), Americas +2 % (U.S. flat vs. +6 % last year), EMEA flat as war-driven uncertainty persists.  

Cost, Inflation & Pricing  
• Q3 raw-material and logistics inflation was ≈ $225 million (-2.6 pt to margin, ‑$0.31 EPS). Management still expects $750–$850 million full-year inflation, to be fully offset by “mid-single-digit” pricing.  
• Q4 inflation is projected to moderate to $100–$150 million.  
• FX hurt Q3 revenue by 5 % and EPS by $0.12; full-year EPS hit now pegged at ≈ $0.50.  

Litigation and Legal  
• Aearo Technologies (Combat Arms earplugs) remains in Chapter 11; mediation under way. Bankruptcy-stay appeal accepted by the Seventh Circuit; next MDL trial set for February 2023.  
• PFAS: recently settled with City of Gadsden, Alabama; first AFFF MDL trial slated for June 2023.  

Portfolio Actions & Capital Deployment  
• 1 Sep: closed Food Safety divestiture (≈ $1 billion cash, share count reduced by 16 million via split-off).  
• Early October: sold two Southeast-Asia skin-care brands.  
• Healthcare spin-off planning on track; dedicated team in place.  
• Q3 shareholder returns: $850 million dividends, $155 million buybacks (repurchases resumed post-divestiture). YTD cash returns $3.5 billion. Outstanding shares will be mid-550 million in Q4.  
• 2022 CapEx now $1.75–$1.85 billion (supply-chain delays trimmed prior target); projects focus on growth, automation, and $1 billion sustainability commitment (e.g., new U.S. water-filtration installations).  

Updated 2022 Outlook  
• Organic sales growth: 1.5 %–2 % (was 1.5 %–3.5 %).  
• Adjusted EPS: $10.10–$10.35 (was $10.30–$10.80); additional $0.15 FX headwind since July.  
• FCF conversion: 85 %–95 % (was 90 %–100 %).  
• CapEx: $1.75–$1.85 billion (was up to $2 billion).  

Fourth-Quarter Color  
• Total sales guide $7.9–$8.2 billion; organic +1 % to +3 % but includes 2 pt (≈ $150–200 million) respirator drag and 0.8 pt ($70 million) Russia exit. Excluding those, organic +4 %–6 %.  
• FX to trim revenue ≈ 7 % ($600 million).  
• Operating margin projected 20 %–21 %.  
• Raw-material/logistics inflation $100–$150 million, expected to be offset by price.  

Early 2023 View  
Management expects:  
• Modest global GDP/IP growth, continued geopolitical and energy risk in Europe, strong USD and lingering COVID policy uncertainty.  
• Secular tailwinds in auto electrification, semiconductors, factory automation, safety, digitization and sustainability.  
• Supply-chain stabilization and data-driven productivity as the “biggest opportunity” to cut cost of goods sold and working capital, aiding margin resilience.  

Strategic & Operational Highlights  
• New South Carolina export-consolidation center trims Asia transit by one to two weeks.  
• Fully automated abrasive-belt line in Minnesota boosts labor productivity 32 %, eliminates nine high-risk tasks, and saves ≈ $1 million annually.  
• Digital initiatives: Post-it app for Microsoft Teams, RepairStack SaaS for body shops, battery-component test lab for EV customers.  

Bottom Line  
3M beat Q3 earnings expectations through price discipline, productivity and portfolio pruning, but lowered full-year growth, EPS and cash-flow ranges to reflect stronger dollar, softer consumer end-markets and respirator runoff. Management is leaning on continued price recovery, factory digitization and litigation management while investing in secular-growth platforms and the impending Healthcare spin-off to position the company “for long-term value creation” amid macro uncertainty.
