# NVIDIA Q3 FY 2022 Earnings Call – Financial & Strategic Summary

NVIDIA reported record third-quarter results for fiscal 2022, delivered strong guidance for Q4, and highlighted multiple long-term growth vectors led by Data Center, Gaming RTX and the new Omniverse platform.

Third-Quarter FY 2022 (three months ended 31 October 2021)  
• Revenue reached an all-time high of **$7.10 billion**, up **50 % year on year** and 9 % sequentially.  
• Segment records were set in Gaming, Data Center and Professional Visualization (Pro Viz).  
• GAAP gross margin rose **260 bps YoY** and **40 bps QoQ** to **65 %** (non-GAAP: 67 %).  
• GAAP EPS was **$0.97** (+83 % YoY); non-GAAP EPS **$1.17** (+60 % YoY, split-adjusted).  
• Operating cash flow was **$1.5 billion** (up from $1.3 billion YoY, down from $2.7 billion QoQ due to $1.6 billion prepayment for long-term supply).

Segment detail  
• Gaming: **$3.22 billion** (+42 % YoY, +5 % QoQ). Demand stayed strong; channel inventories remain low even as supply improves. Approximately 25 % of the installed base has upgraded to RTX. CMP (crypto-mining processors) contributed **$105 million**, but nearly all high-end Ampere gaming GPUs shipped as “lite‐hash-rate” parts.  
• Data Center: **$2.94 billion** (+55 % YoY, +24 % QoQ). Strength was broad-based; hyperscale compute revenue doubled YoY, while vertical-industry demand (e.g., consumer internet, cloud AI services) also set records. AI inference revenue again outpaced overall Data Center growth; 25 000+ companies now deploy NVIDIA AI.  
• Professional Visualization: **$577 million** (+144 % YoY, +11 % QoQ) driven by Ampere mobile and desktop workstations and hybrid-work demand. Omniverse Enterprise reached general availability; >700 firms and 70 000 individual creators are evaluating/using the platform.  
• Automotive: **$135 million** (+8 % YoY, –11 % QoQ) as cockpit programs were constrained by OEM component shortages. New DRIVE Orin design wins include Kodiak Robotics, Lotus, QCraft and WM Motor.  
• OEM & Other: includes CMP; CMP revenue will “decline to very negligible levels” in Q4.

Balance sheet & supply  
• NVIDIA has **$3.4 billion** in long-term capacity agreements; **$1.6 billion** was prepaid during Q3 to secure additional foundry, substrate, packaging and test capacity. Management “feels very good” about supply from 2H FY22 onward.

Fourth-Quarter FY 2022 Outlook (ending Jan-22)  
• Revenue **$7.4 billion ± 2 %**, led by further growth in Data Center and Gaming; CMP expected to fall sharply.  
• GAAP / non-GAAP gross margin **65.3 % / 67.0 % ± 50 bps**.  
• GAAP / non-GAAP opex **$2.02 billion / $1.43 billion**.  
• Other expense approx. **$60 million**; tax rate 11 % ± 1 %.  
• Capex **$250–275 million**.

Strategic & qualitative highlights  
1. **Omniverse** – positioned as a multi-billion-dollar annual software and hardware opportunity. Licensing model targets **$1 000 per “main user” or AI avatar per year**; potential base includes 40 million 3D designers, 100 million cars and ~25 million retail/ customer-service locations. Near-term deployments (drive-thrus, smart retail, digital twins) expected “within five years.”  
2. **AI Software Stack** – Triton, TensorRT, Megatron, Riva, Merlin and new large-language-model inference across multi-GPU/multi-node clusters underpin continued Data Center momentum.  
3. **Enterprise AI** – NVIDIA AI Enterprise suite is now generally available on VMware vSphere and through Equinix LaunchPad sites in nine global metros, broadening reach to mainstream IT organizations.  
4. **Networking** – Demand exceeded supply; Quantum-2 400 Gb/s InfiniBand platform (Quantum-2 switch, ConnectX-7 NIC, BlueField-3 DPU) launched for HPC and cloud.  
5. **Developer ecosystem** – 150+ SDKs; nearly 3 million registered developers; CUDA downloads surpassed 30 million.  
6. **Future products** – Jetson AGX Orin (200 TOPS edge computer) ships Q1 CY22; BlueField DOCA 1.2 adds zero-trust security; Clara Holoscan targets medical devices; Earth-2 climate AI supercomputer announced.  
7. **Regulatory status of Arm acquisition** – FTC, UK, EU and Chinese authorities have moved to deeper reviews; NVIDIA continues to engage and “remains confident” in the transaction’s merits but provided no closing timetable.

Margin dynamics  
• Mix toward high-end RTX, Ampere Data Center GPUs and embedded software content lifted margins; cost inflation at foundry and packaging partners is being managed within existing margin profile. Long-term, standalone software sales (e.g., Omniverse, AI Enterprise) could be further accretive.

Demand visibility  
• Approximately **50 % of Data Center revenue comes from cloud service providers** and **50 % from enterprise verticals (plus ~1 % HPC)**. Hyperscale customers are locking in capacity for large AI model training and inference build-outs in calendar 2022. Management expects Data Center to grow faster than Gaming in Q4 and describes FY 2023 demand outlook as “terrific.”

Overall, NVIDIA enters Q4 with record backlogs, secured supply, expanding software monetization, and multiple secular tailwinds in AI, graphics, simulation and autonomous machines, supporting confidence in sustained revenue and earnings growth while preserving industry-leading gross margins.
