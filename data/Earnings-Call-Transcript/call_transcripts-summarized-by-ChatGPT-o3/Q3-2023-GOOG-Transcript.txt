# Alphabet Inc. – Q3 2023 Earnings Call Summary  

Alphabet’s third-quarter results showed double-digit top-line growth, expanding margins and robust free-cash-flow generation, while management emphasized heavy but disciplined investment in Artificial Intelligence across Search, Ads, Cloud and Devices.

Alphabet’s consolidated revenue grew 11 % year-on-year to $76.7 billion (also +11 % in constant currency). GAAP diluted EPS rose to approximately $1.55 (net income $19.7 billion) versus $1.06 a year ago, helped by operating-margin expansion to 28 % (25 % in Q3-22). Free cash flow reached $22.6 billion in the quarter and $78 billion for the trailing 12 months; the company ended September with $120 billion in cash and marketable securities. (A previously deferred U.S. tax payment of $10.5 billion was remitted on 16 October and will lower Q4 cash.)

## Segment performance and key metrics  

• Google Services revenue rose 11 % to $68.0 billion.  
  • Search & Other Advertising: $44.0 billion, +11 %, led by retail.  
  • YouTube Ads: $8.0 billion, +12 %, with growth in both brand and direct-response spend; Shorts now averages 70 billion daily views and reaches 2 billion logged-in users monthly.  
  • Network Ads: $7.7 billion, –3 %.  
  • Other (mainly YouTube subscriptions, Google Play and hardware): $8.3 billion, +21 %. Subscriptions were driven by YouTube TV, YouTube Music and NFL Sunday Ticket (only a few weeks of revenue booked in Q3; a full quarter will appear in Q4).  
  • Traffic-acquisition costs (TAC) were $12.6 billion, +7 %.  
  • Services operating income was $23.9 billion, up 27 %, for a 35 % operating margin.

• Google Cloud revenue grew 22 % to $8.4 billion (GCP above segment growth; Workspace also “strong”), while operating income turned positive for a second straight quarter at $266 million (3 % margin). Growth has slowed somewhat as customers still optimize spend, but new AI workloads are accelerating: active generative-AI projects on Vertex AI jumped 7× quarter-over-quarter, and more than half of all funded Gen-AI start-ups now run on Google Cloud.

• Other Bets produced $297 million of revenue and a $1.2 billion operating loss. Waymo continues to scale ride-hailing in San Francisco (100 k+ wait-listed riders) and will launch in Austin; Wing expanded drone delivery with Walmart in Dallas–Fort Worth.

## Capital allocation, cost structure and outlook  

Q3 capex was $8.0 billion, driven “overwhelmingly” by servers and data-centres to support AI demand; management expects capex to “increase in Q4 ’23 and grow again in 2024,” so full-year 2024 capex will exceed 2023. Alphabet continues a company-wide programme to “durably re-engineer” the cost base: slower head-count growth, real-estate rationalisation (incurring $207 million of charges in Q3), AI-driven productivity tools, and centralised procurement. Management reiterated the long-term goal of revenue growth outpacing expense growth even as AI investment stays aggressive.

Fourth-quarter modelling considerations:  
• A full three months of NFL Sunday Ticket will lift “Other revenue” but also raise content-acquisition costs (CAC) and pressure Services gross margin.  
• Hardware revenue faces a headwind from a narrower Pixel go-to-market strategy and year-over-year launch-timing differences (Pixel 6a vs 7a).  
• Sales & marketing spend will seasonally skew to Q4 for holiday support.  

## Strategic and product highlights  

Artificial Intelligence remains the central theme:  

Search & Generative AI  
• The Search Generative Experience (SGE) is still experimental but is now live in the U.S., India and Japan; initial user feedback is positive, with “strong growth and adoption.” Alphabet is integrating ads native to SGE and says link-out frequency is higher than in classic Search, broadening traffic opportunities for publishers.  
• Bard, a conversational LLM interface, now integrates Workspace, Maps, YouTube, Flights and Hotels data; “Assistant with Bard” will launch on Android and iOS in coming months.  
• Gemini, Google DeepMind’s next-generation multimodal foundation model, is in training and will roll out in multiple sizes in 2024, powering both consumer products and Vertex AI.

Advertising innovation  
• Roughly 80 % of advertisers already use at least one AI-powered Search-ads product; Performance Max campaigns deliver 18 % more conversions at similar CPA.  
• New Demand Gen and Video Reach campaigns leverage Google AI across YouTube, Shorts and in-feed inventory, delivering >50 % incremental reach at materially lower cost in tests.  
• Retail advertisers are adopting AI-driven tools such as merchant promotions, virtual try-on, Product Studio and omni-channel Performance Max for store goals ahead of an extended holiday season.

YouTube ecosystem  
• YouTube remains the No. 1 U.S. streaming destination on connected-TV, viewed by 150 million+ people monthly. Shorts monetisation progress continues; closing the ad-revenue gap with long-form remains a key priority.  
• NFL Sunday Ticket has launched successfully; fan reception of multi-view and low latency has been strong, and Alphabet expects attractive returns over the life of the deal.

Cloud & AI platforms  
• Google Cloud now serves >60 % of the Global 1000. AI interest is translating into infrastructure (A3 VMs with NVIDIA H100; TPU v5e), Vertex AI model-building, and Duet AI copilots integrated across Cloud, Workspace and security products.  
• Duet AI reduces security-operations query time sevenfold and boosts developer productivity for PayPal, Deutsche Bank and others.  

Hardware  
• Pixel 8, Pixel 8 Pro and Pixel Watch 2 debuted to “very positive” reviews; Pixel remains the fastest-growing smartphone brand in its top markets and the only one posting unit-growth year-over-year. The in-house Tensor G3 chip brings new AI image- and audio-editing features straight to devices.

Regulatory & privacy  
• Chrome will begin phasing out third-party cookies in H2 2024; 1 % of users will lose cookies in Q1 2024 to facilitate real-world testing of Privacy Sandbox APIs. Early ad-platform tests combining privacy-preserving signals with AI optimisation show comparable, sometimes better, campaign performance.

Balance-sheet health is strong, with no net debt, significant liquidity and retained capacity for share repurchases (not specifically quantified on the call) alongside elevated AI capex.

Management concluded by reiterating confidence that disciplined cost controls can free up funding for AI, drive sustainable revenue and profit growth, and create long-term shareholder value.
