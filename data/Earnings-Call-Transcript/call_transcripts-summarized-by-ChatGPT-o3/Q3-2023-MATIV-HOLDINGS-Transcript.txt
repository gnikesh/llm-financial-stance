# Mativ Holdings Q3 2023 Earnings Call – Financial Summary

Mativ’s third quarter of 2023 is the first period that is fully comparable to the prior-year quarter following the July 2022 SWM–Neenah merger, and it is also the first quarter in which the soon-to-be-divested Engineered Papers (EP) business is reported as discontinued operations.  

For continuing operations, net sales were $498 million, 5 percent lower than the second quarter and 10 percent below the prior-year period. A 12 percent decline in volume was only partly offset by approximately 3 percent benefit from price and currency. Adjusted EBITDA was $55 million, essentially flat versus the second quarter but down from $70 million a year ago; volume-driven under-absorption of fixed costs and other manufacturing headwinds created a roughly $40 million drag that was partially offset by $23 million in net price/input-cost relief, lower SG&A, realized merger synergies, and favorable currency.

Within segments, Advanced Technical Materials (ATM) produced sales of $394 million, down 8 percent year-on-year and 6 percent sequentially, reflecting softer macro demand, customer caution, and normal European seasonality. Nonetheless, EBITDA of $59 million fell only 6 percent year-on-year, and the margin improved 30 basis points to 15.0 percent thanks to synergies and a better mix. Fiber-Based Solutions (FBS)—now consisting solely of premium packaging and specialty papers—recorded sales of $104 million, 17 percent below a very strong prior-year quarter that had been boosted by customer inventory builds. FBS EBITDA fell to $15 million, influenced by volume pressure, de-stocking across the paper value chain, and the absence of a one-time $7 million inventory-cost harmonization benefit that had lifted the prior year.

Corporate adjusted EBITDA expense remained roughly flat at $18 million. Interest expense rose by $2 million to $17 million because of higher variable-rate debt, but management expects annual interest savings of more than $40 million once more than $575 million of EP sale proceeds are applied to debt reduction. Net debt at 30 September stood at a little over $1.6 billion, and available liquidity was $414 million; maturities are staggered between 2026 and 2028. The company re-affirmed its leverage goal of 2.5×–3.5× EBITDA, acknowledging that the path there is extended by current market conditions but will accelerate after the EP divestiture and an earnings recovery.

GAAP results include several non-cash items: a $401 million goodwill impairment linked mainly to merger-era valuations, a $19 million asset write-down associated with the upcoming closure of three smaller manufacturing sites, and just over $5 million of EP-related transaction costs. A negative effective tax rate for the quarter stemmed from the nondeductible goodwill charge and a valuation-allowance increase on a Luxembourg tax asset that will no longer be utilizable once EP is sold.

Cash preservation and cost-out initiatives are a major focus. Merger synergies are tracking ahead of the $65 million goal; more than $25 million of value will be delivered in 2023, with a further $25 million identified for 2024. Inventory has already been reduced by over $50 million since the first quarter, with a targeted improvement of at least another 100 basis points when measured as a percentage of sales. Three smaller, lower-margin plants will be consolidated beginning in early 2024, an action expected to contribute roughly $5 million of annual savings after minor up-front costs. An outside consultant has been engaged to ensure SG&A is right-sized post-EP divestiture and to prevent stranded overhead. Capital expenditures will be held below $70 million in 2024, versus the higher levels of the past two years.

Strategically, management will concentrate investment on its highest-growth, highest-margin platforms—release liners and filtration. A new release-liner coater in Mexico has just come on line, and a fine-fiber melt-blown filtration asset remains on schedule to start up early in the second quarter of 2024. Together, these two projects are expected to add more than $50 million of incremental annual revenue once fully ramped. Near-term product launches—including pearlized premium packaging papers (for which Mativ is the sole North-American source), a faster-dissolving label stock, a mid-tier paint-protection film, and certain ballistic-resistant optical films—are projected to generate at least $10 million of new revenue over the coming 12 months.

Macroeconomic headwinds persist. The U.S. manufacturing PMI contracted during the quarter, de-stocking by customers was still evident, and construction and transportation markets remain the softest. Healthcare applications were the company’s best performer, posting 12 percent sales growth. Management believes channel de-stocking is largely over but warns that normal year-end inventory tightening and routine maintenance downtime could create a modest sequential sales dip in the fourth quarter, along with $2–$3 million of incremental costs.

Looking to 2024, Mativ anticipates demand stabilization in the first half, an acceleration of recovery in the back half, and modest inflation in input costs. Assuming roughly 5 percent sales growth and a further $25 million of synergy capture, management expects to exit 2024 at a quarterly EBITDA run-rate of about $70 million, versus the current mid-$50 million level. Improvement will be driven by recovering volumes, synergy realization, ongoing cost-saving initiatives, and contributions from the new filtration and release-liner capacity. Once the EP transaction closes, approximately 80 percent of revenue will derive from ATM, a business that consistently delivers mid-teens EBITDA margins and is leveraged to global growth themes such as clean air, clean water, and infrastructure upgrades.

Capital allocation priorities remain intact. The quarterly dividend was reset in August to $0.10 per share, freeing up additional cash while still offering an attractive yield; the first payment under the new rate totaled $5.5 million in September. During the quarter the company also repurchased roughly $4 million of shares and plans to continue repurchases in similar or smaller amounts mainly to offset stock-compensation dilution, with the bulk of free cash flow still earmarked for debt reduction.

Management reiterates confidence that the strategic reshaping of the portfolio, aggressive cost actions, and disciplined capital deployment will strengthen Mativ’s balance sheet, enhance profitability, and position the company for sustainable long-term growth once macro conditions normalize.
