# 3M Q3 2023 Earnings Call – Financial Summary  

3M generated Q3 2023 sales of **$8.31 billion**, down 3.6 % year-on-year but roughly **$280 million above** consensus.  Adjusted EPS of **$2.68** exceeded expectations by **$0.33** and rose 3 % versus the prior-year quarter.  GAAP results were burdened by ongoing restructuring charges and litigation items, but on an adjusted basis the company posted:  

• **Adjusted operating margin of 23.2 %**, up 160 bp Y/Y and 390 bp sequentially.  
• **Free cash flow of $1.9 billion**, +39 % Y/Y, yielding **130 % free-cash-flow conversion** (up 360 bp).  
• Inventory fell **$200 million sequentially and $550 million Y/Y** as data-driven supply-chain actions accelerated working-capital turns.  
• Net debt ended the quarter at **$10.8 billion**, down 11 % Y/Y.  

Management raised 2023 guidance:  

• Adjusted EPS now **$8.95–$9.15** (prior $8.60–$9.10).  
• Free-cash-flow conversion now **100–110 %** (prior 90–100 %).  
• Adjusted organic sales are expected to finish **~3 % lower** Y/Y, versus “flat to –3 %” previously, reflecting an incremental $50 million fall-off in disposable respirators (full-year decline now pegged at ~$600 million).  

Fourth-quarter planning assumptions: revenue of **$7.6–$7.7 billion** (normal seasonality and fewer selling days), Q4 restructuring charges of **$70–$120 million** and benefits of **$145–$195 million**, and adjusted EPS of **$2.13–$2.33**.  Full-year restructuring benefits remain targeted at **$400–$450 million**, entirely offset by charges; the two-year program still totals **$700–$900 million** of both costs and benefits.  

Segment performance (all figures adjusted):  

• Safety & Industrial: sales **$2.8 billion** (–5.8 % organically, but only –1.5 % ex-respirators/Russia); operating margin **25.7 %**, +250 bp Y/Y. Personal Safety excluding disposable respirators grew high-single digits; packaging, electronics adhesives and abrasives remained weak; roofing granules rose high-single digits.  

• Transportation & Electronics: sales **$1.9 billion** (–1.8 %); margin **26.3 %**, +460 bp Y/Y.  Electronics revenue fell mid-single digits as semiconductor and data-center demand stayed soft, but Auto OEM grew ~16 % on low-single-digit global builds, and the auto-electrification portfolio is on track for **$600 million** of 2023 revenue (+30 % YTD).  

• Health Care: sales **$2.1 billion**, +2.4 % organically; margin **22.2 %**, +50 bp.  Oral Care grew high-single digits, Medical Solutions low single digits; Health Information Systems declined low single digits amid tight hospital budgets.  The spin-off of the Health-Care business remains on schedule for **H1 2024**; Bryan Hanson was named CEO and Kerry Cox Board Chair.  3M will retain 19.9 % of the new company and expects a one-time dividend at 3–3.5× EBITDA leverage, enhancing financial flexibility.  

• Consumer: sales **$1.3 billion** (–7.2 % organically); margin **20.5 %**, –70 bp Y/Y but +230 bp sequentially.  Discretionary hard-line categories, a muted back-to-school season and higher interest-rate-related housing weakness weighed on demand; retailers continued cautious inventory management.  

Macro and cost commentary: management still sees disinflation rather than outright deflation.  Carry-over inflation in raw materials, logistics and energy was **~$25 million** in Q3 (-30 bp to margin); low-single-digit price increases offset most cost pressures.  Productivity, restructuring savings, disciplined OPEX and improved supply-chain yields drove the bulk of the margin expansion.  

Strategic and legal developments:  

• Operational redesign announced in Q1 is “largely on track,” slimming corporate overhead, simplifying the global supply chain, and pushing decision-making closer to customers.  
• PFAS public-water-supplier settlement received preliminary court approval; final hearing is set for February 2024.  Combat-arms earplug settlement administration has begun and is funded.  
• More than 60 Kaizen events year-to-date underpin continuous-improvement efforts at major plants.  

Outlook: management expects Q4 end-market conditions to resemble Q3—ongoing weakness in consumer, China and certain electronics niches, but strength in Auto OEM and resiliency in U.S. industrial demand.  Assuming normal seasonality, 3M projects full-year adjusted operating margin of roughly **20 %**, versus 19 % at the start of 2023.  The company reiterates its capital-allocation priorities: fund organic growth (R&D, capex, sustainability), maintain a strong dividend (Q3 payout $828 million), preserve balance-sheet flexibility for litigation and the Health-Care spin, and pursue share buybacks opportunistically.
