# Alphabet Inc. Q3 2024 Earnings Call – Financial Summary

Alphabet reported another “great quarter,” with artificial-intelligence advances driving both product momentum and financial out-performance.  Below is an integrated recap of every critical financial and strategic data point discussed on the call.  

Alphabet’s consolidated revenue grew 15 % year-over-year (16 % in constant currency) to **$86.3 billion**.  Operating income rose 34 % to **$28.5 billion**, and operating margin expanded to **32 %**.  Net income likewise increased 34 % to **$26.3 billion**, pushing diluted **EPS up 37 % to $2.12**.  Free cash flow was **$17.6 billion for the quarter** and **$55.8 billion for the trailing twelve months**.  Alphabet closed the quarter with **$93 billion in cash and marketable securities** and returned almost **$18 billion to shareholders** during Q3 ($15.3 billion of buybacks plus $2.5 billion in dividends), bringing total capital returned over the past year to nearly **$70 billion**.

Q3 **CapEx reached $13 billion**, 60 % of which went to servers (TPUs and GPUs) and 40 % to data-center buildings and networking gear; management expects Q4 CapEx to be comparable and signaled a further but smaller dollar increase in 2025 to support AI-related capacity.  A $607 million real-estate optimization charge, along with the full-quarter impact of moving additional AI personnel into Google DeepMind, was recorded in Alphabet-level “Other Activities.”

### Google Services

•  Revenue: **$76.5 billion**, +13 %.  
•  Operating income: **$30.9 billion**, +29 %; operating margin **40 %**.  

Search & Other Ads revenue grew 12 % to **$49.4 billion**, led by financial services (especially insurance) and retail.  **YouTube Ads revenue rose 12 % to $8.9 billion**, with brand spend slightly ahead of direct-response; combined YouTube advertising and subscription revenue exceeded **$50 billion over the past four quarters**.  **Network Ads slipped 2 % to $7.5 billion.**  

The “Subscriptions, Platforms & Devices” line climbed 28 % to **$10.7 billion**, buoyed by YouTube TV, YouTube Music Premium and Google One plus the earlier-than-usual launch of Pixel 9 hardware.  Management cautioned that this Q3 hardware pull-forward will create a headwind to year-on-year comparisons in Q4.

### Google Cloud

•  Revenue: **$11.4 billion**, +35 %.  
•  Operating income: **$1.9 billion**, vs. $266 million a year ago.  
•  Operating margin: **17 %**, aided by scale and cost controls.  

GCP again out-paced Cloud’s headline growth, with AI infrastructure, Vertex GenAI usage and core data-platform demand all accelerating.  Gemini API calls expanded almost **40-fold in six months**, and BigQuery ML operations grew **80 %** over the same span.  Cloud is winning larger deals, deeper product adoption and citing measurable ROI for customers (e.g., LG AI Research cut inference costs 72 %, Hiscox reduced underwriting time from days to minutes, Snap saw 2.5× engagement in “My AI”).

### Other Bets

Revenue was **$388 million**; operating loss **$1.1 billion**.  Waymo now completes **150,000 paid robo-taxi rides per week**, logging more than **1 million fully autonomous miles weekly**, has expanded through Uber partnerships in Austin and Atlanta and signed a multiyear pact with Hyundai.  Wing surpassed the first anniversary of scaled U.S. drone delivery with Walmart.

### Liquidity, Capital Allocation and Debt

No new debt metrics were disclosed; Alphabet remains net-cash-positive with $93 billion in liquid assets.  The company continues to prioritize share repurchases and dividends while funding elevated AI-driven CapEx.

### Product & Commercial Highlights Tied Directly to Revenue

•  **AI Overviews** have now rolled out to 100+ new countries, reaching **over 1 billion monthly users**.  Engagement is high, query depth is increasing and ads shown inside Overviews monetize “at approximately the same rate” as classic search ads, giving Alphabet incremental monetization opportunities on complex or previously low-value informational queries.  

•  **Lens** handles **20 billion visual queries per month**; 25 % show commercial intent.  Shopping ads are now inserted above or alongside Lens results.  

•  **Circle to Search** is live on **150 million** Android devices; one-third of try-outs have become at-least-weekly users.  

•  YouTube Shorts delivers **70 billion daily views**; monetization gaps versus long-form inventory continue to narrow, especially in the U.S., and advertisers can now reserve first-position slots in nearly 40 markets.  

•  Live-room (Connected-TV) viewing keeps rising; creators earning a majority of revenue from TV screens grew over **30 % year-on-year**.  The second season of NFL Sunday Ticket is underway, and upfront commitments rose roughly **20 %**.  

•  Seven Google products with over **2 billion monthly users** now integrate Gemini models.  Internally, **>25 % of new code is AI-generated**, reviewed, and merged by engineers, boosting developer productivity.  

### Forward-Looking Commentary

Management reiterated commitment to “aggressive” AI investment while continuing cost discipline.  Q4 ad-revenue growth faces a tougher comparison, especially in APAC retail, and device revenues will lap the Pixel launch timing shift.  Depreciation expense will rise with 2024–25 data-center builds, though some pressure will be offset as hardware COGS move from Q4 into Q3 results.  The new CFO, Anat Ashkenazi, intends to extend ongoing cost-re-engineering—optimizing head-count, real estate, and technical-infrastructure efficiency—so that elevated AI CapEx can be funded without compromising long-term operating leverage.
