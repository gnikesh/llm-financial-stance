# Meta Platforms, Inc. – Q3 2024 Earnings Call Summary  

Meta reported another quarter of double-digit top-line growth, expanding margins and strong cash generation, while management emphasized accelerating investments in generative AI infrastructure, continued Reality Labs spending and prudent cost control across the rest of the company.  

Meta’s fiscal year is the calendar year; all figures are for the quarter ended 30 September 2024 and are year-over-year (YoY) unless noted otherwise.

---

Meta Platforms, Inc. (Ticker: META)  

Consolidated Financial Highlights  
• Revenue climbed 19 % (20 % constant currency) to $40.6 billion.  
• Operating expenses rose 14 % to $23.2 billion; operating income reached $17.4 billion, yielding a 43 % operating margin (up from 34 % a year ago).  
• Net income surged 35 % to $15.7 billion; diluted EPS was $6.03.  
• Free cash flow was $15.5 billion.  
• Meta ended Q3 with $70.9 billion in cash & marketable securities and $28.8 billion of total debt (including a new $10.5 billion bond issuance).  
• Capital returned: $8.9 billion of share repurchases plus $1.3 billion in dividends during the quarter.

Segment Performance  
Family of Apps (FoA)  
– Revenue: $40.3 billion (+19 %).  
– Advertising revenue: $39.9 billion (+19 %; +20 % c.c.).  
   • Impressions +7 %; average price per ad +11 %.  
   • Strongest vertical contributors: online commerce, healthcare, entertainment/media.  
   • Geography: Rest-of-World +23 %, Europe +21 %, APAC +18 %, North America +16 %.  
– Other FoA revenue (mostly WhatsApp paid messaging) rose 48 % to $434 million.  
– Expenses: $18.5 billion (+13 %); FoA operating income $21.8 billion (54 % margin).

Reality Labs (RL)  
– Revenue: $270 million (+29 %), driven by hardware launches.  
– Expenses: $4.7 billion (+19 %).  
– Operating loss: $4.4 billion. Meta reiterated RL operating losses will “increase meaningfully” in 2024 as the company funds its long-term AR/VR roadmap and the unexpectedly strong demand for smart glasses.

Usage & Product Metrics  
• 3.20 billion people use at least one Meta app daily; 3.96 billion monthly.  
• WhatsApp crossed 2 billion daily calls; U.S. remains one of its fastest-growing markets.  
• Threads reached almost 275 million monthly actives, adding >1 million sign-ups per day; still not expected to be a material 2025 revenue contributor.  
• AI-powered feed and video recommendations lifted time spent 8 % on Facebook and 6 % on Instagram YTD.  
• Meta AI now exceeds 500 million monthly actives; over 1 million advertisers used Meta’s generative-AI ad tools last month, producing 15 million ads and driving ~7 % higher conversions for image-generation adopters.  
• Llama open-source model usage is “growing exponentially”; Llama 4 is in training on a >100,000-H100 GPU cluster, with smaller versions expected early 2025.  
• Hardware: Ray-Ban Meta smart-glasses selling out; new $300 Quest 3 S mixed-reality headset launched; first fully holographic AR “Orion” glasses demoed.

Operating & Cost Structure  
• Headcount stood at ~72,400 (+9 % YoY), with hiring concentrated in monetization, infra, Reality Labs, generative AI, and compliance.  
• Cost of revenue up 19 %, mainly higher infrastructure depreciation; R&D up 21 % (headcount & infra); marketing and sales down 2 %; G&A down 10 % (lower legal costs).

Cash, Capital Allocation & Leverage  
• $15.5 billion FCF comfortably covered $8.9 billion in buybacks and $1.3 billion in dividends.  
• Net cash position remains strong despite the new bond deal; management views external debt as a cost-effective way to fund large-scale AI capex while preserving flexibility.

Guidance & Outlook  
Q4 2024  
• Revenue expected at $45 – $48 billion; FX assumed neutral.  
Full-Year 2024  
• Total expenses trimmed to $96 – $98 billion (from $96 – $99 billion).  
• Reality Labs loss still projected to widen markedly.  
• CapEx raised to $38 – $40 billion (from $37 – $40 billion) due to server deliveries shifting into Q4; infrastructure spend to accelerate significantly again in 2025, implying higher depreciation & opex next year.  
• Effective tax rate for Q4 expected in the low-teens.  
• Management continues to monitor mounting EU/U.S. regulatory risk.

Strategic & Qualitative Commentary  
• Mark Zuckerberg underscored the “inflection point” in AI: Meta is aligning its entire product roadmap—ads, engagement, infrastructure, hardware—around generative and multimodal models.  
• Management argues open-sourcing Llama drives ecosystem standardisation, yielding both quality improvements and cost efficiencies (chip optimisations, shared research).  
• Near-term ROI-driven AI investments focus on ad ranking, creative generation and content recommendations, already lifting conversions 2 – 4 % in early tests while growing watch-time 10 % in Facebook’s new unified video player.  
• Longer-term bets include Meta AI agents (consumer and business), AI-enabled customer service for SMBs, and fully wearable computing (smart glasses, AR/MR headsets).  
• Capital discipline remains a message: incremental hiring and spending must clear either a quantifiable near-term ROI bar or pass a strict long-term strategic filter, while back-office functions continue efficiency initiatives launched in 2023’s “Year of Efficiency.”

Bottom Line  
Meta delivered robust Q3 growth, record profitability, and significant cash returns while aggressively scaling its AI infrastructure and sustaining heavy Reality Labs investment. Management guided to another double-digit revenue quarter in Q4, modestly trimmed 2024 expense expectations, but signalled 2025 will bring “significant” further capex growth to support larger Llama models and AI-driven product expansion.
