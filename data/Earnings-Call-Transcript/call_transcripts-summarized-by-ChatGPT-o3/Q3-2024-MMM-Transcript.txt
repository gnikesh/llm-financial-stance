# 3M Company – Q3 2024 Earnings Call Summary

3M delivered another quarter of solid execution while continuing the multi-year effort to reinvigorate growth, lift margins and optimize the portfolio.  Management raised the low end of 2024 EPS guidance, reiterated its cash-flow outlook and detailed a widening set of operations- and innovation-related initiatives.

### Consolidated Q3 2024 Performance  
• Adjusted (non-GAAP) sales were $6.1 billion; organic growth +1 % year-on-year (+2 % excluding portfolio/geographic pruning).  
• Adjusted operating margin expanded 140 bps to 23 %.  The improvement came from productivity and restructuring (+160 bps) and was partly offset by FX (-20 bps).  
• Adjusted EPS was $1.98, up 18 % ($0.30) versus prior-year, helped by the higher margin, lower tax, net interest benefits and a lower share count.  
• Free cash flow (FCF) reached $1.5 billion; conversion 141 %.  Year-to-date (YTD) FCF is $3.5 billion (102 % conversion).  
• Capital returned: $1.1 billion in Q3 (all dividends plus $0.7 billion of buybacks); $2.7 billion YTD (buybacks $1.1 billion).  

### Full-Year 2024 Guidance (updated)  
• Organic growth: ≈ +1 % (unchanged; Safety & Industrial flat to +LSD, Transportation & Electronics +LSD, Consumer –LSD).  
• Adjusted operating-margin expansion: +250 – 275 bps (prior +225 – 275).  
• Adjusted EPS: $7.20 – $7.30 (raised bottom end by $0.20).  
• Adjusted free-cash-flow conversion: “100 %+”.  

### Segment & Geographic Detail  
| Segment (Adjusted Sales) | Q3 Organic Growth | Qualitative Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| Safety & Industrial – $2.8 B | +0.9 % | Industrial adhesives & tapes strong (electronics bonding); roofing granules up; electrical up; other divisions slightly negative. |
| Transportation & Electronics – $1.9 B | +2 % | Electronics high-single-digit (OEMs building for holidays); Auto & Aero –mid-SD (global builds down); Advanced Materials +HSD; Commercial Branding +LSD. |
| Consumer – $1.3 B | –0.7 % | Includes –2.3 ppt headwind from portfolio exits; Command strips/home improvement +mid-SD; rest of portfolio down on retailer price sensitivity. |

Regionally, organic growth: Asia-Pac +mid-SD (electronics-led), U.S. flat, EMEA –LSD (auto exposure).  China (≈10 % of sales) is up ≈11 % YTD; electronics drove gains (ex-electronics +3 %).

### Balance-Sheet & Capital-Deployment Highlights  
• Ending cash $7.3 B; net-leverage 0.8× EBITDA; strong A3/A- rating maintained.  
• $13 B annual cost-of-goods base targeted for ≥2 % net productivity (~$260 M/year).  
• Small divestitures representing “a few points of revenue” are in early sale processes.  
• 19.9 % stake in Solventum and future portfolio reshaping provide optionality.  
• Insurance recoveries (PFAS & Combat Arms): $54 M in Q3; $175 M YTD; active arbitration/litigation expected to accelerate recoveries.  

### Operations-Excellence Program – Key Metrics & Early Wins  
• On-Time-In-Full (OTIF) to customers reached 89 % (↑5 pps YTD; ↑10 pps vs Q4 22); supplier OTIF improved from low-60 % to low-70 %.  
• Operating-Equipment Efficiency (OEE) now tracked on major assets in 38 largest plants—currently about 50 % utilisation versus 80 %+ “best-in-class”; data will guide consolidation and cap-ex discipline.  
• Forecast-accuracy overhaul: two large divisions in a 15-week pilot using advanced analytics; better demand signals expected to cut inventory (≈100 days currently) and lift service levels.  
• Restructuring outlays on track for $275 M in 2024 (front-loaded); benefits flowing through gross margin.  

### Innovation & Growth Agenda  
• Three priorities reiterated: (1) sustain organic growth by revitalising innovation & commercial excellence; (2) elevate operational performance; (3) deploy capital effectively.  
• R&D spend ≈4.3 – 4.4 % of sales (~$1 B/year); mix shifting toward new-product development:  
  – 100 engineers redeployed from PFAS work; >50 new engineers hired for specialty materials & films (auto, aero, electronics, semis).  
  – SKU setup time cut from ~100 to ~60 days; administrative tasks outsourced/offloaded.  
  – New-product launches to rise ~10 % in 2024 and accelerate a further ~25 % in 2025; nevertheless, management concedes vitality is still well below historic peak levels.  
• Longer-term goal is to push gross margin back into the high-40 % range (currently ≈43 – 44 % ex-healthcare) through volume leverage, 2 % net productivity, mix and positive price-cost.  

### Pricing & Inflation  
• Price contributed roughly half of 1 % organic growth YTD and fully offset material inflation.  Management expects to keep a positive price/cost spread by combining surgical price moves with tighter control of rebates and discounts.  

### 2025 Early Color  
• Headwinds: ≈$0.30 EPS from higher pension expense and lower interest income.  
• Tailwinds: further productivity, lower restructuring charge, volume leverage, buybacks.  
• Full operating-margin expansion expected again in 2025; detailed targets will be presented at an Investor Day slated for late February 2025.  

### Other Notables  
• PFAS manufacturing exit still targeted for mid-2025; related under-absorption costs are being managed.  
• Net labor cost base cited at $8 – $10 B; wage inflation is the main structural cost risk.  
• Return-to-office: employees at large sites now expected on-site Tues–Thurs to enhance collaboration and innovation, with flexibility retained Mon/Fri.  

Management closed by reaffirming confidence in hitting the raised 2024 outlook, emphasizing the “early-innings” nature of the operational transformation and promising more comprehensive medium-term targets at the February Investor Day.
