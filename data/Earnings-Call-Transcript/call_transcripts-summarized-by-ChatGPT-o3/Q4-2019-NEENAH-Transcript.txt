# Neenah, Inc. – Q4 2019 and Full-Year Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah closed 2019 with a markedly stronger financial and operational profile despite soft demand and portfolio changes. Fourth-quarter net sales were $214 million, down year on year because of weaker markets, the late-2018 sale of the Brattleboro fine paper mill, and the mid-2019 repositioning of a major fine-paper distributor. Nevertheless, fourth-quarter adjusted EPS jumped 64 % to $0.87 (GAAP EPS $0.92, +21 %), as operating margins expanded more than 400 basis points and returned to double-digit levels.  

Across the year Neenah generated record free cash flow of $76 million, used $37 million for dividends and modest share repurchases, and reduced total debt by nearly $40 million to just over $200 million. Return on invested capital improved roughly 30 basis points and remains comfortably above the firm’s cost of capital.  

Segment performance in Q4 showed contrasting top-line trends but broadly higher profitability. Technical Products sales fell to $124 million from $134 million, largely because of lower industrial-backing shipments to Asia and a weaker euro that cut sales by about $2 million. A richer product mix (more filtration media) plus better manufacturing efficiencies and lower input costs lifted operating income to $11.3 million from $6.7 million, pushing operating margin up by more than 300 basis points. Fine Paper & Packaging sales declined to $90 million from $106 million, with about one-third of the drop attributable to the Brattleboro divestiture and another third to the distributor transition. Despite lower volume, higher prices and cost actions raised adjusted operating income to $15.6 million versus $11.0 million a year earlier, producing a 17 % operating margin—at the high end of the segment’s mid-teen target range.  

Corporate SG&A in the quarter was $23 million (unallocated corporate cost $4.4 million), reflecting timing of incentive accruals. Interest expense slid to $2.8 million from $3.2 million thanks to lower average debt and rates. The company’s capital structure is dominated by $175 million of senior unsecured notes due May 2021; management intends to refinance these within months, potentially increasing interest expense depending on size and rate.  

Looking into 2020, management guided to:  
• Average quarterly SG&A of ~$25 million and unallocated corporate cost of ~$5 million.  
• A book tax rate near 22 % versus 17 % in 2019 (the prior year benefited from reserve releases).  
• Pension/OPEB expense and cash funding each rising by roughly $1 million.  
• Capital spending of about $30 million, or 2 %–4 % of sales, with less than half earmarked for maintenance.  
• Overall input costs broadly neutral for the year—lower versus 1H 2019, higher in 2H—without a repeat of 2018’s sharp run-up.  
• A moderate currency headwind: every $0.05 drop in the euro reduces annual sales by ~$10 million and EPS by ~$0.10.  

Strategic priorities for 2020 include (1) further safety gains using DuPont Sustainable Solutions tools, (2) accelerating sustainable top-line growth through filtration, digital transfer papers, premium packaging, and a still-active M&A pipeline, and (3) launching a company-wide Neenah Operating System aimed at cost, quality, service, and asset-efficiency improvements. Innovation remains central: the firm is commercialising Texcol (waterless digital-transfer paper), Dispersa (dissolvable food-service labels), recyclable wide-format signage, hemp-based fine-paper grades, and low-emission and water-filtration media, the latter supported by a small cap-efficient upgrade that will nearly double capacity.  

Organisationally, Neenah adopted a functional global structure in December, placing Sales & Marketing and Operations under Chief Operating Officer Julie Schertell. CEO John O’Donnell announced he will retire in May 2020 after a decade at the helm, and the Board has chosen Schertell as his successor, ensuring continuity.  

Management sees limited direct exposure to coronavirus-related disruptions—China represents little direct revenue, though port delays are being monitored—and expects no repetition of 2019’s sharp Asia-backing decline as year-over-year comparisons normalise by mid-2020. The fine-paper distributor change will continue to weigh on the top line during the first half but is expected to support stronger brands and better profitability longer term. Overall, Neenah believes it is entering 2020 with a stronger balance sheet, improving margins, a deeper innovation pipeline, and multiple catalysts—organic and acquisitive—to raise both sales and EPS while maintaining disciplined capital deployment and an attractive, growing dividend.
