# Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) – Q4 2019 & Full-Year 2019 Earnings Call Summary

Schweitzer-Mauduit International closed 2019 with another year of organic EPS and cash-flow growth, continued profit stability in Engineered Papers (EP) and double-digit profit expansion in Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS). Management issued 2020 guidance that implies up to 6 % core-business EPS growth, exclusive of the accretion from the pending Tekra/Trient acquisition.

### Consolidated 2019 performance  
• Net sales were $1.02 billion, down 2 % (flat ex-FX).  
• Adjusted operating profit reached ~$160 million, up 1 % (+4 % ex-FX; +7 % ex-FX and deferred-comp adjustments).  
• Adjusted EPS was $3.55, the high end of the $3.40–$3.60 range, up 2 % year-on-year and up 5 % excluding a $0.09 FX drag.  
• GAAP EPS was $2.76 versus $3.06 in 2018, with the disparity reflecting non-cash purchase-accounting items and other special charges.  
• Free cash flow rose 16 % to $126 million; capex was $34 million (below the $35–$40 million plan; deferred spending will flow into 2020).  
• Net debt was cut by nearly $90 million; year-end leverage stood at 2.1× net debt/adjusted EBITDA (2.5× a year earlier) with the entire $500 million revolver undrawn.

### Segment detail  
Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• 2019 sales $477 million, +2 % (+3 % ex-FX); two-year organic sales CAGR ~4 %.  
• 2019 operating profit nearly $86 million, up 20 %; margin expanded 270 bp to 18 %.  
• Q4 sales $104 million (-4 %) versus a tough +9 % comp in Q4-18; Q4 operating profit still rose to $15.8 million with 90 bp margin expansion.  
• Growth drivers: transportation paint-protection films, optical glazing films, water-filtration media, and medical finger-bandage substrates.  
• Input-cost relief—mainly lower polypropylene—plus efficiency projects lifted profitability; resin prices have stabilised and limited volatility is expected near-term.

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• 2019 sales $546 million, ‑5 % (-2 % ex-FX). Volume fell 10 % but price/mix added 8 % as lower-margin printing & packaging grades were exited and higher-value LIP/binder/wrapper grades expanded.  
• Segment margin climbed 130 bp to 22.6 %; operating profit held in the low-$120 million range for a third consecutive year (would have been up 4 % ex-FX).  
• Q4 sales $135 million (-4 %; ‑2 % ex-FX); price/mix +11 % offset a 12 % volume drop. Q4 operating profit jumped 15 % with >400 bp margin improvement thanks to favourable mix and lower pulp costs.  
• Management expects another year of profit stability in 2020, aided by a more favourable pulp-price environment, incremental share gains in premium cigarette papers, heat-not-burn launches, and experimental hemp/coco fibre products.

Unallocated & corporate items  
• Unallocated expense rose to $49.5 million (+$13 million) due to higher deferred‐comp liabilities, IT modernisation, and M&A work. Ex-deferred-comp, 2019 unallocated was ~$47 million.  
• 2020 unallocated expense is guided down to roughly $45 million.  
• Adjusted EPS tax rate ended 2019 at 19.3 % (vs. 18.1 %); Q4 rate was 15.8 % versus 4.9 % a year ago, creating a $0.09 EPS headwind. 2020 tax rate is projected to rise 1–2 pp to ~20–21 %.

### Q4 2019 highlights  
• Adjusted EPS was $0.80 (vs. $0.87) as segment profit growth was offset by the higher tax rate and unallocated costs.  
• Consolidated Q4 sales declined 4 % (-3 % ex-FX); adjusted operating profit slipped 2 % to $31.5 million but would have been flat ex-FX.  
• Combined AMS + EP operating profit actually grew $4.6 million (+11 %) in the quarter.

### 2020 guidance (base business only; excludes Tekra/Trient)  
• Adjusted EPS: $3.50–$3.75 (flat to +6 %). GAAP EPS: $2.74–$2.99.  
• Segment outlook: continued AMS profit growth; fourth consecutive year of EP profit stability.  
• Capex: $40–$45 million; free cash flow expected to exceed $100 million.  
• JV income: ~$2 million, back-half weighted.  
• Interest expense assumes a 5 % blended rate.  
• Seasonal EPS cadence expected to mirror 2019 (Q2 & Q3 strongest).  

### Tekra & Trient acquisition (announced 17 Feb 2020)  
• Two technical-film converting businesses to be acquired for $155 million in cash; closing targeted late Q1 2020.  
• Combined annual sales ≈ $100 million with ~16 % EBITDA margin.  
• Funded entirely with revolver; pro-forma net leverage expected at ~2.7×, still comfortably below the 4.5× covenant ceiling.  
• Accretion: ~$0.10 to 2020 adjusted EPS (three quarters of ownership and ~$2 million one-time costs); annualised run-rate accretion about $0.20 with incremental upside from revenue synergies.  
• Strategic fit: broad slitting, sheeting, laminating, die-cutting and packaging capabilities that complement SWM’s extrusion know-how, deepen presence in medical diagnostics, digital graphics and transportation, and open cross-selling avenues.

### Other discussion points  
• Coronavirus: Chinese operations have resumed; Q1 output is not yet at full capacity but management expects any modest first-quarter AMS sales softness to be recovered later in the year. EP exposure to China is minimal.  
• Share repurchase: no current authorisation, but the Board will re-evaluate capital-allocation priorities once leverage trends lower post-acquisition; M&A remains the primary use of capital.  
• Dividend policy remains intact.  

### Bottom line  
SWM delivered 2019 results at the top end of guidance, posted its second consecutive year of organic EPS and free-cash-flow growth, and maintained sub-2.5× leverage. 2020 guidance calls for another year of EPS growth and >$100 million of free cash flow before factoring in the Tekra/Trient acquisition, which should lift earnings further while keeping the balance sheet well within targeted leverage parameters.
