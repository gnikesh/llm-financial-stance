# Facebook, Inc. – Q4 and Full-Year 2020 Earnings Call Summary  

Facebook (Nasdaq: FB) reported solid year-end results, highlighted by accelerating revenue growth, record profitability, expanding cash balances and continued investment in long-term strategic themes: communities, private messaging, commerce and immersive computing (AR/VR). Management also detailed looming ad-targeting headwinds from Apple’s iOS 14 changes and tougher revenue comparisons in 2H 2021.

## Key Consolidated Financial Metrics  

• Q4 revenue was **$28.07 billion**, up **33 % YoY** (32 % constant currency).  
 – Advertising revenue: **$27.19 billion**, +31 %.  
 – “Other” revenue (driven by Oculus Quest 2 hardware): **$885 million**, +156 %.  

• Q4 operating income: **$12.76 billion**; operating margin **46 %** (versus 42 % a year ago).  

• Q4 net income: **$11.22 billion**, delivering **EPS of $3.88** (diluted).  

• FY 2020 revenue: **$85.97 billion**, up **22 %**; full-year operating margin **38 %**.  

• Free cash flow in Q4: **$9.22 billion**; year-end cash & marketable securities: **$62 billion**; the company carries **no material debt**.  

• Shareholder returns: 2020 repurchases totaled **$6.3 billion**; Board authorized an additional **$25 billion** buy-back.  

## Users & Engagement  

• Family Daily Active People (DAP): **2.6 billion**; Family Monthly Active People (MAP): **3.3 billion**.  

• Facebook DAUs: **1.84 billion** (+11 % YoY); DAU/MAU ratio held at **66 %**.  
 – U.S./Canada DAUs fell **1 million** sequentially as usage normalized post-lockdown.  

• Facebook MAUs: **2.80 billion** (+12 % YoY).  

• Platform utilisation: >**200 million** businesses use free tools; >**175 million** people message a WhatsApp business account daily; >**600 million** users belong to a “meaningful” Facebook Group.  

## Advertising Dynamics  

• Q4 ad impressions grew **25 %** YoY; average price per ad rose **5 %** (first positive pricing comp in several quarters).  

• Regional ad-revenue growth: Europe +35 %, U.S./Canada +31 %, APAC +29 %, Rest of World +25 % (currency headwinds persist).  

• Direct-response performance improved notably in Instagram Stories; Facebook mobile feed remains the main driver of pricing gains.  

## 2021 Outlook & Guidance  

• Revenue: Expect YoY growth rates to remain “stable or modestly accelerate” in Q1–Q2 21 (lapping COVID-depressed 1H 2020). Growth will “significantly decelerate” in 2H 2021 as comps toughen and ad-targeting headwinds mount.  

• iOS 14 / privacy: Management anticipates “high opt-out rates” when Apple’s ATT prompt arrives (likely late Q1) and calls it a **significant headwind**, especially for SMB advertisers and for Audience Network on iOS.  

• Expense guidance unchanged: **$68–73 billion** in 2021 (versus $53 billion in 2020), driven by talent and infrastructure.  

• Capital expenditures forecast **$21–23 billion** (data centres, servers, network, offices; includes 2020 projects delayed by the pandemic).  

• Effective tax rate projected in the “high-teens %” for 2021.  

## Strategic & Product Highlights  

1. Communities  
   • More tools will help admins run self-sustaining groups (fund-raising, merchandise, memberships).  
   • Civic & political groups removed from recommendations in the U.S.; policy now extended globally. Facebook is also testing reductions of political content in News Feed.  

2. Private Messaging & Privacy  
   • Continued push toward end-to-end encryption, message ephemerality and cross-app interoperability (Messenger, Instagram Direct, WhatsApp).  
   • Zuckerberg positioned **Apple** as a primary competitor, citing iMessage’s platform advantages and Apple’s forthcoming privacy changes that hamper targeted ads.  

3. Commerce for SMEs  
   • Facebook Shops can now be created once and surfaced across Facebook and Instagram; Checkout expanded to all U.S. merchants. Integration with WhatsApp and Messenger is underway.  
   • Business Suite, Paid Online Events and shopping functionality in Instagram Reels/Stories aim to streamline digital storefront management.  

4. Next Computing Platform (AR/VR)  
   • **Oculus Quest 2** “on track to be the first mainstream VR headset”; strong holiday demand lifted other revenue. >**60** developers are already generating multi-million-dollar revenue.  
   • First Ray-Ban branded smart glasses, co-developed with Luxottica, expected in 2021; full AR glasses “still years away.”  
   • Portal devices saw elevated usage for remote family gatherings; expanding into enterprise collaboration in 2021.  

## Operating Metrics & Workforce  

• Headcount: **58,604** full-time employees at year-end, up **30 %** YoY (+13,600 net hires, meeting the goal of 10,000 new tech/product roles).  

## Regulatory & Policy Commentary  

• Facebook continues to advocate updated, clear internet rules: broader reforms to Section 230, harmonised privacy legislation and explicit election-integrity standards.  

• FTC & state antitrust suits: management offered no substantive comment but reiterated belief that its acquisitions were lawful and beneficial to consumers and businesses.  

• Trans-Atlantic data-transfer uncertainty (Schrems II) remains a risk the company is “closely monitoring.”  

## Capital Allocation & Liquidity  

• With **$62 billion** in cash/marketables, robust free cash flow and no significant debt, Facebook plans to continue heavy capex, R&D funding and aggressive share repurchases while retaining balance-sheet flexibility.  

---

Facebook finished 2020 with record revenue and earnings momentum, buoyed by a pandemic-driven surge in e-commerce advertising and the successful launch of Quest 2. Management cautions that iOS privacy changes and post-pandemic spending mix shifts will pressure growth in late 2021, yet believes ongoing investment in messaging privacy, SME commerce tools and AR/VR platforms will sustain long-term value creation.
