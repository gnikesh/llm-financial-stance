# 3M Q4 2020 Earnings Call – Comprehensive Financial Summary

3M ended a tumultuous 2020 with a notably strong fourth quarter, combining resilient top-line growth, widening margins, robust cash generation and rapid deleveraging while laying out measured but confident targets for 2021.

Fourth-Quarter 2020 Performance  
•  Sales reached $8.58 billion, up 5.8 % year on year; organic local-currency growth was 6 %.  
•  Adjusted EPS was $2.38 (up 22 %), beating consensus by $0.21.  
•  Adjusted operating margin expanded 250 bp to 21.5 %; adjusted EBITDA margin exceeded 27 %.  
•  Volume leverage, disciplined cost control and higher selling prices more than offset headwinds from an incremental $107 million respirator warranty reserve; gains from a small property sale added roughly $0.09 per share (60 bp of margin).  
•  All four business groups grew organically and posted segment EBITDA margins above 26 %:

  – Safety & Industrial: organic +11.4 % (≈10 ppt from pandemic respirator demand); operating margin 27.7 % (+690 bp).  
  – Transportation & Electronics: organic +1.4 %; Auto OEM +18 % versus a 3 % industry build increase; margin 21.8 % (+100 bp).  
  – Health Care: organic +6.6 %; strength in medical respirators and biopharma filtration offset procedure-related softness; margin 24.7 % (+340 bp, 31.7 % EBITDA).  
  – Consumer: organic +10 % on sustained home-improvement demand and e-commerce; margin 23.5 % (flat despite stepped-up advertising).

Cash Flow, Balance Sheet & Capital Allocation  
•  Q4 adjusted free cash flow was $2.1 billion, up 16 %, with conversion of 151 %.  
•  Full-year adjusted FCF climbed 18 % to $6.7 billion (132 % conversion).  
•  Net debt was cut by $1.3 billion sequentially and by $4.1 billion for the year; net-debt-to-EBITDA ended at 1.5× (vs. 2.3× in 2019).  
•  3M retired $1 billion of 2021 notes early, paid $848 million in Q4 dividends ($3.4 billion for 2020) and suspended buybacks for the year; repurchases will resume in 2021.  
•  Year-end cash and marketable securities stood at $5.1 billion.

Full-Year 2020 Highlights  
•  Organic sales fell a modest 2 % despite the pandemic; adjusted EPS came in at $8.74.  
•  Adjusted EBITDA margin improved 100 bp to just over 27 %.  
•  ROIC was a healthy 18 %.  
•  The company integrated the Acelity wound-care acquisition, divested Drug Delivery, and increased its annual dividend for the 62nd consecutive year.

Operational & Strategic Actions  
•  COVID Response: 2 billion respirators supplied in 2020 (about half to the U.S.); current capacity runs at 2.5 billion annually, quadruple the 2019 level.  
•  December restructuring (annual savings $250–300 million) will deliver $75–100 million of 2021 benefit; the remaining $110–160 million pre-tax charge will be recognized in 2H21.  
•  2020 R&D plus capex totaled $3.4 billion; 2021 capex budget is $1.8–2.0 billion, including $100 million for water-quality and sustainability projects.  
•  Digital transformation, supply-chain analytics and further operating-model streamlining remain central productivity levers.  
•  PFAS: management reiterated proactive remediation, transparency and cooperation with regulators; no new financial disclosures or milestones were announced.

2021 Outlook & Financial Guidance  
•  Total sales growth of 5–8 % (organic +3–6 %); foreign exchange expected to add ≈2 ppt.  
•  Adjusted EPS of $9.20–9.70, up 5–11 % year on year.  
•  Free-cash-flow conversion targeted at 95–105 %.  
•  Segment organic growth assumptions: Safety & Industrial mid-single digits; Transportation & Electronics low-to-high single digits (dependent on auto builds and semiconductor supply); Health Care and Consumer low-to-mid single digits.  
•  Headwinds to EPS (≈$0.35–0.45) include the non-recurring 2020 real-estate gain, normalization of travel and incentive compensation, and higher advertising; these are largely balanced by restructuring savings ($0.20–0.30) and operating leverage.  
•  Share repurchases will restart but pacing will depend on economic conditions; dividends remain a top capital-allocation priority.

Management Priorities for 2021  
The company will continue to invest in high-growth platforms—personal safety, home improvement, healthcare, data-center and electric-vehicle solutions—while advancing sustainability, reducing debt and preserving margin discipline. CEO Mike Roman expressed confidence that 3M’s strengthened balance sheet, expanded respirator capacity, accelerating digital initiatives and ongoing portfolio management position the firm for “healthy growth, margin expansion and strong cash flow” through the economic recovery and beyond.
