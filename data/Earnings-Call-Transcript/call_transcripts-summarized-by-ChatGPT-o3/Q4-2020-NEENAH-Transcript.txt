# Neenah, Inc. – Q4 2020 Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah closed an unprecedented 2020 with a fourth-quarter performance that matched prior-year earnings, generated sequential improvements across both operating segments and preserved a very solid balance-sheet position.  

Adjusted earnings were $0.87 per diluted share, identical to Q4 2019, on adjusted operating income of $21 million, also flat year-on-year.  Relative to Q3 2020, consolidated sales rose 8 percent, operating income climbed more than 30 percent and adjusted EPS expanded almost 60 percent, reflecting continued post-COVID demand recovery.  GAAP results exclude $6 million ($0.28 per share) of mostly non-cash items, compared with a $1.2 million ($0.05) net gain a year earlier.

Technical Products – now roughly 65 percent of company revenue – was the clear earnings driver.  Segment sales reached $137 million, up 11 percent from the prior year, helped by a $5 million currency tail-wind and strong volume.  Filtration revenues hit a record $66 million, rising nearly 30 percent as transportation media grew sharply in Europe and the U.S. and industrial filters advanced more than 20 percent.  About $4 million of revenue came from newly-developed face-mask media.  Industrial Solutions backings grew almost 20 percent, led by higher tape-related demand.  Segment adjusted operating income jumped to $18 million from $10 million, lifting the margin from 8 percent to 13 percent on higher volume, manufacturing efficiency, favorable mix, reduced SG&A and currency gains.

Fine Paper & Packaging posted net sales of $70 million, higher than Q3 but below Q4 2019 because of ongoing COVID headwinds in commercial print.  Consumer products were hurt by back-to-school timing, while premium packaging grew on strength in labels and folding board.  Adjusted operating profit was just under $8 million, up 15 percent sequentially but lower year-over-year on reduced volume and mix.  Management still expects this business to recover to roughly 90 percent of its pre-COVID quarterly run-rate (about $80 million) during 2021; roughly half of segment revenue is now in consumer and premium categories with better secular growth prospects.

Corporate SG&A expense was $21.5 million, nearly $2 million below last year thanks to tight cost controls; as travel and other activities normalize, quarterly SG&A is expected to average about $25 million in 2021, including roughly $5.5 million of unallocated corporate cost.  Interest expense was $3.1 million versus $2.8 million, reflecting higher non-cash amortization following the refinancing of 2021 senior notes with a more flexible Term Loan B maturing in 2027.  The Q4 effective tax rate was 15 percent (19 percent last year), aided by CARES Act provisions; the normal run-rate is projected around 22 percent.

Cash flow and liquidity remain strong.  Neenah generated $13 million of operating cash in Q4 and $75 million of free cash flow for the year, one of its best years ever after aggressive working-capital reduction and curtailed capex.  The company ended December with $37 million of cash, an undrawn revolver and total liquidity exceeding $175 million.  Capital expenditures were $7 million this quarter and $19 million for 2020; management plans to resume a more normal $35 million spend in 2021, including coating capacity to support release-liner growth.  Net debt metrics were not explicitly quoted, but leverage is characterized as conservative and the balance sheet “remains in excellent shape.”

Cost inflation is the main 2021 headwind.  Management foresees more than $20 million of higher input costs (fibers, chemicals, freight), with most fiber increases hitting in Q2 because of contractual lags.  The company has already announced price increases in Fine Paper & Packaging and certain Industrial categories, is negotiating annual filtration contracts, and expects that pricing actions, productivity gains and the Neenah Operating System (targeting >$20 million of annual savings when fully rolled out) will offset those cost pressures over the full year, though timing mismatches could affect interim quarters.  A weaker U.S. dollar is a tail-wind: every €0.05 move above the 2020 average adds roughly $10 million of sales, under $2 million of operating income and $0.10 of EPS; the euro is currently more than €0.05 stronger.

Guidance is for “modest sequential quarterly gains” through 2021 rather than a straight extrapolation of Q4 results, owing to normal seasonality, planned maintenance shutdowns in the second half and raw-material timing.  Management continues to target mid-teen operating margins in both segments as volumes recover and margin-accretive new products scale.

Strategic initiatives achieved in 2020 include:  
• implementing strict COVID safety protocols with no major operational disruptions;  
• launching the Neenah Operating System at two large mills to improve safety, quality, delivery and cost while freeing capacity;  
• commercializing high-performance face-mask media;  
• issuing a sustainability report showing five-year reductions in carbon footprint and enhanced diversity and governance;  
• refinancing near-term debt;  
• reinvigorating innovation (new head of global innovation hired) and introducing multiple new products in filtration, dissolvable labels, digital transfers, planners, journals and plastic-alternative packaging;  
• strengthening leadership and updating the corporate vision focused on four growth platforms – filtration, specialty coatings, custom engineered materials and premium packaging.

M&A remains an active priority; the company maintains a robust pipeline in the four focus areas and believes its balance-sheet flexibility positions it to act quickly on attractive, accretive opportunities.  Beginning in 2021, the sub-$30 million, mid-single-digit-margin Publishing business will be managed within Fine Paper & Packaging to capture SG&A synergies.

Management reiterated its commitment to disciplined capital deployment and to returning cash to shareholders via an “attractive” dividend while pursuing organic growth, margin expansion and strategic acquisitions.
