# Schweitzer-Mauduit International – Q4 and Full-Year 2020 Financial Summary  

Schweitzer-Mauduit International (SWM) closed 2020 with results that management repeatedly described as “resilient,” delivering year-over-year growth in earnings, cash flow and liquidity despite COVID-19 disruptions.  The company also announced a pending acquisition of Scapa Group that will materially alter its scale and leverage profile in 2021.  All figures below relate to continuing operations unless noted otherwise.

For the full year SWM grew consolidated sales 5 % and lifted both adjusted operating profit and adjusted EBITDA 8 %.  Fourth-quarter sales rose 17 %, adjusted operating profit 14 % and adjusted EBITDA 15 %.

EPS  
• GAAP EPS declined to $2.66 from $2.76 in 2019, reflecting restructuring charges (largely the Spotswood, NJ paper-mill closure) and prior-year Brazil tax items.  
• Adjusted EPS increased 4 % to $3.68, the company’s third consecutive year of adjusted EPS growth and squarely within the guidance range withdrawn at the pandemic’s outset.  
• In Q4, GAAP EPS fell to $0.48 (vs $0.64) on restructuring costs; adjusted EPS slipped to $0.77 (vs $0.80) as higher tax rates offset operating strength.  
• The 2020 adjusted tax rate rose to 21.0 % from 19.3 %; Q4’s rate was 21.7 % versus 15.8 % a year earlier.

Cash flow, capex and leverage  
• Operating cash flow reached $162 million, up year on year; free cash flow increased to $128 million despite a pandemic-related pause in some customer receivables early in the year.  
• Capital expenditures were $33 million, below plan; several projects were deferred into 2021 but none are expected to impair 2021 free-cash-flow generation materially.  
• Year-end net-debt-to-adjusted-EBITDA was 2.3×.  Management reminded investors of its post-acquisition deleveraging record (3.2× after Conwed in 2017, 2.1× by YE-2019, 2.7× after the Tekra deal in Q1-20, now back to 2.3×).  
• Cash plus revolver availability totaled roughly $500 million on 31 Dec 2020.  
• To finance the Scapa purchase the company is arranging a Term Loan B; leverage at closing is expected between 4.0× and 4.5× EBITDA, after which SWM intends to resume delevering.

Segment performance  
Advanced Materials & Structures (AMS)  
• Reported 2020 sales rose 14 % to just over $500 million, its first half-billion-dollar year.  The Tekra acquisition (closed late Q1) added $77 million.  
• Organic sales slipped only 2 % for the year, demonstrating portfolio resilience; excluding the COVID-hit transportation sub-segment, organic sales would have grown 1 %.  
• Fourth-quarter organic growth rebounded to 21 %, led by a 70 % surge in transportation films plus double-digit gains in filtration.  
• Adjusted operating profit increased 6 % for the year; margin contracted 120 bps due to the mid-year volume dip but expanded 230 bps in Q4 on mix and volume recovery.  

Engineered Papers (EP)  
• Sales fell 3 % (2 % ex-FX) on a 3 % volume decline consistent with industry attrition and a deliberate exit from lower-margin business.  
• Positive price/mix—driven by growth in heat-not-burn wrapper, LIP, Recon and other high-value grades—pushed adjusted operating profit up 8 % (roughly +$10 million) to the low/mid-$120 million range, well above 2017-2019 levels; segment margin expanded 250 bps.  
• Management cautions that 2020 EP profit is “difficult to repeat,” expecting reversion toward the multi-year trend in 2021 as pandemic-related inventory builds normalize.

Corporate and other items  
• Unallocated expenses rose $3 million for the year (and $4 million in Q4) due mainly to consulting and acquisition-diligence costs; these are not expected to repeat in 2021.  
• The Spotswood (NJ) paper-mill closure was completed in Q4 with production successfully transferred to other sites; related restructuring charges depressed GAAP results but will provide future cost savings.  

Outlook (base business, excluding Scapa)  
Because UK Takeover Panel rules limit forward-looking disclosures while the Scapa offer is pending, management issued only directional guidance:  
1. SWM expects another year of adjusted EPS growth on a standalone basis.  
2. EP profit should drift back toward its pre-2020 run-rate, but this will be more than offset by strong organic growth and higher profitability within AMS.  
3. Unallocated expenses should fall from 2020’s elevated level.  
4. Free cash flow is projected to remain robust even with deferred capex.

Strategic developments – Scapa acquisition  
• On 27 Jan 2021 SWM announced an agreement to acquire UK-listed Scapa Group, an innovation-led healthcare and industrial materials manufacturer.  
• Scapa will add roughly $400 million in annual revenue, lifting combined SWM sales toward $1.5 billion and AMS segment sales toward $1 billion.  
• The deal broadens SWM’s medical platform to approximately $250 million in sales, deepens exposure to transportation, construction and other growth verticals, and brings significant technical and commercial talent.  Closing is targeted for Q2-2021, after which SWM will update full-year guidance including Scapa’s contribution.

Management’s key messages  
SWM believes 2020 validated its balanced portfolio strategy, with AMS and EP each posting profit growth and generating substantial cash despite the pandemic.  Management remains “exceptionally bullish” on transportation films, optimistic on filtration and medical, and confident in its ability to integrate Scapa while continuing its proven pattern of post-deal deleveraging and cash generation.
