# Meta Platforms, Inc. – Q4 & FY 2021 Earnings Call Summary  

Meta closed 2021 with record revenue but enters 2022 facing material headwinds from Apple’s privacy changes, a mix-shift toward lower-monetizing short-form video, and rising competition from TikTok. Management reiterated seven major investment priorities—Reels, community messaging, commerce, ads, privacy, AI and the metaverse—and provided a cautious near-term outlook while stressing confidence in long-term monetization.

### Consolidated Financials  
• Q4 2021 revenue was $33.67 billion, up 20 percent year over year (YoY); constant-currency growth was 21 percent (FX shaved ~$307 million).  
• Full-year 2021 revenue reached $117.93 billion, up 37 percent YoY, marking the first year above the $100 billion threshold.  
• Q4 operating income was $12.59 billion, yielding a 37 percent operating margin.  
• GAAP net income for the quarter was $10.29 billion; diluted EPS came in at $3.67.  
• Total Q4 expenses rose 38 percent YoY to $21.09 billion, driven by Reality Labs (RL) hardware, infrastructure, hiring and a 107 percent surge in G&A tied mainly to legal costs.  
• Free cash flow was $12.6 billion; the company ended the quarter with $48 billion in cash/marketable securities after repurchasing $19.2 billion of Class A shares.  
• Headcount grew 23 percent YoY to 71,970, with 3,700 net adds in Q4, mostly technical talent.

### Segment Reporting (new in Q4)  
Family of Apps (FoA)  
• Q4 FoA revenue: $32.79 billion (+20 percent).  
 – Advertising revenue: $32.64 billion (+20 percent; +21 percent cc).  
  • Ad impressions +13 percent; average price per ad +6 percent.  
  • Asia-Pac led growth (+31 percent); Rest of World +28 percent; Europe +20 percent; North America +15 percent.  
 – Other FoA revenue (payments & other): $155 million (-8 percent).  
• FoA expenses: $16.9 billion (+35 percent).  
• FoA operating income: $15.94 billion; operating margin 48 percent.

Reality Labs (RL)  
• Q4 RL revenue: $877 million (+22 percent) on strong Quest 2 holiday sales; cumulative Quest Store spend has topped $1 billion.  
• RL expenses: $4.24 billion (+48 percent).  
• RL operating loss: $3.30 billion; full-year RL operating loss was $10.2 billion, and management expects the loss to “increase meaningfully” in 2022.

### Users & Engagement  
• Family Daily Active People (DAP): ~2.8 billion in December; Family Monthly Active People (MAP): ~3.6 billion.  
• Facebook DAUs: 1.93 billion (+5 percent YoY; +84 million); DAU/MAU ratio steady at 66 percent.  
• Facebook MAUs: 2.91 billion (+115 million, +4 percent).  
• Management cited COVID pull-forward, higher data pricing in India, and intensified competition (especially among younger users) as limiting Facebook user growth in Asia-Pac and Rest of World.

### Key Business Themes  
Competition & Product Mix  
• TikTok’s rapid growth is putting pressure on Meta’s engagement and impression growth.  
• Reels is now the fastest-growing format across Instagram and Facebook and the largest contributor to Instagram engagement growth. Reels monetizes at a lower rate than Feed or Stories, creating a near-term revenue headwind that the company views as a deliberate trade-off to secure long-term gains.

Apple iOS / Measurement Headwinds  
• Apple’s ATT and other privacy moves are expected to reduce Meta’s 2022 revenue by roughly $10 billion.  
• Two challenges: less accurate targeting (raising the cost of driving outcomes) and harder measurement/attribution (under-reporting and delayed conversion data).  
• Meta is closing the under-reporting gap for larger advertisers via Aggregated Event Measurement and SKAdNetwork tools but notes the impact remains more acute for small businesses. Additional headwinds from iOS 15 and European regulation are expected in 2022.

Macroeconomic Factors  
Supply-chain disruptions, labor shortages, inflation and advertisers’ earlier holiday spend also weighed on Q4 ad demand; many marketers front-loaded budgets to avoid shipping delays.

### 2022 Guidance & Capital Allocation  
Revenue  
• Q1 2022 revenue outlook: $27 – $29 billion, representing 3 – 11 percent YoY growth.  
 – Impression growth will be constrained by competition and greater Reels consumption (fewer ads per time spent).  
 – Pricing growth will be hurt by the full-year impact of ATT, tougher comps, advertiser budget pressure from macro issues, and an expected FX headwind.

Expenses & Capex  
• 2022 total expense outlook trimmed slightly to $90 – $95 billion (prior $91 – $97 billion), driven mainly by accelerated hiring in technical/product roles and infrastructure.  
• 2022 capex remains $29 – $34 billion, heavily weighted to data centers, servers, and AI/ML capacity for the FoA business; metaverse infrastructure needs are minimal near term.

Tax & Share Listing  
• 2022 effective tax rate expected to resemble 2021 (19 percent).  
• Class A ticker symbol will change from “FB” to “META” on NASDAQ in 1H 2022.

### Strategic & Product Priorities for 2022  
1. Reels – simplify video surfaces, improve creator/money-making tools, expand global distribution.  
2. Community Messaging – bolster group features in WhatsApp, Messenger and Facebook; click-to-message ads already connect >1 billion users with businesses weekly.  
3. Commerce – enhance Shops, Checkout, Live Shopping, ratings/reviews and product discovery; enable seamless in-app purchasing.  
4. Ads – rebuild infrastructure for privacy-centric personalized ads.  
5. Privacy – deepen privacy engineering, end-to-end encryption, and large-scale infrastructure to embed privacy by design.  
6. AI – scale compute (AI Research SuperCluster to be world’s fastest), improve content ranking, safety, ads relevance and commerce.  
7. Metaverse – continue hardware traction (high-end VR headset due in 2022; Project Nazare AR glasses under development) and scale Horizon Worlds to VR, mobile and 2D apps; avatars SDK now open to Unity developers, with digital goods such as NFL apparel launching.

### Cash Returns & Balance Sheet  
• Share repurchases: $19.2 billion in Q4; $48 billion cash on hand provides ample capacity for ongoing buybacks and investment.  
• No debt metrics disclosed; Meta remains debt-free at the operating level.

### Management Perspective  
Mark Zuckerberg framed 2022 as “the first page of the next chapter” focused on execution after rebranding to Meta. Leadership expects near-term revenue pressure but is “confident these are the right investments,” drawing parallels to prior transitions from desktop to mobile feed and from feed to Stories, both of which ultimately improved engagement and monetization.

### Takeaways  
Meta delivered solid 2021 growth and strong cash flows but guided to a material deceleration as it navigates platform privacy changes, macro headwinds and strategic shifts toward Reels and the metaverse. Expense growth and higher Reality Labs losses will compress margins in 2022, yet management emphasizes long-run value creation through aggressive investment in video, messaging, AI and immersive platforms.
