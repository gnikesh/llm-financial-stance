# 3M Company – Q4 2021 Earnings Call Summary

3M finished 2021 with better-than-expected fourth-quarter results, navigating raw-material inflation, logistics bottlenecks and COVID-19 volatility while continuing to invest in growth, productivity and sustainability.

Fourth-Quarter 2021 Highlights  
•  Sales were $8.61 billion, up 0.3 % year-on-year (up 1.3 % organically) and $31.6 million ahead of consensus.  
•  Adjusted EPS of $2.31 beat expectations by $0.29; tax-rate benefits (17.8 % for the quarter) added $0.12 / share.  
•  Operating income was $1.6 billion; operating margin fell 240 bp to 18.8 %, hurt by higher input costs and factory inefficiencies partially offset by price and cost controls.  
•  Price realization accelerated to +2.6 % (vs. +1.4 % in Q3 and +0.1 % in Q2).  Higher prices offset inflation in dollars (+$0.03 to EPS) but were still a 20 bp margin headwind.  
•  Free cash flow was $1.5 billion (-30 % Y/Y) with 110 % conversion; capex climbed to $556 million.  
•  Q4 share buybacks were $938 million; dividends paid were $848 million.  
•  Net debt slipped $1.2 billion versus year-end 2020; year-end net-debt/EBITDA is 1.4× with $4.8 billion of cash.  
•  A $1.5 billion bond redemption cut net interest expense, boosting EPS by $0.04.  

Segment Performance – Q4 Organic Growth (Δ Y/Y) and Margins  
•  Safety & Industrial: –1.3 % org.; N95 respirator revenue fell ~$110 million (≈ –4 ppt impact).  Operating margin 17.7 % (-440 bp) on lower volume, legal costs and inflation despite price and restructuring savings.  
•  Transportation & Electronics: slight organic decline; Auto OEM –mid-teens (global builds –13 %).  Operating margin 17.6 % (-270 bp) on inflation and lower leverage.  
•  Health Care: +1.6 % org.; respirators dragged almost 4 ppt.  Operating margin 23.6 % (-50 bp).  Elective procedures ~90 % of pre-COVID.  
•  Consumer: +4.9 % org. on continued strength in Command, Filtrete, Scotch and Post-it.  Operating margin 21.4 % (-100 bp) as inflation offset volume leverage.  

Full-Year 2021 Metrics  
•  Organic sales up 9 % across all segments.  
•  Adjusted operating margin 20.8 % (-50 bp), reflecting an ~80 bp raw-material/logistics drag.  
•  Adjusted EPS grew 14 %.  
•  Free cash flow $6 billion; conversion 101 %.  
•  Capex $1.6 billion; combined R&D + capex investment $3.6 billion.  
•  Returned $5.6 billion to shareholders (dividends + buybacks); 63rd consecutive annual dividend increase.  
•  Respirator sales totaled $1.5 billion (vs. $1.4 billion 2020, $0.6 billion 2019); 2.3 billion respirators shipped in 2021.  

Balance-Sheet & Capital-Allocation Notes  
Management’s priorities remain: 1) organic investment, 2) dividend, 3) bolt-on M&A / portfolio moves, 4) opportunistic buybacks.  A $0.5 billion expansion of the Clinton, Tennessee plant (Command & Filtrete, 600 jobs) was announced; the $1 billion, multi-year sustainability capex programme (water, carbon, PFAS) is under way.

Portfolio Actions & Legal Matters  
•  Food Safety business to be merged with Neogen; close expected by end-Q3 2022.  
•  Dual-credit segment reporting will be eliminated and volume/price disaggregation will cease beginning Q1 2022.  
•  PFAS: treatment systems to cut discharges 90 % are being installed (Cordova, IL completion end-2022; Zwijndrecht, Belgium already online).  Permit changes in Belgium could materially disrupt production; discussions with authorities continue.  
•  Combat-arms earplug litigation: 10 bellwether trials held (5 favourable to 3M); six more slated for 2022.  

2022 Qualitative Outlook (quantitative guidance due 14 Feb)  
•  Price increases should remain a tailwind; inflation expected to be toughest in H1 then ease.  
•  COVID-related N95 demand is assumed to decline as 2022 progresses, but 3M can flex capacity.  
•  Semiconductor constraints, logistics congestion and labour shortages likely persist into mid-year; management is pushing operating-rigor initiatives and expects another ~$70 million of restructuring carry-over benefit.  
•  3M plans continued elevated spending on growth, productivity, digital transformation and ESG.  Net debt/EBITDA targeted to stay low, preserving flexibility for strategic moves and shareholder returns.
