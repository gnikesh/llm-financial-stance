# Neenah, Inc. ‒ Q4 2021 Earnings Call Summary  

Neenah closed 2021 with record revenue momentum but continued margin pressure from unprecedented inflation and supply–chain disruptions. Management detailed decisive pricing, cost-containment and strategic investment measures designed to restore margins and accelerate long-term growth.

Neenah’s financial year is now reported in two segments: Technical Products (TP) and Fine Paper & Packaging (FPP). 2021 also includes the first full quarters of the Itasa release-liner acquisition (consolidated 1 April 2021).

---

### Fourth-Quarter 2021 Performance  

• Net sales reached $264 – $265 million, an all-time quarterly high, up 28 % year-on-year.  
 • Volume added 22 %, of which Itasa contributed 18 %.  
 • Price/mix contributed 6 %; currency was a 2 % headwind.  

• Adjusted EPS was $0.45 (ex‐$0.86 unusual items) versus $0.87 (ex‐$0.28) a year ago.  

• Adjusted operating margin improved sequentially but remained below long-term targets; combined price–cost headwinds trimmed margins by >500 bps and cut EPS by >$0.50 versus Q4 20.  

Segment detail  
– Technical Products: sales $167 million (+20 %); excluding Itasa and the recently closed Appleton mill, core TP sales rose 3 %. Adjusted earnings fell to $6 million (vs. $18 million) as raw-material, energy, freight and labor costs outpaced contractual price resets.  
– Fine Paper & Packaging: sales $98 million (+29 %); adjusted earnings $12 million (vs. $8 million). Segment margin expanded from 7 % to 15 % on strong commercial print, consumer and premium-packaging demand plus aggressive pricing.  

---

### Full-Year 2021 Highlights  

• Net sales grew ~30 %; adjusted EBITDA rose 16 % to $117 million (2020: $101 million) despite $47 million of unrecovered input-cost inflation.  

• Cash from operations was $53 million (2020: $93 million); working-capital build supported volume growth.  

• Capex was $28 million (1.1 × 2020). 2022 spending is guided to 4 – 5 % of revenue.  

• Liquidity remains ample; adjusted net leverage closed at 3.7× EBITDA after the Itasa purchase and debt refinancing.  

• Pension-risk transfer triggered a non-cash $17 million settlement charge but will save >$7 million over the plan’s life.  

• Quarterly dividend was raised for the 11th consecutive year.  

---

### Cost Inflation, Pricing & 2022 Earnings Bridge  

• Q4 raw-material/energy inflation was ~$25 million vs Q4 20; realized pricing was $13 million.  

• 2021 price realization totalled $19 million; 2022 actions already announced amount to ~$60 million.  

• Management sees net 2022 price benefit of ~$25 million after assuming ~$35 million of additional cost rollover, largely weighted to H1.  

• Filtration contracts (normally annual) were converted to semi-annual reviews; industrial-solutions pricing formulas now include energy and key chemical indices.  

• Appleton (US) mill closure should save $7 – 8 million per year, of which ~$6 million benefits 2022.  

• SG&A is expected to run near $120 million in 2022 (≈$100 million legacy Neenah + $20 million Itasa).  

• Q1 22 is expected to resemble Q4 21 earnings because of lingering inflation, supply-chain friction and up to $3 million EBIT impact from a January fire at the Brownville, NY FPP mill (insured). Margins are projected to improve progressively, especially in H2.  

---

### Strategic Investments & Growth Initiatives  

• €25 million expansion: fourth melt-blown line in Germany to meet soaring HVAC/industrial filtration demand; start-up early 2024.  

• $13 million specialty-coatings line in Mexico; start-up mid-2023.  

• R&D focus aligned with four platforms—Filtration, Specialty Coatings, Engineered Materials, Image & Package—aiming at mega-trends (sustainability, health & wellness). Recent launches include radiation-shielding fabrics and membership in the Soteria battery-safety consortium for EV applications.  

• M&A remains central; the Itasa acquisition (GDP+ growth, mid-teens margin) is performing ahead of plan and serves as the cornerstone of the Specialty Coatings platform.  

• Neenah Operating System roll-out and 30 % SKU rationalisation in FPP are driving safety (recordable rate ‑30 %) and unlocking capacity.  

---

### ESG & Safety  

• Energy, water and greenhouse-gas intensity all declined during 2021.  
• EcoVadis awarded Gold to Spain operations and Silver at all other sites.  
• Recordable incidents are at an historic low; long-term objective remains zero injuries.  

---

### Capital Structure & Outlook  

Neenah targets 5 % annual sales growth, 10 % earnings growth and EBITDA margins above 15 %. Management expects continued robust demand, full asset utilisation and progressively improving margins as pricing, surcharges, supply-chain normalisation and cost-savings offset inflation. Debt leverage is projected to decline, enabling further bolt-on acquisitions around the four growth platforms.
