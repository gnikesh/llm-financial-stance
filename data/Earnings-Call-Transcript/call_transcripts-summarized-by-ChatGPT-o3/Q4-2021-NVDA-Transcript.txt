# NVIDIA Q4 FY 2021 Earnings Call – Comprehensive Financial Summary  

NVIDIA ended fiscal 2021 with record‐setting results, propelled by surging demand in Gaming and sustained strength in Data Center. Management also provided detailed guidance for Q1 FY 2022, discussed crypto-currency dynamics, supply constraints, and gave an update on the pending Arm acquisition.

### Consolidated Financial Performance  
• Q4 FY 2021 revenue reached $5.00 billion, up 61 % year-on-year (YoY) and above $5 billion for the first time.  
• FY 2021 revenue was $16.68 billion, rising 53 % YoY.  
• GAAP gross margin for Q4 came in at 63.1 %; non-GAAP gross margin was 65.5 % (flat sequentially, +10 bp YoY).  
• Q4 GAAP EPS was $2.31 (+51 % YoY); non-GAAP EPS was $3.10 (+64 % YoY).  
• Operating cash flow in Q4 hit a record $2.07 billion.  

### Segment Results – Q4 and FY 2021  
Gaming  
• Q4 revenue: $2.50 billion (-inventory still “well below channel needs”), +10 % sequentially and +67 % YoY.  
• FY 2021 Gaming revenue: $7.76 billion, +41 % YoY.  
• Demand for GeForce RTX 30 (Ampere) desktop and laptop GPUs is “incredible”; channel inventory likely to remain tight through Q1 despite higher production.  
• Crypto-mining estimated to have contributed $100–300 million to Q4 Gaming revenue.  
• New Cryptocurrency Mining Processor (CMP) line expected to generate about $50 million in Q1.  

Data Center  
• Q4 revenue: $1.90 billion, up 97 % YoY and roughly flat sequentially; compute revenue grew >45 % YoY, offsetting a sequential dip in Mellanox revenue.  
• FY 2021 Data Center revenue: $6.70 billion, +125 % YoY.  
• A100 adoption broad-based: all major cloud service providers (CSPs) plus strong vertical uptake (supercomputing, financial services, higher education, consumer Internet).  
• Mellanox revenue was up >30 % versus the stand-alone quarter a year ago; company expects a return to sequential growth in Q1 on ConnectX adapter and HDR InfiniBand demand.  
• Management reiterated a “multi-billion-dollar” design-win pipeline for BlueField-2 DPUs, with meaningful revenue inflection expected in the second half of calendar 2021.  

Professional Visualization (Pro Viz)  
• Q4 revenue: $307 million, +30 % sequentially, ‑10 % YoY; FY 2021 revenue: ~$1.1 billion (-13 % YoY).  
• Recovery driven by desktop workstation rebound; notebook GPUs reached record levels on remote-work demand.  

Automotive  
• Q4 revenue: $145 million, +16 % sequentially, ‑11 % YoY.  
• FY 2021 revenue: $536 million (-23 % YoY).  
• Pipeline for DRIVE Orin and AI cockpit solutions now totals several billion dollars; ramp with Mercedes-Benz, NIO, SAIC, Li Auto, Xpeng, Zoox and Einride highlighted.  

### Balance-Sheet & Capital Allocation  
• No new debt metrics were disclosed; cash generation remained strong ($2.07 billion in Q4).  

### Q1 FY 2022 Outlook (ending April 2021)  
• Revenue guided to $5.30 billion ± 2 % with “most” sequential growth from Gaming, but Data Center also expected to rise QoQ.  
• GAAP / non-GAAP gross margin: 63.8 % / 66.0 % ± 50 bp.  
• GAAP / non-GAAP opex: ~$1.67 billion / $1.20 billion; full-year non-GAAP opex to grow in the “mid-20 %” range.  
• Other income/expense: ~$50 million expense.  
• Effective tax rate: ~10 % ± 1 % (GAAP & non-GAAP).  
• Capital expenditures: $300–325 million.  
• CMP expected to contribute ~$50 million to Q1 revenue.  

### Supply, Crypto, and Inventory Commentary  
• Company-wide demand exceeds supply; cycle-time improvements and allocation discipline are expected to allow sequential growth each quarter.  
• New software driver halves Ethereum hash-rate on GeForce RTX 3060 cards to steer supply toward gamers.  
• CMPs (lacking display outputs, optimized for mining) target “industrial” miners, offering transparency into crypto-related revenues.  

### Strategic & Qualitative Highlights  
• Adoption of RTX ray-tracing remains early: only ~15 % of the 200 million-plus GeForce desktop installed base owns an RTX-class GPU, leaving substantial upgrade runway.  
• GeForce NOW cloud gaming surpassed 6 million members; library now 800+ PC games from 300+ publishers.  
• Omniverse real-time 3-D collaboration platform entered open beta; over 500 creators/professionals already testing.  
• BlueField DPUs and the EGX / Aerial 5G edge stack underpin NVIDIA’s view that every data-center node will ultimately include a DPU for security and networking offload.  
• Management touts “Phase 3” of AI: the “smartphone moment for every industry,” where connected, autonomous devices (cars, warehouses, factories, retail stores) run AI workloads linked to cloud services.  

### Arm Acquisition Status  
• Regulatory reviews in the U.S., U.K., EU, China and others progressing “as expected”; closing still targeted within the original 18-month window (announced Sept 2020).  
• NVIDIA reiterated commitments to Arm’s open-licensing model and increased R&D investment to expand Arm’s roadmap, aiming to offer a compelling alternative CPU ecosystem for data centers, mobile and embedded markets.  

### Management’s Closing Tone  
CEO Jensen Huang characterized FY 2021 as a “break-out” year, with secular trends—AI adoption, gaming as mainstream entertainment, industrial automation and cloud computing—setting the stage for continued growth. The company believes it is “perfectly positioned” for a post-Moore’s-Law era in which accelerated computing, DPUs, and AI span from the data center to the autonomous edge.
