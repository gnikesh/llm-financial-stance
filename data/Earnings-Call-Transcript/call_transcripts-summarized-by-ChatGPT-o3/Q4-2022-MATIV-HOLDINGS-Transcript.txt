# Mativ Holdings, Inc. – Q4 2022 Earnings Call Summary

Mativ reported its first full quarter of combined operations following the July 6, 2022 merger of SWM and Neenah.  All year-over-year “comparable” figures adjust 2021/2022 numbers to show the businesses on a like-for-like basis.

Q4 2022 RESULTS  
• Net sales were $660 million, up 6 % organically at constant currency (+2 % including FX).  
  – Advanced Technical Materials (ATM) grew 9 % and Fiber-Based Solutions (FBS) 3 %, both entirely price-driven.  
  – Release liners and Protective Solutions each rose “over 20 %”, leading portfolio growth.  
• Adjusted EBITDA reached $92–93 million, +30 % year on year, expanding margin 310 bp to roughly 14 %.  
  – Price/mix exceeded raw-material inflation by >$35 million.  
  – ATM EBITDA jumped 45 % (margin +460 bp) and FBS rose 7 % (margin +140 bp).  
• Inflation in the quarter: pulp/fiber + $10 m, resins + $10 m, energy + $15 m.  
• Adjusted EPS was $0.56.  
• Interest expense was ~$27 m; roughly 75 % of the $2.1 billion debt is fixed-rate.  
• Net leverage (credit-agreement basis, which credits planned synergies) closed at 3.7×.

FY 2022 (COMBINED BASIS – PRO-FORMA)  
• Sales grew 11 % organically (constant FX).  
• Adjusted EBITDA was $370 million, also +11 %.  
• Price exceeded cost by ~ $75 million for the year.  

MERGER SYNERGIES  
• Total targeted cost synergies: $65 million.  
• Exit-2022 run-rate achieved: “a little over” $20 million (largely SG&A).  
• 2023 incremental realization expected: ~$25 million (≈ $15 m from actions already taken plus ≥ $10 m more this year).  
• Half of the total $65 million run-rate should be in place by July 2023.  
• Early savings include elimination of duplicate executive/public-company costs, insurance and benefit consolidation, reduced 3rd-party services, and freight/procurement wins.

COST/PRICE 2023 OUTLOOK  
• Management expects roughly $100 million of additional 2023 input inflation (energy, pulp, chemicals, freight).  
• Resin prices have retreated and should be stable; pulp should ease meaningfully in 2H; energy is hedged but up YoY.  
• The company plans to more than offset inflation with pricing, though “minimal additional over-recovery” is assumed versus 2022’s large catch-up.  
• Margin should improve modestly year over year, aided by synergies.

2023 GUIDANCE FRAMEWORK (NO FORMAL RANGE)  
• Baseline EBITDA: 2022’s $370 m + $25 m incremental synergies ≈ “nearly $400 million”.  
  – Consensus of ~$390 m is called “reasonable”.  
• Q1 2023 will be the weakest quarter:  
  – Revenue expected to be slightly above Q4 but EBITDA down, ~10 % below Q1 2022.  
  – Drivers: customer destocking (especially paper/industrial, ~1/3 of portfolio), higher-cost inventory rolling through, and French labor strikes hitting Engineered Papers.  
  – Destocking expected to peak in Q1 and normalize by mid-year.  
• 2H 2023 should benefit from normalized demand, lower pulp/energy costs, and full synergy capture.  
• Over 60 % of the portfolio (release liners, protective solutions, filtration, engineered papers) is viewed as recession-resilient.

CAPITAL STRUCTURE & CASH-FLOW ASSUMPTIONS, 2023  
• Depreciation ~$100 m; stock-comp $15 m; purchase-accounting amortization $55–60 m (excluded from “adjusted”).  
• Interest expense “just north of” $100 m plus ~$7 m of securitization-related interest in “other expense”.  
• Effective tax rate low-20 % range; JV income ≈ $5 m; diluted shares ~55 m.  
• Cap-ex about $90 m; working capital expected to be a neutral-to-positive contributor after an abnormal 2022 build.  
• Resulting free cash flow estimated around $150 million.  
• De-leveraging is the top cash priority; target net leverage 3.0 ×–3.5 × by year-end.

STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK (“NORTH STAR”)  
• Ambition: become the global leader in specialty materials, solving customers’ complex challenges.  
• Core competitive advantages: broad material-science know-how, collaborative customer approach, and a global manufacturing footprint.  
• Strategic pillars: (1) lean into growth, (2) focus efforts, (3) drive value creation.  
• Cultural values: prioritize safety, be curious, win with customers, have a voice, make it happen.  
• Portfolio management:  
  – Two segments, seven categories; all profitable but with differing growth/margin profiles.  
  – “Growth platforms” earmarked for disproportionate investment: Filtration, Protective Solutions, Release Liners.  
  – Mature FBS units (Engineered Papers; Packaging & Specialty Papers) are cash generators positioned for sustainable/eco-friendly trends.  
  – Future M&A, when deleveraging permits, will be bolt-ons that deepen positions in the three growth platforms rather than diversification.  
  – Around 75 % of current portfolio has underlying end-market growth of GDP+.  

KEY TAKEAWAYS  
1. Q4 matched guidance: $93 m EBITDA, 30 % YoY growth, and >$20 m synergy run-rate.  
2. 2023 baseline EBITDA of ~$400 m is supportable via $25 m incremental synergies and continued price discipline, though Q1 will be soft due to destocking and one-offs.  
3. The $65 m synergy pipeline is “largely within our control” and viewed as a built-in profit catalyst.  
4. Management is committed to de-levering toward 3× while investing cap-ex ~$90 m and generating FCF ≈ $150 m.  
5. Strategy centers on accelerating organic growth in Filtration, Protective Solutions, and Release Liners, complemented by selective bolt-on M&A once balance-sheet targets are met.
