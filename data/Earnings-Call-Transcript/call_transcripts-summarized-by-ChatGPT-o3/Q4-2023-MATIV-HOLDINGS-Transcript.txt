# Mativ Holdings, Inc. — Q4 & FY-2023 Earnings Call Summary

Mativ closed its first full fiscal year as a merged company amid a pronounced industry-wide destocking cycle, high interest rates and geopolitical uncertainty, all of which suppressed volume in most end-markets. Management believes the volume trough has been reached and sees early signs of demand recovery heading into 2024, particularly in filtration, release liners and several industrial applications, although commercial print remains weak.

Full-year net sales from continuing operations were $2.0 billion, 9 % lower on a comparable basis, while Q4 sales fell 14 % year-over-year to $452 million. Lower volume was the primary headwind. Adjusted EBITDA declined 17 % for the year to $213 million and 20 % in Q4 to $50 million; nevertheless, both operating segments expanded quarterly margins thanks to cost controls, pricing discipline and merger synergies.  

• Advanced Technical Materials (ATM): Q4 sales $362 million (-12 % Y/Y); adjusted EBITDA $56 million (-9 %) with margin up 30 bps Y/Y and 50 bps sequentially.  
• Fiber-Based Solutions (FBS): Q4 sales $90 million (-22 %); adjusted EBITDA $13 million (-35 %).  
Unallocated corporate expense held flat at roughly $19 million, and Q4 tax was a 19 % benefit.

The July-2022 SWM/Neenah merger targeted $65 million of synergies. During 2023 Mativ realized more than $30 million, exceeding its first-year goal of $25 million and running ahead of schedule to capture the remaining savings—principally in procurement and supply-chain—over the next 18 months.

In November Mativ divested its Engineered Papers business, receiving $632 million in net cash (above the originally projected $575 million) and using the proceeds to retire more than $600 million of debt (≈ 35 % of total). Quarter-end net debt was just under $1 billion, available liquidity stood at $454 million, and net leverage fell 0.3 turns sequentially to 3.9× credit-agreement EBITDA. Debt maturities are staggered between 2026 and 2028. Management reiterated that free cash flow will be directed primarily toward further deleveraging; share repurchases will merely offset equity dilution. The quarterly dividend was previously reduced, and 2024 capex has been trimmed by over 10 %.

Targeted growth investments remain intact. A new melt-blown filtration line in Germany will come on-stream in Q1-2024, and a silicone release-liner coater in Mexico has begun customer trials; together the two assets are expected to support roughly $50 million of incremental annual revenue at full utilization. Meanwhile, three smaller, less-profitable manufacturing sites are being consolidated and the number of warehouses has already been cut 10 % since the merger—with another 10 % reduction planned by year-end—to lower fixed costs and improve service levels.

To match a now narrower portfolio, Mativ launched a two-phase overhead restructuring focused on non-operating expenses:  
1. Wave 1 (underway) centers on senior-level workforce reductions and should deliver an annualized $20 million cost-savings run-rate by year-end 2024; related severance and other charges of $15-$20 million will be recognized mainly in H1-2024.  
2. Wave 2, driven by systems integration and process automation, aims for another $20 million of annualized savings by the end of 2026. In aggregate, overhead will be reduced about 15 % over the period.

Concurrently, the company realigned into two operating segments effective Q1-2024:  
• Filtration & Advanced Materials (FAM) – ~40 % of sales, expected to grow faster than GDP with normalized EBITDA margins in the mid-to-high teens; led by Christophe Stenzel.  
• Sustainable & Adhesive Solutions (SAS) – ~60 % of sales, expected to grow at GDP-like rates with low-to-mid-teens margins; led by newly hired Ryan Elwart.

Outlook. While Mativ did not issue formal 2024 guidance, management offered several planning assumptions:  
• Q1-2024 sales should rise sharply from Q4 because higher-cost inventory sold through early in the year inflates revenue; however, Q1 adjusted EBITDA is expected to be roughly flat with Q4’s $50 million.  
• Sequential EBITDA improvement is anticipated each quarter, subject to typical year-end seasonality, building toward a $70 million quarterly run-rate by Q4-2024, supported by the $20 million overhead-savings run-rate.  
• Full-year interest expense is projected at approximately $70 million (about $17-18 million per quarter) and depreciation & amortization at roughly $100 million.  

Management is encouraged by rising order backlogs in several businesses, believes destocking has largely run its course, and expects demand momentum to increase as 2024 unfolds, positioning Mativ to translate its leaner cost base, synergy capture and focused growth investments into improved earnings and further debt reduction.
