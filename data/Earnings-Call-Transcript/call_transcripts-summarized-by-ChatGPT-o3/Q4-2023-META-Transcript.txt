# Meta Platforms, Inc. – Q4 2023 & Full-Year 2023 Earnings Call Summary  

Meta ended 2023 with a strong fourth quarter that combined a return to solid top-line growth with sharply improved profitability and disciplined spending. Management reiterated its dual long-term priorities—artificial intelligence and the metaverse—while keeping a tight focus on near-term execution in the Family of Apps advertising and messaging businesses.

Meta’s community continued to expand, with more than 3.1 billion people using at least one Meta app every day. Although the company will stop disclosing Facebook-only DAU/MAU statistics, it will keep reporting Family Daily Active People and will add regional ad-impression and price data beginning in 1Q24.

## Consolidated Q4 2023 results  

• Revenue reached $40.11 billion, up 25 percent year-over-year (22 percent in constant currency).  
• GAAP operating income rose to $16.40 billion, delivering a 41 percent operating margin versus 20 percent a year ago.  
• Net income jumped to $14.02 billion, and diluted EPS more than tripled to $5.33.  
• Total expenses fell 8 percent to $23.71 billion, reflecting lower restructuring charges, reduced marketing and G&A, and tight head-count control. Meta ended the year with 67,317 employees, down 22 percent Y/Y but 2 percent above Q3 as limited hiring resumed.  
• Free cash flow was $11.46 billion; full-year FCF reached $43.0 billion.  
• Cash and marketable securities stood at $65.4 billion against $18.4 billion of debt.

## Segment detail  

Family of Apps (FoA)  
• Q4 revenue: $39.02 billion, +24 percent Y/Y.  
• Q4 operating income: $21.04 billion, 54 percent margin.  
• Ad impressions grew 21 percent; average price per ad increased 2 percent. Impression gains were led by Asia-Pacific and Rest-of-World; European and RoW regions posted the strongest ad-revenue growth (33 percent and 32 percent, respectively).  
• Other FoA revenue (largely WhatsApp business messaging) rose 82 percent to $334 million.  
• China-based advertisers contributed 10 percent of Meta’s 2023 revenue and accounted for 5 percentage points of worldwide growth.

Reality Labs (RL)  
• Q4 revenue surged 47 percent to $1.07 billion, driven by Quest 3 holiday demand.  
• Q4 operating loss widened to $4.65 billion; management reaffirmed that RL losses will “increase meaningfully” in 2024 as investment in AR/VR and mixed-reality continues.  
• Quest headsets generated the first $1 billion RL revenue quarter; Quest was the most-downloaded iOS app on Christmas Day.  
• Ray-Ban Meta smart-glasses demand exceeded initial production plans, with higher engagement and retention than the first-generation glasses.

## Full-year 2023 highlights  

• Revenue: $134.90 billion (+16 percent); FoA ad revenue: $131.94 billion (+16 percent).  
• Operating income: $46.75 billion; operating margin rebounded to 35 percent from 20 percent in 2022.  
• Net income: $39.10 billion; EPS: $14.87.  
• Total expenses: $88.15 billion (-13 percent).  
• Capital expenditures: $28.92 billion; principal finance-lease repayments: $3.68 billion.  
• Share repurchases: $20.0 billion; $30.9 billion remained on the prior authorization as of 31 Dec. The Board has now approved an additional $50 billion buy-back capacity and initiated a quarterly cash dividend of $0.50 per share, beginning with Q1 2024.

## Guidance & outlook  

Revenue  
• Q1 2024 revenue is projected between $34.5 billion and $37.0 billion, implying 20 – 29 percent growth despite a tougher comparison and assuming FX is neutral. February 29 gives a modest one-day benefit.

Expenses  
• 2024 total expenses remain forecast at $94 – $99 billion. Drivers include:  
  – Higher depreciation and operating costs from the enlarged data-center footprint;  
  – A resumption of selective hiring, shifting the mix toward higher-cost technical talent;  
  – An appreciable increase in Reality Labs operating losses.

Capital Expenditures  
• 2024 CapEx outlook is lifted to $30 – $37 billion (up $2 billion at the high end). Incremental spending targets servers (AI and non-AI) and construction of new, AI-optimized data-center architecture. Management signaled that ambitious AI research will likely necessitate “growing infrastructure investments beyond this year.”

Tax  
• Meta expects a 2024 GAAP tax rate in the mid-teens, absent U.S. tax-law changes.

## Strategy & operational themes  

Year of Efficiency achievements  
Mark Zuckerberg credited 2023’s “year of efficiency” with making Meta a leaner, faster-moving technology company and restoring profitability. He intends to preserve this operating discipline even as revenue recovers; new head-count additions will remain “relatively minimal.”

Artificial Intelligence  
• Meta’s goal is to build world-class AI assistants for users, creators, and businesses, underpinned by full general intelligence.  
• The company will have about 350,000 Nvidia H100 GPUs by year-end 2024—roughly 600,000 H100-equivalent GPUs when including other chips—after learning from prior under-investment during the Reels rollout.  
• Open sourcing remains core: Llama 3 is now training, and future models (Llama 4–7) are mapped out. Meta argues that open source improves safety, efficiency, adoption, and talent recruitment without eroding product differentiation.  
• AI product rollout follows Meta’s classic playbook: limited tests (Meta AI assistant, Creator/Business AIs, smart-glasses integration) followed by iterative tuning, then large-scale deployment and, finally, monetization.  
• Near-term revenue potential lies in AI-driven ad tools (Advantage+ suite, Text Variations, Image Expansion, Background Generation). Longer term, conversational AIs in messaging could open sizable monetization avenues.

Metaverse & devices  
• Quest 3 is “the most popular mixed-reality device,” while Horizon Worlds has entered Quest’s top-ten apps and will focus on mobile expansion in 2024.  
• Smart-glasses equipped with multimodal AI are viewed as a precursor to full AR; Meta and EssilorLuxottica are increasing production to meet demand.

Advertising & messaging  
• Reels sharing averages 3.5 billion re-shares daily; unifying recommendation systems across video formats is the next growth lever.  
• WhatsApp is gaining traction in the U.S.; Channels has surpassed 500 million MAUs since September launch.  
• Messaging monetization advances through click-to-message ads (strong growth with purchase optimization) and paid marketing messages on WhatsApp; Flows enables richer in-thread commerce experiences.  
• Shops ads surpassed a $2 billion annual run-rate following broader availability and a new Buy-with-Prime pilot with Amazon.

Capital allocation  
• Besides rising CapEx for AI clusters and data centers, Meta will continue sizable buy-backs and now a regular dividend, providing a “more balanced” return program.  
• Management acknowledged intensifying regulatory and legal headwinds, highlighting the FTC’s attempt to expand Meta’s consent decree as a significant risk.

**Bottom line:** Meta exited 2023 with revived revenue momentum, markedly higher margins, and a fortress balance sheet. Management will keep tight cost discipline while accelerating AI and metaverse investments. The 2024 financial framework calls for mid-teens expense growth, a higher CapEx ceiling of $37 billion, larger Reality Labs losses, and sustained shareholder returns via both repurchases and a new dividend—all while aiming to scale AI products, deepen messaging monetization, and maintain leadership in social engagement.
