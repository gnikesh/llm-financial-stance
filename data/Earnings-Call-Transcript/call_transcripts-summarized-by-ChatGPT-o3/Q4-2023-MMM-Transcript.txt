# 3M Company Q4 2023 Results, FY-23 Review and 2024 Outlook – Financial Summary  

3M closed 2023 with materially better earnings, margins and cash generation, while continuing to de-risk the portfolio, prepare the spin-off of its Health-Care business (to be named Solventum) and advance major legal settlements.  

For the fourth quarter of 2023 the company reported adjusted sales of $7.7 billion, a year-on-year organic decline of 1.4 per cent (down 0.8 per cent excluding the expected fall-off in disposable respirators).  Adjusted operating margin expanded 180 basis points to 20.9 per cent (320 bps excluding current-year restructuring costs) and adjusted EPS rose 11 per cent to $2.42, $0.11 above consensus, helped by a 14.9 per cent tax rate.  Free cash flow was $2.0 billion, up 18 per cent, with conversion of 145 per cent, and inventories were reduced by $550 million to 14.8 per cent of sales.  Net debt at year-end stood at $10 billion, down $2 billion (-17 per cent) versus December 2022.  

Full-year 2023 organic sales fell 3 per cent, but disciplined pricing, sourcing and a broad-based restructuring effort produced 60 basis points of adjusted margin expansion (excluding restructuring costs) and adjusted EPS of $9.24.  Free cash flow reached $6.3 billion (123 per cent conversion), permitting $3.3 billion of dividends and a $2 billion reduction in net debt.  The 2023 restructuring—the largest in 3M’s history—generated more than $400 million of savings and an equal amount of charges.  Management still targets $700-900 million of annual run-rate savings with a similar level of cumulative charges; most actions and costs will be completed by year-end 2024, with benefits continuing into 2025.  Incremental 2024 savings are estimated at $150-250 million against $250-350 million of additional charges.  

Segment performance in Q4:  
• Safety & Industrial sales were $2.7 billion (-3.9 % organic), hurt by weaker respirator demand (-160 bps) and cautious distribution channels; operating margin was 19.7 % (-70 bps).  
• Transportation & Electronics delivered $1.8 billion of sales (+2.7 % organic).  Auto OEM revenue grew 13 % versus a 9 % increase in global builds; Electronics was flat as consumer-device demand stabilised.  Margin improved 380 bps to 20.9 %.  
• Health Care posted $2.0 billion in sales (-1 % organic); Medical Solutions rose low-single-digits but Separation & Purification, Oral Care and Health Information Systems declined.  Spin-related costs and lower volumes reduced margin 190 bps to 18.3 % (26 % EBITDA).  
• Consumer revenue was $1.2 billion (-2.2 % organic).  Home Improvement grew, but Stationery & Office fell high-single-digits.  Margin expanded 100 bps to 18 %.  

2024 guidance (all figures adjusted, excluding Health-Care spin or settlement financing effects):  
• Organic sales: flat to +2 % (+1 % to +3 % if geographic and portfolio exits are excluded).  
• EPS: $9.35–$9.75.  The range embeds a $0.29 combined head-wind from higher tax (18.5-19.5 % vs 17.5 % in 2023) and ‑$0.15 from non-operating pension expense; absent proceeds from the pending spin, incremental financing for legal settlements could cost up to an additional $0.20 per share.  
• Operating‐margin expansion: 75-100 bps including restructuring effects; up more than 250 bps year-on-year in Q1 excluding restructuring.  
• Free-cash-flow conversion: 95-105 % (cap-ex budget $1.4-1.6 billion).  

Segment outlooks: Safety & Industrial and Transportation & Electronics are each expected to grow organically flat–low-single-digits; Health Care flat–low-single-digits; Consumer down low-single-digits owing to subdued discretionary demand and an estimated $100 million (~2 ppt) sales head-wind from portfolio pruning.  

Q1-24 guidance: sales about $7.6 billion (slightly lower year-on-year), EPS $2.00-2.15, operating margin 19.5-20 % after >100 bps of restructuring charges; Health-Care stand-up costs will reduce EPS by $0.07-0.08 and pension by a further $0.04.  

Strategic and balance-sheet actions:  
• Spin-off of Solventum (Health Care) remains on schedule for 1H-24.  The new entity will be levered 3.0-3.5 × EBITDA and pay a one-time dividend to 3M equal to roughly 1 × EBITDA; 3M will retain a 19.9 % equity stake, providing ongoing liquidity.  After the transaction closes, 3M will host an investor meeting to reset 2024 guidance and present a medium-term framework, including treatment of transition-service income, stranded costs and below-the-line items.  
• Legal settlements: the public-water-supplier PFAS agreement is slated for final approval on 2 February, and Combat Arms milestones are progressing; 3M may satisfy up to $1 billion of the ear-plug payments with its own shares, which would be treated as an adjustment to EPS.  
• PFAS manufacturing exit remains firm for end-2025 (volumes already reduced 20 %); investments in advanced water-filtration systems at chemical plants will be completed during 2024.  

Management reiterated its priorities for 2024: sustain operational-execution gains, finish the Health-Care separation, continue portfolio and geographic optimisation, step up innovation in high-growth platforms (EVs, climate tech, industrial automation), further improve working-capital turns and free cash flow, and reduce risk and uncertainty tied to litigation and PFAS.
