# Alphabet Inc. Q4 2024 and FY 2024 Earnings Call Summary  

Alphabet closed 2024 with strong top- and bottom-line growth, record cash generation, expanding operating margins and accelerating artificial-intelligence adoption across its businesses. Management highlighted continued leadership in Search, YouTube and Cloud, rapid product iteration in Gemini/AI, and disciplined but elevated capital spending to meet surging demand for AI infrastructure.

---

### Consolidated financial performance  

•  Fourth-quarter revenue was $96.5 billion, up 12 % year-on-year (y/y) in both reported and constant currency.  
•  Full-year revenue reached $350 billion, up 14 % y/y (15 % in constant currency).  

•  Q4 operating income rose 31 % y/y to $31.0 billion; operating margin expanded 4.6 ppt to 32 %.  
•  Net income was $26.5 billion (+28 %), and diluted EPS climbed 31 % to $2.15.  
•  FY 2024 net income totalled $112 billion, +33 % y/y.

•  Free cash flow was $24.8 billion in Q4 and a record $72.8 billion for the year.  
•  Cash and marketable securities stood at $96 billion; Alphabet remains in a net-cash position (no debt was disclosed).  
•  Shareholder returns totalled $17.4 billion in Q4 ( $15 billion buybacks, $2.4 billion dividends) and nearly $70 billion for the full year.

---

### Segment highlights  

Search & Other  
•  Google Services revenue grew 10 % y/y to $84.1 billion in Q4.  
•  Search & Other advertising revenue rose 13 % to $54 billion, led by Financial Services and Retail.  
•  AI Overviews are live in 100+ countries; early data show higher user satisfaction, rising query volumes and monetisation “approximately on par” with traditional results.  
•  Circle to Search is now on >200 million Android devices; users who try it initiate >10 % of their searches via this feature.  
•  Lens handles >20 billion visual queries per month; most are incremental.  

YouTube  
•  Advertising revenue advanced 14 % to $10.5 billion, driven by U.S. election spend, brand advertisers and rising Shorts monetisation.  
•  Nielsen ranks YouTube #1 in U.S. streaming watch-time; viewers watched >1 billion hours per day on TV screens in 2024.  
•  Shorts’ monetisation rate improved >30 ppt y/y in the U.S.; Shorts now generate 15 % of CTV viewing.  
•  Over 250 k creators participate in YouTube Shopping; 3 million channels are in the YouTube Partner Program.  

Google Cloud  
•  Q4 Cloud revenue surged 30 % y/y to $12.0 billion; Google Cloud Platform (GCP) again grew “much faster” than the segment.  
•  Operating income grew to $2.1 billion, lifting margin from 9.4 % to 17.5 %.  
•  Demand outpaced available capacity exiting 2024, constraining near-term revenue; management is accelerating infrastructure build-out.  
•  First-time commitments more than doubled y/y; deals >$250 million doubled, and several >$1 billion closed.  
•  Vertex AI customers increased 5× y/y; overall usage jumped 20× in 2024.  

Subscription, Platforms & Devices  
•  Revenue rose 8 % to $11.6 billion, powered by Google One, YouTube TV and YouTube Music Premium subscriber growth; hardware revenue fell because Pixel launches moved to Q3.  

Other Bets & Waymo  
•  Other Bets posted $400 million revenue and a $1.2 billion operating loss (vs. a milestone payment in Q4 2023).  
•  Waymo completed 4 million paid rides in 2024, averages >150 k rides weekly, and will expand to Austin, Atlanta and Miami while cutting hardware cost with its 6th-generation driver.

---

### Capital allocation and outlook  

•  CapEx was $14 billion in Q4, primarily for servers and data centres; full-year 2024 CapEx was not specified but will rise sharply.  
•  2025 CapEx is projected at roughly $75 billion, with $16-18 billion earmarked for Q1, reflecting heavy investment in AI infrastructure (servers first, then data centres & networking).  
•  Depreciation expense grew 28 % in 2024 and will “accelerate” in 2025 given recent CapEx levels.  
•  Headcount will rise selectively in AI and Cloud, while management pursues “further efficiencies” in real estate, organisational structure and AI-driven internal tooling.  

Revenue guidance considerations for Q1 2025:  
1. FX headwind from a stronger U.S. dollar.  
2. One fewer day versus leap-year Q1 2024.  
3. Tough comparisons in Financial Services advertising (especially insurance).  
4. Cloud growth will fluctuate with timing of new capacity coming online.

---

### AI strategy and product momentum  

•  Alphabet pursues a “full-stack” AI approach—custom hardware (TPUs), compilers, models and products—to maximise performance and cost efficiency; data centres now deliver 4× more compute per kWh than five years ago.  
•  Gemini 2.0 (and the lower-latency Gemini 2.0 Flash) underpin all seven Google products with >2 billion users.  
•  More than 4.4 million developers (double six months ago) use Gemini; new reasoning and agentic capabilities (e.g., Deep Research, Project Mariner, Project Astra) are in testing.  
•  AI overviews, Circle to Search, image generation (Imagen 3) and video generation (Veo 2) are rolling out to consumers and creators.  
•  Quantum computing milestone: launch of Willow chip, reducing error rates exponentially as qubits scale.  

---

### Key takeaways for investors  

1. Alphabet delivered double-digit revenue and >30 % EPS growth while expanding margins, underscoring the resilience of its core ad franchises and the improving profitability of Cloud.  
2. AI is already a material revenue driver—Cloud’s AI infrastructure and generative-AI services are generating “billions” in annual revenue—and is improving user engagement and ad opportunities in Search and YouTube.  
3. Management will markedly increase CapEx to satisfy AI demand, yet emphasises rigorous ROI, efficiency gains and continued share repurchases/dividends.  
4. Near-term revenue faces FX, leap-year and vertical-mix headwinds, but secular trends in AI, streaming and digital commerce position Alphabet for sustained growth.
