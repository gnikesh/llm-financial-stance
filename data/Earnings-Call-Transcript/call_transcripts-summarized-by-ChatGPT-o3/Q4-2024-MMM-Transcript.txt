# 3M Q4 2024 Earnings Call – Key Financial and Strategic Take-aways  

3M closed a “pivotal” year with solid fourth-quarter results and set 2025 guidance that calls for continued margin expansion, stronger cash generation and low-single-digit organic growth despite an uneven macro outlook.  Below is an integrated recap of the critical financial metrics, trends and qualitative commentary discussed on the call.

---

### Fourth-Quarter 2024 Highlights  
• Total adjusted sales were $5.8 billion; reported revenue (post-Healthcare spin) was $6.01 billion, down 25 % YoY but **up 2.1 % organically**, the first quarter since 1Q 2022 in which all business groups grew simultaneously.  
• Adjusted EPS came in at **$1.68**, $0.14 above consensus and $0.05 above management’s own October midpoint, helped by $0.09 of volume leverage and productivity but hurt by a $0.04 FX head-wind.  
• Adjusted operating margin was **19.7 %**, down 20 bp YoY, largely on FX and growth investments.  
• **Free cash flow (FCF) was $1.3 billion**; conversion was a robust **145 %**, helped by an 8-day sequential and 2-day YoY reduction in inventory (to 94 days).  
• Shareholder returns were **$1.1 billion** in the quarter (dividends plus buybacks).  

---

### Full-Year 2024 Snapshot  
| Metric | 2024 | YoY | vs. Guidance |
|--------|------|-----|--------------|
| Adjusted sales | $23.6 B | +1.2 % org. | In-line |
| Adjusted EPS | **$7.30** | +21 % | High end |
| Adj. op. margin | **21.4 %** | +280 bp | Above guide (250–275 bp) |
| Free cash flow | **$4.9 B** | conv. 111 % | Strong |
| Capital returned | **$3.8 B** | ($2 B div., $1.8 B buybacks) | — |

Management attributes “over 80 %” of the EPS improvement to operational execution: volume leverage, restructuring savings and productivity.

---

### Business-Group Color (4Q 24 unless noted)  
• **Safety & Industrial (SIBG)**: +2.4 % org. (3rd straight growth quarter); six of seven divisions up. Drivers were eBonding, hearing/body protection and large power-grid orders. FY 24 sales ≈$11 B, +0.7 %.  
• **Transportation & Electronics (TEBG)**: +2.0 % org.; consumer electronics +HSD, aerospace up DD, auto OEM –MSD (global builds weak). FY 24 org. +3.4 %; electronics +≈12 %, auto +2 %, aerospace DD.  
• **Consumer (CBG)**: Returned to growth at +1.2 % org.; holiday sales strong for Command and Scotch. FY 24 org. –1.2 % (home-improvement up LSD, other divisions down).  

---

### Geographic Trends  
• **China**: Q4 sales +HSD on electronics share gains and tariff front-loading; FY 24 China +~10 %. Company expects **low-single-digit** China growth in 2025.  
• **U.S.**: Q4 +LSD despite soft retail; FY 24 +0.7 % versus –0.3 % IPI.  
• **EMEA**: Q4 –LSD; auto builds –HSD weighed. FY 24 –1.3 %.  

---

### Operational & Innovation Update  
• Restructuring: The largest program in 3M history is “substantially complete”; lower restructuring cost is a $200 million tail-wind in 2025.  
• OTIF service level averaged **88 %** in 2024 (+3 ppt YoY, +8 ppt vs. 2022); Consumer & T&E are >90 %, but SIBG is “low-80s.” Goal is >90 % overall while compressing inventory to **75 days** over time.  
• Productivity target remains **2 % net** in COGS; broader op-ex excellence should lift gross margin toward the “high-40s” percent over time.  
• **R&D**: 169 new product launches in 2024 (+32 % YoY); 2025 launches to rise DD%. Flagship programs include LCD 2.0 optical films (OLED-like brightness for LCDs) and Expanded Beam Optics data-center connectors. Headcount was increased by ~50 and ~100 more employees re-deployed into R&D in Q4.  

---

### Legal & Insurance  
• 2024 saw settlement of two major legal matters (PFAS/PWS and Combat Arms) and the spin of Healthcare (Solventum).  
• Q4 insurance recoveries were **$170 million** (cumulative $340 million to date, mostly Combat Arms). 3M “expects carriers to honor policies” and continues arbitration/litigation.  
• PFAS-related **stranded cost** is a ~$100 million EPS head-wind embedded in 2025 guidance.  

---

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity  
• Year-end **cash & marketable securities: $7.7 B** (≈2× working-capital needs).  
• 2025 cash uses: ~$3 B legal settlements, ~$1.5 B dividends, **$1.5 B gross share repurchase**, ~$1 B cap-ex (≈D&A). Management still expects to exit 2025 with **> $6 B cash** and plans to refinance the bulk of 2025 maturities.  

---

### 2025 Outlook (all adjusted)  
• **Organic sales growth:** **2 %–3 %**; each business group to grow LSD, roughly at or above macro.  
• **EPS:** **$7.60–$7.90** (up 4 %–8 %).  
     • Operational gains contribute **+$0.70–$1.00** (volume leverage, restructuring tail-winds, $150 M net productivity).  
     • **Non-operational head-winds ~–$0.40** (½ FX, ½ below-the-line items).  
• **Operating-margin expansion:** **+130 bp to +190 bp**.  
• **Free-cash-flow conversion:** ≈**100 %** (inventory reduction to offset higher receivables).  
• **Cap-ex:** ≈**$1 B**; R&D and sustainability remain priorities.  
• **Buybacks:** **$1.5 B** gross in 2025.  
• **Quarterly cadence:** 1H vs. 2H earnings roughly equal; Q1 EPS to mirror prior-year Q1 (~$1.70) because annual equity grants shift back to Q1 (–$0.15 YoY impact).  

Management sees 2025 global IPI at 1.9 %, notes auto builds will be slightly negative (–3 % to –4 % in U.S./Europe), consumer electronics up LSD–MSD, and U.S. discretionary retail spending flat. They pledge to “control what we can”—service, commercial execution and accelerated innovation—while continuing to simplify the portfolio and return cash to shareholders.

---

### Strategic Priorities Emphasized  
1. **Commercial Excellence:** quota setting pulled forward; cross-selling pilots with distributors already yielding traction; renewed pricing discipline.  
2. **Supply-Chain & Service:** raise OTIF toward mid-90s while driving inventory to 75 days; standardize demand planning, improve supplier delivery, tighten logistics.  
3. **Innovation Engine:** shorten development cycles, focus dollars on high-“octane” programs, and convert NPIs into higher sales and margins.
