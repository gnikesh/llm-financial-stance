# NVIDIA Q4 FY 2025 Earnings Call – Financial & Strategic Summary

NVIDIA closed fiscal 2025 with another record‐setting quarter and year, driven by an unprecedented ramp of its new Blackwell architecture and broadening demand for accelerated and generative AI computing.

Revenue and Profitability  
• Fourth-quarter revenue reached $39.3 billion, rising 12 % sequentially and 78 % year on year; it exceeded the company’s outlook of $37.5 billion.  
• Full-year revenue jumped 114 % to $130.5 billion.  
• GAAP gross margin for Q4 was 73 % and non-GAAP 73.5 %, both lower sequentially as expected because of the first Blackwell shipments; management guides gross margin to the low-70 % range during the ramp and a return to the mid-70 % range late in fiscal 2026.  
• NVIDIA returned $8.1 billion to shareholders in Q4 through buy-backs and dividends.

Segment Performance  
Data Center (the core growth engine)  
• FY 2025 Data Center revenue more than doubled to $115.2 billion.  
• Q4 Data Center revenue hit a record $35.6 billion, up 16 % sequentially and 93 % year on year.  
• Approximately $11 billion of Q4 revenue came from initial Blackwell shipments, marking the fastest product ramp in the company’s history.  
• Data-center compute revenue climbed 18 % sequentially and more than doubled year on year.  
• Large cloud service providers (CSPs) accounted for about half of Data Center revenue and grew nearly 2× year on year.  
• Networking revenue slipped 3 % sequentially, with GPU attach rates above 75 %; management expects networking to resume growth in Q1 on the back of Spectrum-X and NVLink Switch adoption.  
• China remains “well below” pre-export-control levels and is expected to stay at roughly the current sub-scale percentage of Data Center sales unless regulations change.

Gaming  
• Gaming revenue was $2.5 billion, down 22 % sequentially and 11 % year on year because of holiday-season supply constraints; fiscal-year Gaming revenue nevertheless rose 9 % to $11.4 billion.  
• NVIDIA anticipates a strong sequential rebound in Q1 as GeForce RTX 50-series desktop and laptop GPUs built on Blackwell become broadly available.

Professional Visualization  
• Q4 revenue grew 5 % sequentially and 10 % year on year to $511 million; full-year revenue rose 21 % to $1.9 billion, helped by AI-driven engineering and simulation workloads.

Automotive & Robotics  
• Automotive revenue set a record at $570 million, up 27 % sequentially and 103 % year on year; fiscal-year revenue climbed 55 % to $1.7 billion.  
• Management expects Automotive vertical revenue to approach $5 billion in the current fiscal year, buoyed by AV wins at Toyota, Hyundai, Aurora, Continental and others.

Cash Flow, Expense, Tax  
• GAAP operating expenses rose 9 % sequentially (non-GAAP +11 %) due to higher engineering and infrastructure costs.  
• Q1 FY 2026 guidance: GAAP opex about $5.2 billion; non-GAAP opex about $3.6 billion. Full-year FY 2026 opex will grow into the “mid‐30s” billions.  
• GAAP and non-GAAP tax rates guided at 17 % ±1 %.

Forward Guidance (Q1 FY 2026)  
• Revenue of $43 billion ±2 % (another double-digit sequential increase) on a “significant” Blackwell ramp.  
• Sequential growth is expected in both Data Center (compute and networking) and Gaming.  
• GAAP gross margin guided to 70.6 % ±50 bp (non-GAAP 71 % ±50 bp).

Capital & Balance Sheet  
• Management did not discuss outstanding debt; no material changes were flagged.  
• Share repurchases and dividends remain the primary vehicle for capital return.

Strategic & Product Highlights  
• Blackwell architecture targets the entire AI lifecycle—pre-training, post-training fine-tuning, and inference—delivering up to 25× token throughput and 20× lower inference cost versus the prior Hopper generation.  
• Massive clusters of 100,000-plus GPUs are already shipping, and early GB200 racks (each comprising 1.5 million components sourced from 350 sites) are being deployed at Azure, AWS, Google Cloud, OCI, CoreWeave and others.  
• Three complementary scaling laws—pre-training, post-training reinforcement learning, and inference-time “reasoning” compute—are together expanding total addressable demand for NVIDIA platforms.  
• Upcoming roadmap: Blackwell Ultra remains on track for 2H 2025, followed by the “Vera Rubin” architecture; both slot into existing NVLink 72 systems.  
• Spectrum-X Ethernet and NVLink Switch fabric add new growth vectors; Cisco will resell Spectrum-X to enterprises worldwide.  
• Sovereign AI, enterprise “agentic” AI and “physical AI” (robotics, autonomous machines) are flagged as the next major demand waves beyond current cloud workloads.

Management Commentary from Q&A  
• Jensen Huang reiterated that accelerated computing and AI will capture most future data-center CapEx, predicting that every enterprise will operate or rent an “AI factory.”  
• Reasoning/inference models such as OpenAI’s GPT-o3, xAI’s Grok-3, and DeepSeek-R1 can already require 100× the compute of traditional one-shot inference, creating durable demand for GPU capacity.  
• The company sees limited competitive threat from custom ASICs because of CUDA’s broad software ecosystem, rapid yearly cadence, and superior performance-per-watt translating directly into revenue for cloud operators.  
• Older GPU generations (Pascal, Volta, Ampere) remain actively deployed for less intensive tasks like data curation, underscoring long equipment life and high TCO value.

Key Dates  
• GTC (San Jose) begins March 17 2025; Jensen Huang’s keynote is on March 18, with a financial-analyst Q&A on March 19.  
• FY 2026 Q1 results will be reported on May 28 2025.

In sum, NVIDIA closed fiscal 2025 with triple-digit full-year growth, strengthened gross margins despite the Blackwell launch, and guided to another record quarter on explosive AI demand. Management expects Data Center to remain the principal growth driver, supported by rapid product cadence (Blackwell, Blackwell Ultra, Vera Rubin), rising enterprise and sovereign AI adoption, and an expanding automotive and robotics pipeline, while continuing aggressive shareholder returns.
