# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations – Executive Summary  

This summary follows the order and headings of the original MD&A and retains all material quantitative and qualitative disclosures concerning sales, earnings, cash flows, capital structure, segment results, critical accounting estimates, and market-risk sensitivities.  

---

## Organizational Realignment Plan  

Effective 1Q 2024 the Company reorganized into two reportable segments—Filtration & Advanced Materials (FAM) and Sustainable & Adhesive Solutions (SAS)—to streamline operations and sharpen market focus.  All 2023 data have been recast to this structure.

## EP Divestiture  

On 30 November 2023 Mativ sold its tobacco-related Engineered Papers (“EP”) business to Evergreen Hill Enterprise.  EP results are reflected as discontinued operations for all periods; the Company no longer participates in tobacco-based markets.

---

## Critical Accounting Estimates (Highlights)

• Income taxes—valuation allowances and uncertain-tax-position reserves remain key; effective rate in 2024 benefited from the absence of the prior-year, non-deductible $401 million goodwill impairment.  
• Goodwill—an interim 2023 test produced the $401 million non-cash impairment (now in SAS).  The 1 October 2024 annual test indicated no additional charge, though management will continue to monitor macro headwinds.  
• Property, plant & equipment—25 % of total assets; no impairments recognized in 2024.

## Results of Operations  

### Net Sales by Segment  

| 2024 | 2023 | $ Δ | % Δ |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| FAM | $766.5 m | $810.0 m | –$43.5 m | –5.4 % |
| SAS | $1,214.6 m | $1,216.0 m | –$1.4 m | –0.1 % |
| Total | $1,981.1 m | $2,026.0 m | –$44.9 m | –2.2 % |

• FAM decline due to lower volumes (–3.7 %) and pricing (–1.9 %).  
• SAS essentially flat; higher volumes (+2.3 %) offset by price pressure (–0.6 %) and the impact of closed/divested sites (–2.0 %).

### Gross Profit  

Gross profit increased to **$364.1 million**; margin widened 80 bps to 18.4 %, reflecting improved price–cost balance partially offset by volume/mix.  FAM gross profit fell $22.5 million (–11.4 %); SAS rose $30.8 million (+19.4 %).

### Non-manufacturing Expenses  

Selling, general & administrative fell $30.1 million (–11.4 %) to **$233.8 million**, driven by synergy capture and lower integration/divestiture costs.  R&D rose modestly to $23.0 million; intangible amortization was $62.9 million.

### Restructuring & Other Impairment  

Total 2024 charge: **$38.1 million** (1.9 % of sales) vs. $22.6 million in 2023.  
• $16.4 million relates to the 2024 organizational realignment (severance), split $10.0 m SAS, $3.1 m FAM, $3.3 m unallocated.  
• SAS also booked a $16.2 million impairment tied to the sale of the Eerbeek, Netherlands facility.

| SAS | $45.4 m | –$376.3 m | +$421.7 m |

### Interest, Taxes & EPS  

• Interest expense: **$74.7 million**, up 20 %, on a 6.41 % blended rate (5.98 % in 2023) owing to higher floating-rate balances.  
• Effective tax rate: 38.3 % benefit vs. –5.6 % expense in 2023; change driven by the absence of non-deductible impairment and valuation-allowance adjustments.  
• Diluted EPS from continuing operations: **–$0.90** (vs. –$9.33).

### Discontinued Operations  

No activity in 2024.  The $198.2 million after-tax income in 2023 included a $170.0 million after-tax gain on the EP sale.

## Working Capital  

Operating working capital (including cash) fell to **$386.2 million** from $433.9 million, mainly on lower cash balances.

## Outlook  

Management expects:

• FAM long-term growth above general economic trends in the U.S. and Europe; SAS growth roughly in line with broad GDP.  
• Continued focus on synergy realization, price-cost discipline, and cash generation to delever toward targeted leverage metrics.  
• Monitoring macro pressures (construction, automotive) that could trigger interim goodwill testing in 2025.

---

## Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures about Market Risk  

• Foreign currency: a 10 % unfavorable move would cut pre-tax earnings by ~$35.2 million; selective use of forward and swap contracts mitigates risk.  
• Interest rates: after existing swaps, 89 % of debt is fixed; a 100 bp rate rise would lower annual pre-tax earnings by ~$1.2 million.  
• Commodity prices: a 10 % rise in resin costs would reduce pre-tax earnings by ~$19.4 million; a similar pulp move would cut ~$14.1 million.  
• Energy: a 10 % increase in purchased-energy prices would lower pre-tax earnings by ~$6.3 million.
