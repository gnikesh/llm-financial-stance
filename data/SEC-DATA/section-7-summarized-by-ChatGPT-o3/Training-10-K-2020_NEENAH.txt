# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations – Summary  

## Introduction  
Management discusses 2020 operating performance, financial condition and prospects, comparing results with 2019 and explaining major drivers, notably the COVID-19 pandemic. Topics addressed include the overview of the business, segment performance, liquidity, capital resources, accounting pronouncements and critical estimates.

---

## Overview of Business  
Neenah, Inc. is a global producer of specialty materials operating two reportable segments:

1. **Technical Products** – filtration media, tape and abrasive backings, performance labels, digital transfer papers and other engineered substrates, produced in Germany, the Netherlands, the U.K. and the U.S.  
2. **Fine Paper and Packaging** – premium print, packaging and specialty papers primarily sold in North America, produced in Wisconsin and Massachusetts (with shared U.S. facilities for certain items).

The company seeks growth in niche markets where product performance and brand image command a premium, emphasizing leadership positions, cost control, optimal capital allocation and risk management.

---

## Results of Operations and Related Information  

### Impact of COVID-19  
Both segments continued operating as “essential,” yet pandemic-related demand contraction materially reduced volumes, net sales and earnings. Management curtailed discretionary spending, capital outlays, stock repurchases and payroll costs, leveraged government relief programs and consolidated manufacturing to protect liquidity and employee safety.

### Executive Summary (all figures for year ended 31 Dec 2020 vs. 2019)  
• Consolidated net sales fell 16 % to **$792.6 million** (2019: $938.5 million).  
  – Technical Products down 6 % to $508.9 million.  
  – Fine Paper & Packaging (“FP&P”) down 29 % to $283.7 million.  
• GAAP operating income swung to a **loss of $6.1 million** (2019 income: $78.3 million).  
  – Adjusted operating income: **$64.4 million** (2019: $83.1 million).  
  – Adjusting items totaled **$70.5 million** (non-cash impairments, restructuring, COVID-19 costs, debt extinguishment, pension/SERP settlements and M&A due-diligence).  
• Cash from operations: **$93.4 million** (-$4.2 million YoY).  
• Capital expenditures: **$18.9 million** (-$2.5 million YoY).  
• Cash & equivalents at year-end: **$37.1 million**; total debt: **$194.4 million**; net debt reduced **$34.5 million**.  
• No revolver borrowings outstanding; **$138.6 million** of availability.

## Critical Accounting Policies and Estimates  
Key areas requiring significant judgment include inventory valuation (LIFO), income-tax uncertainties and valuation allowances, pension/OPEB assumptions (discount rate, expected return, healthcare cost trend), long-lived asset and goodwill impairment analyses, and acquisition accounting. • Pension expense: $4.0 million; OPEB expense: $2.9 million; weighted discount rate 2.28 % (pensions).

## Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk – Highlights  

• **Commodity risk**: A $100/ton pulp price rise would increase annual costs ~$22 million.  
• **FX risk**: 10 % USD strengthening would reduce pretax income by ~$3.9 million and shareholders’ equity by ~$25 million (translation).  
• **Interest-rate risk**: 100 bp rate increase would raise annual interest expense on variable-rate debt by ~$2 million.  

Management employs operating and financing strategies to mitigate these exposures and does not expect material adverse impacts under reasonably foreseeable scenarios.

---

### Overall Financial Position  
Despite a pandemic-driven 16 % revenue contraction and a GAAP operating loss, Neenah generated solid operating cash flow, preserved liquidity, reduced net debt and maintained access to substantial committed credit, positioning the company to invest selectively and return capital to shareholders while navigating ongoing market recovery.
