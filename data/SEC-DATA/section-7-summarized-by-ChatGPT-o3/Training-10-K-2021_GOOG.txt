# ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT’S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS — SUMMARY  

The following narrative preserves the logical flow and major headings of the original Management’s Discussion and Analysis while condensing the content. All significant quantitative and qualitative disclosures—especially those concerning revenues, EPS, debt, cash flows, and other key financial metrics—have been retained.

## Trends in Our Business  

Management continues to highlight several secular forces: the sustained migration of users and advertisers to digital channels; a proliferation of access devices that is changing ad‐format mix and compressing margins; rising contributions from lower-monetizing emerging markets; a steadily larger share of non-advertising revenue (Cloud, Play, hardware, YouTube subscriptions) that carries lower gross margins; and an investment agenda that remains capital-intensive across data centres, offices, cloud infrastructure, M&A and strategic funds such as the $10 billion Google for India Digitization Fund.  In parallel, evolving regulatory regimes and the competition for talent are expected to drive costs higher over time.

---

## The Impact of COVID-19 on Results and Operations  

COVID-19 produced a sharp, two-phased impact during 2020.  In March and the entire second quarter, commercial search queries and advertiser spend fell, depressing Google advertising revenue.  Through the third and fourth quarters, query mix normalised and advertiser budgets recovered, particularly in direct-response formats, stabilising margins.  The Company recorded higher allowances for credit losses, faced valuation volatility in equity investments, and deliberately moderated the cadence of new office construction, marketing, and travel.  Management continues to evaluate hiring, data-centre builds, and other discretionary outlays as the pandemic evolves, while accelerating investment in Google Cloud to capture the structural shift to digital.

## Our Segments  

Beginning Q4-20 Alphabet reports three operating segments:  

• Google Services (ads, Android, Chrome, Maps, Play, Search, YouTube, hardware, and subscription products)  
• Google Cloud (Google Cloud Platform and Google Workspace)  
• Other Bets (early-stage businesses combined because no single unit is material)

Unallocated corporate costs include shared functional expenses, R&D programs, and revenue-hedging results.

Paid clicks on Google Search & other properties grew 19 % while cost-per-click fell 10 %; impressions on Network properties rose 15 % and cost-per-impression declined 8 %.  Regionally, the United States generated 47 % of revenue, EMEA 30 %, APAC 18 %, and Other Americas 5 %.

### Costs and Operating Expenses  

Cost of revenues climbed 18 % to $84.7 billion; TAC increased 9 % to $32.8 billion, keeping the TAC rate stable at 22.3 %.  Other cost of revenues grew 24 % due to higher data-centre and content-acquisition expenses (especially YouTube), partly offset by lower hardware costs.  

Operating expenses were $56.6 billion (+5 %).  R&D rose 6 % to $27.6 billion (15.1 % of revenue).  Sales & Marketing fell 3 % to $17.9 billion (9.8 % of revenue) because of reduced advertising and travel.  General & Administrative expenses increased 16 % to $11.1 billion (6.1 % of revenue), reflecting headcount growth and a higher allowance for credit losses.

### Other Income (Expense), Net  

Other income was $6.9 billion, up $1.5 billion, driven by $3.5 billion of higher net gains on equity and debt securities, partly offset by a $0.9 billion derivative loss and lower interest income.

### Taxes  

The effective tax rate increased to 16.2 % (from 13.3 %) largely because 2019 benefitted from audit resolutions that did not recur, a less favorable geographic earnings mix, and higher valuation allowances on deferred tax assets.

## Liquidity and Capital Resources  

Alphabet closed 2020 with $136.7 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.  Operating activities produced $65.1 billion of cash; investing activities used $32.8 billion; financing activities used $24.4 billion, mainly for $31.1 billion of Class C share repurchases.  

Capital expenditures of $22.3 billion were weighted toward technical infrastructure, while office construction slowed because of COVID-19.  Operating lease assets added another $2.8 billion.  Future contractual obligations total $62.4 billion, including $15.1 billion in operating-lease commitments, $10.7 billion in purchase obligations, $19.8 billion in long-term debt service, and $7.4 billion in transition taxes payable through 2025.

### Debt Profile  

In August 2020 the Company issued $10 billion of senior unsecured notes (average coupon 1.57 %, average duration 21.5 years), of which $5.75 billion are Sustainability Bonds.  Outstanding senior notes total $13.8 billion.  A $5 billion commercial-paper programme and $4 billion of undrawn revolving credit lines provide additional liquidity.  The balance sheet lists no commercial paper outstanding at year-end.

## Capital Return  

Under Board authorisation, Alphabet repurchased 21.5 million Class C shares for $31.1 billion in 2020 and retains $17.6 billion of authorisation with no expiration date.

## Market Risks  

The Company remains exposed to a 10 % adverse currency move that could reduce pre-tax income by an estimated $497 million despite hedging; a one-day 95 % VaR of $144 million on its marketable debt portfolio; and a hypothetical 30 % decline in marketable equity securities that would lower their fair value by roughly $1.8 billion.  Non-markable equity securities, carried at $18.9 billion, are subject to valuation and impairment risk, amplified by COVID-19–related uncertainty.

---

## Critical Accounting Policies and Forthcoming Change  

Key areas of judgement include revenue principal/agent determinations, income-tax contingencies, loss contingencies, long-lived-asset impairment, fair-value measurements, and valuation of non-marketable equity securities.  Effective January 2021 the Company is extending server useful lives from three to four years and certain network equipment from three to five years, a change expected to reduce 2021 depreciation expense by about $2.1 billion.

---

**In summary, Alphabet entered 2021 with record revenue, earnings, and cash generation; stronger operating margins; a reinforced balance sheet; and ample liquidity to fund ongoing growth initiatives, share repurchases, and contractual obligations, while continuing to navigate the operational uncertainties posed by COVID-19 and evolving regulation.**
