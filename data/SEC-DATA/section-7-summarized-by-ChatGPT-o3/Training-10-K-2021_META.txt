# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations — Summary  

(Amounts in USD unless otherwise noted)

## Executive Overview of Full-Year 2020 Results  
Facebook ended 2020 with strong community expansion and improved profitability despite COVID-19–related volatility.

• Community metrics: December-2020 Facebook DAUs averaged **1.84 billion** (+11 % y/y); MAUs reached **2.80 billion** (+12 %). Across the Family of apps, DAP averaged **2.60 billion** (+15 %) and MAP totaled **3.30 billion** (+14 %).  

• Financial metrics:  
– Revenue rose **22 %** to **$85.97 billion**; advertising revenue accounted for **$84.17 billion** (+21 %).  
– Total costs and expenses were **$53.29 billion**; income from operations was **$32.67 billion**, yielding a **38 % operating margin**.  
– Net income increased to **$29.15 billion** and diluted EPS to **$10.09**.  
– Capital expenditures (including finance-lease principal) totaled **$15.72 billion**.  
– Effective tax rate: **12.2 %** (down from 25.5 % in 2019).  
– Year-end cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities stood at **$61.95 billion**; the company carries no material debt.  
– Headcount rose 30 % to **58,604**.

• 2021 outlook: management projects **capital expenditures of $21-23 billion** and **total expenses of $68-73 billion** as it continues to build data-center capacity, network infrastructure, and headcount.

COVID-19 accelerated on-line engagement and e-commerce, boosting ad demand in the second half; however, management cautions that ad growth faces uncertainty from pandemic trends, platform privacy changes, and regulatory pressure.

---

## Trends in Facebook User Metrics  

• DAU/MAU ratio remained stable at **66 %** worldwide.  
• Geographic growth was led by India, the Philippines and Indonesia.  
• Nearly all users access the services on mobile devices.

---

## Monetization by Geography  

• 2020 worldwide ARPU (sum of quarterly ARPU) increased **10 %** y/y to **$32.03**.  
• Regional ARPU growth: United States & Canada +18 %, Europe +15 %, Asia-Pacific +9 %; “Rest of World” was flat.  
• U.S./Canada ARPU is more than **11×** Asia-Pacific ARPU; future user growth is expected to remain concentrated in lower-monetizing regions, tempering blended ARPU expansion.

---

## Family Metrics  

• Family DAP grew **15 %** to **2.60 billion**; Family MAP grew **14 %** to **3.30 billion**.  
• Worldwide ARPP reached **$27.51** (+8 %).

Key drivers:  
• Ads delivered rose **34 %**; average price per ad fell **10 %** (COVID-19 pressure in 1H, mix shift to Stories and lower-priced geographies).  
• R&D spending up **36 %** to **$18.45 billion** (21 % of revenue) on a 40 % engineering headcount increase.  
• Marketing & Sales up **17 %** to **$11.59 billion** (13 % of revenue).  
• Cost of revenue up **31 %** to **$16.69 billion** (19 % of revenue) due to data-center expansion, traffic-acquisition/content costs, and hardware units.  
• Share-based compensation was **$6.54 billion** (8 % of revenue).  

Quarterly trend: Q4-20 revenue **$28.07 billion**, net income **$11.22 billion**, diluted EPS **$3.88**; advertising remained ~97 % of revenue and seasonality persisted (Q4 ad revenue +28 % q/q).

Foreign exchange: the stronger U.S. dollar reduced 2020 revenue by roughly **$0.12 billion** versus constant-currency.

## Taxation  

• 2020 effective tax rate fell to **12.2 %** (vs. 25.5 %) driven by absence of non-deductible 2019 FTC charge, 2019 Altera-related expense, and a 2020 election to capitalize & amortize certain R&D costs (yielding a **$1.07 billion** benefit).  
• Unrecognized tax benefits total **$3.48 billion**; ongoing IRS transfer-pricing disputes could add up to **$9 billion** (plus interest/penalties) of additional federal tax if the IRS prevails.

## Critical Accounting Policies and Estimates (Highlights)  

• Revenue recognition (gross vs. net), collectibility assessments amidst COVID-19, expected-credit-loss modeling, and valuation of equity investments.  
• Income-tax judgments (uncertain tax positions, Altera outcome).  
• Goodwill/intangible-asset impairment and useful-life estimates.

## Market Risk  

• FX: Euro and other non-USD currencies affect revenues/expenses; 2020 FX losses **$129 million**.  
• Interest-rate sensitivity: a 100 bp rate increase would reduce AFS debt-security values by **$794 million**.  
• Equity-investment risk: privately-held stakes carried at **$6.23 billion** and evaluated for impairment each period; pandemic-era valuation volatility noted.

---

### Debt  

The company reported **no long-term or short-term borrowings** as of December 31 2020.

---

## Outlook and Considerations  

Management expects continued ad-revenue growth to hinge on:  
1. Durability of the pandemic-driven shift to on-line commerce;  
2. Pace of global macroeconomic recovery and fiscal stimulus;  
3. Impact of regulatory changes and mobile OS/browser privacy initiatives on ad targeting and measurement.

Expense growth will track investments in infrastructure, headcount, product innovation, privacy & safety, and support for advertisers. Facebook believes its strong cash position and free-cash-flow generation will fund these priorities alongside an expanded share-repurchase program.
