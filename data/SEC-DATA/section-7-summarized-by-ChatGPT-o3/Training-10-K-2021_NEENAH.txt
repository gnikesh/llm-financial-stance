## Introduction  
The 2021 discussion compares results with 2020 and should be read with the consolidated financial statements. The Company uses both GAAP and certain non-GAAP measures (primarily “adjusted operating income”) to illustrate underlying operating trends. Forward-looking statements are subject to risk.

---

## Overview of Business  
Neenah is a global producer of specialty materials with two reportable segments:  
• Technical Products – filtration media, specialty tapes and liners, abrasive backings, digital transfer papers, and other engineered substrates produced in dedicated facilities in Europe, Mexico and the U.S.  
• Fine Paper and Packaging – premium printing, packaging and specialty papers produced mainly in North America.  

The mission is to make critical components that deliver performance and image advantages in markets where the Company can hold or gain a leadership position. 2021 strategy focused on: safety, cost control, capital discipline, and growth through acquisition (Itasa) and organic volume recovery.

---

## Business Segments  
In January 2021 the publishing grades formerly reported in Technical Products ($26.3 net sales; $0.9 operating loss in 2020) were realigned to Fine Paper and Packaging. On 6 April 2021 Neenah acquired Global Release Liners, S.L. (“Itasa”) for $240.2 (including debt extinguishment and FX hedge losses). Itasa, now in Technical Products, added $106.9 of 2021 sales. Acquisition costs totaled $12.8, of which $5.1 related to forward-contract losses; transaction funded with cash and a new $450 Term Loan B.

### Net Sales  
Sales rebounded strongly from COVID-19 lows:

• Technical Products rose 38 % to $664.2, driven by organic volume recovery, $106.9 from Itasa, and $8.3 in price increases, partly offset by an unfavorable value mix (supply-chain-driven).  
• Fine Paper and Packaging rose 18 % to $364.3, reflecting higher volumes across categories, $10.6 in price increases and richer product mix.

Currency added $11.9 to consolidated growth.

Key drivers:  
• Volume recovery and acquisition synergies (+$49.8).  
• Net pricing/mix (+$6.5) offset by sharp cost inflation in pulp, energy, chemicals, freight (–$46.7).  
• Higher labor turnover, training, bonus and overtime reduced productivity.  
• Unallocated corporate costs include $16.5 pension settlement loss and $12.6 acquisition costs; in November the Company annuitized $64 of U.S. pension obligations, triggering a $15.6 settlement charge but expected $7 lifetime savings.

Adjusted operating income by segment: Technical Products $51.1 (-4 %), Fine Paper & Packaging $41.7 (+37 %), Corporate (20.8) (worse by 1.8).

Gross margin fell to 15.9 % from 19.3 %, primarily on cost inflation and mix; SG&A rose to 9.9 % of sales (Itasa inclusion and normalization of 2020 cost curtailments). Interest expense increased to $17.9 (vs. $12.6) owing to the upsized Term Loan B. The effective tax rate remained a 16 % benefit, reflecting pretax loss, valuation-allowance changes, CARES Act NOL carrybacks and write-off of a $2.9 deferred tax asset related to the Appleton closure.

### Significant Special Charges  
• Appleton, Wisconsin mill permanently closed; $32.4 non-cash impairment + $4.9 other restructuring (obsolete inventory, severance, environmental).  
• Total 2021 impairment & restructuring: $37.3 (vs. $57.8 in 2020).  
• Loss on debt extinguishment: $7.2 from replacing the 2020 Term Loan B.

## Liquidity and Capital Resources  

### Cash Flow  
Operating cash flow fell by $40.2 to $53.2, mainly from $218 sales rebound requiring higher working capital and payment of $2.2 deferred 2020 payroll taxes plus higher foreign tax payments.  

Investing used $264.3 (vs. $19.5) largely for the Itasa purchase ($240.2) and higher capex ($28.2).  

Financing provided $199.2 (vs. used $47.0) principally from net debt issuances to fund Itasa; share repurchases were $5.0 and dividends $31.9 (unchanged per-share payout of $1.885 for 2021).

### Capitalization & Facilities  
• Cash 12/31/21: $23.9 (-$13.2 YOY); only $0.4 held in U.S.  
• Debt 12/31/21: $441.3 (Term Loan B $448.7 variable; €1.9 in German project loans), maturing April 2028; scheduled principal $6.4 due in 2022.  
• Net leverage increased; total leverage ratio >3.0× but <4.0×, restricting discretionary dividends/repurchases above $8.75 per quarter unless basket capacity used.  
• Global Revolving Credit Facility: $175 commitment, $0.9 drawn, $0.3 LCs, $146.1 available, matures December 2023.  
• Material cash requirements (undiscounted): $818 over all years, with $161 in 2022 dominated by open purchase orders ($116.5) and debt/interest ($23.2).  

Management believes existing liquidity and borrowing capacity are sufficient for the next 12 months; long-term cash generation depends on cost control, pricing actions, demand trends and successful strategy execution.

## Critical Accounting Policies and Estimates  
Key areas requiring significant judgment include inventory costing (LIFO in U.S.), income-tax valuation allowances and uncertain positions, pension assumptions (discount rate, return on assets, mortality), impairment testing for long-lived and indefinite-lived assets (trigger events included Appleton closure), and purchase-price allocation for acquisitions (Itasa).  

---

## Conclusion  
2021 showed sharp top-line recovery (+30 %) and accretive acquisition growth, but profitability was pressured by unprecedented input-cost inflation, supply-chain constraints and labor inefficiencies, resulting in a GAAP operating loss but higher adjusted earnings. Liquidity remains adequate after the leveraged Itasa purchase, with manageable near-term debt service and strong revolver availability. Strategic priorities include further pricing/mix improvement, integration synergies, cost mitigation, safety performance, and disciplined capital allocation to achieve long-term value creation.
