## ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT’S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS – SUMMARY  

### Overview  
NVIDIA, headquartered in Santa Clara, California, invented accelerated computing and now addresses Gaming, Professional Visualization, Data Center, Automotive, OEM & Other markets. Beginning in fiscal 2021 the company reports through two segments:  

* **Graphics** – GeForce GPUs and services for gaming/PCs, NVIDIA RTX/Quadro for workstations, vGPU software, automotive infotainment.  
* **Compute & Networking** – Data Center AI/HPC platforms, Mellanox networking, automotive AI cockpit & autonomous driving solutions, Jetson embedded/robotics.  

### Recent Developments, Future Objectives and Challenges  

#### Pending Acquisition of Arm Limited  
• Announced September 13 2020; total value ≈ $40 billion.  
• Consideration: $2 billion cash paid at signing; at closing NVIDIA will pay $10 billion cash and issue 44.3 million shares (≈ $21.5 billion).  
• Earn-out of up to $5 billion (cash or 10.3 million shares) tied to Arm’s FY22 performance; up to $1.5 billion in RSUs for Arm employees post-close.  
• If the agreement is terminated under specified conditions, $1.25 billion of the $2 billion Signing Consideration will be refunded.  
• Subject to regulatory approvals in the U.S., U.K., EU, China and other jurisdictions; closing is expected in Q1 CY 2022.  

#### Demand and Supply Dynamics  
Lead-times remain long; NVIDIA may place non-cancellable orders, pay premiums or deposits to secure capacity. Gaming GPUs are sensitive to cryptocurrency-driven demand volatility. COVID-19–driven work-from-home trends have boosted Gaming and Data Center demand but complicate forecasting.

#### COVID-19  
Most employees have worked remotely since March 2020. Gaming and Data Center platforms experienced stronger demand; Professional Visualization desktop demand is recovering; Automotive is largely normalized. Supply-chain tightness, especially for Gaming, persists. Management believes existing cash, equivalents and marketable securities plus short-term facilities are sufficient for ongoing liquidity needs.

Professional Visualization introduced new mobile workstations, Quadro View and AI partnerships (e.g., Adobe, Autodesk).  
Data Center unveiled the A100 GPU/DGX A100, BlueField-2 DPU, EGX Edge AI platform, VMware partnership; powered most TOP500 supercomputers; set 16 MLPerf records.  
Automotive secured design wins with Mercedes-Benz (software-defined vehicles from 2024), Hyundai, SAIC, Nio, Li Auto, Xpeng, Zoox and Einride.

## ITEM 7A. QUANTITATIVE AND QUALITATIVE DISCLOSURES ABOUT MARKET RISK – SUMMARY  

### Investment and Interest-Rate Risk  
A ±0.5 % parallel shift in the yield curve would change the fair value of the fixed-rate investment portfolio by –$24 million / +$8 million. All outstanding notes are fixed-rate; therefore, reported interest expense is not rate-sensitive.

### Foreign Exchange Risk  
Sales, manufacturing contracts and debt are U.S.-dollar-denominated; direct FX exposure is limited. NVIDIA hedges forecast operating expenses and monetary assets/liabilities with forward contracts:  
• A 10 % U.S.-dollar strengthening would lower pre-tax AOCI on designated cash-flow hedges by ≈ $84 million (31 Jan 2021) and ≈ $43 million (26 Jan 2020).  
• A 10 % adverse move on balance-sheet hedges would reduce income before tax by ≈ $44 million (2021) and ≈ $29 million (2020); the effect is offset by re-measurement of the underlying positions.
