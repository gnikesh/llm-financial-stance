# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations – Condensed Summary  

The following narrative preserves the logical flow of the original MD&A while condensing non-essential prose.  All material figures, trends and qualitative explanations concerning sales, earnings, debt, cash flow, liquidity, capital commitments and outlook are retained.

---

## COVID-19 Pandemic  
Management reports that all plants remain operational, customer interruptions have been “isolated and temporary,” and dialogue across the supply chain is continuous.  Demand rebounded strongly in 2021 (especially in transportation and construction), yet the ensuing global recovery drove sharp inflation in polypropylene, wood pulp, freight, energy and other inputs, pressuring margins.

---

## Liquidity & Debt Overview  
At 31 December 2021 the Company carried **$1.270 billion** of total debt and **$74.7 million** of cash.  Net leverage, as defined in the $700 million Credit Agreement, stood at **4.8× EBITDA** versus a temporary covenant ceiling of 5.5×.  Undrawn capacity on the **$500 million Revolving Credit Facility** equalled **$102.2 million**; the Revolver is the nearest maturity (2023).  A new **$350 million Term Loan B** was added in February 2021 to fund the Scapa purchase.

---

## SUMMARY  

• 2021 net sales were **$1.440 billion**, up **34 %** or **$365.6 million** versus 2020.  
 – **$305.6 million** came from the April-2021 acquisition of **Scapa**;  
 – Underlying AMS sales (ex-Scapa) rose >20 % in filtration, transportation and construction; healthcare sales fell against 2020’s pandemic-driven spike.  
 – Engineered Papers (EP) sales declined **4 %** (**$21.6 million**) on lower tobacco-related volume and unfavourable mix, partly offset by **3 %** positive FX and growth in heat-not-burn (HnB) products.  

• Net income increased to **$88.9 million** (2020: $83.8 million); diluted EPS rose to **$2.80** (2020: $2.66).  
 – Earnings absorbed **$21.4 million** of Scapa transaction/integration expenses, **$17.5 million** of incremental purchase-accounting amortisation and **$6.9 million** of acquisition-related FX loss.  
 – These were partly offset by a **$35.2 million** gain on sale of the closed Spotswood, NJ site.  
 – Tax expense swung to a **$9.4 million benefit** from an $18.4 million provision, driving a (12.9 %) effective rate.  

• Operating cash flow fell to **$58.1 million** (2020: $161.6 million) because of working-capital outflows, higher inventories (inflated resin/pulp costs) and transaction cash costs.  

• Key 2021 cash uses/proceeds:  
 – **$630.6 million** for Scapa acquisition (net consideration)  
 – **$688.6 million** net debt issuances  
 – **$55.3 million** dividends to shareholders  
 – **$38.9 million** capital expenditures (incl. software)  
 – **$35.3 million** proceeds from Spotswood property sale

## LIQUIDITY AND CAPITAL RESOURCES  

### Cash Flow and Working Capital  
Operating working capital ended 2021 at **$366.7 million**, up from $232.3 million, largely due to Scapa consolidation and cost-inflated inventories.  Working-capital changes consumed **$61.4 million** cash (2020 use: $5.7 million).

### Investing & Financing  
Capital spending of **$38.9 million** targeted asset maintenance, new-product development, paper-machine rebuilds, cost reductions and IT upgrades.  Financing inflows of **$688.6 million** consisted of $744.5 million new borrowings (Revolver draw, Term Loan B) less $55.9 million principal repayments; the Company also issued $55.3 million in dividends and repurchased $3.4 million of shares for employee-tax settlements.

Total debt-to-capital (Credit-Agreement basis) was **65.1 %** at year-end 2021 (2020: 47.7 %).  The Company was in full covenant compliance; the amended agreement gradually steps the net-debt/EBITDA limit back to 4.5× by mid-2023.

### Contractual Obligations  
Aggregate future cash obligations total **$1.695 billion**, of which **$169.5 million** falls in 2022.  Key five-year buckets include $399.7 million Revolver maturities (2023) and $192.5 million Term Loan A (2025).  Pension plans were underfunded by **$11.4 million**; plan assets stood at $335.4 million.

## OTHER FACTORS AFFECTING LIQUIDITY AND CAPITAL RESOURCES  
The Company outlines purchase commitments for raw materials ($55.0 million) and energy ($43.1 million), restructuring cash obligations ($6.2 million), lease liabilities ($29.8 million) and the remaining U.S. Tax Act transition-tax instalments ($18.8 million).

## OUTLOOK  
Management expects:  
• AMS to generate above-GDP organic growth in water/process filtration, surface-protection films and healthcare; construction and industrial sales should track broader economic activity.  
• EP to face continued secular cigarette decline, FX swings and potential regulatory/tax shifts, but HnB growth provides a partial offset.  
• Overall profit growth is projected, contingent on raw-material inflation moderation and continued cost-recovery pricing.  With AMS now the larger segment post-Scapa, SWM views its long-term sales and earnings trajectory more favourably diversified.

---

## FORWARD-LOOKING STATEMENTS  
All projections are subject to the extensive risk factors enumerated in the filing, including pandemic impacts, commodity inflation, supply-chain disruptions, regulatory changes, competitive dynamics, successful integration of acquisitions, labour constraints and financial-market volatility.
