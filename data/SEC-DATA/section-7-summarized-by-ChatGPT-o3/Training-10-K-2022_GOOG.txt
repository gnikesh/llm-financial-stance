# ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT’S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS — SUMMARY

The following narrative condenses Alphabet’s 2021 Form 10-K MD&A while preserving all material quantitative and qualitative information. Headings mirror the original structure; tables reproduce the principal financial metrics. Currency is U.S. dollars unless noted.

## Understanding Alphabet’s Financial Results

Alphabet continues to report three segments: Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets.  Google Services supplies the bulk of sales and earnings; Google Cloud is scaling but remains loss-making; Other Bets are early-stage ventures.

## Trends in Our Business and Financial Effect

Management reiterates six multi-year trends: the migration of consumer and advertiser activity online; rising mobile and multi-device usage with lower margins; evolving ad formats such as YouTube and Play that monetize at lower rates than Search; increasing contributions from emerging markets accompanied by foreign-exchange (FX) volatility; a growing mix of lower-margin non-advertising revenues (Cloud, Play, hardware, subscriptions); and sustained heavy investment in R&D and capex.  Regulatory scrutiny, talent investment, and seasonality also remain structural factors.

## Other Income (Expense), Net

Other income rose to $12.0 billion (from $6.9 billion) on larger unrealized gains in marketable and non-marketable equity securities, partly offset by higher performance fee accruals.

## Provision for Income Taxes

Tax expense doubled to $14.7 billion as pretax earnings rose, while the effective tax rate held at 16.2 % thanks to stock-based compensation and FDII deductions.

Capital expenditures were $24.6 billion (data centers, servers, office real estate).  Alphabet repurchased $50.3 billion of stock (20.3 million shares), leaving $17.4 billion under the April/July 2021 authorization.  The Board declared a 20-for-1 stock split effective July 2022.

## Capital Structure, Debt, and Commitments

• Senior unsecured notes outstanding: $12.8 billion with $4.2 billion future interest.  
• Commercial paper capacity: $10 billion; none drawn.  
• Revolving credit facilities: $10 billion (4- and 5-year tranches); undrawn.  
• Operating lease liabilities: $15.5 billion (8-year weighted average term).  
• Purchase commitments (mainly data centers/hardware): $13.7 billion.  
• Transition tax payable under the 2017 U.S. Tax Act: $6.5 billion, due through 2025.  
• European Commission antitrust fines totaling €8.2 billion remain under appeal and are secured by bank guarantees.

## The Effect of COVID-19 on Financial Results

Advertising revenue fell sharply in Q2-2020 but recovered as commercial search activity resumed, distorting 2021 growth rates.  Management warns that the pandemic’s trajectory could still affect demand, costs, credit losses, and investment valuations.

## Capital Expenditures and Leases

Alphabet invested $24.6 billion in property and equipment (up from $22.3 billion), chiefly server and network hardware and data-center construction.  Depreciation on PP&E declined to $11.6 billion.  Operating lease assets rose to $3.0 billion; annual lease expense was $3.4 billion.

## Share Repurchase Program

The April 2021 authorization for $50 billion of Class C stock was amended in July to include Class A shares.  As noted, $17.4 billion of capacity remained at 12/31/21.

## Risk Management and Market Risk

Alphabet hedges major currency exposures (AUD, GBP, CAD, EUR, JPY) with forwards/options, reducing but not eliminating FX volatility.  A 10 % adverse move would cut 2021 pretax income by ~$285 million after hedges.  Interest-rate VaR on the fixed-income portfolio was $139 million (one-day, 95 % confidence).  A 10 % price drop in marketable equities would trim their fair value by ~$0.8 billion; non-marketable stakes carry higher valuation risk but are re-measured only on observable transactions or impairment.

## Critical Accounting Estimates

Key judgments concern:  
1. Fair value of non-marketable equity securities;  
2. Depreciable lives of servers/network gear (extended in 2021);  
3. Income-tax uncertainties;  
4. Legal contingencies.

Management believes estimates are reasonable but notes material variability is possible.

# ITEM 7A. QUANTITATIVE AND QUALITATIVE DISCLOSURES ABOUT MARKET RISK — SUMMARY

Alphabet remains exposed to FX, interest-rate, and equity-price fluctuations, but mitigates these through a diversified investment portfolio, derivative hedges, and conservative liquidity management.  The company’s cash generation, $139.6 billion liquidity pool, low leverage, and undrawn credit lines afford substantial capacity to absorb market shocks.

---

Overall, Alphabet finished 2021 with record revenue of $257.6 billion, operating margin of 31 %, net income of $76.0 billion, and diluted EPS of $112.20, achieved while enlarging cash holdings, repurchasing $50.3 billion of stock, investing $24.6 billion in growth capex, and keeping net debt negligible.
