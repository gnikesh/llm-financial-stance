# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations  
(The headings mirror the original document for ease of reference. All figures are in U.S. dollars unless otherwise noted.)

## Management’s Discussion and Analysis – Overview  
Mativ Holdings, Inc. (“Mativ” or “the Company”) completed its transformational merger with Neenah, Inc. on 6 July 2022. 2022 results therefore include Neenah from the acquisition date.  Prior‐year comparatives include the April 2021 Scapa acquisition.  Throughout 2022 the Company faced substantial merger‐related costs, higher input prices, a cyber-attack, and rising interest rates, all of which materially influenced earnings, cash flow and leverage.

## Merger  
• Each Neenah share was exchanged for 1.358 Mativ shares; c. 22.8 million new shares were issued, valued at $534.1 million.  
• A $650 million Delayed-Draw Term Loan funded at closing repaid Neenah’s $504.9 million debt and repaid $100 million on Mativ’s revolver.  
• Post-closing, the Company adopted two reportable segments:  
  – Advanced Technical Materials (ATM) – legacy SWM AMS + allocated Neenah businesses  
  – Fiber-Based Solutions (FBS) – legacy SWM Engineered Papers + allocated Neenah businesses  
• “MATV” began trading on the NYSE on 6 July 2022.

## Critical Accounting Estimates (snapshot)  
Management highlights complex judgments in income taxes, revenue recognition, contingencies, PP&E valuation, business combinations, and goodwill/intangibles testing.  No goodwill or indefinite-lived intangible impairments were recorded in 2022.

## Summary of 2022 Consolidated Performance  
• Net sales: $2.167 billion, +50.5 % versus 2021, driven by Neenah, price increases, and select volume growth, partially offset by adverse FX.  
• Gross profit: $437.6 million (+32.5 %); gross margin contracted to 20.2 % (from 22.9 %) due to higher pulp, resin, energy and $19.3 million in purchase-accounting COGS.  
• Operating profit: $51.4 million (-38.3 %); operating margin fell to 2.4 %.  
• Net loss: $(6.6) million versus 2021 net income of $88.9 million.  
• Diluted EPS: $(0.18) versus $2.80.  
• Merger & integration costs: $72.6 million; non-cash purchase-accounting charges: $29.4 million; restructuring & impairment: $19.3 million.  
• Weighted-average diluted shares: 42.7 million (vs. 31.4 million in 2021).

### Gross Profit  
Gross profit rose $107.3 million to $437.6 million; however, margin compressed 270 bps due to unfavorable mix, inflation and purchase-accounting COGS.

### Non-Manufacturing Expenses  
Selling, R&D and general expenses increased 54.9 % to $366.9 million (16.9 % of sales) largely from:  
• $72.6 million direct/indirect merger costs  
• $10 million incremental intangible amortization  
• Inclusion of Neenah overhead.

### Restructuring & Impairment  
Expense doubled to $19.3 million:  
• ATM: $17.2 million (write-down and loss on sale of an industrial-products unit, Appleton facility closure, contract termination).  
• FBS: $1.3 million (benefits linked to Winkler closure).  
• Corporate: $0.8 million lease modifications tied to the merger.

### Interest, Taxes and Earnings  
• Interest expense: $86.1 million (up $40 million) reflecting Neenah debt assumption and higher floating rates; weighted-average cost = 5.11 %.  
• Other income: $10.3 million (vs. $35.9 million 2021) – prior year benefited from $35.2 million Spotswood gain; 2022 included $7.3 million CO₂ credit sales.  
• Income-tax benefit: $12.6 million; effective rate 51.6 % versus (12.9 %) in 2021; swings driven by valuation-allowance adjustments and merger items.  
• Equity affiliates: $5.2 million (down $1.2 million) on higher input costs in Chinese JVs.

## Outlook  
ATM (≈ 65 % of post-merger sales) is projected to outpace GDP over the cycle, supported by filtration, protective solutions, release liners and healthcare. Cost synergies from the Neenah merger are anticipated over one-to-three years. FBS faces secular decline in traditional cigarette papers but seeks growth in premium packaging and consumer papers. Near-term visibility is tempered by inflation, higher interest rates and geopolitical uncertainty, especially in Europe, but management expects long-term organic growth, strong cash generation, and steady deleveraging.

## Quantitative and Qualitative Market Risk  
• Foreign exchange: a 10 % adverse rate movement would reduce pre-tax income by ≈ $16 million; selective hedging employed.  
• Interest rates: 100 bp rise on current variable-rate debt, net of swaps, would cut annual pre-tax earnings by ≈ $4 million.  
• Commodity inputs: 10 % rise in resin costs would cut pre-tax earnings ≈ $27.6 million; 10 % rise in wood-pulp costs ≈ $24.8 million; no commodity derivatives in place.  
• Energy: 10 % increase in purchased energy costs would lower pre-tax earnings ≈ $11.6 million; partial fixed-price contracts in place.
