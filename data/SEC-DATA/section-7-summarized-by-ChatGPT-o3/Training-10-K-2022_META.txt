# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations — Condensed Summary  
*(All amounts are in U.S. dollars; fiscal year ended 31 December 2021 unless otherwise noted.)*

You should read this condensed MD&A in conjunction with the full Form 10-K. It preserves every material quantitative or qualitative statement related to revenue, EPS, cash-flow, capital-allocation, debt-like commitments, tax, and forward-looking expense or sales targets.

## Executive Overview of Full-Year 2021 Results

Family of Apps (FoA) usage continued to expand: December 2021 Family daily active people (DAP) averaged **2.82 billion** (+8 % y/y) and monthly active people (MAP) reached **3.59 billion** (+9 % y/y). Facebook-specific DAUs averaged **1.93 billion** (+5 %) and MAUs were **2.91 billion** (+4 %).

Advertising volume and pricing were strong. Ad impressions across FoA rose **10 %**, while average price per ad increased **24 %**.

The company reorganised reporting into two segments:

| 2021 vs 2020 | Family of Apps | Reality Labs | Consolidated |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|--------------|
| Revenue      | **$115.66 bn** (+36 %) | **$2.27 bn** (+100 %) | **$117.93 bn** (+37 %) |
| Costs & expenses | $58.71 bn (+29 %) | $12.47 bn (+61 %) | $71.18 bn (+34 %) |
| Operating income (loss) | **$56.95 bn** (+45 %) | **$(10.19) bn** (loss ↑54 %) | **$46.75 bn** (+43 %) |
| Operating margin | 49 % (prior-year 46 %) | (448 %) | 40 % (prior-year 38 %) |

Net income was **$39.37 billion** and diluted EPS **$13.77**. The effective tax rate was **16.7 %**.

Other key 2021 figures  
• Capital expenditures (incl. finance leases): **$19.24 bn**  
• Free cash flow: **$38.44 bn**  
• Cash, cash equivalents & marketable securities: **$48.0 bn** (of which $10.6 bn held offshore)  
• Share repurchases: **$44.81 bn** (136 m Class A shares); $38.79 bn remaining authorisation  
• Headcount: **71,970** (+23 %)

Management emphasised four investment priorities—privacy/safety, new consumer experiences, business enablement, and transparency—while noting continuing headwinds from Apple iOS privacy changes, COVID-19 volatility, and competitive products (e.g., Reels monetises below feed/Stories).

---

## Outlook and 2022 Targets

• Capital expenditures projected at **$29-34 billion**.  
• Total 2022 expenses expected between **$90-95 billion**; year-over-year expense growth may “significantly exceed” revenue growth, pressuring operating margin.  
• No long-term funded debt; contractual commitments total **$52.33 bn**, dominated by operating-lease and infrastructure purchase agreements.  
• The company believes existing liquidity plus operating cash flow will cover operational needs, CAPEX and buybacks for at least the next 12 months and “for the foreseeable future”.

---

## Trends in Family Metrics

• DAP/MAP ratio remained stable at **78-79 %** throughout 2021.  
• Worldwide DAP increased from 2.60 bn to **2.82 bn**; MAP from 3.30 bn to **3.59 bn**.  
• Annual worldwide average revenue per person (ARPP) was **$33.68**, up **22 %**.

---

## Facebook-Specific User and Monetisation Trends

• Facebook DAU/MAU ratio held around **66 %** globally.  
• Top DAU growth markets: India, Bangladesh, Vietnam.  
• Annual worldwide ARPU reached **$40.96** (+28 %); U.S./Canada ARPU was over 12× that of Asia-Pacific.  
• Regional ARPU growth 2021: Rest of World +40 %, Europe +35 %, U.S./Canada +31 %, Asia-Pacific +26 %.  
• Management cautions ARPU growth may slow as future user growth skews toward lower-monetising regions.

---

## Revenue Discussion

FoA advertising revenue rose **37 %** to **$114.93 bn**, lifted by a 24 % price gain and 10 % more delivered ads. Other FoA revenue grew **10 %** to $721 m. RL revenue doubled to **$2.27 bn**, driven by higher sales volume of Quest, Portal and wearables.

Seasonality persists: historically strong Q4 followed by Q1 decline (e.g., 7 % sequential drop in Q1 2021).

Currency tailwinds: constant-currency revenue would have been **$1.64 bn** lower; FX contributed roughly 2 ppt to 2021 growth.

No single customer accounted for 10 %+ of revenue.

---

## Expense Discussion

• Cost of revenue **$22.65 bn** (+36 %); increases in RL cost of goods and data-centre opex partially offset by lower depreciation growth after extending server/network useful lives (cut 2021 depreciation $620 m, raised EPS $0.18).  
• R&D **$24.66 bn** (+34 %); headcount up 30 % in technical roles, incl. heavy RL investment.  
• Marketing & sales **$14.04 bn** (+21 %); promotional spend and 9 % staff growth.  
• G&A **$9.83 bn** (+50 %); higher legal costs and 25 % headcount growth. No additional FTC accrual booked in 2021 (the $5 bn settlement was expensed in 2019).

Total share-based compensation expense was **$9.16 bn** (8 % of revenue).

---

## Segment Profitability

FoA operating income climbed to **$56.95 bn** (+45 %), maintaining a 49 % margin. Reality Labs operating loss widened to **$(10.19) bn**, reflecting R&D hiring and hardware gross losses.

|-----------------|------------|
| Operating cash | **$57.68 bn** |

Finance-lease obligations total **$2.30 bn**; transition tax payable **$1.54 bn**. Off-balance-sheet arrangements are not material.

---

## Taxation

Provision for income taxes: **$7.91 bn** (up 96 %); effective tax rate **16.7 %** versus 12.2 % in 2020. Increase reflects higher pre-tax income and the 2020 R&D capitalization election that produced a one-time $1.07 bn benefit. Mandatory U.S. capitalization of R&D beginning 2022 may raise cash taxes “several billion dollars” but could lower the effective rate; if deferred, the 2022 ETR could be “a few percentage points higher.”

---

## Market Risks

• FX: 2021 foreign-currency losses **$140 m**; a strong USD would pressure revenues.  
• Interest: a 100 bp rate hike would cut the fair value of the debt-securities portfolio by ~$714 m.  
• Equity investments: privately-held stakes carried at **$6.78 bn**; subject to impairment based on market/operational conditions.

---

## Critical Accounting Policies

Summaries retained regarding gross-vs-net revenue recognition, valuation of equity investments, income-tax estimates, loss contingencies, and valuation of long-lived assets (including the 2021 change in server/network useful lives).

---

## Forward-Looking Statements and Risk Factors

Management reiterates that actual results could differ materially due to regulatory changes (e.g., privacy regimes, iOS tracking), competitive dynamics (notably monetisation gap for Reels), macroeconomic fluctuations, pandemic-related demand shifts, and ongoing legal or tax proceedings.
