## Overview  

3M remains a diversified global manufacturer that, beginning in the first quarter of 2022, revised both its measure of segment performance and the definition of “special items.”  The Company now reports four operating segments—Safety and Industrial, Transportation and Electronics, Health Care, and Consumer—and all historical information has been realigned to this structure.  Throughout 2022 3M faced persistent inflation, supply-chain constraints, the lingering effects of COVID-19, Russia-related market disruption, and sizeable litigation and PFAS-related charges.  Nevertheless it generated $34.23 billion of net sales and $10.18 of diluted EPS on a GAAP basis.  Special-item litigation, divestiture, Russia-exit, and PFAS charges aggregated to a $0.08 net EPS benefit in 2022 because the $4.73 per-share gain on the Food Safety split-off outweighed other costs.

Cost of sales rose to 56.2 % of sales (from 53.2 %) and SG&A to 26.5 % (from 20.4 %) primarily because of $1.3 billion of Combat Arms Earplugs charges, $355 million for Belgian PFAS remediation, a $0.8 billion PFAS-exit impairment, higher raw-material and logistics expense, and Russia-exit costs.  These headwinds more than offset $2.7 billion of divestiture gains, strong pricing and ongoing productivity initiatives.  

Organic volume and price added one percentage point to 2022 operating margin and $0.56 to EPS, but raw-material inflation removed 2.4 points (or $1.13 per share).  Foreign-exchange translation reduced EPS by $0.39, while a lower share count added $0.30.  The statutory tax rate fell to 9.6 % because the Food Safety gain was realized in a tax-efficient manner; on an adjusted basis the rate was 17.7 %.

## Performance by Geographic Area  

Worldwide sales decreased 3.2 %.  Organic growth was +2.6 % in the Americas, +0.3 % in Asia-Pacific, and –0.6 % in EMEA; translation lowered EMEA revenue almost ten percentage points.  United States revenue was flat with 1 % organic growth, while China fell 6 % (–3 % organic).  A stronger U.S. dollar cut operating income by $271 million, despite 3M’s multi-horizon hedging program.  

## Critical Accounting Estimates and New Pronouncements  

Key estimates relate to litigation reserves, pension discount rates and asset returns, goodwill and long-lived-asset impairment, and uncertain tax positions.  At year-end 2022 pension plans were 96 % funded globally and 97 % funded in the main U.S. plan; a higher 5.18 % discount rate offset 2022’s –17.4 % asset return, keeping funded status stable.  The expected long-term U.S. asset return assumption for 2023 was raised to 7.5 %.  Total goodwill stood at $12.8 billion; the PFAS-exit prompted an Advanced Materials impairment but no other reporting units showed impairment risk.  

## Financial Condition and Liquidity  

Cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities ended the year at $3.92 billion, down from $4.79 billion, after funding $3.37 billion of dividends, $1.46 billion of share repurchases, $1.75 billion of capital expenditures, and $1.18 billion of long-term debt repayments.  Free cash flow conversion was lower year-on-year, reflecting the litigation charge and mandatory U.S. R&D capitalization.  Net debt declined $0.55 billion to $12.02 billion, with gross debt of $15.94 billion.  Moody’s and S&P rate the Company A1 (stable) and A+ (credit-watch negative), respectively.  No commercial paper was outstanding at year-end; the $5 billion CP program remains available and supported by robust bank lines.  

Working capital fell to $5.17 billion (from $6.37 billion) as lower cash and higher short-term debt offset an inventory build of $387 million.  Capital spending is targeted at $1.5–1.8 billion for 2023 to enhance manufacturing capability and sustainability.  

## Cash Flows  

Operating cash flow declined to $5.59 billion mainly because the Food Safety gain was non-cash, litigation payments increased, and working-capital outflows totaled $0.62 billion.  Investing cash outflow was $1.05 billion, including $1.75 billion of PP&E partially offset by $478 million net cash received in the Food Safety transaction.  Financing consumed $5.35 billion: debt-net repayments of $0.84 billion, dividends of $3.37 billion, and treasury-share purchases of $1.46 billion.  As of December 31 2022 approximately $4.2 billion remained on the Company’s $10 billion share-repurchase authorization.  In February 2023 the quarterly dividend was raised 1 % to $1.50 per share, marking 65 consecutive annual increases.  

## Financial Instruments and Market Risk  

At year-end 2022 3M had $3.2 billion in notional foreign-exchange cash-flow and net-investment hedges plus $2.8 billion of non-designated FX forwards, €2.4 billion of euro-denominated debt serving as a net-investment hedge, and $800 million of interest-rate swaps.  A 95 %-confidence Monte-Carlo simulation indicates potential annual after-tax earnings swings of ±$314 million for currency, ±$18 million for interest-rate, and ±$7 million for commodity exposures.  A one-percent move in purchased-material prices equates to an $85 million pre-tax earnings effect, and a ten-percent change in global energy prices would move earnings by about $45 million.  

---  

### Concluding Perspective  

Despite a 3.2-percent topline contraction and extraordinary litigation and PFAS-exit costs, 3M preserved a 19.1 percent GAAP operating margin, generated $5.6 billion of operating cash, and delivered $10.18 of diluted EPS.  Management prioritized price discipline, structural cost reductions, and portfolio actions such as the $2.7 billion Food Safety split-off and initiation of a Health Care spin-off.  The balance sheet remains strong with net-debt-to-EBITDA below 2×, ample liquidity, and pension plans nearly fully funded.  Looking forward, 3M expects continued macro and litigation uncertainty but plans to exit PFAS manufacturing by 2025, complete the Health Care separation, sustain dividend growth, and invest in growth, productivity, and sustainability to support long-term shareholder value.
