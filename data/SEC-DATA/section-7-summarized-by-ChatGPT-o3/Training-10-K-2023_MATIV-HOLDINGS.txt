# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations - Summary

The following summary condenses Mativ Holdings, Inc.’s 2023 Form 10-K MD&A while retaining all essential financial information, comparative data, strategic developments and forward-looking considerations.

---

## EP Divestiture  

• On 1 August 2023 Mativ accepted Evergreen Hill Enterprise’s offer to purchase its Engineered Papers (“EP”) business for **$620 million cash** (subject to customary adjustments).  
• Sale closed **30 November 2023**; the Company recorded a **pre-tax gain of $176.3 million ($170.0 million net)** in discontinued operations and exited all tobacco-based product markets.  
• EP results are reported as discontinued operations; all MD&A figures below relate to continuing operations.

---

## Merger (Neenah)  

• The Neenah, Inc. merger closed **6 July 2022**; Neenah is now a wholly-owned subsidiary.  
• 2023 therefore reflects a **full-year contribution** from Neenah versus a partial-year contribution in 2022.

---

## Critical Accounting Estimates (highlights)  

• Complex global tax calculations; valuation allowances and uncertain-tax positions are material.  
• Goodwill and indefinite-lived intangibles are tested annually and when impairment indicators arise; a **$401.0 million non-cash goodwill impairment** was recorded in Q3 2023.  
• Property, plant and equipment represents 25 % of total assets; impairment reviews accompany restructuring actions.  
• Business-combination asset valuations rely on discounted-cash-flow and market multiples.

• Growth derived almost entirely from the incremental Neenah revenues; base volumes declined.

### Gross Profit  

Gross margin slipped to **17.6 %** (2022: 18.7 %) as cost inflation outpaced pricing gains.

### Non-manufacturing Expenses  

Selling, R&D and general expenses rose **5.8 %** to **$346.1 million** (17.0 % of sales); increase tied to full-year Neenah integration.

### Restructuring and Other Impairments  

• **$22.6 million** in 2023 (ATM $12.4 m; FBS $9.9 m).  
• ATM expense reflects fixed-asset impairments at facilities slated for 2024 closure or sale.  
• FBS expense relates to asset impairments at Eerbeek, Netherlands after adverse market shifts.

## Outlook  

Beginning Q1 2024 Mativ will report two new segments:  
1. **Filtration & Advanced Materials (FAM)** – filtration, industrial netting, protective solutions; expected to outpace long-term economic growth in the U.S./Europe and withstand downturns better than GDP.  
2. **Sustainable & Adhesive Solutions (SAS)** – tape, release liners, healthcare, packaging and specialty papers; growth expected roughly in line with long-term GDP.

---

## Market-Risk Highlights  

• A 10 % adverse foreign-exchange move on transactional exposures would reduce pre-tax earnings by **≈ $44 million** (unhedged basis).  
• A 100 bp rise in rates on current floating-rate debt (post-swap) would cut pre-tax earnings by **≈ $2.6 million**.  
• Raw materials: every 10 % change equals **≈ $21.4 million** (resin) and **≈ $12.8 million** (wood pulp) impact on annual pre-tax earnings, absent pricing offsets.  
• Energy: 10 % price swing affects annual pre-tax earnings by **≈ $6 million**.
