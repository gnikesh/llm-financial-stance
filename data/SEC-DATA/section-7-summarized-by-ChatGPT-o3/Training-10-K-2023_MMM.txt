# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations – Summary  

The following narrative condenses 3M’s 2023 Form 10-K MD&A while retaining the full financial context—especially data tied to sales, EPS, debt, cash-flow, margins, tax, and major special-item adjustments.  Headings mirror the Company’s original presentation.

---

## Overview  

3M operates through four global business segments—Safety & Industrial; Transportation & Electronics; Health Care (slated for a mid-2024 spin-off of which 3M initially intends to keep 19.9%); and Consumer.  Effective 1Q-23 the Company:  
1. realigned its Consumer segment from four to three divisions and updated the CODM profitability metric; and  
2. broadened its non-GAAP definition of “special items” to exclude the results of manufactured PFAS products, which 3M will exit by end-2025.  

For 2023, special-item litigation charges reached **$15.2 billion pre-tax**—principally the **$10.5 billion** Public Water Systems (PWS) PFAS settlement and the **$4.3 billion** Combat Arms Earplugs (CAE) settlement (both inclusive of imputed interest).  

---

## Results of Operations  

### Consolidated Sales and Earnings  
• Net sales fell **4.5 %** to **$32.68 billion** (-3.2 % excluding currency and M&A).  
• GAAP net loss was **$(6.99) billion** (vs. $5.78 billion income in 2022) owing to the litigation charges.  
• GAAP diluted EPS swung to **$(12.63)** from **$10.18** in 2022.  
• Adjusted (non-GAAP) diluted EPS, which excludes special items, declined 6 % to **$9.24** (2022: $9.88).  

### Bridge of Diluted EPS (2023 vs 2022, in $/share)  
| Key driver | ΔEPS | Comment |  
|------------|------|---------|  
| Organic growth, productivity & other | +0.30 | Pricing discipline and productivity outpaced lower volumes in electronics/consumer retail.  
| Restructuring & related | –0.62 | $437 million restructuring expense plus accelerated depreciation.  
| Raw-material/logistics inflation | –0.24 | Carry-over cost pressure.  
| FX translation/transaction | –0.17 | ~$162 million operating headwind after hedging.  
| Share count | +0.21 | Buybacks accretive; 2023 repurchase only $33 million vs $1.5 billion in 2022.  

### Cost Structure (as % of sales)  
2023 cost of sales 56.5 % (-56.2 % in 2022); SG&A spiked to 65.9 % of sales (26.5 % in 2022) due to lawsuit settlements, restructuring, and divestiture costs; R&D edged up to 5.6 %.

### Taxes  
GAAP effective tax rate was **27.8 % on a pre-tax loss** (2022: 9.6 %).  On an adjusted basis, the tax rate was **17.5 %, unchanged from 2022**.

## Financial Condition and Liquidity  

Cash & marketable securities ended 2023 at **$6.0 B** (up from $3.9 B).  
Net debt (non-GAAP) fell **$2.0 B to $10.0 B** on higher cash.  
Total debt stable at **$16.0 B**; $1.8 B of commercial paper outstanding (none in 2022).  
EBITDA/interest covenant on the undrawn $4.25 B revolver stood at 15× (minimum 3×).  
Credit ratings: Moody’s A3 (neg), S&P BBB+ (CreditWatch-neg), Fitch A- (stable).  

Free cash flow improved as operating cash rose $1.1 B on inventory reductions; 2023 capex was $1.6 B (2024 plan $1.5-1.7 B).  

Shareholder returns: dividend raised 1 % to $1.51/quarter; buybacks only $33 M with $4.2 B still authorized.

---

## Financial Instruments and Market Risk  

• A 10 % instantaneous FX move would change the fair value of hedging contracts by ~$175 M and of non-functional debt hedges by ~$192 M.  
• A 100 bp interest-rate swing would move annual pre-tax earnings by ~$13 M on floating-rate debt and swaps.  
• Commodity exposure managed via supply contracts; no derivative commodities used.

---

## Forward-Looking Considerations  

Management continues to prioritize:  
1. completing the Health Care spin in 1H-24;  
2. executing the PFAS manufacturing exit by 2025;  
3. delivering productivity benefits from 2023-24 restructurings; and  
4. maintaining a strong balance sheet to fund dividends, select acquisitions, and potential additional litigation outflows.
