## Overview

### Our Company and Our Businesses  
NVIDIA, headquartered in Santa Clara, California, pioneered accelerated computing and now addresses four main end-markets—Data Center, Gaming, Professional Visualization and Automotive—through two reporting segments: Compute & Networking and Graphics.

---

## Recent Developments, Future Objectives and Challenges

* **Supply-chain and inventory:** Purchase obligations and prepaid supply agreements now exceed one-half of aggregate supply; inventory provisions of **$2.17 billion** were recorded in FY 2023 for Ampere-based Gaming and Data Center products ($1.04 billion on-hand inventory, $1.13 billion purchase commitments).  
* **Product transitions:** Simultaneous shipment of new and legacy architectures (e.g., Hopper, Ada Lovelace) may create revenue volatility.  
* **New AI cloud services:** Multi-year agreements signed in 2H FY 2023—including “NVIDIA DGX Cloud” and other AI-as-a-service offerings—are hosted on CSP partners’ infrastructure; success and monetisation remain uncertain.  
* **U.S.–China export controls:** A100/H100-class device exports now require licences; NVIDIA has offered the A800 alternative and is relocating certain R&D/supply functions out of China.  
* **COVID-19:** Office re-openings resumed, yet Chinese lockdowns hurt Gaming demand and broadened logistical pressures.  
* **Russia:** Direct sales ceased and operations closed in FY 2023; FY 2022 revenue exposure was immaterial.  
* **Arm acquisition termination:** A $1.35 billion termination charge was booked in FY 2023.

## Market Platform Highlights

* **Data Center:** Revenue **$15.01 billion** (+41 %). Growth driven by hyperscalers and CSP purchases linked to multi-year AI cloud deals. Hopper H100, Spectrum-4 400 Gbps networking and Quantum-2 began shipping.  
* **Gaming:** Revenue **$9.07 billion** (–27 %). Channel inventory reductions and macro/COVID headwinds. Ada Lovelace RTX 4090/4080/4070 Ti launched; DLSS 3 introduced.  
* **Professional Visualization:** Revenue **$1.54 billion** (–27 %). OEM inventory corrections; new Ampere RTX workstation GPUs and Omniverse Cloud/ACE announced.  
* **Automotive:** Revenue **$0.903 billion** (+60 %). DRIVE Orin SOC production, next-gen DRIVE Thor unveiled.  
* **OEM & Other:** Down 61 %, reflecting notebook OEM weakness and CMP decline (CMP FY 2023 revenue negligible vs $0.55 billion FY 2022).

## Critical Accounting Policies and Estimates

Key judgments involve inventories (lower-of-cost-or-NRV write-downs and excess purchase commitments), revenue recognition for product/long-term IP arrangements, and income-tax valuation allowances. Inventory write-offs adversely impacted FY 2023 gross margin by **7.5 percentage points**.

A change in useful life estimates for servers, storage, networking (3 → 4–5 years) and assembly/test equipment (5 → 7 years) takes effect in FY 2024, expected to raise FY 2024 operating income by **≈ $133 million**.

### Gross Profit and Margin  
FY 2023 gross margin fell to **56.9 %** due chiefly to the **$2.17 billion** inventory charge. Prior-year charge was $0.354 billion.

### Operating Expenses  
* R&D: **$7.34 billion** (27.2 % of revenue, +39 % YoY) — higher headcount, compensation, data-center infrastructure and engineering costs.  
* SG&A: **$2.44 billion** (9.1 % of revenue, +13 % YoY).  
* Arm termination: **$1.35 billion** (5.0 % of revenue).  
Total opex reached **41.3 %** of revenue (27.6 % FY 2022).

### Other Income (Expense), Net  
Interest income surged to **$267 million** (higher yields); interest expense **$262 million** (reflecting $5 billion 2021 note issue). Mark-to-market losses on strategic investments drove the **$(48) million** “Other, net.”

### Income Taxes  
Tax benefit **$187 million** (–4.5 % effective rate) vs FY 2022 expense of $189 million (+1.9 %). FY 2023 rate reflects R&D capitalization under the TCJA, higher FDII deduction, stock-based-compensation benefits and lower profitability.

## Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk

* **Investment/interest-rate risk:** A ±50 bp parallel yield-curve shift would change investment portfolio fair value by **≈ $17 million**. Fixed-rate debt eliminates P&L rate exposure.  
* **Foreign-exchange risk:** Hedging programme offsets operating-expense and balance-sheet exposures (primary hedge: Israeli shekel). A 10 % USD strengthening would lower OCI hedging balances by **≈ $112 million**; a 10 % adverse move on balance-sheet hedges would cut pre-tax income by **≈ $36 million**, offset by remeasurement gains on underlying monetary items.

### Overall Financial Position and Outlook

Despite flat topline performance, FY 2023 profitability compressed as NVIDIA absorbed large inventory write-downs, Arm deal costs and macro-driven Gaming weakness. Liquidity remains ample (**$13.3 billion**), leverage manageable (**$11 billion** in notes, next maturity $1.25 billion) and shareholder-return capacity intact. Management expects FY 2024 operating income to benefit from lower depreciation ($133 million) and will continue to focus on Data Center expansion, AI cloud services and next-generation product ramps while navigating export-control, supply-chain and macroeconomic challenges.
