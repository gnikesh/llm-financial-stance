# ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT’S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS — SUMMARY  

The following text condenses Alphabet’s Form 10-K MD&A while preserving every material financial metric, comparison, and qualitative statement. Headings mirror the source document; figures are stated exactly as reported.  

---

## Understanding Alphabet’s Financial Results  
Alphabet reports three segments: Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets. All non-Google businesses are aggregated in Other Bets (see Note 15).  

---

## Trends in Our Business and Financial Effect  

* Digital advertising is still migrating on-line, but post-COVID growth is moderating amid rising competition for users and advertisers.  
* Users access products through more devices (smartphones, wearables, CTVs, smart-home), enabling new ad formats that grow revenue but generally compress margins; Traffic Acquisition Costs (TAC) are expected to rise with revenue, and the TAC rate will fluctuate with mix shifts.  
* Expansion of newer ad formats (e.g., YouTube, Play) and AI-driven products could alter monetization; these formats monetize below traditional Search.  
* Emerging-market usage (India, etc.) is rising. These regions monetize more slowly and face FX volatility; international revenues face currency risk despite a hedging program.  
* Non-advertising revenues from Cloud, subscriptions, platforms, and devices are growing but carry structurally lower margins; Pixel hardware negatively affects consolidated margin.  
* Significant OpEx and CapEx (servers, network, data centers, AI infrastructure) will continue.  
* The legal and regulatory environment is increasingly complex (U.S. DOJ antitrust, EU DMA/DSA, etc.), potentially raising costs and changing practices.  
* Alphabet continues to hire globally and maintain competitive compensation.  

---

## Revenues and Monetization Metrics  

Alphabet recognizes revenue from:  
1. Google advertising (Search & other, YouTube ads, Google Network).  
2. Google subscriptions, platforms, and devices (YouTube TV, Music & Premium, Google One, Google Play, Pixel, etc.).  
3. Google Cloud (Google Cloud Platform, Google Workspace).  
4. Other Bets (health-care and internet services).  

Macro items affecting revenue: FX rates, pricing structures, macro-economy, new launches, and seasonality.  

Key advertising metrics:  
* Paid clicks (+7% YoY) and cost-per-click (+1%) on Search & other.  
* Impressions (-5%) and cost-per-impression (flat) on Google Network.  

---

## Costs and Expenses  

• Cost of revenues = TAC + Other costs (compensation for data centers, content acquisition, depreciation, device inventory). TAC on Network ads remains proportionally higher than on Search.  
• Operating expenses = R&D, Sales & Marketing, G&A. SBC vests over four years; depreciation benefits from the 2023 server/network life extension (from 4 yrs to 6 yrs).

## Financial Condition  

### Liquidity  

As of 12/31/23 Alphabet held $110.9 bn in cash, cash equivalents and short-term marketable securities (time deposits, money-market funds, government/corporate bonds, MBS/ABS, marketable equities). Management expects these plus operating cash flows to cover all obligations for the next 12 months and “foreseeable future.”

### Capital Expenditures  

CapEx was $32.3 bn (vs $31.5 bn 2022), focused on technical infrastructure (servers, network, data-center land/buildings) and office facilities. 2024 CapEx is expected to increase to support AI initiatives. Depreciation on property & equipment: $11.9 bn (down from $13.5 bn) after useful-life change.  

### Leases  

• Operating lease assets: $2.9 bn (2023).  
• Future operating lease payments: $17.7 bn (weighted-avg 8 yrs), incl. $3.2 bn within 1 yr.  
• Not-yet-commenced leases: $4.0 bn (2024-2026 start).  
• 2023 operating lease expense: $4.5 bn.  

### Financing Facilities & Debt  

• $10.0 bn commercial-paper program (none outstanding 12/31/23).  
• $10.0 bn revolving credit (undrawn): $4.0 bn matures 2024; $6.0 bn matures 2028; interest tied to sustainability metrics.  
• Senior unsecured notes: $12.9 bn outstanding; future interest: $3.8 bn (short- & long-term).

# ITEM 7A. QUANTITATIVE AND QUALITATIVE DISCLOSURES ABOUT MARKET RISK — SUMMARY  

## Foreign Currency  

• 10 % adverse FX move on monetary items → ‑$503 mm pretax impact (net of hedges) at 12/31/23 (vs ‑$136 mm 2022).  
• Cash-flow hedges: 10 % USD weakening would lower AOCI by ~$1.5 bn.  
• Net-investment hedges: 10 % USD weakening would lower CTA by ~$946 mm.  

## Interest Rates  

Value-at-Risk on marketable debt securities (95 % one-day):  
• 12/31/22: $256 mm (avg $198 mm).  
• 12/31/23: $296 mm (avg $271 mm).  

## Equity Investments  

• Marketable equities: $6.0 bn; 10 % adverse move → ‑$597 mm.  
• Non-marketable equities (measurement-alternative): $28.8 bn; valuations inherently volatile.  
• Equity-method investments: $1.7 bn; subject to impairment if fair value falls below carrying value.  

---

### Debt, EPS, Sales Targets  

Alphabet carries $12.9 bn of senior unsecured notes, but no short-term borrowings; diluted EPS was $5.80 in 2023, up 27 %. Consolidated sales (revenues) reached $307.4 bn, up 9 % (10 % in constant currency).
