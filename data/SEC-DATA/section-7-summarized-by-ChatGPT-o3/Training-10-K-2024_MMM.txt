# Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations - Summary

(Amounts in U.S. dollars unless otherwise noted • $ = millions, except per-share data)**

---

## Overview  
3M completed the spin-off of its former Health Care business on 1 April 2024, distributing 80.1 % of Solventum Corporation to shareholders and retaining a 19.9 % stake valued at $2.3 billion. 2024 results therefore reflect only the three continuing segments—Safety & Industrial, Transportation & Electronics, and Consumer—together with “Corporate and Unallocated” and “Other.”

Key 2024 consolidated highlights versus 2023:  

* Net sales: **$24.58 billion, -0.1 %** (adjusted, excl. PFAS: **$23.63 billion, +1.3 %**).  
* GAAP diluted EPS from continuing operations: **$7.26** (2023: ‑$15.17).  
* Adjusted diluted EPS (ex-special items): **$7.30**, up 21 % from $6.04.  
* GAAP net income from continuing operations: **$4.01 billion** (2023 loss: $8.40 billion).  
* Adjusted effective tax rate: **19.6 %** (2023: 19.2 %).  
* Total debt fell to **$13.0 billion** (-$3.0 billion); net debt nearly halved to **$5.3 billion**.  
* Cash & equivalents rose to **$7.7 billion**.  
* Share repurchases: **$1.80 billion**; new $7.5 billion program authorized February 2025.  
* Dividends paid 2024: **$3.61 per share**; Q1-2025 dividend raised 4 % to $0.73.  
* 2024 capital spending: **$1.10 billion**; 2025 plan ≈ $1.1 billion.  
* Pension plans 95 % funded worldwide; $0.8 billion non-cash settlement charge recorded.  
* PFAS and Combat Arms Earplug (CAE) settlements: up to **$18.5 billion** through 2036; $5.0 billion paid by year-end 2024.

## Capital Allocation  

1. **Organic investment** – R&D (4.4 % of sales) and CAPEX (~$1.1 bn).  
2. **Dividends** – 66th consecutive year of increases; payout ratio 50 % of GAAP EPS.  
3. **Share repurchase** – $1.80 bn in 2024; open-ended $7.5 bn authorization.  
4. **M&A / Portfolio** – Continuing PFAS product exit by 2025; plan to divest remaining Solventum stake within five years.  
5. **Debt management** – Target strong “A” credit metrics; near-term maturities comfortably covered by liquidity profile.

---

## Critical Accounting Estimates  
No change in policies; key judgments involve legal contingencies (ASC 450), pension assumptions (discount rate 5.64 % U.S., expected return 8.00 % for 2025), and goodwill (no impairments in 2024, goodwill $6.3 billion).

---

## Financial Instruments and Market Risks  

* FX: 10 % instantaneous currency move would swing hedge portfolio fair value by ~$24 million.  
* Interest rates: 100 bp move affects annual pre-tax earnings by ~$10 million.  
* Commodity exposure managed via supply contracts; no derivatives outstanding.  

---

## Outlook  
Management expects:  

* Continued PFAS production wind-down by end-2025.  
* Ongoing productivity benefits from 2023-2025 restructuring (cumulative pre-tax charges to date: $646 million).  
* 2025 CAPEX around $1.1 billion; pension cash contributions $100 – $200 million.  
* Maintenance of strong liquidity, disciplined capital deployment, and execution of litigation settlement payment schedules while targeting sustainable EPS growth.
