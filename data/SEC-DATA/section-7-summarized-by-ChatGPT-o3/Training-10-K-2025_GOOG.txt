# ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT’S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS — SUMMARY

Please read this condensed discussion together with the company’s risk factors, consolidated financial statements and related notes. All figures are stated in U.S. dollars.

## Understanding Alphabet’s Financial Results

Alphabet reports three operating segments — Google Services, Google Cloud and Other Bets — plus Alphabet-level activities. For the year ended 31 December 2024:

* Consolidated revenue reached **$350.0 billion**, up **14 %** (15 % in constant currency) versus 2023.  
* Diluted EPS rose **39 %** to **$8.04**.  
* Net income increased **36 %** to **$100.1 billion**.  
* Operating income was **$112.4 billion**; operating margin expanded from 27 % to **32 %**.

## Trends in the Business and Their Financial Effect

Management continues to highlight:  
1. Ongoing but moderating shift of users and advertisers online amid intensifying competition, including from AI-based offerings.  
2. Diversified device usage, which lifts revenue yet pressures margins through higher TAC and lower mobile margins.  
3. Expansion of AI functionality in Search and other products that may alter monetisation patterns.  
4. Growing revenue contribution from emerging markets that initially monetise at lower rates and are sensitive to FX swings.  
5. Rapidly growing non-advertising revenues (cloud, subscriptions, devices) that typically carry lower margins.  
6. Substantial future operating and capital spending — especially on AI-supportive technical infrastructure — alongside continued M&A.  
7. A complex, evolving regulatory landscape potentially affecting costs, practices and results.  
8. Ongoing investment in global workforce.

## Costs and Expenses

### Cost of Revenues  

Total cost of revenues rose **10 %** to **$146.3 billion**.  
• TAC increased $4.0 billion to **$54.9 billion**; TAC rate fell to 20.7 % (lower Network mix) despite higher mobile TAC.  
• Other costs grew $9.0 billion, led by YouTube content acquisition, depreciation and data-centre operations.

## Other Income (Expense), Net

OI&E swung from **$1.4 billion** to **$7.4 billion**, principally on $3.7 billion net gains in equity securities and reduced FX losses. Interest income also increased to $4.5 billion. A subsequent $8 billion unrealised gain on a private investment was recognised in January 2025.

## Taxes

Income tax provision rose to **$19.7 billion**; the effective tax rate climbed to **16.4 %** (from 13.9 %) owing to IRS rule changes on foreign tax credits and R&D amortisation, partly offset by higher SBC-related benefits.

## Financial Condition and Liquidity

* Cash, cash equivalents and short-term marketable securities: **$95.7 billion**.  
* Operating cash flow: **$125.3 billion** (+23 %).  
* Investing cash outflow: **$45.5 billion**, driven by $52.5 billion of capex (mainly technical infrastructure).  
* Financing cash outflow: **$79.7 billion**, reflecting $62.0 billion share buybacks and $7.4 billion dividends.  
* Short-term commercial paper outstanding: **$2.3 billion**.  
* Long-term senior notes: **$11.9 billion**.  
* Revolving credit facilities: $10.0 billion unused.

### Capital Expenditures and Leases

Capex jumped to **$52.5 billion** (vs $32.3 billion) and is expected to rise further in 2025 for AI-oriented servers, network gear and data centres. Future operating lease obligations total $17.0 billion; finance lease obligations total $1.9 billion.

### Commitments and Contingencies

Purchase commitments and other contractual obligations stand at **$55.4 billion** ($32.5 billion short-term). Income taxes payable include $2.7 billion under the 2017 U.S. transition tax and $8.8 billion for uncertain positions.

### European Commission Matters

In Q3 2024 Alphabet paid **$3.0 billion** for the 2017 EC shopping fine after its final appeal failed. A €1.5 billion fine related to 2019 was annulled; the EC has appealed. Outstanding EC fines remain accrued.

## Shareholder Returns

During 2024 Alphabet repurchased **379 million** shares for **$62.0 billion** (73 million Class A; 306 million Class C). The Board authorised an additional **$70.0 billion** repurchase programme in April 2024. Quarterly dividends began in June 2024; total 2024 payouts were **$7.4 billion**.

## Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures about Market Risk

* A 10 % adverse FX move on net monetary positions would have reduced 2024 pre-tax income by **$135 million** after hedges (vs $503 million in 2023).  
* A 10 % U.S.-dollar weakening would lower AOCI by **$1.6 billion** for cash-flow hedges and **$0.66 billion** for net-investment hedges.  
* One-day 95 % VaR on the fixed-income portfolio was **$208 million** at year-end 2024 (down from $296 million in 2023).  
* A 10 % drop in the value of marketable equity securities would cut their fair value by **$508 million**. Non-marketable equity securities carried at $35.5 billion are subject to valuation volatility and potential impairment.

## Critical Accounting Estimates

Alphabet’s most significant judgments involve:  
• Fair-value measurements of non-marketable equity securities under the measurement-alternative method;  
• Useful-life assessments for rapidly evolving technical infrastructure;  
• Recognition and measurement of uncertain tax positions; and  
• Estimating loss contingencies in complex, often multi-jurisdictional legal and regulatory matters.
